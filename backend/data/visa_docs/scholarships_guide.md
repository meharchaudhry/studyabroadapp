# International Scholarships for Indian Students — Comprehensive Guide

## Overview

This guide covers the most prestigious and accessible scholarships available to Indian students for studying abroad. Scholarships can significantly reduce or eliminate the financial burden of international education.

---

## 1. UK Scholarships

### Chevening Scholarship
- **Funder:** UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)
- **For:** 1-year Master's degree at any UK university
- **Coverage:** Full tuition, monthly stipend (~£1,200/month in London), return flights, visa costs, travel grants for Chevening events
- **Eligibility for Indians:** Indian citizen, minimum 2 years of full-time work experience after undergraduate degree, hold an undergraduate degree equivalent to a UK 2:1, apply to 3 UK universities before applying to Chevening
- **CGPA requirement:** Not explicitly stated, but a 2:1 equivalent (~CGPA 6.5/10 or above) is needed
- **Deadline:** Typically November (for scholarships starting the following September)
- **Website:** www.chevening.org
- **Selection:** Application + 2 references + interview for shortlisted candidates

### Commonwealth Scholarships
- **Funder:** Commonwealth Scholarship Commission (CSC), UK
- **For:** Master's and PhD at UK universities
- **Coverage:** Full tuition, monthly stipend, travel, warm clothing allowance
- **Eligibility:** Commonwealth citizen (India qualifies), strong academic record, application through home government (India's Ministry of Education nominates candidates)
- **Apply through:** Association of Indian Universities (AIU) + India's Ministry of Education
- **Deadline:** Typically December

### Rhodes Scholarship (Oxford)
- **Funder:** Rhodes Trust
- **For:** Postgraduate study at University of Oxford (any subject)
- **Coverage:** Full Oxford tuition, monthly stipend (~£18,180/year 2024), health coverage, flights
- **Eligibility:** Indian citizen, age 18–25, exceptional academic achievement and leadership
- **CGPA:** Near-perfect (CGPA 9.5+/10 typical for Indian Rhodes scholars)
- **India-specific component:** Offered for various Indian states — apply through Rhodes Trust India regional offices
- **Deadline:** Usually August for the following October start

## 2. USA Scholarships

### Fulbright-Nehru Master's Fellowships
- **Funder:** US-India Educational Foundation (USIEF), jointly funded by US State Department and Indian government
- **For:** 1–2 year Master's degree at accredited US universities
- **Coverage:** Tuition, living stipend, travel, insurance, a book and equipment allowance
- **Eligibility:** Indian citizen, Bachelor's degree from a recognized Indian institution, minimum 3 years professional experience, strong English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS not mandatory for this scholarship but helps)
- **Fields prioritized:** Arts, education, community leadership, journalism, social sciences, STEM (varies by year)
- **Apply at:** USIEF India (www.usief.org.in)
- **Deadline:** Typically July for scholarships starting the following August

### Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowships
- **For:** Enrolled doctoral students in India wanting to conduct research at a US institution for 6–9 months
- **Coverage:** Partial — stipend, housing, health insurance, no tuition (you remain enrolled in India)

### Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation Scholarship
- **Funder:** Private foundation based in India
- **For:** Study at a top 30 US or UK university OR leading EU institution
- **Coverage:** Up to USD 100,000 total (tuition + living)
- **Eligibility:** Indian citizen, age 30 or below at time of applying, outstanding academic/professional record, admitted to a top-ranked university
- **Deadline:** April (for the same year's intake)
- **Website:** www.inlaksfoundation.org

## 3. Germany Scholarships

### DAAD Scholarships (German Academic Exchange Service)
- **Funder:** German Federal Government through DAAD (Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst)
- **For:** Study and research scholarships at German universities
- **Types relevant for Indians:**
  - **DAAD Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS):** Full scholarship for Master's programs with a development angle — covers tuition, stipend (€934/month for graduates, €1,200/month for PhD candidates), health insurance, travel
  - **DAAD Research Grants:** For doctoral and post-doctoral research at German institutions (4–24 months)
  - **Helmut Schmidt Programme:** For professionals in public policy, economics, law, engineering
  - **DAAD-IIT Master's Sandwich Programme:** Specific for IIT students to spend part of their Master's at a German university
- **Apply at:** www.daad.de/en/ and in-DAAD office in New Delhi
- **Deadline:** Varies by program — typically October–December

### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarships
- **Funder:** Heinrich Böll Foundation (associated with Green Party, Germany)
- **For:** Undergraduate, Master's, and PhD scholarships in Germany
- **Coverage:** Monthly stipend (€934 for Bachelor's/Master's; €1,200 for PhD) + study and social allowances
- **Eligibility:** Strong social commitment, above-average academic achievement
- **Open to all nationalities** including Indians
- **Apply:** www.boell.de/en/foundation/scholarships

## 4. Australia Scholarships

### Australia Awards Scholarships
- **Funder:** Australian Government (Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade — DFAT)
- **For:** Full Master's and PhD scholarships
- **Coverage:** Full tuition, return flights, establishment allowance, living stipend (approximately AUD 3,000/month), Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)
- **Eligibility:** Indian citizen, work in a development-related field, nominated development-priority fields (public health, agriculture, governance, etc.)
- **Apply at:** www.australiaawards.gov.au
- **Deadline:** Typically April–May for January intake

### Endeavour Leadership Program (discontinued in 2019, replaced by Australia Awards)

### Destination Australia
- **Funder:** Australian Government + participating universities
- **For:** Study in regional Australia (outside major cities)
- **Coverage:** Up to AUD 15,000/year for up to 4 years
- **Eligibility:** International students, must study at a regional Australian university

## 5. Canada Scholarships

### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
- **Funder:** Canadian Government
- **For:** Doctoral (PhD) studies at Canadian universities
- **Coverage:** CAD 50,000/year for 3 years
- **Eligibility:** Nominated by a Canadian university; exceptional academic achievement and leadership
- **Apply:** Nominated by university — cannot apply independently

### IDRC Research Awards
- **Funder:** International Development Research Centre (Canada)
- **For:** Doctoral and postdoctoral research on development issues
- **Coverage:** CAD 20,000–50,000 depending on type
- **Eligible for Indians**

### University-Specific:
- University of Toronto Lester B. Pearson Scholarship (undergrad only, full ride)
- McGill University Fellowships for graduate students
- UBC Graduate Fellowship

## 6. European Country-Specific Scholarships

### Netherlands — Holland Scholarship (Nuffic)
- **Amount:** €5,000 one-time grant
- **For:** Non-EU students starting Bachelor's or Master's in the Netherlands
- **Apply through:** Dutch university at time of admission application

### Sweden — Swedish Institute Scholarships for Global Professionals (SISGP)
- **Coverage:** Monthly living grant (SEK 11,000), travel grant, insurance
- **For:** Master's programs at Swedish universities in specific themes (sustainability, democracy, global security)
- **Apply at:** www.si.se/scholarships

### Norway — Quota Scheme (discontinued but many Norwegian universities offer tuition-free education to all)

### Denmark — Danish Government Scholarships (via universities)
- Several Danish universities offer partial/full scholarships for non-EU students in specific programs — check individual university websites.

## 7. Asian Country Scholarships

### Japan — MEXT (Ministry of Education) Scholarship
- **Funder:** Japanese Government
- **Types:** Research (Master's/PhD), Undergraduate, College of Technology, Specialist Training College, Teacher Training
- **Coverage:** Full tuition, monthly stipend (¥117,000 for research students; ¥143,000 for graduate), round-trip airfare
- **Eligibility:** Indian citizen, strong academic record (CGPA 6.5+/10 typically), age criteria varies
- **Apply through:** Embassy of Japan in India or directly to Japanese universities
- **Deadline:** Embassy route: May–June; University route: varies

### Singapore — Singapore Government Scholarship (MOE)
- For undergraduate study at NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD, SIT, SUSS
- **Tuition-free + monthly stipend**
- Competitive — typically for top IIT/NIT graduates or equivalent

### South Korea — KGSP (Global Korea Scholarship)
- (See South Korea Visa guide for full details)
- Full scholarship: tuition, stipend, airfare, Korean language training

### China — Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)
- Full scholarship (tuition, accommodation, stipend) for studying in China
- Increasingly popular with Indian students for MBBS and engineering
- Apply at: www.campuschina.org

## 8. Tips for Indian Scholarship Applicants

### Statement of Purpose (SOP)
- Every scholarship requires a compelling SOP — see the SOP guide document for detailed tips.

### Letters of Recommendation (LoR)
- Most scholarships require 2–3 academic/professional references.
- Choose recommenders who know your work deeply — professors who supervised your thesis, or managers who observed your leadership.

### CGPA Benchmark
| Scholarship Tier | Typical CGPA Required |
|---|---|
| Rhodes, Gates Cambridge, ETH Excellence | 9.5+/10 |
| Chevening, DAAD, MEXT | 7.5+/10 |
| Australia Awards, Fulbright-Nehru | 7.0+/10 |
| Holland Scholarship, SISGP | 6.5+/10 |

### Timeline
- Most scholarship applications open **12–18 months** before the program start date.
- Scholarship applications are due **before** university applications in many cases — plan carefully.

### Common Mistakes
1. Applying late (deadlines are strict — no extensions).
2. Generic SOP not tailored to the specific scholarship's goals.
3. Weak LoRs from professors who barely know the applicant.
4. Not checking citizenship or field-of-study eligibility.
5. Assuming the scholarship covers everything — read coverage details carefully.
