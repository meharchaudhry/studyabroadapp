# UK Graduate Route (Post-Study Work Visa) — Complete Guide

## What is the Graduate Route?

The UK Graduate Route is a post-study work visa that allows international students who have completed a degree at a UK university to remain in the UK after graduation to work or look for work. It was reintroduced in 2021 (after being abolished in 2012) and has been widely used by Indian students.

Unlike the Student visa, the Graduate Route does not require a job offer, salary threshold, or employer sponsorship to apply. You simply apply after completing your degree and get 2 or 3 years to work in any field.

**Duration:**
- **2 years** for bachelor's and master's degree holders
- **3 years** for PhD/doctoral degree holders

---

## Eligibility Requirements

### Academic Eligibility
- Must have completed a UK degree at bachelor's level or above
- Degree must be from a UKVI-licensed student sponsor (check the register at gov.uk)
- The degree must be a qualification leading to a degree — not just completing a single module or short course
- Integrated master's degrees (e.g., MEng, MPhys) qualify for 2-year Graduate Route

### Immigration Status Eligibility
- Must be physically in the UK when you apply
- Must currently hold a valid UK Student visa (or have held one and not had it expire)
- Cannot have studied on a Tier 4 visa that was for research purposes only
- Must not have been sponsored by the UK government for your studies (some scholarships restrict switching to Graduate Route)
- No criminal record for serious offences

### Government-Sponsored Students — Important Exception
If you were sponsored by the British government (e.g., Chevening Scholarship, Commonwealth Scholarship, certain British Council awards), you may be required to return to India before your Student visa expires and cannot apply for the Graduate Route.

---

## What You Can Do on the Graduate Route

### Permitted Activities
- Work in **any job** at any salary level for any employer — no restriction on job type
- Work for multiple employers simultaneously
- Take on freelance and self-employed work
- Work as a contractor
- Start or own a business
- Look for work (you don't need a job at the time of application)
- Pursue voluntary work
- Study (but cannot claim Student visa benefits)

### Not Permitted
- Claim public funds (benefits, Universal Credit, housing benefit, etc.)
- Extend the Graduate Route itself — it is a one-time, non-renewable permission
- Be the main applicant for certain types of leave (e.g., cannot come back on Graduate Route after leaving UK)

---

## Application Process

### Step 1: Confirm Completion of Degree
- Most universities confirm completion via a results letter, completion letter, or notification through their student portal
- You don't need the physical degree certificate to apply — a letter from the university confirming completion is sufficient
- Usually available 2–6 weeks after final exams/thesis submission

### Step 2: Apply Online
- Apply at **gov.uk/graduate-visa**
- You must apply while physically in the UK
- Apply at least 4 weeks before your Student visa expires to avoid overstaying

### Step 3: Documents Required
- Current valid passport
- Evidence of degree completion:
  - University letter confirming completion, OR
  - Official results transcript showing all requirements met, OR
  - Completion letter from the university's international office
- You do NOT need to provide proof of accommodation, finances, or employment

### Step 4: Biometrics
- If you already enrolled biometrics for your Student visa (all Student visa holders do): no new biometric appointment needed
- In some cases you may need to attend a UKVCAS (UK Visas and Citizenship Application Services) appointment — the online application will tell you

### Step 5: Fees
- **Visa fee: £715**
- No Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) on the Graduate Route itself — you keep using NHS as before
- Priority processing (5 working days): approximately £500 extra
- Standard processing: 8 weeks

Apply early — if your Student visa expires while your Graduate Route application is pending, you have "section 3C leave" (maintained status) while waiting, but you cannot travel outside the UK until you receive the Graduate Route.

## Switching to Skilled Worker Visa

Once you find qualifying employment, you can switch from the Graduate Route to a Skilled Worker visa while remaining in the UK. This is the most common next step for Indian students who want to stay in the UK long-term.

### Skilled Worker Requirements
- **Job offer:** From a UK employer licensed as a Home Office sponsor
- **Salary threshold (2024):** £26,200/year OR the going rate for the specific occupation, whichever is higher
- **Skill level:** Job must be at RQF Level 3 or above (roughly A-Level equivalent)
- **Application:** Apply from inside UK; fee £610 for in-country switch for up to 3 years
- **English language:** Demonstrated through your UK degree (you're exempt from proving English again)

### Points in the Skilled Worker System
Skilled Worker is a points-based system:
- Job offer from licensed sponsor: 20 points (mandatory)
- Salary at or above going rate: 20 points (mandatory)
- English language (UK degree): 10 points (mandatory)
- Meeting salary threshold OR acceptable tradeable points
Total needed: 70 points

### Occupation Shortage List (ISL)
Some occupations on the Immigration Salary List (formerly Shortage Occupation List) have lower salary thresholds:
- Nurses, social workers, engineers, some IT roles
- Check gov.uk for current list

---

## Can You Extend the Graduate Route?

**No.** The Graduate Route is explicitly a one-time, non-renewable permission. You cannot extend it.

Once your Graduate Route expires:
- Switch to Skilled Worker before it expires if employed (this is the most common route)
- Apply for Global Talent visa if you qualify (exceptional talent/promise in your field)
- Apply for any other eligible visa category
- Leave the UK if none of the above applies

---

## NHS Access on Graduate Route

Graduate Route holders have maintained their NHS rights from the Student visa period. However:
- When you switch to Skilled Worker visa, you pay the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) — currently £1,035/year (2024 rate)
- The IHS is paid upfront when applying for Skilled Worker (e.g., 3-year Skilled Worker = 3 × £1,035 = £3,105 IHS)

On the Graduate Route itself, no additional IHS is payable — your prior IHS payment covers the period.

## Settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain) Pathway

The Graduate Route counts toward the 5-year continuous lawful residence requirement for ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain — equivalent to permanent residence).

**Typical Indian student pathway to UK settlement:**
1. Student visa: 1–4 years study
2. Graduate Route: 2 years (bachelor's/master's) or 3 years (PhD)
3. Skilled Worker: 5 years required for ILR
4. ILR: Apply after 5 years on Skilled Worker

The Graduate Route time does NOT count toward the 5-year ILR clock — only time on Skilled Worker (or other qualifying categories) counts toward ILR.

---

## Key Tips for Indian Students

1. **Apply before your Student visa expires** — you cannot apply from outside the UK. Once you leave the UK on your expired Student visa, the Graduate Route is gone.

2. **Get your completion letter early** — universities can be slow with these documents. Request it as soon as you receive your results notification.

3. **Job hunting timeline:** Most Indian students start job hunting 3–6 months before graduation. The Graduate Route gives you 2 years, but the earlier you secure a Skilled Worker sponsorship, the better.

4. **Salary negotiations:** Don't accept a salary below £26,200 from a licensed sponsor — it won't qualify for Skilled Worker switch. Many Indian students in tech/finance earn significantly above this.

5. **Using Graduate Route for UK work experience before Canada/Australia PR:** Some Indian students use the 2-year Graduate Route to gain UK work experience before applying for Canadian Express Entry or Australian 189/190, boosting their points score with international experience.

## FAQ

**Q: I completed a 1-year master's in the UK. Do I qualify for the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes. 1-year master's programs qualify for the 2-year Graduate Route, as long as the degree is from a UKVI-licensed provider and you held a Student visa for your studies.

**Q: Can I travel outside the UK while my Graduate Route application is pending?**
A: No. While your application is pending (section 3C leave), you cannot travel outside the UK. Once you receive the Graduate Route visa, you can travel freely.

**Q: My student visa has expired but I haven't applied for the Graduate Route. Can I still apply?**
A: No. Once your Student visa has expired without you applying for the Graduate Route, you are in the UK without leave. You would need to leave and cannot apply for the Graduate Route. This is why applying before expiry is critical.

**Q: Does the Graduate Route lead directly to ILR?**
A: No. Graduate Route does not lead directly to ILR. It counts toward overall residence but the ILR clock only starts from Skilled Worker (or other qualifying routes). You need 5 years on Skilled Worker specifically.

**Q: Can my spouse come on the Graduate Route?**
A: Your spouse/partner can apply for a Student Dependant visa alongside or after your Student visa. Once you switch to Graduate Route, your dependants must also extend or change status. Dependants of Graduate Route holders can also stay and work.
