# USA F-1 OPT, CPT and STEM Extension Guide for Indian Students

## Overview

After arriving in the USA on an F-1 student visa, Indian students have significant work authorization opportunities — both during studies (CPT) and after graduation (OPT and STEM OPT extension). Understanding and properly using these authorizations is critical because violations can result in immediate loss of F-1 status.

---

## CPT (Curricular Practical Training)

### What is CPT?
CPT is work authorization for F-1 students that allows you to work in a paid or unpaid internship, cooperative education program, or other practical training that is:
- An integral part of your established curriculum, OR
- Required by your degree program, OR
- Offered for academic credit by your university

CPT can be full-time (more than 20 hours/week) or part-time (20 hours or fewer per week).

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must have been enrolled full-time for **at least one full academic year** (typically 2 semesters) before CPT begins
  - Exception: Graduate programs that begin with an internship may allow earlier CPT — check with your DSO
- Must have a specific job offer or placement that connects to your field of study
- The practical training must be listed in your degree program requirements or be for academic credit
- Must maintain full-time enrollment during CPT (except during official breaks)

### CPT Authorization Process
1. Secure a job offer or internship placement (in writing from the employer)
2. Get a Cooperative Education Agreement, internship letter, or course registration form from your academic department
3. Submit all materials to your DSO (Designated School Official) at the International Student Office
4. DSO authorizes CPT in SEVIS (takes 1–3 business days)
5. DSO issues you an updated **I-20** with CPT authorization — this is your work authorization document
6. Start work on or after the start date shown on the CPT-authorized I-20 — NEVER before

### Cost: $0
There is no USCIS filing fee for CPT. It is processed entirely through your school.

### The Critical CPT Rule
**12 months of full-time CPT = ZERO OPT eligibility remaining**

This is the most important CPT rule. If you use full-time CPT for 12 or more months total during your F-1 program, you become ineligible for OPT.

- Part-time CPT (≤20 hrs/week) does NOT count toward this limit — you can do unlimited part-time CPT
- Multiple CPT authorizations are counted cumulatively: 3 months here + 4 months there = 7 months total full-time CPT
- Summer full-time CPT counts if you are enrolled at a US school during that summer

### What CPT Covers
- Paid internships at companies
- Unpaid research or clinical placements
- Co-op programs (alternating semesters of work and study)
- Externships if required by program

## OPT (Optional Practical Training)

### What is OPT?
OPT is 12 months of work authorization for F-1 graduates (or for current students in "pre-completion" OPT) to work in a job directly related to their major field of study. Unlike CPT, OPT is managed by USCIS (not just the school) and requires filing a formal application.

### Types of OPT

**Pre-Completion OPT:**
- Can be used while still enrolled
- Works full-time during official school breaks; part-time (≤20 hrs/week) during enrollment
- Any pre-completion OPT used is deducted from your 12-month OPT total
- Rarely used because it reduces post-completion time

**Post-Completion OPT (Most Common):**
- Used after graduation
- Allows full-time work for any employer
- 12 months total
- Can be extended by 24 months for STEM degrees (see below)

### OPT Application Timeline — Strict Deadlines

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Earliest you can apply | 90 days before program end date (graduation date on I-20) |
| Latest you can apply | 60 days after program end date |
| OPT start date | Can be up to 60 days after graduation |
| Employment must begin | Within 90 days of OPT start date (or status violation) |

**Critical:** Apply to USCIS at approximately **90 days before** your expected graduation date. USCIS processing takes 3–5 months, so applying early ensures your EAD arrives on time.

### OPT Application Process

**Step 1: Request OPT Recommendation from DSO**
- Notify your International Student Office you want to apply for OPT at least 2 weeks before you plan to file with USCIS
- DSO updates your SEVIS record and recommends OPT
- DSO issues a new I-20 with "OPT Recommendation" endorsement

**Step 2: File Form I-765 with USCIS**
- File online at uscis.gov/i-765
- Required documents:
  - Completed Form I-765
  - Copy of current passport photo page
  - Copy of I-94 (arrival/departure record)
  - Copy of current I-20 with OPT recommendation
  - Copy of all previous I-20s
  - Two passport-sized photos (if filing by mail)
- **Fee:** $520 (by credit card online or check/money order if by mail)

**Step 3: Receive EAD (Employment Authorization Document)**
- USCIS mails EAD to your US address
- Processing time: 3–5 months (apply early!)
- You can track status at uscis.gov
- **You cannot start work until:**
  1. Your EAD is physically in hand, AND
  2. The start date printed on the EAD has arrived

**While Waiting for EAD:**
Your I-20 with OPT recommendation + I-765 receipt notice together prove you have a pending OPT application. This is sufficient for most purposes but you CANNOT start work until EAD arrives.

### Where Can You Work on OPT?

On OPT, your employment must be:
- Directly related to your major field of study
- For at least 20 hours/week (if full-time OPT)
- With any lawful US employer (any size, any industry, as long as it relates to your degree)
- Self-employment is allowed if you establish your own legitimate business related to your field

### Unemployment Tracking on OPT — Very Important

**Maximum 90 days of unemployment** on regular OPT.

If you accumulate 90 days without employment, your F-1 status is violated — even if your EAD is still valid.

Unemployment accumulates from:
- Time between OPT start date and your first job
- Time between jobs
- Time if you are terminated or laid off

**Tips:**
- Start job hunting 6 months before graduation
- Maintain records of every application, interview, and offer
- Report your employer to your DSO within 10 days of starting work

## STEM OPT Extension (24 Additional Months)

### What is STEM OPT?
If you have a degree in a STEM field (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics), you can apply for a 24-month extension of your OPT, giving a total of 36 months of post-graduation work authorization.

This is the most significant benefit for Indian STEM graduates — 36 months is enough time to get through 3 H-1B lottery cycles.

### STEM OPT Eligibility Requirements

**You must:**
- Have a qualifying STEM degree (check the official DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List at ice.gov)
- Be working in a job directly related to your STEM degree
- Have an employer who is enrolled in **E-Verify** (a federal employment eligibility verification system)
- Have a valid regular OPT EAD or apply before it expires

**Common qualifying STEM degrees for Indians:**
- Computer Science (most common)
- Electrical Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Chemical Engineering
- Data Science / Analytics
- Biomedical Engineering
- Mathematics and Statistics
- Physics, Chemistry, Biology

### STEM OPT Application Timeline
- Apply up to **90 days before** your regular OPT expires
- File Form I-765 again with USCIS
- Fee: $520 again
- Include updated I-20 from DSO (with STEM OPT recommendation)

### Form I-983 (Training Plan) — Mandatory
The employer and student must complete Form I-983, which outlines:
- How the job is related to your STEM degree
- Learning objectives and goals
- Employer attestations (salary, hours, supervision)
- Must be submitted to DSO before STEM OPT begins
- USCIS may audit employers for compliance

### Reporting Requirements on STEM OPT
Every 6 months, you must confirm your current employment to your DSO by validating your I-983.

If you change jobs during STEM OPT:
- New employer must be E-Verify enrolled
- New Form I-983 required
- Report change to DSO within 10 days

### Unemployment During STEM OPT
- Maximum **150 days** of total unemployment allowed during STEM OPT period
- This is in addition to any unemployment during regular OPT (90 days)
- Total: up to 240 days across regular + STEM OPT combined

## H-1B Cap-Gap Protection

### What is Cap-Gap?
If your OPT or STEM OPT expires before October 1 (the H-1B start date), the Cap-Gap provision automatically extends your F-1 status and work authorization to bridge the gap.

### How It Works
1. Your employer files H-1B petition by April 1 (for the April H-1B lottery)
2. Your petition is selected in the lottery
3. If your OPT expires between April 1 and September 30:
   - Your F-1 status and OPT work authorization are automatically extended until October 1
   - No additional filing needed
4. On October 1, you change to H-1B status

### What If H-1B Petition is Not Selected?
- Your OPT ends on its original expiration date
- Must leave the USA or change to another immigration status
- Can apply for H-1B again next year (next lottery cycle)
- Many Indian students apply 3 times (over 3 years of STEM OPT) before getting selected

## SEVIS Reporting Requirements — Compliance Critical

| Event | What to Do | Deadline |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Start new job | Report employer name, address, job title to DSO | Within 10 days |
| Change employer | Report new employer details | Within 10 days |
| Change address | Update in SEVIS via DSO | Within 10 days |
| Layoff / lose job | Inform DSO; start unemployment clock | Immediately |
| Travel outside USA | Inform DSO before traveling | Before departure |
| Change degree program | Discuss with DSO | Before making change |

**Consequences of Non-Compliance:** SEVIS record becomes inactive → F-1 status considered violated → potential bars from re-entry to the USA.

## OPT to H-1B Pathway for Indian Students

This is the most common immigration pathway for Indian students in the USA:

1. Graduate → Apply for OPT (12 months)
2. Get STEM OPT extension (+24 months) = 36 months total work authorization
3. Year 1–3: Employer files H-1B petition in April lottery each year
4. H-1B lottery odds: ~18–25% per year (regular cap) + ~20% (master's cap-exempt for US master's)
5. If selected: Change status to H-1B from F-1 while in USA
6. H-1B → Green Card: Employer sponsors PERM → I-140 → I-485 (long wait for Indians due to backlog)

**Indian students with US master's degrees** get access to the "master's cap" lottery (6,800 advanced degree exemptions) before entering the general 65,000 cap lottery — improving overall odds.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I freelance or do contract work on OPT?**
A: Yes, self-employment is allowed on OPT if it is directly related to your major and you can clearly document it. Keep detailed records of contracts, income, and hours.

**Q: My STEM OPT employer fired me. What happens?**
A: Inform your DSO immediately. You have 150 days total unemployment allowance across STEM OPT. Start job hunting actively. If you find a new E-Verify employer, resume STEM OPT with them.

**Q: Does my degree from India count as STEM for the extension?**
A: No. The STEM OPT extension is based on your US degree. Your Indian degree is irrelevant — what matters is the degree you're graduating from in the USA.

**Q: I applied for OPT but my EAD hasn't arrived yet. Can I graduate and stay?**
A: Yes. As long as you applied for OPT before your graduation date and have the I-765 receipt notice, you maintain lawful F-1 status while waiting. You cannot work until EAD arrives, but you can stay in the USA.
