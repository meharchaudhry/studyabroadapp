# Complete Scholarship Guide for Indian Students Studying Abroad

## Overview

Scholarships can significantly reduce the financial burden of studying abroad. Indian students are eligible for scholarships from the Indian government, host country governments, universities, and private foundations. This guide covers the most significant opportunities with deadlines, eligibility criteria, and application tips.

---

## Government Scholarships — Indian Government

### National Overseas Scholarship (NOS)
**Offered by:** Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India

**For:** SC (Scheduled Caste) and ST (Scheduled Tribe) students; some categories for OBC and Denotified Tribes

**Covers:**
- Full tuition fees paid directly to the institution
- Living allowance: approximately ₹15,400/month (varies by country)
- Return air travel (economy class)
- Contingency allowance and medical insurance

**Eligibility:**
- Age: Under 35 years
- Family income: Below ₹6 lakh per annum
- Minimum qualification: Bachelor's degree with at least 60% marks for master's; master's for PhD
- Must secure admission to a recognized foreign university in approved subjects (STEM, economics, law, social sciences)

**Application:** socialjustice.nic.in
**Deadline:** Typically March–May annually; check portal for exact dates
**Number of awards:** Approximately 125 slots per year across all categories

---

### ICCR (Indian Council for Cultural Relations) Scholarships
**Offered by:** Government of India through ICCR

**For:** Indian nationals to study at foreign universities with ICCR bilateral agreements

**Covers:** Tuition fees + monthly living allowance (amount varies by country)

**Eligibility:**
- Open to all categories (no caste restriction)
- Specific to countries with bilateral ICCR agreements
- Age limits vary by scholarship; typically under 45

**Countries with ICCR scholarships:** UK, USA, Germany, France, Russia, Japan, South Korea, China, Brazil, Italy, Israel, Egypt, Malaysia, and 25+ others

**Application:** a2ascholarships.iccr.gov.in
**Total slots:** 3,200+ per year across all agreements

---

### J.N. Tata Endowment Loan Scholarship
**Offered by:** J.N. Tata Endowment, Mumbai

**Type:** Loan scholarship (must be repaid, but interest-free or very low interest)

**For:** Meritorious Indian students going abroad for higher studies

**Amount:** ₹4–10 lakh one-time grant + loan component
**Eligibility:** Applicants must be Indian citizens, have secured admission to a top foreign university, minimum 60% in last qualifying exam
**Application:** jntataendowment.org; typically April–May deadline

## United Kingdom Scholarships

### Chevening Scholarship (UK Government)
**Offered by:** Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO), UK Government

**Award:** Fully funded
**Covers:**
- Full tuition fees at any UK university for any 1-year master's program
- Monthly living allowance: approximately £1,391/month (2024 rate)
- Return flights (economy) from India to UK
- Arrival and departure allowances
- Health surcharge covered

**Eligibility:**
- Any nationality (citizens of countries with UK foreign relations)
- Minimum 2 years' work experience (after graduating)
- Bachelor's degree with minimum upper second-class honors equivalent
- Must commit to returning to India for at least 2 years after completing the scholarship
- IELTS 6.5+

**Competition:** Approximately 1,500 awards globally; India receives 100+ each year; highly competitive

**Application process:** Online at chevening.org; 2 application essays (leadership, studying in UK + career) + 3 references + university choices
**Deadline:** Early November (for September following year entry)
**Timeline:** Apply November → shortlisted February → interviews March–April → results May → enrollment September

---

### Commonwealth Scholarship (UK)
**Offered by:** Commonwealth Scholarship Commission with UK government funding

**For:** Students from Commonwealth nations (India is a Commonwealth member)

**Covers:** Full tuition, airfare, living stipend

**Types:**
- Commonwealth Shared Scholarship (joint with UK universities)
- Commonwealth Split-site Scholarships (partial study in UK)

**Application:** Through Association of Indian Universities (AIU) as National Nominating Agency
**Deadline:** December (India nominations)

---

### GREAT Scholarship (British Council + UK Universities)
**Award:** Minimum £10,000 per student (some up to full tuition)
**For:** Master's programs at participating UK universities
**Subjects:** Engineering, tech, entrepreneurship, hospitality, healthcare and more
**Indian students:** Dedicated India GREAT scholarships available
**Application:** Through individual participating UK universities; typical deadline February–March
**Website:** study-uk.britishcouncil.org/scholarships

---

### Scotland Saltire Scholarships
**Award:** £8,000 one-time award for 1-year study in Scotland
**For:** Indian students, Canadian students, US students (separate pools)
**Subjects:** STEM, creative industries, healthcare, social sciences
**Universities:** All Scottish universities (Edinburgh, Glasgow, St Andrews, Strathclyde, etc.)
**Deadline:** April annually
**Apply through:** Your Scottish university's scholarship portal

## United States Scholarships

### Fulbright-Nehru Master's Fellowships
**Offered by:** US-India Educational Foundation (USIEF) — joint US and Indian government funding

**Award:** Fully funded
**Covers:**
- Tuition at US university
- J-1 Exchange Visitor visa sponsorship
- Monthly stipend (approximately $1,600–2,000/month)
- Return airfare
- Health insurance

**Eligibility:**
- Indian citizens; age 22–35 for master's fellowships
- Minimum bachelor's with 55% marks
- IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL 90+
- Strong leadership and community engagement record
- Must return to India after fellowship (J-1 2-year home residency requirement)

**Fields:** Priority: STEM, management, public policy, social sciences, arts
**Deadline:** July 15 annually for fellowships starting August the following year
**Application:** usief.org.in; very competitive (~100 awards annually for India)

---

### Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowships
**For:** Indian PhD candidates to spend 6–9 months in USA conducting research
**Award:** Monthly stipend, health insurance, airfare
**Eligibility:** Currently enrolled in PhD program in India

---

### University Research Assistantship (RA) / Teaching Assistantship (TA)
**For:** Virtually all sciences, engineering, and social science PhD students in USA

**RA (Research Assistantship):**
- Funded by faculty research grants
- Full tuition waiver + stipend $18,000–28,000/year (varies by university and department)
- Assigned by your supervising professor

**TA (Teaching Assistantship):**
- Work as teaching assistant for 15–20 hrs/week
- Full tuition waiver + stipend $15,000–22,000/year
- Assigned by department

**How to get:** Apply to PhD programs; funded offers come with admission. Contact professors directly before/after applying.

## Canada Scholarships

### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship
**Award:** CAD $50,000/year for up to 3 years (total CAD $150,000)
**For:** Doctoral (PhD) students in natural sciences, health sciences, social sciences, humanities
**Eligibility:** Nominated through Canadian institution; international students eligible if enrolled at Canadian university
**Criteria:** Academic excellence + research potential + leadership
**Website:** vanier.gc.ca

### Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS)
**Award:** CAD $10,000–15,000 per year
**For:** Master's and PhD students at Ontario universities
**Distributed by:** Individual Ontario universities
**Apply through:** Your Ontario university's financial aid office

### University-Specific
**University of Toronto — Connaught International Scholarship:**
- Full tuition + stipend for top doctoral international students
- Awarded at admission; no separate application

**University of British Columbia — International Tuition Award:**
- $12,000/year tuition reduction for international PhD students
- Automatic consideration at admission

## Germany Scholarships

### DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service)
**Most popular scholarship for German study** — DAAD funds approximately 100,000 people annually worldwide.

**DAAD Scholarship for Foreign Students:**
- Monthly stipend: €934 (master's) or €1,200 (PhD/postdoc)
- Health and liability insurance allowance: €120/month
- Travel subsidy for flights
- Does NOT cover tuition (German public universities have no tuition for DAAD holders)

**Eligibility:**
- Strong academic record (75%+ equivalent recommended)
- IELTS 6.5+ or German B2 level
- Not more than 6 years after bachelor's degree for master's DAAD
- Maximum 1 completed previous year in Germany

**Application:** daad.de; through your German university portal OR direct to DAAD
**Deadline:** November–December for next year intake (varies by program)
**Number of awards to India:** Several hundred each year

---

### Deutschlandstipendium
**Award:** €300/month (€150 from German federal government + €150 from private company sponsoring it)
**For:** All students enrolled at German universities (including international students)
**How to apply:** Through your German university (each university runs its own program)
**Number of awards:** 26,000+ nationally per year
**Competition level:** Moderate; varies by university

---

## Netherlands Scholarships

### Holland Scholarship
**Award:** €5,000 one-time scholarship
**For:** Non-EEA students at Dutch research universities or universities of applied sciences
**Eligibility:** Bachelor's or master's first-year students; GPA above 80% equivalent
**Application:** Through your Dutch university; deadline February–March
**Note:** Not all Dutch universities participate; check your specific university

### Orange Tulip Scholarship
**Award:** Varies — €3,000 to full tuition depending on sponsor company/institution
**For:** International students at participating Dutch universities
**Application:** orangetulipscholarship.com; apply before March 1

### TU Delft Excellence Scholarship
**Award:** €24,000 for 1 year of master's study
**For:** Top 2% of international applicants to TU Delft master's programs
**Criteria:** Exceptional academic record, relevant research experience
**Apply through:** TU Delft application portal

## Australia Scholarships

### Australia Awards Scholarships
**Offered by:** Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade

**Award:** Fully funded
**Covers:** Tuition, return airfare, establishment allowance, living allowance (~AUD $28,000/year), health cover (OSHC)

**For:** Citizens of designated developing countries (India is eligible)
**Programs:** Master's and doctoral; some bachelor's in specific fields
**Commitment:** Must return to home country for 2 years after scholarship

**Application:** australiaawards.gov.au
**Deadline:** April 30 annually (for February following year intake)

---

### Research Training Program (RTP)
**For:** International PhD and research master's students at Australian universities
**Award:** Tuition fee offset (full tuition covered) + stipend approximately AUD $29,000/year (non-taxable)
**Eligibility:** Competitive; based on academic merit + research proposal quality
**Application:** Through individual Australian universities at time of PhD application

---

## France Scholarships

### Eiffel Excellence Scholarship (French Government)
**Offered by:** French Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs through Campus France

**Award:**
- Master's: €1,181/month for 12 or 24 months
- PhD: €1,400/month for 10 months/year
- Transportation contribution + health insurance + repatriation allowance
- Cultural events access

**Eligibility:**
- Age: Under 25 for master's; under 30 for PhD
- Must be nominated by your French university (you cannot apply directly)
- Excellent academic record

**Process:** Apply to French university → university nominates you for Eiffel scholarship → Campus France evaluates → notification February
**Deadline for university submission:** January each year

## Sweden Scholarships

### Swedish Institute Study Scholarships (SISGP)
**Award:**
- Full tuition (wherever applicable)

- Travel grant

**For:** One-year master's programs in Sweden
**Criteria:** Academic excellence + work experience (post-bachelor's) + sustainability leadership commitment
**Deadline:** January 10 each year
**Competition:** Highly competitive — approximately 350 awards globally
**Application:** si.se/en or studyinsweden.se/scholarships

## Application Strategy and Tips

### Start Early
Most scholarship deadlines are 6–12 months before the program start date:
- Chevening: November (for September entry the following year)
- Australia Awards: April (for February entry the following year)
- Fulbright-Nehru: July (for August entry the following year)

This means starting preparation 8–10 months before the deadline itself.

### Strong Scholarship Essays
Scholarship essays are different from admission SOPs:
- Focus on **leadership, community impact, and social contribution** (not just academic achievement)
- Explain specifically **what you will do with this education** to benefit India/your home country
- Be concrete: "I will return to work in renewable energy policy in India" is more compelling than "I want to contribute to society"
- Research each scholarship's values and align your narrative accordingly

### Multiple Applications
Apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously:
- Government scholarship (Chevening/Fulbright/Australia Awards) + university scholarship can sometimes stack
- Apply to your university's own scholarships even if applying for government scholarships
- Departmental scholarships, community scholarships, diaspora scholarships

### Build Your Profile for Competitive Scholarships
- **Work experience:** Chevening requires 2 years; start thinking about this during undergrad
- **Community leadership:** President of clubs, social entrepreneurship, volunteer work — scholarships value demonstrated leadership
- **Publications or research:** Especially valuable for research-focused scholarships
- **Strong references:** Choose referees who know your leadership AND academic capability, not just one or the other
