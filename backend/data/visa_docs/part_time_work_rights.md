# Part-Time Work Rights for Indian Students Abroad

## Overview

Work rights are attached to your student visa/permit. **Always check your visa conditions** — working more than allowed can result in visa cancellation, deportation, and a ban on re-entry. This guide covers official limits, how to obtain work authorization, and tax registration requirements.

---

## United Kingdom

### Hours Allowed
- **During term time:** Up to **20 hours per week**
- **During vacations (Christmas, Easter, summer):** Unlimited hours
- **Definition of "term time":** Your university's official academic term, not just when you have classes

### What You Can Do
- Work for any employer in the UK
- Self-employment is NOT permitted on a Student visa
- Cannot be a professional sportsperson or entertainer

### Work Authorization
- No separate permit needed — your visa vignette/BRP already grants work rights
- Employer will check your BRP/visa

### Tax Registration
- Get a National Insurance (NI) number: apply online at gov.uk or call DWP
- You will pay Income Tax if you earn more than £12,570/year (personal allowance)
- File a Self Assessment if you have multiple jobs or freelance

### Penalty for Violation
- Visa cancellation and removal from UK
- Bar on future UK visa applications

## United States

### F-1 Student Visa — On-Campus Work
- **Allowed:** Up to **20 hours per week** while school is in session
- **During official breaks (summer, winter):** Up to 40 hours/week
- No separate authorization needed — DSO records it in SEVIS

### CPT (Curricular Practical Training)
- Work authorization for internships integral to your degree curriculum
- Must be authorized by your DSO (Designated School Official) before starting
- If you use CPT for 12+ months full-time, you lose OPT eligibility

### OPT (Optional Practical Training)
- 12 months post-degree work authorization in your field of study
- Apply to USCIS through your DSO (Form I-765), $520 fee, apply 90 days before graduation
- STEM OPT Extension: additional 24 months if your degree is in STEM and employer is E-Verify

### Off-Campus Work
- Generally **not permitted** unless through CPT/OPT or severe economic hardship (very rare)
- Unauthorized off-campus work = immediate visa violation

### Tax Registration
- Apply for Social Security Number (SSN) once you have employment authorization
- File taxes annually (W-2 from employer, Form 1040-NR for non-residents)
- India-USA DTAA (Double Tax Avoidance Agreement): scholarship income often exempt

## Canada

### Study Permit — Work Rights
- **Off-campus:** Up to **20 hours per week** during academic sessions
- **Full-time during scheduled breaks** (summer, winter, spring)
- Applies if enrolled full-time at a designated learning institution (DLI) with a valid study permit

### PGWP (Post-Graduate Work Permit)
- Full-time work authorization after graduation
- Duration: length of your program up to 3 years (minimum 8-month program for any PGWP)
- Apply within 180 days of receiving final transcript

### Spouses
- Spouses of eligible students (enrolled in certain full-time programs ≥ 16 months) may get Open Work Permit

### Tax Registration
- Get a Social Insurance Number (SIN) — apply at Service Canada, free
- File annual T1 tax return — may get tax refund as a student

## Australia

### Student Visa (Subclass 500) — Work Rights
- **During term:** Up to **48 hours per fortnight (2 weeks)**
- **During scheduled course breaks:** Unlimited hours
- Work rights are automatic — no separate permit

### Note on "Fortnight"
- 48 hours per 2-week period = approximately 24 hours per week
- Averaging across the fortnight is allowed (e.g., 40 hours week 1, 8 hours week 2)

### Tax Registration
- Apply for Tax File Number (TFN) immediately — online via ATO website
- Without TFN, employer withholds 47% tax — never work without TFN

### Penalty
- Working over 48hrs/fortnight is a visa condition breach = cancellation

## Germany

### Student Work Rights
- **120 full working days** OR **240 half-days (≤ 4 hours each)** per calendar year
- This is approximately 20 hours/week for half the year
- Werkstudent (working student) contracts are popular: allowed up to 20 hours/week year-round

### Werkstudent Status
- Tax benefits: if working ≤ 20hrs/week during semester, only pension and unemployment insurance apply (not health insurance separately)
- Ideal for part-time jobs at German companies

### Freelancing
- Technically possible with Freiberufler (freelancer) status for creative/intellectual work
- Register at Finanzamt for tax ID

### Tax Registration
- Apply for Steuer-ID (tax ID) — sent automatically by post after Anmeldung (address registration)
- Lohnsteuer (income tax) withheld by employer

## France

### Student Visa — Work Rights
- Students with valid VLS-TS or residence permit may work up to **964 hours per year** (approximately 20 hours/week year-round)
- Students from India are entitled to work without a work permit if enrolled at French institution

### Tax Registration
- Register with French tax authority (impôts.gouv.fr) if earning above €11,000/year
- Social contributions (cotisations) apply above certain thresholds

---

## Netherlands

### MVV/Residence Permit — Work Rights
- **During academic year:** Up to **16 hours per week**
- **During summer (June–August):** Full-time (40 hours/week)
- Employer must apply for a TWV (work permit) on your behalf if you are not EU citizen

### Tax Registration
- Apply for BSN (Citizen Service Number) at municipality
- Belastingdienst (tax authority) handles annual filing

---

## Ireland

### Stamp 2 (Student) — Work Rights
- **During term time:** Up to **20 hours per week**
- **During holiday periods (June–September, December–January):** Up to 40 hours per week

### Tax Registration
- Apply for PPSN (Personal Public Service Number) — essential for any employment
- Apply at local Intreo Centre or DSP office
- Revenue.ie for tax registration (PAYE system)

---

## Singapore

### Student Pass — Work Rights
- Students at universities (NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD) may work up to **16 hours per week** during term
- **Full-time during vacations** — notify ICA/university
- Part-time work authorization usually endorsed on your Student Pass

---

## Japan

### Status of Residence: College Student
- Must obtain separate **Permission to Engage in Activity other than that Permitted** (資格外活動許可)
- Apply at local Immigration Services Agency office — free, approved same day usually
- **Up to 28 hours per week** during term
- **Full-time (40 hours/week) during summer/winter/spring vacation**

### Tax Registration
- Register at local municipal office for Residence Card
- Individual Number (My Number) issued — needed for tax and social security

---

## Sweden

### Work Rights
- No specific hour limit for international students with residence permit
- You may work **full-time** alongside studies, as long as you remain enrolled

### Tax Registration
- Apply for Personnummer (personal identity number) at Skatteverket
- Very easy to work and pay tax; progressive tax starts around SEK 20,000/month

---

## Denmark

### Residence Permit for Study — Work Rights
- Up to **20 hours per week** during term
- Full-time during official holidays

### Tax Registration
- CPR number (civil registration number) — get from municipal office (Borgerservice)
- SKAT (tax authority) handles tax cards — get a "frikort" (tax-free allowance) if earning below DKK 48,000/year

---

## New Zealand

### Student Visa — Work Rights
- Up to **20 hours per week** during study
- **Full-time during scheduled breaks**
- Work authorization is usually endorsed on student visa

---

## Spain

### Student Visa — Work Rights
- Up to **20 hours per week** (from 2023 immigration changes allowing this)
- Must apply for student work authorization (autorización de trabajo para estudiantes) at immigration office

---

## UAE

### Student Visa
- **No automatic work rights** — must be sponsored separately
- Some universities offer campus employment — check with your institution
- Working without authorization results in immediate deportation and blacklisting

---

## South Korea

### D-2 Visa — Work Rights
- Up to **25 hours per week** (increased from 20 in 2023)
- Must obtain part-time work permit from immigration office (HIKOREA) — free to apply
- University students may do campus jobs without permit

---

## Norway

### Residence Permit — Work Rights
- Up to **20 hours per week** during semester
- Full-time during academic breaks
- Simply inform your employer — no separate permit needed beyond residence permit

---

## General Advice

1. **Always check your visa document** — work conditions are printed on your BRP/residence permit/visa sticker
2. **Never work cash-in-hand** if it puts you above your hour limit — this is still a violation
3. **Keep records:** Save pay stubs and timesheets — useful if immigration asks
4. **Notify your university:** Most DSO/international offices track student employment
5. **Tax filing:** Even students must file taxes in most countries — often you get a refund
6. **Indian students commonly work as:** Teaching assistants, research assistants, baristas, retail assistants, restaurant workers, tutors, delivery drivers (check specific visa conditions for each)
