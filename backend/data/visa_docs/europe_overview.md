# European Student Visa — Complete Overview for Indian Students

## Schengen vs Non-Schengen Countries

European countries fall into two categories for visa purposes:

**Schengen Area countries** (26 countries sharing a common visa zone):
Germany, France, Netherlands, Spain, Italy, Portugal, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Switzerland, Belgium, Austria, Poland, Czech Republic, and others.

**Non-Schengen EU** (separate national visas required):
- UK (no longer in EU/Schengen — requires separate UK Student Visa)
- Ireland (own immigration system)

**Important**: A Schengen visa does NOT automatically allow you to study in a Schengen country. For study stays longer than 90 days, each country requires its own national long-stay visa (Type D) or residence permit.

## Financial Proof: Savings vs. Parental Income

**For all European countries, you can use a combination of:**

### 1. Parent/Sponsor Bank Statements (Most Common)
- Last 3–6 months of bank statements showing consistent balance
- Balance must cover at least the required monthly amount × duration of study (or first year)
- Source of funds must be explainable — salary credits, business income, property rental, investments

### 2. Savings (Fixed Deposits, Savings Account)
- Savings alone are accepted if the amount is sufficient and the account history is clean
- **Germany** specifically requires a "Blocked Account" (Sperrkonto) with €11,208 deposited — this IS savings, not income
- France, Spain, Netherlands, Italy, Portugal all accept savings accounts showing the required balance
- The savings must have been in the account for at least 3–6 months (avoid large sudden deposits)

### 3. Scholarship Letters
- If you have a scholarship covering fees + stipend, this dramatically reduces the funds needed from family
- DAAD (Germany), Eiffel (France), Orange Tulip (Netherlands), etc.

### 4. Education Loans
- A sanctioned education loan letter from an Indian bank is accepted by many European embassies as proof of funds
- SBI, HDFC Credila, Axis Bank loans are well-recognised

### 5. Do Parents Need to Be Currently Earning?
- **No** — retired parents with sufficient savings/investments/rental income can sponsor
- A regular pension income is acceptable as proof
- The key is showing the **source of the funds is legitimate and the balance is maintained**
- Self-employed parents: ITR (Income Tax Returns) for last 2–3 years + CA certificate

## Common Documents Required Across All European Countries

**Every European student visa application from India typically requires:**

1. **Valid Passport** — minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay + all old passports
2. **University Acceptance/Enrollment Letter** — from an accredited institution
3. **Visa Application Form** — filled online or at VFS/Embassy
4. **Financial Proof** — see section above (€600–€1,000/month depending on country)
5. **Health Insurance** — valid for the full duration of study in the target country
6. **Accommodation Proof** — university hostel confirmation or rental agreement
7. **Academic Transcripts** — 10th, 12th, Bachelor's certificates + transcripts (apostilled)
8. **English/Language Proficiency** — IELTS, TOEFL, or language-specific test (DELF for France, TestDaF for Germany, etc.)
9. **Statement of Purpose (SOP)** — explaining why you chose the specific country/university/program
10. **Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)** — from Indian passport issuing authority, apostilled, often translated
11. **Passport-size Photos** — biometric format (35×45mm, white background)

### India-Specific Requirements
- **Apostille**: Most European countries require Indian documents to be apostilled by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), India. Takes 2–4 weeks.
- **Sworn Translation**: Documents in regional Indian languages (Hindi, Tamil, etc.) must be translated by a sworn/notarised translator into the country's language or English.
- **APS for Germany**: Academic Preliminary Review (Akademische Prüfstelle) — mandatory evaluation of Indian 12th + Bachelor's documents. Takes 4–8 weeks. Apply at APS India office (New Delhi or Chennai).

## Application Process (General European Flow)

1. **Receive Acceptance Letter** from university (conditional or unconditional)
2. **Gather documents** — allow 4–8 weeks for apostille + PCC
3. **For Germany**: Open Blocked Account (Deutsche Bank, Fintiba, Expatrio) and complete APS
4. **Book VFS/Embassy appointment** — most European countries use VFS Global in India (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, etc.)
5. **Submit application** — biometrics taken at VFS
6. **Processing** — 4–12 weeks depending on country and season
7. **Collect visa** — from VFS on approval

## Financial Requirements: Earnings vs Savings Summary

| Country | Blocked Account? | Savings Accepted? | Min Amount |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Germany | Yes (mandatory) | Yes (blocked account = savings) | €11,208/year |
| France | No | Yes | €615/month |
| Netherlands | No | Yes | ~€900/month |
| Spain | No | Yes | ~€600/month |
| Sweden | No | Yes | SEK 8,514/month |
| Norway | No | Yes | NOK 11,810/month |
| Switzerland | No | Yes | CHF 21,000/year |
| Ireland | No | Yes | €10,000 (year 1) |
| Portugal | No | Yes | ~€760/month |
| Italy | No | Yes | ~€460/month |

**Summary**: Parents do NOT need to be currently earning. Savings, fixed deposits, property income, pension, and education loans are all acceptable. The key is the balance must be present, maintained for 3–6 months, and the source must be documented.
