# Banking Setup in India Before Leaving — Guide for Indian Students

## Pre-Departure Banking Checklist

Proper banking setup before leaving India saves enormous stress once you're abroad. This guide covers everything: NRI account setup, forex cards, international transfers, TCS rules, and what to do with your existing Indian accounts.

---

## Your Existing Indian Savings Account

### What Happens to Your Indian Account After You Become an NRI?

Once you spend more than 182 days outside India in a financial year, you become a **Non-Resident Indian (NRI)** for tax purposes.

**Legal requirement:** NRIs are required to convert their Indian savings account to an NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account. You cannot legally continue operating a regular resident savings account as an NRI.

**Practical reality:** Many students don't do this immediately. Banks ideally should convert automatically, but in practice many accounts remain as regular accounts. However, to be compliant and avoid issues, convert early.

**How to convert:**
- Contact your Indian bank branch or online banking portal
- Many banks allow NRI conversion through their NRI services portal (HDFC, ICICI, SBI, Axis all have online NRI services)
- Alternatively, contact them before leaving India and initiate the process

## Types of NRI Bank Accounts

### NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) Account

**What it is:** Your existing Indian account converted to NRO status.
- Holds money earned or received IN India (rent from Indian property, dividends from Indian investments, gifts from family)
- Interest income taxable in India at 30% (+ surcharge + cess) — TDS deducted automatically
- Can be used to pay Indian bills (EMIs, insurance premiums, property taxes)
- Can repatriate (send abroad) up to USD 1,000,000 per financial year (after paying applicable taxes)
- Joint operation with Indian resident family member allowed

**Suitable for:** Students who receive rental income from family property in India, receive financial gifts, or have ongoing Indian financial obligations.

### NRE (Non-Resident External) Account

**What it is:** A rupee account specifically designed for NRI remittances.
- Money you earn ABROAD (your stipend, salary, savings from abroad) deposited here
- Interest on NRE account is completely TAX-FREE in India
- Fully and freely repatriable — you can take money out anytime
- Principal and interest can be transferred back abroad without any limit
- **Not for Indian-source income** (if you earn rent in India, it should go to NRO, not NRE)

**Best use for students:** Send your part of foreign savings/stipend to NRE account when visiting India; build a tax-free corpus for when you return.

### FCNR (Foreign Currency Non-Resident) Account

**What it is:** A fixed deposit account in foreign currency (USD, GBP, EUR, etc.) held at Indian banks.
- Interest tax-free in India
- No exchange rate risk when depositing/withdrawing (in same currency)
- Useful for blocking large amounts for a fixed term at competitive interest rates

**Best use:** For large one-time amounts you want to park in India in foreign currency for 1–5 years.

## Setting Up Before Leaving India

### Step 1: Open an NRI Account (NRO + NRE)
Most Indian banks allow NRI account applications:
- **HDFC Bank NRI Services:** hdfc.bank/nri — apply online; fastest process
- **ICICI Bank NRI Services:** icicibank.com/nri — online application with video KYC
- **SBI NRI Services:** sbi.co.in/nri — slower but highly trusted; wide global correspondent network
- **Axis Bank NRI Services:** Strong forex services; good rates

**Typical process:**
1. Apply online through bank's NRI portal
2. Submit: Passport copy, proof of residence abroad (visa or admission letter), Indian address proof, PAN card
3. Video KYC verification or in-branch if still in India
4. Receive NRI account details in 2–5 days

**Even if not leaving immediately:** You can open NRE/NRO accounts while still in India if you have an Indian visa for a foreign country (student visa, work visa) as evidence of NRI intent.

### Step 2: Link Aadhaar to PAN and Bank Account
- If not already done, link your Aadhaar to your PAN card (mandatory for financial operations)
- Check linkage at incometaxindiaefiling.gov.in
- Link Aadhaar to your bank accounts for seamless operations

### Step 3: Update Nominee Details
- Before leaving, update the nominee on your bank accounts and any investments to a trusted family member
- This ensures your accounts can be managed/accessed in an emergency

### Step 4: Enable International Transactions on Indian Debit Card
- Call your bank or log in to net banking
- Enable "International transactions" on your debit card
- Check the international withdrawal limit (usually $500–1,000/month by default; may need to increase)
- Test: Use your card once internationally to verify it works before relying on it

## Forex Cards — Choosing and Loading Before Departure

### Niyo Global Card
- Apply online at niyo.co.in or through Niyo app (iOS/Android)
- Verification: Aadhaar + PAN KYC
- Linked to your Indian bank account; transfer money to Niyo wallet; it auto-converts when you spend
- Zero forex markup — you pay mid-market rate
- ATM withdrawals: 3 free/month internationally; subsequent withdrawals: small fee
- **Load before leaving:** Transfer ₹50,000–1,00,000 to your Niyo wallet as a cushion for first 2 weeks abroad

### HDFC ForexPlus Multicurrency Card
- Available at HDFC branches
- Load specific currencies at the branch: GBP, USD, EUR, AUD, CAD, SGD, etc.
- Lock in today's exchange rate when loading
- Good if you want rate certainty
- **Forex markup:** 2–3.5% — higher than Niyo but protects against further INR depreciation

### Axis Bank Multicurrency Forex Card
- Apply at Axis Bank branches
- Multiple currencies; reload online or at branch

### Thomas Cook/HDFC/SBI Travel Cards
- Available at travel agencies and bank branches
- Reasonable rates; good brand trust for parents

**Which to carry:**
- Primary: Niyo Global (zero markup, everyday spending)
- Secondary: HDFC ForexPlus (emergency backup loaded with 3 months expenses in local currency)
- Emergency backup: Keep your Indian debit card for international ATM use

## Setting Up Wise Before Departure

**Wise (formerly TransferWise)** is essential for regular money transfers between India and abroad. Set this up while still in India.

### Creating Wise Account
1. Go to wise.com or download the Wise app
2. Sign up with your email; verify identity (passport + selfie video)
3. Indian verification: Use passport details; verification takes 1–3 days

### What Wise Gives You
- A multi-currency wallet: hold GBP, USD, EUR, AUD, CAD simultaneously
- A local bank account in each currency: UK sort code + account number; US routing + account number; etc.
- A Wise debit card (international) for spending in any currency
- Send money from India at near-interbank rates

### Using Wise for LRS Transfers
- Your Indian bank initiates the SWIFT transfer to Wise
- Wise receives in INR and converts to your target currency
- Total cost: 0.5–1% (far better than standard SWIFT markup of 2–4%)

## RBI LRS Rules for Students — Quick Summary

**Liberalisation Remittance Scheme (LRS):** Indian residents can remit up to **USD 250,000 per year** abroad.

**TCS (Tax Collected at Source) on LRS (from Oct 2023):**

| Amount Sent | Purpose | TCS Rate |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Up to ₹7 lakh/year | Any | 0% |
| Above ₹7 lakh | Education from education loan | 0.5% |
| Above ₹7 lakh | Education (self-funded/family) | 5% |
| Above ₹7 lakh | General/other purposes | 20% |

**Key action:** Request Form 15CC (TCS certificate) from your bank for every remittance. File Indian ITR every year to claim TCS refund (since you're NRI with no Indian income, you'll get it all back as a refund).

## Investments and Insurance Before Leaving

### Indian Investments — What Happens?
- **Fixed Deposits:** Continue earning interest; mark as NRO FD if converting accounts
- **Mutual Funds:** NRIs can continue holding existing Indian mutual fund investments; check your AMC's NRI policy
- **PPF (Public Provident Fund):** NRIs cannot open new PPF accounts; existing accounts can be continued until maturity but no new deposits as NRI
- **LIC policies:** Continue paying premiums; premiums can be paid from NRO account

### Life and Health Insurance Before Leaving
- Renew any existing Indian health insurance for family members dependent on you
- International/travel insurance for yourself: Get from Indian insurer with Schengen/international coverage (Bajaj Allianz, HDFC ERGO, Star Health)

## Common Banking Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not informing Indian bank of travel:** Cards get blocked for "suspicious foreign transactions." Call your bank OR set up international notifications online before departure.

2. **Not having backup payment methods:** If your primary Niyo card fails abroad, you need your HDFC ForexPlus as immediate backup; don't leave with just one card.

3. **Waiting too long to convert to NRI account:** Your Indian resident account should technically be converted within 3–6 months of becoming NRI. Delaying causes tax compliance issues.

4. **Not claiming TCS on ITR:** Every year your family sends money above ₹7 lakh, TCS is collected. If you don't file ITR, you lose this money permanently. File ITR-2 by July 31 each year.

5. **Carrying too much cash:** Customs requires declaration of cash above $10,000 equivalent. Keep cash to USD 300–500 for immediate airport needs; use cards for everything else.

## FAQ

**Q: My parents want to send money to my Indian account and then I'll transfer abroad. Is this efficient?**
A: Not really. Direct remittances from India to abroad (using Wise or SWIFT) are more tax-efficient and cheaper than sending to your Indian account first. This is because your Indian account is now NRO — interest is taxed, and repatriation from NRO requires paperwork and CA certificate for amounts above certain thresholds.

**Q: Can I keep my ICICI/HDFC savings account as it is while studying abroad?**
A: Technically you should convert to NRO once you become an NRI. Operationally, most banks don't actively monitor this, but it's a compliance issue. Convert to NRI accounts to stay legally compliant and access better NRI-specific benefits.

**Q: My scholarship gives me money in UK. Can my parents receive that in India?**
A: You can transfer from your UK account to your India NRE account (from foreign earnings to NRE — this is the correct pathway). NRE account is freely repatriable and tax-free in India. Your parents can then use the NRE account money.
