# South Korea Student Visa (D-2) — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why South Korea?

South Korea has rapidly emerged as a global education hub, backed by world-class technology, a dynamic culture (K-pop, K-drama, Korean cuisine), and some of the best engineering and science universities in Asia.

**Key attractions:**
- Seoul National University, KAIST, POSTECH, Yonsei, Korea University are globally ranked in top 100–200
- Government scholarship (GKS) covers full tuition + living costs
- Growing number of English-medium programs
- Extremely fast internet, excellent public transport, food culture
- Tech industry: Samsung, LG, SK Hynix, Hyundai, KakaoTalk — strong hiring pipeline
- Safe, modern, clean cities

**Key facts:**
- Capital: Seoul; Population: 51.7 million
- Currency: Korean Won (KRW); USD 1 ≈ KRW 1,350
- National language: Korean (Hangul script)
- Visa-free: Indian citizens are NOT visa-free for Korea; D-2 visa required

## D-2 Student Visa

### Visa Types for Students
| Visa | For |
|------|-----|
| D-2-1 | Pre-university (language institute) |
| D-2-2 | Associate degree |
| D-2-3 | Bachelor's degree |
| D-2-4 | Master's degree |
| D-2-5 | Doctoral degree |
| D-2-6 | Research student |
| D-2-7 | Correspondence university |

Most Indian students apply for D-2-3 (bachelor's), D-2-4 (master's), or D-2-5 (PhD).

### D-2 Application Location

**Korean Embassy New Delhi:**
9, Chandragupta Marg, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi 110021
Apply in person or by mail

**Korean Consulate General Mumbai:**
Nariman Point, Mumbai — for residents of western and southern India

### Required Documents

1. Visa application form (download from overseas.mofa.go.kr)
2. Valid passport + photocopy (front page)
3. Recent passport photo (3.5cm × 4.5cm)
4. **Certificate of Admission (입학허가서)** from the Korean university
5. Proof of financial resources:
   - Bank balance equivalent to USD $3,000–5,000 (approximately ₹2.5–4 lakh) minimum
   - Or scholarship letter from the institution
   - Bank statements last 3–6 months
6. Academic transcripts and certificates (English translation required)
7. Health certificate (some embassies require; check current requirements)
8. No criminal record certificate (PCC from India — apostilled)

### Visa Fee
- **USD $80** (approximately ₹6,700) for Indian nationals
- Single entry or multiple entry depending on program duration

### Processing Time
- 5–10 working days typically

## GKS (Global Korea Scholarship) — Most Important Scholarship

### What is GKS?
The Global Korea Scholarship (previously known as KGSP — Korean Government Scholarship Program) is the primary Korean government scholarship for international students. Highly competitive but generous.

### GKS Coverage
- **Full tuition fees** paid to Korean university
- **Monthly living allowance:** KRW 900,000/month (master's/PhD) = approximately $670/month = approximately ₹56,000/month
- **Korean language training:** 1-year Korean language institute before studies (fully funded)
- **Airfare:** Round-trip economy class India to Korea
- **Settlement allowance:** KRW 200,000 on arrival
- **Research allowance:** KRW 210,000–240,000/semester

### GKS Tracks
1. **University Track:** Apply through a specific Korean university that nominates you
2. **Embassy Track:** Apply through the Korean Embassy in India

### Eligibility
- GPA above 80% (approximately 2.64/4.0 GPA)
- Under 40 for master's/PhD
- TOPIK Level 3+ is advantageous (but Korean language training is provided)
- Strong SOP, research proposal for master's/PhD

### Deadline
- Embassy Track: October–November each year (for September entry following year)
- University Track: Varies; typically February–March

### Application at Korean Embassy India
Apply through the Korean Embassy in New Delhi — submit to the Embassy Track by their deadline.

## TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean)

TOPIK is the standardized Korean language proficiency test. For programs taught in Korean, you need TOPIK.

| Level | Description | Program Eligibility |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| TOPIK I Level 1 | Beginner | Not sufficient for degree programs |
| TOPIK I Level 2 | Elementary | Not sufficient |
| **TOPIK II Level 3** | Intermediate | Minimum for most undergraduate admissions (Korean-medium) |
| **TOPIK II Level 4** | Upper-intermediate | Required for most graduate programs |
| TOPIK II Level 5 | Advanced | Recommended for research-intensive programs |
| TOPIK II Level 6 | Near-native | Highest level; for top research programs |

**TOPIK test fee:** Approximately ₹2,500–3,500
**Test centers in India:** Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Pune, Bangalore, Kochi (check topik.go.kr for current locations)

**For English-medium programs:** TOPIK not required; IELTS 6.0–6.5 or TOEFL 80–90 typically required

## Work Rights on D-2 Visa

- Maximum **20 hours per week** off-campus during semester
- Full-time during official academic breaks
- On-campus work: Research assistant positions, teaching assistant — unlimited hours as part of scholarship
- Work permission: Must apply for "Work permit for student" at Immigration Office (not automatically granted — requires application)

## Post-Study Options

**D-10 Job Seeker Visa:**
After graduating, apply for the D-10 Job Seeker Visa:
- Valid for 6 months to 1 year
- Allows job hunting in Korea
- Must show savings or scholarship continuation to support yourself

**E-7 (Specifically Designated Activities) Work Visa:**
- If offered a qualified job in Korea after graduation
- Employer applies on your behalf
- Pathway to long-term stay and eventual Korean PR (F-5 visa) after 5 years

## FAQ

**Q: Can I do a master's degree in Korea completely in English?**
A: Yes. KAIST, POSTECH, UNIST offer most master's programs in English. SNU, Yonsei, Korea University have English International programs. Check each university's graduate school programs for "International" or "Global" tracks.

**Q: Is the GKS scholarship easy to get?**
A: No — it's highly competitive, particularly the Embassy Track. India sends dozens of GKS students annually but thousands apply. University Track is slightly easier if your target university nominates you. A 85%+ CGPA and strong research/leadership record helps.

**Q: Is learning Korean useful for daily life?**
A: Extremely helpful. Even basic Korean (Hangul reading + survival phrases) transforms your experience. Korean people deeply appreciate foreigners making an effort to speak Korean. Free Korean language courses are available at most universities and cultural centers in India.

**Q: How long is the path to Korean permanent residence?**
A: After graduating and getting an E-7 work visa, you can apply for F-5 (Permanent Resident) status after 5 years of continuous stay with a clean record and sufficient income. Korea is not traditionally an immigration destination but it has become more open to skilled foreign talent.
