# Studying in Portugal — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Portugal?

Portugal has become one of Europe's most attractive destinations for Indian students, combining quality education, affordable costs, excellent weather, and a growing tech ecosystem. Lisbon and Porto are vibrant, modern cities with strong international communities.

**Key attractions:**
- EU member, Schengen zone
- NOVA University Lisbon, University of Porto — solid rankings; good English programs
- Tech industry growing rapidly (Web Summit HQ in Lisbon, major tech companies expanding)
- One of the cheapest EU countries for living costs after Eastern Europe
- Portugal Residence Permit after graduation allows 1–2 years to find work
- Portuguese language globally useful (spoken by 260+ million people in Brazil, Angola, Mozambique)

**Key facts:**
- Capital: Lisbon; Population: 10.4 million; Major student cities: Lisbon, Porto, Coimbra
- Currency: Euro (€)
- Language: Portuguese; English widely spoken in universities and cities

## Portuguese Student Visa Process

Indian students need a **Type D Student Visa (Visto D4)** for programs over 90 days.

### Application Location
**Portuguese Embassy New Delhi:**
4/50 G, Shantipath, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi 110021

**VFS Global Portugal:** Handles applications at VFS centers in major Indian cities.

### Required Documents

1. Completed Portuguese visa application form (fillable at vfsglobal.com or Portuguese Embassy)
2. Valid passport (minimum 3 months beyond visa expiry; preferably full program duration + 3 months)
3. Recent passport photos (35mm × 45mm, white background, 2 copies)
4. **University enrollment letter** or official acceptance/admission letter from Portuguese institution
5. Proof of tuition fees paid (first semester/year receipt)
6. **Financial proof:**
   - Minimum €760/month (approximately €7,600 for 10-month academic year) — this is Portugal's minimum wage
   - Or scholarship letter confirming coverage of living expenses
   - Bank statements (last 3–6 months)
7. **Accommodation proof in Portugal:**
   - University housing confirmation, OR
   - Tenancy agreement
8. Travel and health insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage for Schengen area)
9. Police Clearance Certificate from India (apostilled)
10. Flight booking or itinerary

### Visa Fee: **€90**

### Processing Time: Approximately 15–30 working days

## AIMA (Agency for Integration, Migrations and Asylum) Registration

From November 2023, AIMA replaced SEF (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras) as Portugal's migration authority. Post-arrival registration is now through AIMA.

### Post-Arrival Process

Within **3 working days** of arriving in Portugal, you must register your arrival at:
- The nearest AIMA office in your city
- Or through an online registration portal (check aima.gov.pt for current process)

After registering arrival, you can apply for a **Residence Permit (Autorização de Residência para Estudante)** which formalizes your stay:
- Required documents: Passport, visa, enrollment letter, accommodation proof, financial proof, photos
- Fee: Approximately €83
- Processing: 2–4 months; receive temporary certificate while waiting

## Work Rights

Students with a Portuguese student visa/residence permit can:
- Work maximum **20 hours per week** during semester
- Full-time during official university breaks
- Minimum wage: €820/month full-time (2024) = approximately €5.10/hour — lower than Western EU but growing rapidly

## Healthcare

Portugal has the SNS (Serviço Nacional de Saúde) — national health service.

International students with residence permit:
- Register at the nearest Centro de Saúde (Health Center) with your residence permit and NIF (taxpayer number)
- Doctor visits: Free or very low cost (consultation fee ~€3–5)
- Hospital: Free for enrolled patients
- Private insurance: Optional but useful for faster access (Apollo Munich equivalent available in Portugal)

---

## Post-Study Work Options

Portugal introduced the **Job Seeker / Post-Study Residence Permit** allowing graduates of Portuguese institutions to stay up to **2 years** after graduation to seek employment.

**Requirements for post-study permit:**
- Graduated from a Portuguese institution
- No criminal record
- Proof of financial means during job search

**Portugal's D8 Digital Nomad Visa** (since 2022): If you work remotely for a non-Portuguese employer, you can also use the D8 visa — not specific to graduates but available.

**Pathway to PR:**
- After 5 years of continuous legal residence: Apply for Portuguese permanent residence (AR) or citizenship
- Portuguese citizenship after 5 years enables EU passport (extremely valuable — visa-free/on-arrival access to 186+ countries)

## Scholarships

**Camões Institute (Portuguese Cultural Institute) Scholarships:**
- Scholarships for students from Portuguese-speaking countries and development-linked programs
- Limited availability for Indian students; check bilateral agreements

**Nova SBE Merit Scholarships:**
- 20–50% tuition reduction for high-achieving applicants
- Applied at admission

**FCT (Foundation for Science and Technology) Research Grants:**
- For PhD students in sciences, technology, engineering
- Monthly stipend approximately €1,000–1,300 for PhD researchers
- Apply through your PhD supervisor at Portuguese university

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to learn Portuguese to study in Portugal?**
A: For English-medium programs (many at NOVA, IST, FEUP, University of Porto), no Portuguese required. For daily life, basic Portuguese is helpful — locals appreciate effort and most cities are welcoming of English speakers. A free online course (Duolingo Portuguese, Practice Portuguese) helps significantly.

**Q: Is the tech scene in Portugal good for Indian students?**
A: Growing rapidly. Lisbon hosts Web Summit (Europe's largest tech conference), and companies like Google, Amazon, Volkswagen Digital Solutions, and Farfetch have expanded there. The startup ecosystem in Lisbon and Porto is vibrant with strong English-speaking work culture.

**Q: How is Portuguese weather for Indian students?**
A: Excellent — one of the sunniest countries in Europe. Lisbon averages 300 sunny days per year. Winters are mild (10–15°C); summers are hot (35–40°C). Indian students from warmer climates (Chennai, Mumbai, Hyderabad) adapt very easily.
