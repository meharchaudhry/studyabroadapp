# Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) Guide for Indian Students Applying Abroad

## Overview

A Police Clearance Certificate (PCC), also called a Character Certificate or Criminal Record Certificate, is required for student visas in most countries. It verifies that you have no criminal record and are of good character. This guide explains how to obtain a PCC in India and how to prepare it for use in different countries.

## 2. Types of PCC in India

### A. Passport Office PCC (Most Commonly Required Abroad)
- **Full name:** Police Clearance Certificate issued through the Indian Passport Office
- **Process:** Online through Passport Seva Portal + visit to Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office PSK
- **Coverage:** National — covers criminal records across India
- **Output:** Official Government of India document, valid for verification at any embassy/consulate
- **Cost:** ₹500 (2024 current fee)
- **Processing time:** 7–15 working days (normal); Tatkal: 1–3 working days (fee: ₹2,000)

### B. Local Police Station PCC
- Issued by the local police station at your current/permanent residential address.
- Required for some countries (e.g., UAE) in addition to, or instead of, the Passport Office PCC.
- Process: Submit application to the local SHO (Station House Officer) → physical verification → certificate issued.
- Free or nominal fee; takes 5–21 days depending on state.

## 3. How to Apply for Passport Office PCC — Step by Step

**Step 1 — Apply Online**
1. Go to www.passportindia.gov.in
2. Log in with your existing account (same as your passport application account).
3. Click "Apply for Police Clearance Certificate."
4. Fill in the online form:
   - Personal details
   - Passport number and issue date
   - Reason for PCC (Study Abroad)
   - Address details (current + permanent)
5. Upload documents:
   - Proof of address (Aadhaar, voter ID, utility bill)
   - Proof of identity (Aadhaar, passport)
   - Recent passport copy (if applicable)
6. Book an appointment at your nearest Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office PSK.
7. Pay the fee online (₹500 or ₹2,000 for Tatkal).

**Step 2 — Visit the Passport Seva Kendra**
- Bring originals of all documents submitted online + photocopies.
- Biometrics (fingerprints) will be taken.
- Police verification will be initiated (this is what takes time).

**Step 3 — Police Verification**
- Local police station/CID will verify your address and background.
- If your record is clear (no FIRs, no criminal cases), the PCC is issued.
- **If you have lived at multiple addresses:** Verification may take longer or involve multiple police jurisdictions.
- **If you have a minor traffic violation/compounding offence:** These are typically not included in the PCC but check with authorities if concerned.

**Step 4 — Receive PCC**
- PCC is delivered by post (India Post Speed Post) to your registered address.
- Download digital copy from the Passport Seva portal once issued.

## 4. Apostille — What Is It and When Is It Required?

An **Apostille** is an international certification that verifies the authenticity of a document for use in countries that are signatories to the Hague Convention (1961). It makes the document legally valid in the foreign country without further authentication.

**Countries that require Apostilled PCC from Indian students:**
Germany, France, Spain, Portugal, Italy, Switzerland, Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Belgium, South Korea, Singapore (sometimes), New Zealand (sometimes), and most other Hague Convention signatory countries.

**Countries that do NOT require Apostille (bilateral arrangement):**
USA, UK, Canada (these countries have their own document authentication processes or accept Indian documents directly with Embassy certification).

### How to Get Apostille on PCC in India

The Apostille is done by the **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), India** — not by the Embassy or state government.

**Step 1 — MEA Apostille Process:**
1. **Online application:** Go to mea.apagreement.gov.in or the MEA e-Apostille portal.
2. Submit the original PCC (or certified copy).
3. Pay the fee: ₹50 per document (for e-Apostille) or ₹70 per document (physical Apostille stamp).
4. Physical submission options:
   - MEA HQ, New Delhi (Patiala House Annexe)
   - RPO offices in major cities (check MEA list of authorized centers)
   - Authorized outsourcing agencies (MEA appoints agencies in major cities)

**Processing time for Apostille:**
- In-person at MEA: 1–2 working days
- Through authorized agencies: 3–7 working days
- During peak season (May–August): Can extend to 2 weeks

**Apostille sticker:** The MEA sticks a specially designed sticker (with a unique number) on the back of your document. This sticker can be verified online by foreign authorities.

## 5. Translation Requirements

Many countries require the PCC to be translated into the local language. This must be done by a **sworn/official translator**, not Google Translate.

| Country | Language Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Germany | German | Translation by a sworn translator (beeidigter Übersetzer) |
| France | French | Translation by a certified translator |
| Italy | Italian | Translation required + notarization |
| Spain | Spanish | Sworn (jurado) translator required |
| Portugal | Portuguese or English | Portuguese preferred |
| Netherlands | Dutch or English | English widely accepted |
| Japan | Japanese | Translation required for official submissions |

**Finding translators in India:**
- MEA-empanelled translators: Check MEA website for listed language-specific translators.
- Major cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai) have many certified translators for European languages.
- Cost: ₹2,000–6,000 per document depending on language and translator.

**Recommendation:** Start the PCC process **at least 6 weeks** before your visa application submission date to allow buffer time.

## 7. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

1. **Letting the PCC expire:** PCC is valid for **6 months** from the date of issue in most countries. If your visa application is delayed, you may need a new PCC.

2. **Forgetting Apostille:** Getting the PCC without Apostille when the destination country requires it — you'll need to resubmit to MEA.

3. **Using an unofficial translation:** Translated by a friend or non-certified translator — embassies reject these.

4. **Not keeping copies:** Always keep certified copies of the original PCC before sending to embassy.

5. **Wrong type of PCC:** Some countries ask for police clearance from **all countries you've lived in for more than 12 months**. If you've lived in the UK/USA before and are now applying to another country, you may need PCC from that country too.

6. **PCC issued too early:** If you apply for the visa much later than the PCC was issued, you may need a fresh one.

---

## 8. Overseas PCC for Existing NRI Students

If you're already abroad (e.g., completing a degree there and applying for a new visa in the same country), you may need:
- **Local police clearance from the host country** (e.g., ACRO certificate in UK, FBI background check in USA, RCMP check in Canada, NPIS in Australia).
- **Indian PCC from Indian Embassy/Consulate abroad** (Indian Mission issues PCCs for Indian nationals residing abroad — applicable if asked for PCC from your home country).
