# Singapore Student Pass — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** Student Pass (STP)  
**Issued by:** Immigration and Checkpoints Authority (ICA), Singapore  
**Managed through:** Student's Pass Online Application and Registration System (SOLAR)  
**Validity:** Valid for the duration of the course, usually 1–4 years. Renewable annually.

**Note:** Indian nationals require both a **Visa** (for entry to Singapore) and a **Student Pass** (to study). Indian passport holders need a separate entry visa issued by the ICA. Most Indian students will receive a **In-Principle Approval (IPA)** letter which functions as a single-journey visa to enter Singapore; the Student Pass is then collected in Singapore.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Have been offered a full-time place at an approved Singapore educational institution:
  - Autonomous universities: NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD, SIT, SUSS, UniSIM.
  - Polytechnics: Nanyang Polytechnic, Ngee Ann Polytechnic, Republic Polytechnic, Singapore Polytechnic, Temasek Polytechnic.
  - Private Education Institutions (PEIs) registered with the Committee for Private Education (CPE).
  - ITE (Institute of Technical Education).
- Meet the institution's academic requirements.
- Meet financial requirements.
- Be medically fit (no communicable diseases).
- Have no adverse immigration or criminal history.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

Singapore is an expensive city-state. Students must demonstrate ability to cover:

- **Living expenses:** Approximately SGD 1,500–2,500 per month for a student in Singapore.
- **Annual living cost:** Approximately SGD 18,000–30,000 per year.
- **Tuition fees:**
  - National University of Singapore (NUS), Nanyang Technological University (NTU), SMU: SGD 28,000–37,000/year for undergraduate; SGD 30,000–50,000/year for Master's (non-subsidized rates for international students).
  - Polytechnics: SGD 8,000–15,000/year.
  - PEIs (private): SGD 10,000–30,000/year.
- **Note:** Singapore provides tuition grants (subsidies) through the Ministry of Education (MOE) for qualifying students at autonomous universities and polytechnics — this reduces fees significantly (up to 80%) but comes with a **mandatory 3-year service bond** to work in Singapore after graduation. Indian students can opt for this but must work in Singapore for 3 years post-study.
- **Visa / Student Pass fee:** SGD 30 (In-Principle Approval) + SGD 60 (Student Pass issuance) = SGD 90 total (approximately INR 5,600).

**Proof of funds:**
- Bank statements (personal/parental, last 3–6 months).
- Scholarship award letter (Singapore government scholarships, ASEAN scholarships, institutional scholarships).
- Sponsor's income documents.
- Study loan letters.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

Documents for the SOLAR application (submitted online by the institution):
- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 6 months from the date of application; must be valid throughout the course).
- Recent passport-size photograph (35mm x 45mm, white background — specific ICA specifications required).
- Completed SOLAR application (submitted by the institution on behalf of the student; student fills in personal information on the eForm16 system).
- Academic certificates and transcripts:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets, certificates.
  - Bachelor's degree transcripts and certificates (for postgraduate applicants).
- English language proficiency certificate:
  - IELTS, TOEFL, or SAT/ACT scores (for undergraduate).
  - GMAT, GRE for MBA and some Master's programs.
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements.
  - Scholarship letter.
  - Loan sanction.
- Parent/guardian details and financial undertaking.
- Medical report (basic health declaration; full medical examination may be required upon arrival for certain courses).
- In-Principle Approval (IPA) letter — issued by ICA after successful application; used as the entry visa to Singapore.

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to institution:** Apply to the Singapore institution of your choice.
2. **Receive offer letter:** Receive a conditional or unconditional offer from the institution.
3. **Institution submits SOLAR application:** The institution's international student office submits the Student Pass application to ICA through the SOLAR system on the student's behalf. Students cannot apply directly.
4. **Receive IPA (In-Principle Approval):** ICA issues the IPA letter — this is the authorization to enter Singapore and is also the entry visa for Indian nationals.
5. **Travel to Singapore:** Use the IPA letter as your entry document. Present it at the immigration checkpoint on arrival.
6. **Medical examination in Singapore (if required):** Complete a medical examination at an ICA-approved clinic in Singapore (required for some courses and nationalities; generally for courses in healthcare, food services, etc.).
7. **Collect Student Pass:**
   - Visit the ICA Student Pass Collection (via appointment).
   - Submit original passport, IPA letter, and any additional documents requested.
   - Pay SGD 60 issuance fee.
   - Student Pass is endorsed in the passport.
8. **Register with institution:** Complete enrollment, pay tuition fees, collect student ID.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **SOLAR application processing:** 4–6 weeks after institution submission.
- Applications should be submitted at least 2–3 months before the course start date.
- IPA is typically valid for 2 months — travel to Singapore before it expires.
- ICA processes most straightforward applications within 3–4 weeks.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- **Full-time students** at autonomous universities (NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD, SIT, SUSS) may work part-time **up to 16 hours per week** without a separate work pass, during term time, after completing the first year of study.
- During scheduled vacations, full-time university students may work full-time.
- Students at polytechnics, PEIs, and ITE generally have **no automatic work rights** — they need to apply for a Work Holiday Pass or get specific approval from MOM (Ministry of Manpower) through their institution.
- Internships as part of the official curriculum are permitted under the Student Pass without additional permits.
- Working without the correct authorization is a serious immigration offence in Singapore.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Long-Term Visit Pass (LTVP) or Employment Pass (EP):** After graduation, international students seeking employment in Singapore need to apply for the appropriate work pass.
  - **Employment Pass (EP):** For professionals with a job offer paying at least SGD 5,000/month (minimum salary threshold for EP, with higher thresholds for financial sector). Requires a degree from a recognized institution.
  - **S Pass:** For mid-level skilled workers; minimum salary of SGD 3,150/month.
  - **Work Holiday Programme (WHP):** For Indian graduates under 25 who want to work in Singapore for 6–12 months — allows working and holidaying.
- Singapore does not currently have a general post-study work visa (similar to UK's Graduate Route or Australia's 485 visa). Getting a job and transitioning directly to a work pass is the standard pathway.
- **Singapore PR (Permanent Residence):** Can apply after 2+ years of working in Singapore under an EP or S Pass.
- **Tuition Grant Bond:** Students who took the MOE tuition subsidy must complete their 3-year bond (working for a Singapore-registered entity) before leaving the country permanently.

## 9. Language Requirements

**For English-taught programs (all major Singapore universities teach in English):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–7.0 (varies by institution and program) |
| TOEFL iBT | 85–100 |
| SAT (Evidence-Based Reading & Writing) | 560+ (NUS/NTU undergraduate) |
| GMAT | 600–680 (MBA programs) |
| GRE | As required by specific programs |

- Singapore uses English as the main medium of instruction at all levels of higher education.
- Indian students (educated in English-medium schools) may be exempt from language tests at some institutions.

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **MediShield Life / Integrated Shield Plans:** Singapore citizens and PRs are covered under MediShield Life. International students are not automatically covered.
- **Compulsory health insurance for PEI students:** The Singapore government mandates that all students at registered PEIs must have minimum health insurance coverage (at least SGD 20,000 per year for hospitalization and accidents).
- **University hospitals and clinic access:** Autonomous university students can access subsidised medical services through on-campus health centres. Some universities include basic health coverage in the student fee.
- **Recommended coverage:** At minimum, comprehensive hospitalization and surgical insurance covering SGD 100,000–200,000 per year.
- **Medisave for foreigners:** International students with work experience can contribute to Medisave but most students do not have this option.
- Vaccinations: Ensure routine Indian immunizations are up to date; some clinical/health courses require specific vaccination documentation.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Singapore Government Scholarships for Indian Students:**
  - **ASEAN Undergraduate Scholarship (AUS):** One of the most prestigious; covers full tuition, accommodation, and living allowance. Offered by NUS, NTU, SMU.
  - **Singapore International Graduate Award (SINGA):** For PhD programs; covers tuition fees plus monthly stipend of SGD 2,200–2,500.
  - **A*STAR Scholarships:** For research-focused programs.
- **Tuition Grant (MOE Subsidy):** Available to international students at autonomous universities and polytechnics with a 3-year service bond. This significantly reduces fees but commits you to work in Singapore post-graduation.
- **Singapore's education quality:** NUS and NTU consistently rank in the global top 15 and are among Asia's best universities.
- **Very high cost of living:** Singapore is consistently ranked among the world's most expensive cities. Accommodation alone can cost SGD 600–1,500/month. University hostels are the most affordable option.
- **No casual overstay policy:** ICA is strict about visa compliance. Never overstay your Student Pass.
- **IPA validity:** Enter Singapore before the IPA expiry date (usually 2 months from issuance). Extending IPA for late arrivals is possible but requires institutional support.
- **Social distancing and health rules:** Singapore has strict public health protocols — compliance is mandatory.
