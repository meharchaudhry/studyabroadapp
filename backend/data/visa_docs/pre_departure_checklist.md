# Pre-Departure Checklist for Indian Students

A well-organized pre-departure plan can mean the difference between a smooth start abroad and a stressful one. This comprehensive checklist covers everything Indian students need to do — from 6 months before departure to the night before your flight.

---

## 6 Months Before Departure

### Standardized Tests
- **IELTS / TOEFL / PTE Academic:** Register and sit your English proficiency test. Most universities require IELTS 6.0–7.5 depending on program. IELTS Academic is accepted everywhere; IELTS UKVI is required for the UK Student Visa specifically. Allow time for a resit if needed.
- **GRE:** Required for most USA master's and PhD programs (especially STEM). Quant target: 165+/170 for top programs. Register at ets.org; test centers in major Indian cities. Fee: $228.
- **GMAT:** Required for MBA programs globally. Score target: 680+ for top-50 programs, 720+ for top-20. Fee: $275.
- **SAT/ACT:** For undergraduate study in USA (Class 12 appearing students).

### University Research and Shortlisting
- Identify 8–15 universities across reach, match, and safe categories
- Research program curriculum, faculty research (for PhD/research master's), placement records, and location
- Check application deadlines — UK UCAS: January 31; USA: November–February; Canada: November–March; Germany: May 31 / November 30; Australia: rolling
- Join university-specific Facebook groups and Discord servers for current student insights

### Statement of Purpose (SOP) Drafting
- Begin first draft 5–6 months out — good SOPs take 8–12 iterations
- Tailor each SOP to the specific program and university
- Have a faculty mentor or experienced senior review drafts

### Letters of Recommendation (LOR)
- Request from 2–3 professors/employers as early as possible
- Give them at least 2 months' notice
- Provide them your resume, SOP draft, and specific points to highlight
- Follow up politely every 3 weeks

---

## 3 Months Before Departure

### Submit Applications
- Complete all online applications; keep tracker spreadsheet with status
- Pay application fees (typically $50–200 per application)
- Upload correctly formatted documents (official transcripts, degree certificate, IELTS score, SOP, LORs, resume)

### Financial Planning
- Calculate total first-year costs: tuition + accommodation + living + flights + visa fees
- Discuss funding plan with parents — SWIFT transfer vs. education loan
- Education loan applications: Apply at SBI, Axis Bank, HDFC Credila, Avanse, Auxilo, Prodigy Finance (for top colleges abroad)
- Loan processing takes 4–8 weeks; apply early

### Scholarship Applications
- Chevening (UK): Apply by November deadline
- DAAD (Germany): Apply by October deadline
- Australia Awards: Apply by April
- Fulbright-Nehru (USA): Apply by July
- Check university-specific scholarships — many have February–March deadlines for September intake

---

## 2 Months Before Departure

### Accept Offer and Pay Deposit
- Accept your conditional or unconditional offer on the university portal
- Pay tuition deposit (typically £500–£3,000 / $500–$2,000 / CAD $1,000)
- Confirm enrollment and receive your Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) / Letter of Enrollment / I-20

### Apply for Student Visa
- Gather all required documents (see country-specific guides)
- Book biometrics appointment at VFS Global / BLS International centers
- Apply online and upload documents
- Attend visa appointment with original documents
- Visa processing: UK 3–8 weeks; USA immediate decision after interview; Canada 20–120 days; Australia 20–80 days; Germany 6–12 weeks

### Blocked Account / GIC
- **Germany:** Open Sperrkonto (blocked account) at Deutsche Bank, Fintiba, or Coracle — deposit €11,208
- **Canada (SDS):** Open GIC at CIBC, RBC, TD, or Scotiabank — deposit CAD $10,635+

### Accommodation Application
- Apply for university halls (on-campus accommodation) as soon as you receive your offer
- UK halls: Apply within 2 weeks of accepting offer — popular halls fill fast
- Alternatively, research private student accommodation (PBSA: Unite Students, Sanctuary Students, iQ Student)
- If private renting: Start searching 8 weeks before arrival date

## 1 Month Before Departure

### Health
- Visit doctor: Get prescriptions for 3–6 months of any regular medication (stock up as medications may be expensive or unavailable abroad)
- Dental checkup: NHS/Medicaid/public healthcare has long waiting lists for dental abroad
- Eye checkup: Bring your spectacles prescription
- Vaccination records: Some universities require proof of MMR, Hepatitis B, meningitis, COVID-19 vaccinations
- **UK only: TB Test** — Mandatory if coming from India for a UK student visa of 6+ months. Book at an IOM-approved clinic (Medanta, SRL Diagnostics, Metropolis, etc.). Fee: ~₹2,500–4,000. TB test result letter required for visa application.

### Financial Setup
- **Forex card:** Get Niyo Global, HDFC Multicurrency Forex, or Axis Multi-Currency card
- **Wise account:** Open at wise.com — best exchange rates for receiving/sending money internationally
- **Notify your Indian bank:** Inform them of travel dates to prevent card being blocked for "suspicious foreign transactions"
- **Emergency funds:** Keep USD/GBP/EUR 500 in cash for emergencies at airport

### Book Flight
- Book 4–6 weeks in advance for best prices
- Compare: Skyscanner, Google Flights, MakeMyTrip for international routes
- Popular routes: Delhi/Mumbai/Hyderabad/Chennai to London/New York/Toronto/Sydney
- Check baggage allowance — student tickets often allow 2 checked bags
- Airlines used by Indian students: Air India, Emirates, Qatar Airways, British Airways, Air Canada, United

## 2 Weeks Before Departure

### Visa and Documents
- Collect visa (if passport returned) or confirm visa sticker
- **UK:** Collect BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) pickup letter — it should specify the post office location and collection window
- Print travel insurance and health insurance documents

### City Research
- Research your arrival city: nearest supermarket, Indian grocery store, public transport routes from airport to accommodation
- Map route from airport to accommodation: check if university runs an airport pickup service
- Download offline maps (Google Maps / Maps.me)

### Communication
- Unlock your Indian mobile phone for international SIM use (contact your network provider)
- Research local SIM options: UK (EE, Giffgaff, Three), USA (T-Mobile, AT&T), Canada (Fido, Koodo), Australia (Optus, Woolworths Mobile), Germany (O2, Aldi Talk)
- Set up international roaming on Indian SIM for first 2 days until local SIM acquired

---

## Final Week Before Departure

### Document Preparation
**Print originals AND 5 photocopies of each:**
- Passport (all pages with stamps/visas)
- Student visa / entry clearance vignette
- University admission/enrollment letter
- Financial proof (bank statements, blocked account confirmation, GIC certificate)
- All academic certificates: Class 10 marksheet, Class 12 marksheet, Degree certificate, All semester marksheets
- Official transcripts (sealed envelope — do not open)
- SOP copy
- LOR copies (you may not need them but carry anyway)
- 20 passport-size photographs (various backgrounds as per country requirements)
- Health insurance documents (OSHC / NHS surcharge receipt / international health insurance)
- TB test result letter (UK only)
- Travel insurance documents
- Blocked account / GIC certificate (Germany / Canada SDS)
- For UK: Proof of accommodation in UK (university halls confirmation or tenancy agreement)

### Digital Copies
- Scan all documents as PDFs
- Store in Google Drive and Dropbox (accessible from anywhere)
- Share folder access with one family member

### Tech Setup
- **VPN subscription:** ExpressVPN, NordVPN, or Surfshark (~$3–5/month) — useful for accessing Indian streaming services, some banking apps
- **Cloud storage:** Ensure all important files are backed up
- **WhatsApp group:** Create family group with your travel itinerary and contact in destination country
- **Accommodation:** Share your new address with family and note emergency contact numbers

## Day Before Departure — Packing Checklist

### Documents Folder (Carry-on Bag)
Keep all documents in a single waterproof folder in your hand luggage — never in checked baggage.

### Clothes
- Pack for destination climate — UK/Canada/Germany in September: layers, light jacket
- Winter arrivals (November onwards): Pack one set of warm clothes; buy winter wear abroad (better quality, similar price once you shop sales)

### Indian Items to Bring
- **Spices:** Cumin, mustard seeds, hing, curry leaves (dried), garam masala — hard to find or expensive abroad
- **Medicines:** Ayurvedic items, specific brand medications, Dettol antiseptic, Vicks, ORS sachets
- **Religious items:** As needed
- **Snacks for travel:** Dry snacks (avoid fresh food — restricted at borders)

### Electronics
- **Power adapters:** Type G (UK), Type A/B (USA/Canada), Type C (Europe/Australia) — buy multi-country adapter in India (cheaper)
- **Laptop, chargers, earphones**
- **External hard drive / USB with document backups**

### Miscellaneous
- Extra pair of spectacles
- Basic toiletries (first few days until you shop)
- Small first aid kit (paracetamol, antacids, bandages)

## FAQ

**Q: How much cash should I carry?**
A: Carry USD/GBP/EUR 300–500 equivalent in local currency for immediate needs on Day 1 (SIM card, transport, food). Use Forex card or Wise for all other expenses.

**Q: Can I carry Indian pickles and food on the flight?**
A: Dry, commercially packaged food is generally allowed. Fresh fruits, vegetables, meat, and dairy may be confiscated at customs. Check your destination country's customs rules.

**Q: Should I buy winter clothes in India or abroad?**
A: It's usually better to buy one basic warm layer in India for arrival, then shop for proper winter clothing in sales abroad. Zara, H&M, Primark (UK) and similar stores run excellent seasonal sales.

**Q: Is travel insurance separate from health insurance?**
A: Yes. Travel insurance covers trip cancellations, lost luggage, and emergency medical evacuation. Health insurance covers regular healthcare abroad. You need both.
