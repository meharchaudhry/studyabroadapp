# Travel Insurance and Health Insurance Guide for Indian Students Abroad

## Understanding the Difference

**Travel Insurance:** Covers trip cancellations, flight delays, lost/damaged luggage, and emergency medical evacuation during travel itself. Valid for a specific trip duration.

**Health Insurance:** Covers medical expenses — doctor visits, hospitalizations, prescriptions — while living abroad. Required for the entire duration of your stay.

**You need BOTH as an Indian student going abroad.**

---

## Mandatory Health Insurance by Country

### United Kingdom — NHS and Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)

Indian students in the UK pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** when applying for their Student visa. This grants access to the NHS (National Health Service) — the UK's universal healthcare system.

**IHS Rate (2024):** £470 per year for students (reduced rate; was raised to £1,035 for workers)
**Paid upfront:** For the full visa duration when applying
**Example:** 2-year student visa = 2 × £470 = £940 IHS paid at visa application

**What NHS Covers:**
- GP (general practitioner) visits — free
- Hospital treatment — free (as an NHS patient)
- Emergency care (A&E) — free
- Prescriptions: £9.90 per item (flat rate) — note: NHS prescriptions are cheap
- Dental care: NHS dental treatment available but limited availability; NHS rates apply
- Mental health services: Available through NHS (waiting times can be long)

**What NHS Does NOT Cover Well:**
- Dental treatment: Limited NHS dentists accepting new patients; private dental expensive (£50–250 per appointment)
- Optometry: Eye tests are paid (£25–35); glasses/contacts paid
- Elective procedures: Long waiting times

**Recommendation:** Keep NHS as your primary coverage; optionally add a student health plan from your university for dental/optical extras.

### Germany — Statutory Health Insurance (Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung)

Health insurance is **mandatory** for all students enrolled at German universities. You cannot complete university enrollment (Immatrikulation) without proof of health insurance.

**Public (Statutory) Insurance Options:**

| Provider | Monthly Student Rate | English Support |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| TK (Techniker Krankenkasse) | ~€120/month | Yes (online + phone) |
| AOK | ~€120/month | Partial |
| Barmer | ~€120/month | Partial |
| DAK | ~€120/month | Limited |

**Who qualifies for public insurance:**
- Students under 25 years of age
- Students under 30 (or up to 14 semesters enrolled) qualify for the reduced student rate

**Private Insurance (For Students Over 30 or Older):**
- Students over 30 often must use private insurance (cannot access public insurance student rate)
- Providers: Care Concept, MAWISTA, DR-Walter
- Cost: €60–150/month depending on coverage level

**Tip:** TK (tk.de) is the most popular insurer among international students in Germany — English website and support. Apply online before arriving in Germany.

### France — CPAM (Caisse Primaire d'Assurance Maladie)

Students in France are automatically affiliated with the French national health insurance system (**Sécurité Sociale**) through their university.

**Cost:** No separate premium for students — covered as part of being enrolled
**Process:** Register at ameli.fr or through your university's student health office
**Coverage starts:** From your enrollment date
**Carte Vitale:** Your health insurance card — apply for it as soon as registered; takes 4–8 weeks to arrive

**Coverage:** Approximately 70% of approved medical costs; remaining 30% through optional complementary insurance (mutuelle)
**Optional mutuelle:** ~€20–60/month; covers the 30% co-payment; many university student plans include this

### Australia — OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover)

**OSHC is mandatory** for all students on a student visa (Subclass 500). It must be in place for the entire duration of your visa.

**Must purchase before applying for student visa.** Most universities facilitate OSHC purchase during enrollment.

**Approved OSHC Providers:**
| Provider | Approximate Annual Premium (Single) | Notes |
|----------|-------------------------------------|-------|
| Medibank | AUD $650–800/year | Most widely used |
| Bupa | AUD $650–900/year | Broad coverage |
| Allianz Global Assistance | AUD $550–700/year | Competitive pricing |
| nib | AUD $600–750/year | Good for young adults |
| CBHS Corporate Health | AUD $600–750/year | |

**Coverage includes:**
- Doctor visits (GP): Fully covered when you use a bulk-billing doctor
- Prescription medications: Partially covered (PBS pricing)
- Specialist visits: Partially covered
- Hospital (public): Fully covered
- Mental health: Covered (6–7 standard sessions, more with GP referral)
- Ambulance: Varies by state — most OSHC covers

### Canada — Provincial Health Insurance + Supplemental

Canada's healthcare is provincially administered. As an international student, you're NOT automatically covered:

**Ontario (OHIP — Ontario Health Insurance Plan):**
- **3-month waiting period** for new residents
- After waiting period: free healthcare for eligible residents
- During waiting period: Purchase private insurance (typically through university health plan)
- Annual cost of university plan: CAD $600–1,000/year

**British Columbia (MSP — Medical Services Plan):**
- 3-month waiting period
- After waiting: free coverage
- Pre-coverage: purchase Guard.me or other private insurance

**Quebec (RAMQ):**
- Students from certain treaty countries may be exempt from RAMQ and need private insurance
- Indian students: need to verify Quebec-specific rules

**Alberta, Manitoba, etc.:** Generally 3-month waiting period before provincial coverage

**Most Canadian universities have a mandatory student health plan:**
- Typically $600–1,200/year (bundled into fees or opt-out required)
- Covers dental, dental, prescription drugs, vision, and the waiting period gap

### United States — University Health Plans and ACA

The USA has no universal healthcare. International students on F-1 visas must have health insurance — it's typically a university requirement.

**University Health Plans:**
- Most universities mandate enrollment in their health plan or require proof of equivalent coverage
- Annual cost: $1,500–4,000/year depending on university and coverage
- Usually covers: doctor visits, basic hospital, prescriptions, mental health

**Health Insurance Marketplace (ACA):**
- F-1 students are NOT eligible for ACA marketplace plans (those are for US citizens/certain immigrants)

**Keeping costs down:**
- University student health center: Often much cheaper than off-campus hospitals for routine care
- Use in-network providers: Always check if a doctor/hospital is "in-network" with your insurance before visiting — out-of-network can cost 3–10× more
- Emergency vs Urgent Care: For non-life-threatening issues, use Urgent Care centers ($100–250 visit) instead of Emergency Rooms ($1,000–5,000)

---

### Netherlands — Dutch Health Insurance

**Coverage type:** Dutch residents (including students with a residence permit) must purchase Dutch mandatory basic health insurance (basisverzekering).

**Student exemption rule:** Students who are studying full-time and NOT working are generally exempt from mandatory Dutch insurance — check with your university
**If working in the Netherlands:** Must register for Dutch insurance; approximately €140/month

**Popular insurers:** Zilveren Kruis, VGZ, CZ, Menzis

## Travel Insurance for the Journey

### When to Buy Travel Insurance
- Before your departure flight from India
- Should cover: trip cancellation, flight delays (12hr+), lost luggage, medical emergency during travel
- Coverage period: From departure date to arrival at destination + first week abroad (then switch to health insurance)

### Indian Travel Insurance Providers

| Provider | Annual/Trip Cost | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| HDFC ERGO Travel Insurance | ₹1,500–3,000/trip | Popular; good claim process |
| Bajaj Allianz Travel Insurance | ₹1,500–3,500/trip | Competitive |
| ICICI Lombard Student Travel | ₹4,000–8,000/year | Student-specific plan; longer coverage |
| Tata AIG Travel Guard | ₹1,800–3,500/trip | Comprehensive |
| Star Health (International) | ₹3,500–7,000/year | Good for extended stays |

### What Travel Insurance Should Cover
- Medical emergency: Minimum $100,000 coverage
- Medical evacuation: Repatriation to India if seriously ill — covers cost of medical flight (can be $50,000–200,000 without insurance)
- Trip cancellation: If your flight is cancelled due to illness or family emergency
- Lost or delayed baggage: Especially important for long-haul flights with connections
- Personal liability: If you accidentally cause damage or injury
- Passport loss: Emergency replacement cost

## Common Situations and What Insurance Covers

### Scenario 1: You break your leg playing football in UK
NHS covers: X-ray, plaster, follow-up physiotherapy — all free. Insurance not needed for this in UK.

### Scenario 2: You have appendicitis in USA
Without insurance: $15,000–50,000 medical bill
With university health plan: $0–500 (copay + deductible depending on plan)

### Scenario 3: Your laptop is stolen from your accommodation in Germany
Health insurance doesn't cover this. Personal belongings insurance (separate policy) or renter's insurance covers theft. Cost: €5–15/month. Check if your accommodation offers contents insurance.

### Scenario 4: You need to fly home to India for a family emergency
Travel insurance: Many cover emergency trip home expenses (up to $2,000–5,000). Check your policy terms.

### Scenario 5: Mental health crisis requiring therapy
- UK: NHS covers IAPT (Improving Access to Psychological Therapies) — free but may have waiting list. University counselling service often faster.
- Australia: OSHC covers 7–10 Medicare-subsidized psychology sessions per year.
- USA: University health plan covers mental health visits (check copay — typically $20–50/session).
- Germany: Statutory health insurance (TK/AOK) covers psychotherapy — registration with a therapist and approval needed.

## FAQ

**Q: I already have star health/mediclaim in India. Does it work abroad?**
A: Standard Indian health insurance policies do NOT cover treatment abroad in most cases. Some premium plans have limited international emergency coverage. Do not rely on Indian health insurance for abroad.

**Q: Can I be without insurance for a few days before my university plan starts?**
A: This is risky. Buy travel insurance that covers the gap period (arrival to health plan start date). A single emergency visit can cost thousands of dollars.

**Q: Does OSHC cover dental in Australia?**
A: Limited. OSHC covers emergency dental only (e.g., urgent toothache treatment). Routine checkups and non-emergency dental are not covered by standard OSHC.

**Q: I need glasses and my prescription changed. Is this covered?**
A: Glasses and optometry are generally not covered by basic student health insurance in any country. Bring an extra pair of glasses from India (much cheaper) and buy contact lenses online.
