# Post-Study Work Visas for Indian Students — Country-by-Country Guide

## Overview

After completing your degree abroad, most countries offer a pathway to remain and work. This guide covers post-study work rights for the top study destinations for Indian students.

---

## 1. United Kingdom — Graduate Route Visa

**Name:** Graduate Route (formerly Post-Study Work Visa)  
**Duration:** 2 years (Bachelor's/Master's graduates); 3 years (PhD graduates)  
**Can switch to:** Skilled Worker Visa if you get a job offer from a licensed UK employer  
**Requirements:**
- Completed a bachelor's, master's, or PhD from a licensed UK university (most universities qualify)
- Held a valid Student visa at time of application
- Currently in the UK
**Work rights:** Can work in any job, any hours, any employer (including multiple jobs) — no restrictions  
**Cannot:** Extend the Graduate Route; cannot get a second Graduate Route visa  
**Apply:** Online from within the UK, fee: £822 (2024)  
**Note:** You must switch to Skilled Worker Visa or another visa before the Graduate Route expires to stay longer.

---

## 2. Australia — Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

**Name:** Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485)  
**Duration:**
- Graduate Work stream: 18 months (post-vocational/undergraduate)
- Post-Study Work stream: 2 years (bachelor's), 3 years (master's by coursework), 4 years (master's by research), 5 years (doctoral degree from any Australian uni)
- Additional: Students from regional areas get +1 or +2 years
**Requirements:**
- Studied in Australia for at least 2 academic years (1 year for doctoral programs)
- Apply within 6 months of graduation
- Hold a skills assessment for Graduate Work stream (for specific occupations)
**Work rights:** Unlimited — any job, any hours, any employer  
**Can lead to:** Employer-sponsored visas (subclass 482, 186), Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189), Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)  
**Apply:** Online via ImmiAccount; fee: AUD 1,895 (2024)

---

## 3. Canada — Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

**Name:** Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)  
**Duration:** Equals the length of study program, up to maximum 3 years
- 8-month program → 8-month PGWP
- 2-year master's → 2-year PGWP
- 4-year bachelor's → 3-year PGWP (maximum)
**Requirements:**
- Graduated from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) — virtually all Canadian universities and colleges qualify
- Applied for PGWP within 180 days of receiving final marks/transcript
- Had a valid study permit when graduated
**Work rights:** Open work permit — any employer, any job, any location in Canada  
**Can lead to:** Express Entry (Canadian Experience Class), Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)  
**Apply:** Online through IRCC portal; fee: CAD 255 (2024)  
**Important:** PGWP is a one-time permit — cannot get a second one if you study again in Canada.

---

## 4. Germany — Job Seeker Visa / Residence Permit for Job Search

**Name:** Aufenthaltserlaubnis zur Arbeitsuche (Residence Permit for Job Search)  
**Duration:** 18 months (extended from 18 months in 2023 — one of the most generous in Europe)  
**Requirements:**
- Completed a recognized degree (Bachelor's/Master's/PhD) in Germany — OR — from a foreign university that is recognized in Germany
- Sufficient German language skills (B1 minimum typically) OR job in English-medium sector
- Proof of financial means during job search period (at least €1,027/month in blocked account)
**Work rights during job search permit:** Can work up to 10 hours per week in any job  
**Once employed:** Switch to EU Blue Card or regular work permit  
**Additional:** If you studied in Germany and have a German degree, you can stay directly after graduation on your existing permit while applying for the job seeker extension.  
**EU Blue Card Germany:** For Indian graduates working in Germany in a skilled role with a salary ≥ €45,300/year (2024) — or €43,800 for shortage occupations (STEM, medicine).

---

## 5. Netherlands — Orientation Year for Highly Educated Persons (Zoekjaar)

**Name:** Orientation Year Permit (Zoekjaar hoogopgeleiden)  
**Duration:** 1 year  
**Requirements:**
- Graduated within the last 3 years from a Dutch university or a top 200 globally ranked university
- Apply within 3 years of graduation
- Financial self-sufficiency during the year
**Work rights:** Can work in any job during the orientation year  
**Can lead to:** Highly Skilled Migrant Permit (Kennismigrant) once you find a qualifying job (salary ≥ €5,331/month for graduates under 30, 2024)  
**Apply:** Via IND (Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst)

---

## 6. Ireland — Third Level Graduate Scheme

**Name:** Third Level Graduate Scheme (Stay Back Option)  
**Duration:**
- Ordinary Bachelor's degree (NFQ Level 7): 12 months
- Honours Bachelor's degree / Master's degree (NFQ Level 8/9): 24 months
- PhD (NFQ Level 10): 24 months
**Requirements:**
- Graduated from an Irish higher education institution
- Degree registered with Quality and Qualifications Ireland (QQI)
- Apply within the period specified
**Work rights:** Can work full-time in any job  
**Can lead to:** Critical Skills Employment Permit or General Employment Permit if offered a qualifying job

---

## 7. Sweden — Job Seeker Visa After Graduation

**Name:** Job Seeker Permit for graduates  
**Duration:** Up to 1 year (6-month renewable or single 12-month permit from 2023)  
**Requirements:**
- Completed a degree in Sweden (Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD)
- Have been studying in Sweden on a valid residence permit
- Financial means during the job search period (approx. SEK 10,000/month)
**Work rights:** Can work up to 40 hours/week during job search  
**Once employed:** Apply for a work permit — Sweden's work permit system is employer-driven but relatively efficient.

---

## 8. New Zealand — Post-Study Work Visa

**Name:** Post Study Work Visa  
**Duration:**
- 1 year for diplomas (level 4–6)
- 2 years for Bachelor's/Postgraduate (level 7+)
- 3 years for Master's or PhD
- Additional regional boost: +1 year if studied in a regional institution
**Requirements:**
- Graduated from a New Zealand educational institution
- Applied within 3 months of completing studies
- Have not exceeded the maximum stay on previous post-study visas
**Work rights:** Open — any employer, any role, any location  
**Can lead to:** Skilled Migrant Category Residence visa (points-based)

---

## 9. Singapore — Employment Pass (EP) / S Pass

Singapore does not have a dedicated post-study work visa. However:
- **Employment Pass (EP):** For professionals earning ≥ SGD 5,000/month (2024) — NUS/NTU/SMU graduates are highly sought. Min salary is higher for some sectors.
- **S Pass:** For mid-skill workers earning ≥ SGD 3,150/month.
- **Training Employment Pass:** For internships/training during study.  
Strategy: Get employed before graduating — NUS, NTU, and SMU career offices have excellent placement rates and employer partnerships.

---

## 10. UAE — No Post-Study Work Visa (But 1-Month Grace Period)

UAE student visas expire with course completion. However:
- Universities help students find employment before graduation.
- A new job offer → employer sponsors a UAE work permit within days.
- **Green Visa (UAE):** For skilled professionals earning ≥ AED 15,000/month — self-sponsored, 5-year renewable, no employer required.
- UAE has a very active job market for graduates — especially in finance, tech, engineering, healthcare.

---

## 11. Japan — Post-Study Work in Japan

- **After graduation:** Change status to "Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services" or "Research" work visa with a job offer.
- **Job hunting visa:** Designated Activities visa for 6 months (renewable once) to search for a job in Japan after graduation.
- JASSO and universities assist in connecting international graduates with Japanese employers.
- **N3/N4 Japanese language level** significantly increases employment chances; many IT companies hire English-only speakers in Tokyo.

## 13. Key Indian-Student Advice

1. **Start job searching 6 months before graduation** — don't wait until you have the degree in hand.
2. **Post-study work visa applications have deadlines** — missing the window means you must leave the country.
3. **UK Graduate Route → Skilled Worker transition:** Get a job offer from a licensed sponsor; salary must meet the threshold (currently £38,700/year for most roles, 2024).
4. **Australian 485 + Express Entry Canada:** Some Indian students do a Master's in Australia → 485 visa → then move to Canada on PGWP equivalency for a second pathway. Not advisable without careful immigration advice.
5. **Germany is becoming increasingly attractive** — 18-month job search period + EU Blue Card + pathway to PR after 33 months of contributions to German pension system.
