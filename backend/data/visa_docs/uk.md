# UK Student Visa — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Overview

**Visa Type:** Student Visa (formerly Tier 4 Student Visa — rebranded in 2021)  
**Issued by:** UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI), Home Office  
**Application via:** VFS Global UK in India  
**Validity:** Up to 5 years (length of course + extra time depending on course level)  
**UK is NOT a Schengen country** — separate visa required for EU travel.

The UK is the second most popular destination for Indian students globally. Over 140,000 Indian students receive UK Student Visas each year. Top universities: Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial College London, UCL, LSE, King's College London, Edinburgh, Manchester, Warwick.

---

## 2. Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)

The **CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)** is the foundation of your UK Student Visa application. It is a unique reference number that your UK university generates and sends to you once you have accepted your offer and met all conditions (including paid any deposits).

**CAS contains:**
- Your personal details
- Course details (name, duration, level)
- Institution details
- Tuition fees remaining to be paid
- The CAS reference number

**You cannot apply for a UK Student Visa without a CAS.**

You can collect your CAS up to 6 months before your course start date.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements — The 28-Day Rule

The UK's financial requirements are very specific and must be met precisely:

### What you need to show:

**Tuition fees:** The amount stated in your CAS (remaining tuition for year 1). If your CAS shows £0 (you've paid in full), you don't need to show tuition funds.

**Maintenance (living costs):**
- **London:** £1,334/month
- **Outside London:** £1,023/month
- For the course duration or 9 months, whichever is less (so maximum 9 months of maintenance funds)

**Example:** If studying at a London university for a 1-year course (£18,000 tuition shown on CAS):
- Tuition: £18,000
- Maintenance: £1,334 × 9 = £12,006
- **Total needed in account: £30,006**

### The 28-Day Rule (Critical!):
- The required funds must have been in a **single account** (yours or your sponsor's) **continuously for 28 consecutive days**.
- The **28th day** of this period must be within **31 days of your visa application submission date**.
- Funds cannot dip below the required amount during those 28 days.
- A sudden lump-sum deposit right before the 28-day window starts will be flagged.

**What counts as evidence:**
- Bank letter (from a regulated bank, on official letterhead, showing account number, name, balance)
- Official bank statements (online or paper — must show the required balance maintained for 28 days)
- Building society passbook

## 4. IELTS Requirements for UK Student Visa

The UK uses **SELT (Secure English Language Test)** providers approved by UKVI.

### For Student Visa:
- **IELTS for UKVI (Academic):** Specifically the UKVI-approved version of IELTS — not the general IELTS Academic (though many universities accept standard IELTS Academic)
- **PTE Academic UKVI:** Pearson's UKVI-approved version
- **Trinity ISE:** Another UKVI-approved test

### Typical Score Requirements:

| University Tier | IELTS for UKVI (Academic) |
|---|---|
| Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial | 7.0–7.5 overall, 7.0 each band |
| Russell Group (LSE, UCL, KCL, Warwick) | 6.5–7.0 overall |
| Other UK universities | 6.0–6.5 overall |

### IELTS Waiver:
Many UK universities waive IELTS for Indian students who:
- Completed their Bachelor's degree entirely taught in English in India
- Provide a medium of instruction letter from their Indian university

Check your specific university's English language requirements on their international admissions page.

## 5. Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)

**All UK Student Visa applicants must pay the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) as part of the visa application fee.**

- **Rate:** £776 per year (2024)
- **For a 2-year course:** £776 × 2 = £1,552
- **For a 3-year course:** £776 × 3 = £2,328
- Paid online during the visa application — added to the visa application fee

**What IHS gives you:**
Full access to the National Health Service (NHS) — the same as a UK citizen:
- Free GP (doctor) visits
- Free hospital treatment
- Free A&E (emergency)
- Mental health services
- Prescriptions: £9.90 per item in England (free in Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland)

## 7. Required Documents

1. Valid passport (at least 6 months validity + all previous passports)
2. CAS reference number and CAS details
3. Completed online visa application (gov.uk/student-visa)
4. Financial evidence meeting the 28-day rule
5. IELTS/PTE UKVI scores (if required by your university and not waived)
6. Parental consent letter + birth certificate (if under 18)
7. Academic transcripts and qualifications
8. TB test results (if applying from India — India is on the UK's TB testing country list)
9. ATAS (Academic Technology Approval Scheme) certificate — required for certain sensitive PhD/research subjects
10. IHS payment confirmation
11. Passport photograph (must meet UK photo requirements)
12. Visa application fee payment

### TB Test for Indian Students:
- **Mandatory** for Indian nationals applying for a UK Student Visa for courses > 6 months
- Must be done at a UKVI-approved clinic in India
- Approved clinics listed on gov.uk/tb-test-visa
- TB test cost: approximately INR 3,000–5,000
- Result valid for 6 months from test date

## 8. Application Process

**Step 1 — Receive CAS from UK university**
Apply to UK universities through UCAS (undergraduate) or directly (postgraduate). Once you accept your offer and pay any deposit, the university issues your CAS.

**Step 2 — Apply online**
- Go to gov.uk/student-visa
- Complete the online application form
- Pay fees (visa fee + IHS) online
- Upload documents digitally

**Step 3 — Book biometrics at VFS Global UK**
VFS Global UK in India (New Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Pune, Chandigarh, Cochin, Jaipur) handles biometric collection.

**Step 4 — Submit documents at VFS**
Attend your VFS appointment for biometrics (fingerprints + photograph). Submit physical documents if required (or they may be fully digital).

**Step 5 — Processing**
- Standard processing: 3 weeks
- Priority: 5 business days (additional £250)
- Super priority: Next working day (additional £500)

**Step 6 — Receive decision**
- Passport returned with visa sticker (BRP collection letter if applying from India — collect Biometric Residence Permit on arrival in UK)

**Step 7 — Arrive in UK**
- Collect BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) from the UK Post Office specified in your visa letter — within 10 days of arrival
- Register with NHS GP
- Attend university international student induction

## 9. Work Rights on UK Student Visa

| Study Level | During Term | During Holidays |
|---|---|---|
| Degree level (RQF 6+) | Up to 20 hours/week | Full-time |
| Postgraduate (RQF 7+) | Up to 20 hours/week | Full-time |
| Foundation/Pre-sessional | Up to 10 hours/week | Full-time |
| PhD | Up to 20 hours/week | Full-time |

**No separate work permit needed** — work rights are on the Student Visa itself.

**Cannot:** Work as a self-employed person, set up a business, work as a professional sportsperson or entertainer, or work in a permanent full-time role.

---

## 10. Graduate Route Visa (Post-Study Work)

After graduating from a UK university:
- **Duration:** 2 years (Bachelor's/Master's graduates); 3 years (PhD graduates)
- **Eligibility:** Studied on a valid Student Visa at a UK higher education provider
- **Apply:** From within the UK, before Student Visa expires
- **Work rights:** Any job, any employer, any hours — completely open
- **Fee:** £822 (2024)
- **Cannot be extended** — must switch to Skilled Worker Visa or leave

**Skilled Worker Visa from Graduate Route:**
- Requires a job offer from a UK employer with a sponsor licence
- Salary threshold: £38,700/year (2024) for most roles (lower for shortage occupations)
- Healthcare workers: NHS salary scales apply

## 11. Common UK Student Visa Rejection Reasons

1. **28-day rule not met** — most common reason; funds not maintained for full 28 days or dipped below required amount.
2. **CAS expired or invalid** — CAS is valid for 6 months; must apply before it expires.
3. **TB test not done** — mandatory for Indian nationals; forgetting this is a common mistake.
4. **IELTS below university/UKVI minimum** — check both university AND UKVI minimum (the visa requirement may differ from the university's admission requirement).
5. **Documents not in English** — all documents must be in English or officially translated.
6. **IHS not paid** — the visa application will be rejected if IHS is outstanding.
7. **Financial documents too old** — bank statements must show the current date within the application window.
