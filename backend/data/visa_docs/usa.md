# USA F-1 Student Visa — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Overview

**Visa Type:** F-1 — Academic Student Visa  
**Issued by:** US Embassy (New Delhi) / US Consulates (Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad)  
**Authority:** US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), US Department of State  
**Validity:** Duration of Status (D/S) — valid as long as you maintain full-time enrollment and comply with visa conditions  
**SEVIS:** You must be registered in SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) — your university manages this  

The United States remains the world's most popular study destination. Over 280,000 Indian students study in the USA annually, making India the second-largest source country after China. Top universities: MIT, Stanford, Harvard, Caltech, Columbia, CMU, Georgia Tech, Purdue, UIUC, UT Austin, UMich.

---

## 2. The F-1 Visa Process — Overview

The F-1 process involves:
1. Get admitted to a SEVP-certified US institution
2. Receive the I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status)
3. Pay the SEVIS fee (I-901)
4. Complete DS-160 (online visa application)
5. Schedule and attend visa interview at US Embassy/Consulate in India
6. Receive visa decision

## 3. IELTS / TOEFL Requirements for USA

US universities **do not have a federal minimum** — each university sets its own English requirement.

### Typical Requirements:

| Tier | TOEFL iBT | IELTS Academic | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top 10 universities | 100–110 | 7.0–7.5 | MIT, Stanford, Harvard, etc. |
| Top 50 universities | 90–100 | 6.5–7.0 | Most state flagships |
| General universities | 79–90 | 6.0–6.5 | Wide range of programs |

### IELTS Waiver Conditions (USA):
Many US universities waive IELTS/TOEFL if:
- Your bachelor's degree was fully taught in English at an accredited institution (Indian universities qualify at most schools)
- You studied for 2+ years in an English-speaking country
- You scored very highly on the verbal section of GRE/GMAT

Always check the specific university's English waiver policy.

## 4. Financial Requirements

US universities set their own Cost of Attendance (COA) shown on your I-20. Financial proof must cover at least **1 year of COA**.

**Typical costs:**
- **Tuition:** USD 15,000–60,000/year (state universities: USD 25,000–45,000; Ivy League: USD 55,000–65,000)
- **Living expenses:** USD 12,000–25,000/year (varies by city — NYC, Boston, SF are expensive; Midwest universities are cheaper)
- **Total COA:** USD 35,000–90,000/year depending on university and location

**Funding options:**
1. **Personal/family funds:** Bank statements showing COA × program years
2. **Scholarship:** Award letter (TA, RA, fellowship, external scholarships)
3. **Teaching/Research Assistantship (TA/RA):** Very common for Master's and PhD — covers tuition + stipend (USD 1,500–2,500/month)
4. **Education loan:** Indian bank education loan sanction letter

**CGPA for Financial Aid:**
- PhD and MS (research) programs: TAs/RAs widely available at CGPA 8.0+/10 from premier Indian institutes
- Funded PhD programs: Usually receive full tuition waiver + stipend — essentially free for those admitted
- Master's (coursework): Mostly self-funded; scholarships rare; median self-funded cost USD 50,000–80,000 total for 2 years

## 5. SEVIS Fee (I-901)

**SEVIS fee:** USD 350 for F-1 students  
**Pay at:** www.fmjfee.com  
**When:** Pay before scheduling the visa interview  
**Receipt:** Keep the I-901 payment receipt — required at the visa interview and at the US port of entry

---

## 6. DS-160 — Online Visa Application

**DS-160:** The online nonimmigrant visa application form  
**Complete at:** ceac.state.gov  
**Key sections:**
- Personal information (exact as in passport)
- Travel history to USA or other countries
- US contacts (university's international student office address and phone)
- Previous visa denials (must disclose — lying on DS-160 is grounds for permanent ban)
- "Do you intend to work in the US?" → Answer "No" for F-1 (work authorization is separate)

**Photo:** Upload digitally (required for DS-160 — specific size/background requirements)

---

## 7. Visa Interview

**Book appointment:** ais.usvisa-info.com  
**Locations:** New Delhi (Embassy), Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad (Consulates)  
**Current wait times:** Varies significantly by location and season. Check the official appointment wait time dashboard before planning.

**Documents to bring:**
1. Valid passport (+ all old passports)
2. DS-160 confirmation page (barcode page)
3. Visa appointment confirmation
4. SEVIS I-901 payment receipt
5. I-20 (signed by DSO — Designated School Official)
6. Admission letter from university
7. Academic transcripts (10th, 12th, undergraduate — originals)
8. GRE/GMAT scores (if applicable)
9. TOEFL/IELTS scores (if applicable)
10. Financial proof (bank statements, FD, scholarship, loan sanction)
11. Parental IT returns, salary slips, employer letter (if parent is sponsoring)
12. Visa fee payment confirmation (MRV fee — USD 185)
13. Evidence of ties to India (family, property, future job prospects)

**Interview tips:**
- Most interviews are 2–5 minutes — be concise and direct
- The key test is demonstrating non-immigrant intent (214b) — you'll return to India after studies
- Know your program, university, and career plan thoroughly
- Be honest — inconsistencies lead to 221g administrative processing delays

## 8. Work Rights on F-1 Visa

### During Studies:
- **On-campus employment:** Up to 20 hours/week during semesters; full-time during breaks. No separate authorization needed.
- **Off-campus employment (CPT — Curricular Practical Training):** Work related to your major while enrolled. Must be part of your curriculum. Approved by your DSO and university.
- **OPT — Optional Practical Training (off-campus):** 12 months total post-completion OR pre-completion

### OPT (Optional Practical Training):
- **Duration:** 12 months standard
- **STEM OPT Extension:** If you graduate with a STEM degree from an accredited US institution, you can apply for a **24-month STEM OPT extension** = 36 months total of OPT
- **Eligible STEM fields:** Computer Science, Engineering, Mathematics, Statistics, Data Science, Physics, Biology, Economics (with quantitative focus), and others on the STEM Designated Degree Program list
- **Requirement:** Employed by an E-Verify employer for the STEM extension
- **Apply for OPT:** Through your DSO; USCIS form I-765; apply 90 days before graduation; processing 3–5 months

### OPT → H-1B Visa:
- After OPT, Indian students typically attempt the H-1B lottery (specialty occupation work visa)
- H-1B cap: 65,000 standard + 20,000 master's cap (for US master's degree holders)
- H-1B registration: March; lottery results: April; start: October 1
- Indian applicants face a significant backlog for H-1B → green card (employment-based categories are heavily oversubscribed for Indian nationals)

## 9. Common USCIS/F-1 Rules Indian Students Must Know

1. **Full-time enrollment mandatory:** Minimum 12 credit hours/semester (undergraduate), 9 credit hours (graduate). Dropping below triggers unauthorized status.
2. **Report address change to DSO within 10 days** of moving.
3. **Travel outside USA:** F-1 students can travel during breaks but must have valid F-1 visa stamp, valid I-20 (travel signature from DSO within last 12 months), and valid EAD if on OPT.
4. **Grace period:** 60 days after program end date / OPT end date to depart or change status.
5. **Maintaining status:** Taking online classes only (without special authorization), unauthorized employment, or failing to report are violations that can lead to SEVIS termination.

## 10. GRE / GMAT Requirements

| Program | GRE/GMAT |
|---|---|
| MS in Engineering, CS, Sciences | GRE typically required (many programs waived GRE post-2020, now reinstating) |
| PhD programs | GRE required at most programs |
| MBA | GMAT required; some accept GRE |
| MS in Management/Finance | GMAT or GRE |
| MS in Data Science | GRE often required |

**Competitive GRE scores for top US engineering/CS programs:**
- Quantitative: 165–170 (96th–100th percentile)
- Verbal: 155–165
- Analytical Writing: 4.0–5.0
