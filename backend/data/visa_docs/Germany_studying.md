##  Visa for studying 

With its wide range of study programmes and low tuition fees, Germany attracts many international students every year. Find out how to apply for a student visa here.

Are you interested in studying in Germany? A German degree opens many doors. There is a wide range of international study programmes and the fees are low. With this in mind, you can obtain a [visa](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/visum) for the purpose of studying in Germany. A [residence permit](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/aufenthaltserlaubnis) under [Section 16b of the Residence Act (AufenthG) (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/englisch_aufenthg/englisch_aufenthg.html#p0278) is also valid for preparatory measures prior to enrolment in higher education, such as language courses, preparatory courses for [university](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/universitaet) admission or similar institutions, or doctoral studies at a German university.  
  
To find out if you need a visa to enter Germany, please refer to the “[Do I need a visa?](/en/visa-residence/procedure/do-i-need-visa)” section.

##  Requirements for the visa for studying in Germany 

  * You have been admitted to a state-recognised [higher education institution](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/hochschule) in Germany. To find out how to apply for a place on a study programme in Germany, see the [“Studies in Germany”](/en/study-vocational-training/studies-in-germany) section.
  * You are able to cover your living costs for the duration of your study programme. You can prove that you have sufficient funds through a blocked bank account (**at least €11,904** in 2026), a scholarship or a [declaration of commitment](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/verpflichtungserklaerung).
  * Some programmes require a certain level of language proficiency, usually level B2 of the [Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/gemeinsamer-europaeischer-referenzrahmen-sprachen). You must prove your language skills to the German embassy or consulate.

Do you meet the above requirements? Then find out about how the [entry and visa process](/en/visa-residence/procedure/entry-process) works.

##  Your prospects with the visa for studying 

Residence permits for study purposes are usually issued for an initial period of two years. If you have not obtained your degree within this period, you can request to have your residence permit renewed for a further two years.  
  
During your time at university, you can work up to 140 full days or 280 half days per year, or up to 20 hours per week. You can take part-time student jobs for an unlimited period.

##  Successfully graduated - this is how you can stay in Germany 

After successfully completing your degree, you can extend your stay in Germany by applying for a residence permit for jobseekers under [Section 20 (1) No. 1 of the Residence Act (AufenthG) (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/englisch_aufenthg/englisch_aufenthg.html#p0570). The residence permit is issued for up to 18 months as a means of finding skilled employment in Germany. You can work in any occupation while looking for skilled employment. As soon as you have found a [qualified employment](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/qualifizierte-beschaeftigung) position, you can apply to convert your residence permit for study purposes into a [residence permit for skilled workers](/en/visa-residence/types/work-qualified-professionals) or an [EU Blue Card](/en/visa-residence/types/eu-blue-card). You can find out more about the opportunities available to you while you study in Germany and after you graduate in the “[Prospects after graduation](/en/study-vocational-training/studies-in-germany/prospects-after)” section.

##  Settlement permit after graduating from a German university 

Graduates of German higher education institutions can apply for a settlement permit [(Section 18c (1) sentence 2 of the Residence Act (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/englisch_aufenthg/englisch_aufenthg.html#p0406) [AufenthG]) after working in Germany for more than two years.

##  Explaining mobility in the EU for students from third countries 

Do you have a student visa from another EU country and would like to study in Germany for a while? If your stay in Germany does not exceed 360 days, you do not need a [residence title](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/aufenthaltstitel) for Germany. However, the host educational institution in Germany must inform the [Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF)](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/bundesamt-fuer-migration-und-fluechtlinge-bamf) and the competent authority in the other EU Member State about your intended study period in Germany. There are a number of documents that need to be submitted to the authorities when they are notified of your plans to study abroad.

##  No place to study yet? Visa for the purpose of seeking a place in higher education 

Have you decided to study in Germany but have not yet been admitted to a higher education institution? If you meet certain requirements, you may be entitled to enter Germany on a visa for seeking a place in higher education under [Section 17 (2) of the Residence Act (AufenthG) (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/englisch_aufenthg/englisch_aufenthg.html#p0353).  
  
To find out if you need a visa to enter Germany, please refer to the “[Do I need a visa?](/en/visa-residence/procedure/do-i-need-visa)” section.

###  Requirements for the visa for seeking a place in higher education 

  * You can prove that you have a school-leaving certificate that entitles you to study at a German higher education institution or attend a [preparatory course](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/service/glossary/glossary-detail/glossar/do/show/studienkolleg) for university admission.
  * You meet the language requirements for your chosen study programme. Contact your local German embassy or consulate to find out which documents are required.
  * You can cover your living costs for the entire duration of your stay. You can prove this by opening a blocked bank account with a minimum of **€1,091** per month (as of 2026) or by submitting a declaration of commitment.

Do you meet the above requirements? Then find out about how the [entry and visa process](/en/visa-residence/procedure/entry-process) works.

Here you find an [overview](/fileadmin/1_Rebrush_2022/a_Fachkraefte/PDF-Dateien/3_Visum_u_Aufenthalt/Visagrafik_EN/Lebensunterhaltssicherung_Uebersicht_EN.pdf) of how to secure your livelihood according to the purpose of stay.

##  Prospects with the visa for seeking a place in higher education 

A visa or residence permit for the purpose of seeking a place in higher education allows you to stay in Germany for up to nine months. During this time, you can apply for a place at a university or for a preparatory measure prior to enrolment in higher education. Examples of preparatory measures include attending a language course or a preparatory course for university admission. A residence permit for seeking a place in higher education cannot be renewed for the same purpose.

During this time, you are permitted to work up to 20 hours a week. You also have the opportunity to complete up to two weeks of trial work. This means that you can earn some money during your stay for seeking a place in higher education.

Have you been admitted to a higher education institution in Germany or to a preparatory course for university admission? Then you can apply to your [local foreigners authority (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://bamf-navi.bamf.de/en/) for a [residence permit for the purpose of studying](/en/visa-residence/types/studying) under [Section 16b of the Residence Act (AufenthG) (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/englisch_aufenthg/englisch_aufenthg.html#p0275).

#####  Residence permit 

_German: Aufenthaltserlaubnis_

The Immigration Act provides for two titles which govern entry and residence in Germany: the settlement permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis) and the residence permit (Aufenthaltserlaubnis). The residence permit is temporary and is granted for a specific reason, such as gainful employment, training or family reunification, or else for humanitarian, legal or political reasons. 

Theme: [Visas](/en/service/glossary/glossar/do/bycat?tx_vcglossar_pi1%5Bcat%5D=11&cHash=51980b6c108e44ef95d18ca5d30fd6ca)

#####  Visa 

_German: Visum_

A visa entitles the holder to enter or travel through another country. In Germany, third-country nationals require this kind of residence permit. There are exceptions for EU nationals and nationals of some selected countries. Depending on the purpose and duration of the stay, there are different visa types. The German missions abroad are responsible for issuing visas.

#####  University 

_German: Universität_

A university is a type of higher education institution which serves scientific research and teaching across a broad range of subjects and has the right to award doctorate degrees. It traditionally places a stronger focus on theoretical basic research than other types of higher education institutions.  
In common parlance – including many information portals – the term "university" is often used as a synonym for "higher education institution", although strictly speaking, univeristies are only a subcategory.

#####  Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) 

_German: Gemeinsamer Europäischer Referenzrahmen für Sprachen (GER)_

The CEFR is a reference tool in working with languages. It was developed by the Council of Europe. The CEFR provides a common ground for the comparability of foreign language skills. It is used in Europe, as well as on other continents.  

#####  Declaration of Commitment 

_German: Verpflichtungserklärung_

People from non-EU States who want to apply for a visa to come to Germany may be invited by people living in Germany if the latter submit a Declaration of Commitment (Verpflichtungserklärung) to a Foreigners Authority in Germany. This declaration obliges the inviter to guarantee the financial support of the visa applicant during their stay in Germany. In some German cities, such as [Offenbach am Main (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.offenbach.de/buerger_innen/buerger-service/online-dienstleistungen/verpflichtungserklaerung.php) and [Wiesbaden (External link) <desc>box with arrow to the right</desc>](https://www.wiesbaden.de/vv/produkte/33/141010100000003374.php), the declaration can also be submitted via online application.

Theme: [Visas](/en/service/glossary/glossar/do/bycat?tx_vcglossar_pi1%5Bcat%5D=11&cHash=51980b6c108e44ef95d18ca5d30fd6ca)

#####  Higher education institution 

_German: Hochschule_

A higher education institution is an institution for scientific research and teaching that imparts theoretical and practical knowledge in a scientific form. Students can obtain academic degrees there by passing examinations.  
The term ‘higher education institution’ is also used as an umbrella term for all institutions of tertiary education, i.e., universities, universities of applied sciences (also known as colleges of applied sciences), art and music colleges, teacher training colleges, theological colleges and other specialised institutions.

#####  Qualified employment 

_German: qualifizierte Beschäftigung_

An employment position is considered as qualified, if exercising the occupation requires skills and knowledge which are generally acquired during academic studies or qualified vocational training.

#####  Residence title 

_German: Aufenthaltstitel_

A residence title entitles people from abroad to enter and reside in Germany. Residence titles may be granted in the form of a visa, residence permit, an EU Blue Card, a settlement permit, or a permanent EU residence permit.

#####  Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF) 

_German: Bundesamt für Migration und Flüchtlinge (BAMF)_

The Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF) is the Centre of Excellence for asylum, migration and integration in Germany. Nowadays, its range of responsibilities is very diverse.

Theme: [Institutions & Authorities](/en/service/glossary/glossar/do/bycat?tx_vcglossar_pi1%5Bcat%5D=13&cHash=363f6486b7e279f34a8c4366a1a9ac80)

#####  Preparatory course 

_German: Studienkolleg_

A preparatory course (Studienkolleg) is a preparatory course for people from abroad applying to study in Germany and whose school-leaving certificate is not recognised as being equivalent to the German Abitur. The assessment (Feststellungsprüfung) at the end of this one-year course is equivalent to a German qualification for university entrance.
