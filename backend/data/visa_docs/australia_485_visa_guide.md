# Australia Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485) — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## What is the Subclass 485?

The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) allows international students who have recently graduated from an Australian educational institution to live, study, and work in Australia on a temporary basis after completing their course. It is one of the most popular post-study visas in the world and a primary pathway to Australian permanent residence for thousands of Indian students each year.

The 485 visa replaced the earlier 457-based graduate pathways and has evolved significantly — Australian degrees now lead to progressively longer 485 visas depending on qualification level.

---

## Two Streams

### 1. Graduate Work Stream

**Who it's for:** Graduates whose skills and qualifications match an occupation on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL).

**Duration:** **18 months**

**Additional Requirements:**
- Skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority (e.g., Engineers Australia, VETASSESS, Australian Computer Society, ANMAC for nursing)
- Qualification from CRICOS-registered provider
- Must apply for skills assessment before or concurrently with 485 application

**When to use:** Less common now — most students use the Post-Study Work Stream for the longer duration

### 2. Post-Study Work Stream (Most Common for Indian Students)

**Who it's for:** Students who have completed at least 2 years of study in Australia (cumulative — can be from multiple qualifications)

**Duration based on qualification:**

| Qualification Completed | 485 Duration |
|------------------------|-------------|
| Bachelor's degree (3–4 years) | **2 years** |
| Bachelor's Honours degree (1-year additional) | **2 years** |
| Graduate Certificate (6 months) | **2 years** |
| Graduate Diploma (1 year) | **2 years** |
| Master's by Coursework (1–2 years) | **3 years** |
| Master's by Research | **3 years** |
| Doctoral Degree (PhD) | **4 years** |

**Key rule:** You must have studied in Australia for at least 2 cumulative years. A 1-year master's alone doesn't qualify — you'd need to have done earlier study in Australia (e.g., a prior bachelor's or diploma).

## Eligibility Requirements

### Academic Requirements
- Completed a qualification from a CRICOS-registered provider (all Australian universities and most TAFE colleges are CRICOS-registered)
- Apply within 6 months of receiving your final qualification result notification
- Must have been physically in Australia for at least half of your study period

### English Language Requirements
All 485 applicants must meet the minimum English standard:

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|--------------|
| IELTS Academic | **6.0 in each band** (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking) |
| PTE Academic | **50 in each communicative skill** |
| TOEFL iBT | **12 in each section** |
| Cambridge CAE | 169 in each skill |

**Important:** The English test results cannot be more than 3 years old at the time of application.

### Other Requirements
- Be under **50 years of age** when applying
- Health insurance: Must have or get **OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover)** from a CRICOS-approved insurer
- Health examination: Medical exam required for 485
- Character requirements: No serious criminal convictions (police clearances required)
- Must be physically in Australia when applying (cannot apply from India)

## Application Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Create an ImmiAccount
- Create your account at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au
- All Australian visa applications are online; there is no paper form option for 485
- You must be in Australia when you submit the application

### Step 2: Gather Required Documents

**Academic documents:**
- Transcript showing all courses completed and passed
- Award letter / degree certificate from your institution (if already issued)
- Course completion letter from your institution (if degree not yet issued)

**Identity documents:**
- Passport (valid)
- Birth certificate

**English language:**
- IELTS/PTE/TOEFL result with date (within 3 years)

**Health insurance:**
- Current OSHC policy certificate covering the period you're applying for

**Other:**
- Police clearances from every country where you've lived for 12+ months since age 16
  - India: Request PCC from your local Police Superintendent's office or Passport Seva Kendra
  - Allow 4–8 weeks to get Indian PCC
  - PCC should be apostilled for Australian immigration purposes
- Medical examination documents (see below)
- Skills assessment documents (Graduate Work Stream only)

### Step 3: Pay Application Fee
- **AUD $1,895** (as of 2024) — this is per main applicant
- Dependent family members: AUD $945 each
- Payment by credit card in ImmiAccount
- Note: Fees are non-refundable even if rejected

### Step 4: Health Examination
- Attend a Department of Home Affairs approved Health Panel Physician in Australia
- Health exam includes: chest X-ray, blood tests, general physical examination
- Cost: approximately **AUD $300–450**
- Find panel physicians at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/help-support/contact-us/offices-and-locations/australia
- Results go directly from physician to Department — you receive a HAP (Health Assessment Portal) ID

### Step 5: Police Clearances
- Required for every country you've lived in for cumulative 12+ months since age 16
- Most Indian students need:
  - Australian Federal Police (AFP) check — apply online at afp.gov.au
  - Indian National Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)
- AFP check fee: AUD $42; processing: approximately 15 days
- Indian PCC must be apostilled (see apostille guide)

### Step 6: Submit Application
- Complete form online in ImmiAccount
- Upload all documents
- Pay fee
- Submit

### Step 7: Wait for Decision
- **Bridging Visa A** is automatically granted when you apply, allowing you to stay and work in Australia while waiting
- Bridging Visa A work rights: same as your current visa (usually full-time)
- **Processing time:** 60–90 days typically (can be 4–6 months in busy periods)
- Track status in ImmiAccount

## Regional Australia Bonus

This is one of the most significant incentives in Australian immigration for Indian students.

### If you lived and studied in regional Australia for at least 2 years on your student visa:

You can apply for the **subclass 485 — Regional** stream which gives:
- Duration: **5 years** (compared to 2–4 years for standard 485)
- Same work rights (unlimited)
- Same path to PR afterward

### What counts as "regional"?

**Regional areas for this purpose:** Anywhere in Australia EXCEPT the following main capital cities:
- Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, and Gold Coast

Eligible regional cities include (but not limited to):
- Canberra (ACT)
- Darwin (NT)
- Hobart (TAS)
- Newcastle, Wollongong (NSW)
- Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo (VIC)
- Toowoomba, Cairns, Townsville (QLD)
- All regional university campuses

**Growing universities with regional campuses popular with Indian students:**
- University of Southern Queensland (Toowoomba, Ipswich)
- Charles Darwin University (Darwin)
- University of Tasmania (Hobart)
- Federation University (Ballarat, Gippsland)
- Central Queensland University (Rockhampton, Bundaberg, Cairns)

## What You Can Do on 485

### Work Rights
- **Full-time, unrestricted work** for any employer, in any job, anywhere in Australia
- Work in multiple jobs simultaneously
- Work as a contractor or freelancer
- No need for employer sponsorship

### Study
- Enroll in any course (but you already graduated — most use 485 for work experience)

### Travel
- Multiple entry — travel in and out of Australia freely
- Visit India, travel internationally, and return to Australia during the visa validity

### Family
- Bring dependent spouse and children on the same 485 application
- Dependent spouse: full work rights if you're on Post-Study Work Stream
- Children can attend Australian schools (public school fees may apply for temporary visa holders)

## Pathway to Australian PR from 485

The 485 is a stepping stone — most Indian students use it to gain Australian work experience and then apply for permanent residence.

### Most Common PR Pathways from 485

**1. Employer Nomination Scheme — Subclass 186**
- Employer sponsors you for PR directly
- Direct Entry stream: Employer nominates + ACS/skills assessment + 3 years experience
- Transition stream: 3 years on Temporary Skill Shortage (482) visa first

**2. Skilled Independent — Subclass 189 (Points-Based)**
- No sponsorship needed
- Invitation-only based on points
- English IELTS requirement: **7.0 in each band**
- Skills assessment required from relevant body (e.g., ACS for IT, Engineers Australia for engineering)
- Minimum points to receive invitation: typically 65–90+ depending on occupation and visa processing year

**3. Skilled Nominated — Subclass 190**
- State or territory nominates you (adds 5 points)
- Each state has its own nomination criteria and occupation lists

**4. Skilled Work Regional — Subclass 491 → PR via 191**
- Work in regional area for 3 years on 491
- Apply for Subclass 191 (permanent regional) PR
- Excellent pathway for those in regional occupations

### Points Test Summary for 189/190

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Under 25 | 25 pts |
| 25–32 | 30 pts |
| 33–39 | 25 pts |
| 40–44 | 15 pts |
| 45–49 | 0 pts |
| Bachelor's | 15 pts |
| Master's or Honours | 15 pts |
| PhD | 20 pts |
| Credentialed community language | 5 pts |
| IELTS 7.0 in each band | 10 pts |
| 1 year Australian work experience | 5 pts |
| 3 years Australian work experience | 10 pts |
| 5 years Australian work experience | 15 pts |
| 8 years+ Australian work experience | 20 pts |
| Spouse skills | 5 pts |
| State nomination (190) | 5 pts |
| Regional study/work (491) | 15 pts |

Minimum 65 points required to submit Expression of Interest; invitations typically go to 80–90+ points.

## FAQ

**Q: I completed a 2-year master's in Australia. How long is my 485?**
A: 3 years Post-Study Work stream. Masters by coursework qualify for the 3-year 485.

**Q: I did my bachelor's in India and came to Australia for a 1-year master's. Do I qualify for 485?**
A: You need at least 2 cumulative years of study in Australia. A 1-year master's is not enough on its own. You would qualify if you also did a 6-month+ additional course in Australia (e.g., Graduate Certificate before your master's), making your total Australian study time at least 24 months.

**Q: Can I apply for 485 while my student visa is still valid?**
A: Yes, you must apply for the 485 within 6 months of your qualification completion notification. Your student visa validity is separate from this 6-month window.

**Q: My IELTS score is 5.5 overall. Can I still apply for 485?**
A: No. The 485 requires a minimum of 6.0 in EACH band, not just overall. If any individual band is below 6.0, you need to resit and improve before applying for 485.

**Q: Can I do a PhD in Australia for 3.5 years and then get a 4-year 485?**
A: Yes. Doctoral degree holders get a 4-year 485 visa, regardless of whether the PhD took 3 or 6 years to complete.
