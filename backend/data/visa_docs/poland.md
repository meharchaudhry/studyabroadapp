# Studying in Poland — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Poland?

Poland has emerged as a surprisingly popular destination for Indian students, particularly for medical programs and engineering. It offers:
- **Very low tuition fees** for English-medium programs (€2,000–5,000/year vs £15,000+ in UK)
- **EU-recognized degrees** (Polish degrees are Lisbon Convention-compliant)
- **Low cost of living** (one of the cheapest in the EU)
- Access to Schengen zone for travel
- Growing economy with increasing job opportunities
- English-medium programs widely available since 2010s

**Key facts:**
- Capital: Warsaw; Population: 37 million
- Currency: Polish Złoty (PLN); €1 ≈ PLN 4.3
- EU and Schengen member

## Poland Student Visa

### Type of Visa
India is not in the EU/Schengen "visa-free" zone. Indian students need a **Polish National Visa (Type D)** for stays longer than 90 days.

### Application Location
- **Embassy of the Republic of Poland in New Delhi:** 50-M, Shantipath, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi 110021
- **Polish Consulate in Mumbai:** For residents of western India

### Required Documents

1. Completed visa application form (available at: poland.visahq.in or Polish Embassy website)
2. Valid passport (valid 3 months beyond visa expiry)
3. Recent passport photos (2 × 3.5cm × 4.5cm)
4. Admission/acceptance letter from Polish university
5. Proof of tuition fees payment (receipt of first installment)
6. Financial proof:
   - PLN 5,000–6,000 (approximately €1,100–1,400) per academic year minimum
   - Or: scholarship letter confirming financial support
   - Bank statements last 6 months
7. Health insurance:
   - Minimum coverage €30,000 (valid in Poland and entire Schengen zone)
   - Purchase from Indian insurer with Schengen coverage, or use ISIC student plan
8. Proof of accommodation in Poland:
   - Dormitory confirmation from university, OR
   - Rental agreement
9. Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — for stays exceeding 6 months
10. Proof of educational qualifications (Class 12 + bachelor's if applicable; certified translation to English)

**Translation requirement:** Documents in Hindi/regional languages must be officially translated to English. Get certified translations from sworn translators or notary-certified translators.

### Visa Fee
- **€80** for Type D National Visa (long stay, more than 90 days)

### Processing Time
- Typically 15–30 working days; apply at least 6–8 weeks before departure

## Post-Arrival: Residence Permit

Within 30 days of arriving in Poland, you must apply for a **Temporary Residence Permit (Karta Pobytu)** at the regional Voivode (provincial authority) office.

**Required documents for Karta Pobytu:**
- Completed application form
- Passport + photocopy
- University enrollment confirmation (aktualny stan studiów)
- Accommodation proof
- Financial evidence
- 4 passport photos
- Payment of application fee (approximately PLN 340 = €80)

**Processing time:** 1–3 months; you receive a stamp in your passport allowing you to stay during processing

**Karta Pobytu validity:** Usually valid for duration of your studies + 30 days

## Work Rights

Indian students in Poland with a valid Temporary Residence Permit (Karta Pobytu) may:
- Work up to **20 hours per week** without a separate work permit during the academic year
- Full-time during official university breaks
- Minimum wage in Poland: approximately PLN 28.10/hour (2024) = approximately €6.50/hour — less than Western Europe
- Part-time student jobs available in Warsaw and major university cities

---

## Healthcare in Poland

- Poland has a public healthcare system (NFZ — Narodowy Fundusz Zdrowia)
- International students must enroll in NFZ or get private health insurance
- Many Indian students use student health plans or private insurance (PZU Health, Medicover, LuxMed)
- Medicover and LuxMed offer English-language private clinics in all major Polish cities — excellent quality, approximately PLN 100–200/month student plan

---

## Post-Study Options

Poland does not have a dedicated post-study work visa. Options:
- Find employment in Poland: Work permit required (employer applies on your behalf)
- EU Blue Card: For high-skilled workers with salary threshold
- Schengen freedom: Your Polish experience enables easier applications elsewhere in EU

**Practical reality:** Most Indian students study in Poland for the degree/cost advantage, then pursue careers in Germany, UK, or back in India. Poland has a growing IT and tech sector in Warsaw, Kraków, and Wrocław.

## FAQ

**Q: Is an MBBS degree from Poland valid in India?**
A: Yes, if the medical university is recognized by the National Medical Commission (NMC) of India. Check the NMC list before enrolling. Major Polish medical universities (Jagiellonian, Warsaw, Poznań, Wrocław, Gdańsk) are on the NMC list. You must clear the FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination) to practice in India after returning.

**Q: Do I need to know Polish for daily life?**
A: Basic Polish is very helpful for daily life, especially outside Warsaw. English is widely spoken by young people and in universities. Most university administration communicates in English for international students. Consider taking a beginner Polish course in your first semester.

**Q: Is it hard to get a visa for Germany/France while studying in Poland?**
A: As a Schengen visa holder or Karta Pobytu (Temporary Residence Permit), you can travel visa-free within the Schengen area for short visits. For longer stays or work visas in other EU countries, you'd still apply separately.
