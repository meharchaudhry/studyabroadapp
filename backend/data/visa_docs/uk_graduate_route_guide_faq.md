# UK Graduate Route — Complete FAQ Guide

## What Is the Graduate Route?

The Graduate Route is a UK immigration route for international students who have completed a degree at a UK Higher Education Provider. It allows graduates to stay in the UK and work (or look for work) for 2 years after a bachelor's or master's degree, or 3 years after a PhD.

Unlike the Skilled Worker Visa, the Graduate Route requires no job offer, no salary threshold, and no sponsor. It is entirely flexible, allowing you to work, change jobs, or be self-employed without restriction.

## Eligibility Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who is eligible for the Graduate Route?**
A: You must:
1. Have completed a degree at the bachelor's level or above at a UK Higher Education Provider licensed by UKVI (the immigration authority)
2. Have held a valid UK Student Visa (or Tier 4 student visa) for the course
3. Be in the UK when you apply
4. Apply before your current Student Visa expires

**Q: Is the Graduate Route available to PhD students?**
A: Yes. PhD graduates get 3 years on the Graduate Route instead of 2 years. The degree must be a doctorate-level qualification (PhD, EdD, DBA, or equivalent) from a licensed UK institution.

**Q: What if I studied at a private university in the UK?**
A: The university must be a UKVI-licensed sponsor institution. Most accredited UK universities are licensed. To verify, check the UKVI register of licensed sponsors at gov.uk. Some smaller private colleges or language schools are not licensed for Graduate Route purposes — this is an important check before enrolling.

**Q: Can I apply if I studied abroad (e.g., at a UK university campus in Dubai)?**
A: No. The degree must have been physically studied at the UK campus. Distance learning or offshore campus study does not qualify for the Graduate Route.

**Q: I did a joint degree with a European university. Am I eligible?**
A: Only if the UK institution is on the degree certificate and the course was licensed by UKVI. Contact the UK university's international student advisory service for confirmation.

**Q: I failed my dissertation but passed overall — am I eligible?**
A: The degree must be successfully completed and conferred. If you passed the overall degree classification (even if one component was retaken or resubmitted), you are eligible. "Successfully completed" means the degree has been officially awarded by the institution.

## Application Process

**Q: When can I apply for the Graduate Route?**
A: You can apply once you have received official notification that your degree has been awarded (or that you have met all requirements). You do not need to wait for the physical degree certificate — confirmation from your university registrar or an official results notification is sufficient.

**Q: Where do I apply — online or at a visa application center?**
A: Online at gov.uk/graduate-visa. The application is entirely online. You do not need to attend a visa application center for biometrics for the Graduate Route specifically (since your biometrics are already on file from your Student Visa application).

**Q: What is the application fee?**
A: **£715** (as of 2024; this figure may change; always check current fee at gov.uk before applying).

**Q: How long does it take to process a Graduate Route application?**
A: Standard processing: approximately **3–8 weeks**. Priority service is not available for Graduate Route applications as of current policy.

**Q: Should I apply before or after my graduation ceremony?**
A: You should apply as soon as your results are confirmed — not necessarily after the ceremony. Graduation ceremonies often happen months after results. You need official notification of completion, not the physical ceremony.

**Q: What happens if my Student Visa expires before I can apply?**
A: You must apply before your Student Visa expires. If your visa expires before you apply, you are not eligible for the Graduate Route. Do not delay — apply as soon as your degree results are confirmed.

**Q: What documents do I need for the Graduate Route application?**
A: Typically:
- Valid passport
- Proof of completion of your UK degree (letter from university confirming results)
- Current UK address
- Previous visa reference number (from your Student Visa)

You do not need to submit new English language evidence, new financial evidence, or a sponsor letter.

## What You Can and Cannot Do on Graduate Route

**Q: Can I work full-time on the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes. You can work full-time in any job in any sector at any salary level.

**Q: Can I do self-employment or freelance work?**
A: Yes. Self-employment is allowed on the Graduate Route. You can register as self-employed, work as a freelancer, or start a business.

**Q: Can I do volunteer work?**
A: Yes. Volunteering is permitted.

**Q: Can I start a company or be a director?**
A: Yes. You can be a director of a UK-registered company on the Graduate Route.

**Q: Are there any restrictions on what jobs or sectors I can work in?**
A: No sector or job restrictions. You can work as a barista, in retail, in tech, in the NHS, in finance — any job is permitted. The Graduate Route is a fully open work permission with no occupation requirement.

**Q: Can I study on the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes, you can study while on the Graduate Route, but you cannot switch to a Student Visa from the Graduate Route. If you want to do another full course of study requiring a Student Visa, you must apply for a Student Visa from outside the UK (or from within the UK before your current student visa expires).

**Q: Can I access public funds (benefits) on the Graduate Route?**
A: No. The Graduate Route is subject to the condition of "no recourse to public funds" (NRPF). You cannot claim Universal Credit, housing benefit, or most other welfare benefits.

**Q: Can I travel outside the UK and re-enter on the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes. You can travel freely in and out of the UK. You re-enter on your Graduate Route BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) or digital status.

## Switching to Skilled Worker Visa

**Q: Can I switch from Graduate Route to Skilled Worker Visa without leaving the UK?**
A: Yes. You can apply for a Skilled Worker Visa from inside the UK while on the Graduate Route. You do not need to return to India to switch routes.

**Q: What are the requirements to switch to Skilled Worker?**
A: 
1. Job offer from a UK employer who is a licensed sponsor (employer must have a Certificate of Sponsorship — CoS)
2. Occupation must be on the eligible skilled occupation list (SOC code eligible)
3. Minimum salary: £26,200/year (general minimum, 2024) or the "going rate" for the occupation code, whichever is higher
4. Pass English language requirement (usually waived if you studied in UK and your degree was taught in English)
5. Maintenance requirement (£1,270 in personal bank account for 28 days) — often waived if employer certifies maintenance

**Q: What salary threshold applies for IT/computer science jobs?**
A: For most technology/software roles:
- Software developer (SOC 2136): Going rate approximately £38,700/year; the general £26,200 minimum also applies
- IT business analyst (SOC 2135): Going rate approximately £34,000/year
- Data scientist (SOC 2425): Going rate approximately £32,500/year

Always check the UKVI skilled worker occupation guide at gov.uk for the current going rate for your specific SOC code.

**Q: Does time on Graduate Route count toward Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)?**
A: No. Time on the Graduate Route does NOT count toward the 5-year continuous residence required for ILR. Only time spent on qualifying immigration routes (Skilled Worker, Global Talent, etc.) counts.

**Q: My Graduate Route will expire before I complete 5 years on Skilled Worker. How long until ILR?**
A: The 5-year clock for ILR starts when your Skilled Worker Visa begins. If you spend 2 years on Graduate Route then switch to Skilled Worker, ILR eligibility begins 5 years after your Skilled Worker start date. Total UK time: 7 years from graduation to ILR eligibility.

## NHS and Healthcare on Graduate Route

**Q: Do I still have access to NHS healthcare on the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes. You paid the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) as part of your Student Visa application. That payment covered your entire visa period. Your Graduate Route visa will include NHS access as it was included in the IHS you already paid.

**Q: Do I need to pay IHS again when switching to Skilled Worker?**
A: Yes. When applying for a Skilled Worker Visa, you pay a new IHS for the new visa period at £470/year for each year of the Skilled Worker Visa.

## Dependants on Graduate Route

**Q: Can I bring my spouse/partner to the UK on the Graduate Route?**
A: If your spouse/partner is already in the UK as your dependant on your Student Visa (having been granted permission before January 2024 restrictions), they can usually have their visa extended in line with your Graduate Route. New dependant applications from overseas are subject to restrictions introduced in 2024 — check the latest UKVI guidance as this policy has been changing.

**Q: Can my spouse/partner work on the Graduate Route?**
A: If your dependant partner is granted leave as a dependant on your Graduate Route, they can usually work full-time with no restrictions. Check your dependant's visa conditions in their individual BRP or digital status.

## Global Talent Visa Alternative

For highly skilled individuals in science, research, arts, or technology, the **Global Talent Visa** is an alternative to the Graduate Route → Skilled Worker pathway. It offers:
- 5-year visa
- No employer sponsorship required (you are "endorsed" by a recognized body like the Royal Academy of Engineering or Tech Nation)
- Faster ILR: 3 years if "exceptional talent" (top-level); 5 years if "exceptional promise" (emerging)

This is a more complex application and requires evidence of exceptional achievement. Relevant for Indian PhD graduates with publications, patents, or significant research impact.

## Post-Study Path to British Citizenship

### Timeline for Indian Students

For Indian students who complete studies and want British citizenship:

1. Complete degree (2–4 years) → Apply Graduate Route (2–3 years)
2. Switch to Skilled Worker → 5 years continuous residence → Apply ILR
3. After 12 months of ILR → Apply for British Citizenship
4. Total time from arriving as a student: approximately 9–12 years

**Note:** India does not currently allow dual citizenship. Becoming a British citizen requires renouncing Indian citizenship and obtaining an OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) card. OCI does not grant voting rights or Indian citizenship but provides most other rights in India.

## Common Mistakes Indian Students Make with Graduate Route

**1. Waiting too long to apply:**
Many students wait until after their graduation ceremony (often in November for July-completers). You can and should apply as soon as results are confirmed — you don't need the ceremony.

**2. Letting Student Visa expire:**
Your Student Visa must be valid on the date you submit your Graduate Route application. Monitor your visa expiry date carefully.

**3. Assuming Graduate Route counts toward ILR:**
It does not. This is a frequently misunderstood point that affects your long-term planning.

**4. Not understanding salary thresholds for Skilled Worker:**
The £26,200 general minimum is the floor — many tech, finance, and healthcare jobs have higher going rates that you must also meet.

**5. Not informing UKVI of address changes:**
While on Graduate Route, you're expected to keep your contact details current. Use the UKVI's update address service.
