# Document Attestation and Apostille Guide for Indian Students

Getting your Indian academic documents authenticated is a critical step for study abroad applications, visa applications, and immigration. This guide explains the entire process with costs and timelines.

---

## What is Attestation vs Apostille?

### Attestation
Authentication of a document by a chain of government authorities for use in non-Hague Convention countries. The document goes through multiple levels: Notary → State Government → Ministry of External Affairs → Embassy of destination country.

Used for: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, and other countries that are not members of the Hague Apostille Convention.

### Apostille
A simplified, internationally recognized certificate issued under the 1961 Hague Apostille Convention. Instead of a lengthy chain of attestations, a single Apostille stamp from the MEA (Ministry of External Affairs) is recognized by all 117+ member countries.

Used for: UK, USA, Canada, Germany, France, Netherlands, Australia, New Zealand, Italy, Spain, Portugal, and most other study destinations.

**Key difference:** Apostille is faster, cheaper, and simpler than full embassy attestation.

## When Do Indian Students Need These?

- **WES credential evaluation (Canada):** Requires apostilled degree certificates from most Indian universities
- **German APS certificate:** Requires attested/apostilled academic documents before visa application
- **France Campus France:** Apostilled transcripts required
- **UK university verification:** Some universities ask for apostilled degrees
- **Australian skilled migration (after studies):** Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) needs apostille
- **Dependent visa applications:** Birth certificate, marriage certificate need apostille
- **UAE employment after graduation:** If you return to India and work in UAE, degree apostille needed

## MEA Apostille Process (India)

The process has 3 mandatory steps in sequence. Do NOT skip any step or change the order.

### Step 1: Notarization
- Get the original document notarized by a registered Notary Public
- Any advocate/notary public in India can do this
- You may also use a gazetted officer (Class I or II) for government documents
- **Cost:** ₹100–200 per document
- **Time:** 1 day (same day in most cases)
- What you get: The notary stamps and signs the document or a photocopy, confirming it is a true copy of the original

### Step 2: HRD Attestation (Educational Documents Only)
- Degree certificates, marksheets, and academic transcripts must go through State Education Department / HRD (Human Resource Development Ministry at state level) before MEA
- This step verifies the educational institution is recognized
- **Apply at:** Your state's Education Department / HRD Ministry office
  - Maharashtra: Online at mahauniversity.ac.in or in-person at Mantralaya
  - Karnataka: DDPI office or online
  - Tamil Nadu: Directorate of Higher Education, Chennai
  - Delhi: Directorate of Education
  - UP: Board of High School and Intermediate Education
- **Cost:** ₹300–500 per document
- **Time:** 7–30 days (varies significantly by state — Maharashtra is faster online)
- **What you submit:** Notarized photocopy + original document for cross-checking
- **Non-educational documents** (birth certificate, marriage certificate, affidavit): Skip this step, go directly from Notary to MEA

### Step 3: MEA (Ministry of External Affairs) Apostille
- The final step — MEA affixes the Apostille sticker/stamp
- **MEA Attestation Centers:**
  - New Delhi (HQ): Patiala House Annexe
  - Mumbai: BKC Bandra
  - Chennai: MDDS Complex, Egmore
  - Hyderabad: MEA Regional Passport Office
  - Kolkata: MEA Regional Office
  - Guwahati: MEA Regional Office
  - Chandigarh: MEA Regional Office
- **Alternatively:** Use MEA-registered outsourcing agencies for doorstep pickup (iGoPapers, ProAttestation, etc.) — they charge a service fee of ₹800–2,000 but save you time
- **Official MEA fee:** ₹50–100 per document
- **Time:** 1–3 working days
- **Online tracking:** mea.gov.in/apostille.htm (track using your reference number)
- **What you receive:** A square sticker affixed to the back of the document containing: Country code (IN for India), date, competent authority name, unique alphanumeric serial number

## The Apostille Certificate Explained

The Apostille certificate is a square sticker or stamp typically measuring about 9cm × 9cm that is affixed to the back of your document (or to an attached sheet if space is insufficient). It contains:
- Country of issue: India
- Name of person who signed the document (notary/official)
- Capacity in which they acted
- Any seal/stamp present on the document
- Place and date of issue
- Issuing authority (MEA)
- Serial number (unique for tracking)
- Signature of MEA official

The Apostille does NOT certify the content of the document — it only certifies that the signature/seal on the document is genuine.

## Embassy Attestation (For Non-Hague Countries)

If you plan to work in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, or other non-Hague countries after graduation, you'll need embassy attestation instead of (or in addition to) Apostille.

**Process:**
1. Notarization → 2. HRD Attestation → 3. MEA Attestation (not Apostille) → 4. Embassy Attestation

**Embassy attestation offices in India:**
- UAE Embassy: Plot No. 2/50-G, Shantipath, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi
- Saudi Embassy: New Delhi
- Qatar Embassy: New Delhi

**Embassy attestation fee:** ₹1,500–5,000 per document depending on the embassy
**Time for embassy attestation:** 5–15 business days

## Common Mistakes Made by Indian Students

1. **Wrong order of attestation:** Must be Notary → HRD → MEA, always. MEA will reject if HRD step was skipped for educational documents.

2. **Missing HRD step for educational documents:** Many students submit directly to MEA after notarization for degree certificates — MEA will return the documents.

3. **Using photocopies instead of originals at HRD:** HRD compares your notarized copy against the original. Bring originals to HRD.

4. **Apostilling an expired PCC:** Some countries require Police Clearance Certificate issued within the last 6 months. Get a fresh PCC before apostilling for immigration purposes.

5. **Applying for Apostille then submitting opened sealed transcripts:** Transcripts often need to be in sealed envelopes for WES. Don't apostille sealed transcripts — they need to be sent directly from the university.

6. **Not tracking the status online:** MEA's online tracking at mea.gov.in/apostille.htm lets you check when your documents are ready.

7. **Assuming one apostille covers everything:** Each individual document needs its own apostille sticker — you cannot apostille one document and attach copies.

## WES-Specific Requirements

WES (World Education Services) for Canadian immigration generally requires:
- **Sealed official transcripts** sent directly from the Indian university to WES (these are NOT apostilled — they need to be sealed)
- **Degree certificate:** A notarized/apostilled copy
- WES does its own verification with Indian universities, so apostille is for the degree certificate only, not the transcripts

Check the WES website (wes.org/india) for the specific requirements of your university.

## FAQ

**Q: My degree certificate was issued 5 years ago. Can it still be apostilled?**
A: Yes. There's no expiry on documents for apostille purposes. The MEA will apostille any valid original document regardless of issue date.

**Q: I'm in a foreign country and need to apostille documents urgently. Can I do it remotely?**
A: You can authorize a family member or friend in India (by giving a power of attorney) to collect and submit documents on your behalf. Alternatively, use a registered apostille service agent who handles the entire process with minimal physical presence.

**Q: Does apostille work for Canada immigration?**
A: Most Canadian immigration purposes (Express Entry) use WES evaluation, not apostille directly. However, some PNP streams and LMIA-based work permits may ask for apostilled documents. Always check specific program requirements.

**Q: Is there a difference between Apostille for education and Apostille for a police clearance certificate?**
A: The apostille process is the same. The difference is that PCCs go: Notary → MEA (no HRD step needed since it's not an educational document) → Apostille. PCCs are issued by police and don't need education department verification.
