# Australia Study-to-PR Pathway for Indian Students

## Overview

Australia has one of the clearest and most well-trodden pathways from international student to permanent resident. The system rewards Australian-educated graduates with post-study work visas, and the points-based skilled migration system is structured to benefit those with Australian education and work experience.

---

## Complete Pathway Overview

```
Student Visa (Subclass 500)
    ↓
Complete Australian degree
    ↓
Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485) — 2–4 years
    ↓
Accumulate Australian work experience
    ↓
Skills assessment from relevant authority
    ↓
Expression of Interest (EOI) in SkillSelect
    ↓
Invitation for PR visa (189/190/491)
    ↓
Permanent Residence — Australia
    ↓ (4 years as PR)
Australian Citizenship
```

---

## Step 1: Australian Student Visa (Subclass 500)

### Key Facts
- Allows studying full-time at CRICOS-registered provider
- Work rights: 48 hours per fortnight during semester; unlimited during holidays (as of 2024 — verify current rules)
- Must maintain OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover)
- Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement: Must intend to study genuinely, not use student visa as migration pathway (but migration pathway is valid after visa expires)

---

## Step 2: Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

The 485 is the critical bridge between student and PR. Full details in the 485 guide, but key strategic points:

### Duration Strategy
Choose your program to maximize 485 duration:
- 2+ years coursework master's → **3-year 485**
- PhD (any duration) → **4-year 485**
- Regional study (anywhere outside Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide) → **5-year 485** for eligible programs

### Regional Australia Strategy
If you study in a regional Australian university for 2+ years:
- Your 485 may qualify for the **Regional Australia pathway** (5 years instead of 2–4)
- Additionally: Regional work experience gives you access to **Subclass 491 Regional Skilled Work** visa → which leads to **Subclass 191 Permanent Regional** visa

---

## Step 3: Skills Assessment

Most PR pathways require a skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority for your occupation.

### Major Assessing Authorities

| Authority | Occupations Covered |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **ACS (Australian Computer Society)** | ICT professionals (Software Engineers, Data Scientists, IT Managers, etc.) |
| **Engineers Australia** | Engineers (Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, etc.) |
| **VETASSESS** | Broad range (Business, Science, Trades, etc.) |
| **ANMAC (Nursing)** | Nurses, midwives |
| **Medical Board** | Doctors |
| **TRA (Trades Recognition Australia)** | Skilled trade occupations |
| **Accounting bodies (CPA, CA ANZ, IPA)** | Accountants |

### ACS (Australian Computer Society) Assessment — Most Common for Indian Engineers

Most Indian computer science and IT graduates need ACS assessment for tech roles.

**ACS RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) vs. Qualification Assessment:**
- Graduates with ICT degrees: Apply for qualification assessment
- Graduates with non-ICT degrees working in IT: Apply for RPL assessment
- ICT qualification assessment: **AUD $530** (2024)

**Requirements for positive ACS assessment:**
- Bachelor's degree in ICT from a recognized institution
- Skills assessed as matching an ACS assessed occupation (ICT majors list)
- Work experience if degree is older than 5 years

**Processing:** 4–8 weeks

**ANZSCO Occupation Codes:** Your occupation must be listed on the Skilled Occupation List (SOL) for the visa you're applying for. For IT: 261313 (Software Engineer), 261311 (Analyst Programmer), 263299 (ICT Security Specialist), etc. Check the list at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au.

## Step 4: Understanding the Points System

The Australian skilled migration points test determines your invitation score in SkillSelect:

### Points Breakdown

| Factor | Maximum Points |
|--------|---------------|
| Age 25–32 | 30 pts |
| Age 18–24 or 33–39 | 25 pts |
| Age 40–44 | 15 pts |
| **Education** | |
| Doctorate (PhD) | 20 pts |
| Bachelor's Honors or Master's | 15 pts |
| Bachelor's degree | 15 pts |
| Diploma | 10 pts |
| **Australian qualification bonus** | |
| 2 academic years of Australian study | 5 pts |
| **English Language** | |
| IELTS 8+ in each band (Superior) | 20 pts |
| IELTS 7+ in each band (Proficient) | 10 pts |
| IELTS 6+ in each band (Competent) | 0 pts |
| **Work experience: Skilled** | |
| 8+ years overseas skilled work | 15 pts |
| 5–7 years overseas skilled work | 10 pts |
| 3–4 years overseas skilled work | 5 pts |
| 1–2 years Australian skilled work | 5 pts |
| 3–4 years Australian skilled work | 10 pts |
| 5+ years Australian skilled work | 15 pts |
| **Other factors** | |
| State nomination (190 visa) | 5 pts |
| Regional Skilled (491 nomination) | 15 pts |
| Community language (credentialed) | 5 pts |
| Specialist education qualification | 5 pts |
| Partner skills | 5 pts |

**Minimum required:** 65 points (to submit EOI)
**Invitations typically go to:** 80–100+ points depending on occupation

### Points Calculation Example

Indian student, age 28, 2-year Australian master's, IELTS 7.5 in each band, 1 year Australian work experience, software engineer:
- Age 25–32: 30 pts
- Master's degree: 15 pts
- Australian education bonus (2 years): 5 pts
- English IELTS 7+: 10 pts
- Australian work (1 year): 5 pts
- **Total: 65 points** — barely enough; likely to wait long time

With state nomination (190): 65 + 5 = 70 points — better position

With 3 years Australian work experience instead: 65 - 5 + 10 = 70 points
With IELTS 8+ instead: 65 - 10 + 20 = 75 points

**Implication:** Most Indian students need either a high IELTS score (8+ bands) OR state nomination OR regional pathway to get competitive invitations.

## Subclass 189 — Skilled Independent (No Sponsorship)

### What is Subclass 189?
Australia's most prestigious PR pathway — no employer and no state sponsorship needed. Points-based selection entirely.

### Requirements
- Skills assessed positively
- Occupation on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) or Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL — for state nomination only)
- IELTS minimum 7.0 in each band (to be competitive; technically 6.0 is the minimum for some occupations but you won't get invited at 6.0)
- 65+ points (just to submit EOI; invitations go much higher)

### Current Cut-off Scores (Approximate)
Check the SkillSelect invitation round data at homeaffairs.gov.au:
- ICT occupations: Historically inviting at 85–90+ points
- Engineering: 80–85+ points
- Accounting: 75–85 points
- Nursing: Sometimes lower due to occupation shortage

## Subclass 190 — State/Territory Nominated

### What is Subclass 190?
State government nominates you for PR in exchange for you committing to live and work in that state for 2 years. Adds 5 CRS points to your score.

### Why It's Valuable
- 5 extra points often makes the difference between invitations
- Some states have lower invitation thresholds than the national 189 pool
- States actively want skilled workers in specific occupations

### State Nomination Options for Indian IT/Engineering Students
Each state publishes an occupation list with nomination criteria. For example:

**New South Wales (NSW) — SkillSelect Expression of Interest:**
- ICT and engineering frequently invited
- Typically requires job offer or employment in NSW in relevant occupation

**Victoria — Skilled Nominated (VISM):**
- Regular rounds for skilled workers
- Some offshore streams (apply from India or while on 485 in other states)

**Queensland:**
- Strong demand for ICT and engineering
- Offshore nomination available

**South Australia:**
- SA Skilled Work Regional stream
- Offshore applicants with skills in SA need areas can apply

**Western Australia:**
- Strong mining, engineering, healthcare demand
- Active Skills Migration Unit

## Subclass 491 → 191 — Regional Pathway

### The Regional Strategy for Indian Students

This is the most accessible pathway for many Indian students who cannot reach the high points scores needed for 189/190.

### Subclass 491 (Skilled Work Regional — Provisional)
- Work and live in a specified regional area for 3 years
- Can be nominated by state/territory OR by an eligible relative living in regional Australia
- Duration: 5 years
- **Work rights: Full-time, any employer in regional area**
- Adds 15 points to your score when applying (vs. 5 for 190)

**Subclass 491 Definition of Regional:** Any area OUTSIDE Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide. Includes Canberra, Darwin, Hobart, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Newcastle, Wollongong, Townsville, Cairns, etc.

### Subclass 191 (Permanent Regional)
After living and working in a specified regional area for 3 years on Subclass 491:
- Apply for Subclass 191 — permanent residence
- Requirements: 3 years regional residence + 3 years regional employment + income threshold (AUD $53,900+/year)
- **No additional points required — you get PR automatically if you meet the conditions**

## IELTS Improvement is the Highest ROI for Australian PR

Many Indian students underestimate how much IELTS score affects Australian PR points:

| IELTS Band (each) | Points | Point Difference |
|------------------|--------|-----------------|
| 6.0 | 0 | — |
| 7.0 | 10 | +10 vs. 6.0 |
| 8.0+ | 20 | +10 vs. 7.0, +20 vs. 6.0 |

Moving from IELTS 7.0 to 8.0+ in each band adds 10 more points — often more impactful than years of additional work experience. Dedicate 2–3 months to intensive IELTS preparation specifically targeting the 8.0 level (especially Listening and Reading, which are most achievable).

## Citizenship Timeline

- PR granted: Year 0
- Can apply for citizenship: After 4 years of lawful Australian residence (at least 1 year as PR)
- Citizenship test: Australian values, history, rights, responsibilities
- Citizenship ceremony: Pledge of commitment
- Australian passport: World's 8th most powerful passport (Henley Index 2024) — visa-free/on-arrival to 189 countries

## FAQ

**Q: Can I apply for PR while on a student visa?**
A: You can apply for skills assessment and create your SkillSelect profile on a student visa. However, most PR visa applications require you to be on a non-student visa (like 485). The 485 is designed specifically as a bridge to PR.

**Q: My occupation (Software Engineer) isn't on the MLTSSL anymore. What do I do?**
A: Occupation lists change regularly. Check the current lists at homeaffairs.gov.au. If your exact occupation is removed, look for adjacent occupation codes that match your work. ACS assessments are flexible in matching skills.

**Q: How long is the 189/190 application processing?**
A: After receiving invitation: submit application within 60 days. Processing typically 8–15 months currently (check current IMMI processing times as they vary significantly).
