# Australia Student Visa (Subclass 500) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Subclass:** 500 — Student Visa  
**Issued by:** Department of Home Affairs (DHA), Australian Government  
**Validity:** Tied to the duration of your principal course of study, plus a grace period of up to 2 months after the course end date.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

To apply for the Australian Student Visa (Subclass 500), Indian applicants must:

- Have a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from a registered CRICOS (Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students) provider.
- Be a genuine temporary entrant — must demonstrate intention to study and return to India after studies (Genuine Student requirement replaced the GTE requirement from March 2024).
- Meet English language proficiency requirements.
- Have sufficient financial resources to support themselves and any accompanying family members.
- Meet health and character requirements.
- Be enrolled in a registered course (school, vocational, higher education, postgraduate, English Language Intensive Course for Overseas Students — ELICOS, etc.).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

Indian students must demonstrate they can cover:

- **Tuition fees:** Full course fees (varies by institution and program; typically AUD 20,000–50,000 per year for university degrees).
- **Living expenses:** AUD 29,710 per year (as of 2024) for the student.
  - Spouse/partner accompanying: AUD 10,394 per year additionally.
  - Each dependent child: AUD 4,449 per year.
- **Return airfare:** Approximately AUD 2,000–4,000 (to and from India).

**Proof of funds accepted:**
- Bank statements (personal, parental, or sponsor) showing sufficient balance for the entire duration.
- Loan sanction letters from Indian banks (e.g., SBI, Axis, HDFC Education Loan).
- Scholarship letters that cover tuition and/or living costs.
- Salary slips and income tax returns of sponsor (parents/guardian).

**Visa Application Fee:** AUD 710 (approximately INR 38,000–40,000 as of 2024; subject to change).

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay).
- Completed online visa application (via ImmiAccount portal).
- Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from the CRICOS-registered institution.
- Genuine Student (GS) statement — written statement explaining your reasons for study and choice of institution.
- Proof of financial capacity:
  - Bank statements (last 3–6 months).
  - Loan sanction letter (if applicable).
  - Scholarship award letter (if applicable).
  - Sponsor's financial documents (income tax returns, salary slips).
- English language test results (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, Cambridge C1/C2).
- Academic transcripts and certificates (10th, 12th, undergraduate, postgraduate as applicable).
- Statement of Purpose (SOP) — may be required by institution and recommended for visa.
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — required if aged 16 or over.
- Health examination results (completed through a DHA-approved panel physician in India; usually required for courses over 12 months).
- Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) — proof of insurance from an Australian-approved provider.
- Passport-size photographs.
- Birth certificate (especially if applying with family members).
- Marriage certificate (if spouse is accompanying).
- Proof of relationship for any dependants.
- Visa application fee payment receipt.

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Receive offer and CoE:** Apply to and receive an offer from a CRICOS-registered Australian institution. Accept the offer and pay the deposit to receive the CoE.
2. **Purchase OSHC:** Buy Overseas Student Health Cover from an approved Australian insurer (e.g., Bupa, Medibank, AHM, NIB, CBHS).
3. **Health examination:** Schedule and complete a medical exam with a DHA-approved panel physician in India. Most results are uploaded directly to ImmiAccount.
4. **Create ImmiAccount:** Register at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au and start a new Student Visa (500) application.
5. **Complete online application:** Fill in personal details, upload all documents, and write the GS statement.
6. **Pay visa fee:** AUD 710, paid online via credit/debit card.
7. **Biometrics:** Indian applicants may be required to submit biometrics at a Visa Application Centre (VAC) in India (VFS Global handles Australian visa biometrics).
8. **Await decision:** The department may contact you for additional information.
9. **Visa grant:** If approved, you receive an electronic visa grant (no sticker in passport); the visa is linked to your passport number electronically.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Standard processing time:** 4–6 weeks on average, though it can range from 2 weeks to several months depending on caseload and completeness of the application.
- **75% of applications** are processed within 26 days; 90% within 69 days (DHA statistics, 2024).
- Apply at least 2–3 months before your course start date.
- Processing times can be tracked via the DHA website.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- **During the semester (term time):** Up to **48 hours per fortnight** (biweekly).
- **During scheduled course breaks/vacations:** Unlimited hours.
- **Registered nurses and aged care workers:** No hourly cap (as of January 2023).
- Work rights apply automatically with the visa grant — no separate work permit needed.
- Work rights extend to accompanying spouses/partners (with conditions).

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

**Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485):**

- **Graduate Work stream:** 18 months (for occupations on the skilled occupations list, diploma or trade qualification holders).
- **Post-Study Work stream:**
  - Bachelor's degree: 2 years
  - Bachelor's (Honours) or Master's by coursework: 3 years
  - Master's by research: 3 years
  - Doctoral degree: 4 years
  - Additional 2 years for degrees completed in regional areas (regional universities).
- Must apply within 6 months of completing studies.
- Must have studied in Australia for at least 2 years.

---

## 9. Language Requirements

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | Overall 6.0 (no band below 5.5); some courses require 6.5 or 7.0 |
| TOEFL iBT | 64 overall |
| PTE Academic | 50 overall |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced / C2 Proficiency | 169 overall |
| OET | B in all sub-tests |

- Specific programs (e.g., Medicine, Law, Teaching) may require higher scores.
- Some institutions offer English pathway programs.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Medical examination:** Required for courses over 12 months. Completed with a DHA-approved panel physician. Results valid for 12 months.
- **Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC):** Mandatory for the entire duration of the visa. Must be purchased before applying for the visa. Covers GP visits, hospital treatment, emergency ambulance, and some pharmaceuticals.
  - Cost: Approximately AUD 600–700 per year for a single student.
  - Approved insurers: Bupa, Medibank, AHM, NIB, CBHS.
- Students from countries with reciprocal health agreements may have limited Medicare access (India does not have a reciprocal agreement with Australia).

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **GTE (now GS) statement is critical:** The Department of Home Affairs scrutinises whether students are genuine. Be clear and honest about why you chose Australia, the specific institution, and your career plans.
- **CRICOS check:** Always verify your institution and course are listed on the CRICOS register (cricos.teqsa.gov.au) before paying tuition.
- **Loan letters from Indian banks** are widely accepted as proof of financial capacity.
- **OSHC is mandatory** even if you have private insurance in India.
- **Regional study benefits:** Studying in a regional Australian university can provide additional post-study work benefits (extra 2 years on the 485 visa).
- **No gap in studies:** Taking an unauthorised leave of absence can jeopardise your visa condition (Condition 8202).
- **Aviation security check:** Can add several weeks of processing for some nationalities, though less common for Indian applicants.
- **Biometrics:** Required at VFS Global centres in India (major cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Ahmedabad).
