# Norway Student Residence Permit — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Permit Type:** Student Residence Permit (Studieopphold — Residence Permit for Students)  
**Issued by:** Norwegian Directorate of Immigration (UDI — Utlendingsdirektoratet)  
**Entry Visa:** Indian nationals also require a national visa (type D) for stays over 90 days — this is processed along with the residence permit application through the Norwegian Embassy or VFS Global.  
**Validity:** Usually issued for up to 1 year initially; renewable for the duration of the course of study.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Be admitted to a full-time study programme at a Norwegian university, university college (høgskole), university of applied sciences, or other recognised Norwegian educational institution.
- Demonstrate sufficient funds to cover living expenses and tuition for the duration of the permit.
- Have no serious criminal record.
- Have valid health insurance.
- Not be a threat to public order, national security, or health.
- Intend to return to India after completion of studies (though post-study options exist).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

The UDI requires proof that the student can support themselves without resorting to public funds.

- **Required amount:** NOK 137,907 per year (2024 figure — equivalent to approximately INR 10–11 lakh per year or about NOK 11,490/month).
- This amount must be available for the entire permit period — either in a Norwegian bank account, documented in a scholarship agreement, or as a loan.
- Tuition fees:
  - **Public universities** (e.g., University of Oslo, NTNU, University of Bergen): **NO tuition fees for most programmes** — even for non-EU international students. This is a major advantage of Norway.
  - A semester fee of approximately **NOK 300–600 per semester** (student union and facilities fee) is typically charged.
  - Some specialised programmes and private institutions may charge tuition.
- **Visa/Residence Permit application fee:** NOK 5,300 (approximately INR 42,000 as of 2024) for non-EU applicants.

**Proof of funds accepted:**
- Bank statements (personal or parental/sponsor, last 3 months) showing the required balance.
- Scholarship award letter specifying the amount (must cover living costs).
- Student loan sanction from the Norwegian State Educational Loan Fund (Lånekassen) — available to some international students under specific bilateral agreements; generally not available to Indian students unless they have lived in Norway.
- Indian bank loan sanction letter (acceptable as supporting evidence but must show disbursement to cover the requirement).
- Formal financial guarantee from parents with their income documentation.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (must be valid for at least 3 months longer than the intended stay; at least 2 blank pages).
- Completed UDI online application (via selfservice.udi.no).
- 2 recent passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, white/light background).
- Admission letter from the Norwegian educational institution (unconditional; stating the duration of the programme).
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements (last 3 months).
  - Scholarship confirmation letter.
  - Study loan sanction.
  - Sponsor's income proof (salary slips, ITR, bank statements).
- Academic documents:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's transcripts and degree certificate (for Master's or PhD applicants).
  - Certified English or Norwegian translations if documents are in a regional Indian language.
- English or Norwegian language proficiency:
  - IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, or equivalent (for English-taught programs).
  - Norskprøven (Norwegian Language Test) or Norwegian Upper Secondary equivalent (for Norwegian-taught programs).
- CV/Resume (for postgraduate applications).
- Statement of Purpose / Motivation Letter.
- Proof of accommodation in Norway (university dorm confirmation, lease agreement, or host accommodation letter).
- Health insurance for the initial period until Norwegian public coverage applies (National Health Insurance through Folkeregisteret registration).
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — not always mandatory but recommended; UDI may request it.
- Application fee payment confirmation (NOK 5,300).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to Norwegian institutions:** Apply through the institution's website or through the SAMORDNA OPPTAK portal (for undergraduate programs at public institutions — samordnaopptak.no). For graduate programs, apply directly to the institution.
2. **Receive admission letter.**
3. **Submit UDI online application:** Go to selfservice.udi.no, select "Apply for a student residence permit," create an account, and complete the form.
4. **Pay application fee:** NOK 5,300 (paid during the online application or at the application centre).
5. **Submit documents at the Norwegian Embassy or VFS Global Norway in India:**
   - New Delhi Embassy or VFS Global centres in major Indian cities.
   - Submit passport, photographs, and original/certified copies of all supporting documents.
   - Biometrics (fingerprints) are collected at this stage.
6. **Await UDI decision:** The application is processed by UDI in Norway.
7. **Receive decision:** Approval communicated through the UDI portal; visa sticker issued by the Embassy in your passport.
8. **Travel to Norway.**
9. **Register at the local police district (police station / Politiets Utlendingsenhet):** Within 1 week of arrival, register in person. Collect the Residence Permit card.
10. **Register with Folkeregisteret (Norwegian Population Register):** This enables access to the Norwegian National Insurance Scheme (helsetrygdkort — health insurance card).

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **UDI processing time:** 3–5 months (can be longer — check current estimated times on UDI's website).
- Apply at least 4–6 months before the program start date.
- UDI does not offer priority processing for student permits from India.
- Norwegian academic year: Most programs start in **August** (apply by February–March at the latest).

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Indian students with a valid Norwegian student residence permit may work part-time without a separate work permit.
- **Permitted hours:** Up to **20 hours per week** during semester/term.
- During summer and other long holidays: Full-time work (without specific hour restrictions).
- Employment must not conflict with full-time study obligations.
- Work is subject to Norwegian tax law — must report income and obtain a Norwegian tax identification number (Skattekort / D-number or national identity number).

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Job-seeker permit (oppholdstillatelse for å søke arbeid):**
  - Graduates from Norwegian institutions can apply to extend their stay for up to **1 year** to seek employment.
  - The stay cannot be extended again as a job-seeker permit.
  - Must apply before the student permit expires.
- **Skilled worker permit:** Once a job offer is secured, apply for a skilled worker residence permit. Requires:
  - Job offer in a relevant field.
  - Salary meeting the minimum threshold (approximately NOK 350,000/year or sector collective bargaining minimum).
- **EU Blue Card equivalent:** Norway (as part of the EEA) has its own version for highly qualified professionals.
- Norway offers an excellent quality of life and is a gateway to the broader European job market.

---

## 9. Language Requirements

**For English-taught programs (many Norwegian Master's programs are in English):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–6.5 (varies by institution and program) |
| TOEFL iBT | 80 |
| PTE Academic | 60 |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | 176 |

**For Norwegian-taught programs:**

| Test | Minimum Level |
|------|---------------|
| Norskprøven | B2 or above |
| Norwegian language equivalent from upper secondary | Pass |
| Bergenstesten | Pass |

- Most undergraduate programs are in Norwegian; most Master's research programs are in English. Institutions like NTNU, University of Oslo, NHH Bergen, and BI Norwegian Business School have extensive English-medium postgraduate offerings.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Norwegian National Insurance Scheme (Folketrygden):** All residents registered in Norway (including international students staying more than 12 months) are entitled to coverage under the national health insurance scheme after registering with Folkeregisteret and paying the membership fee.
- Students staying for more than 12 months who register properly receive a **health card (helsetrygdkort)** and access to Norway's public healthcare.
- **Initial health insurance:** For the first period in Norway (before NHI registration takes effect), travel/health insurance is required.
- **GP registration (fastlege):** After arrival and registration, register with a local GP. GP visits: approximately NOK 160–200 per visit (heavily subsidised).
- Students staying less than 12 months should have private international health insurance throughout their stay.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Free tuition at public universities:** This is Norway's most compelling advantage for Indian students. Programs at the University of Oslo, NTNU (Norwegian University of Science and Technology), University of Bergen, and others are tuition-free. Students pay only the semester fee (~NOK 600) and living costs.
- **NTNU (Trondheim):** One of Norway's top technical universities with strong programs in engineering, energy, and computer science. Many English-taught Master's programs available.
- **High cost of living:** Norway is one of the most expensive countries in Europe. Budget approximately NOK 10,000–15,000/month for living expenses (food, transport, accommodation, personal). Oslo is the most expensive city.
- **Lånekassen (Norwegian State Loan Fund):** Generally available to Norwegian citizens and some residents — Indian students on a student permit are typically not eligible unless they have EEA-country residence.
- **Norway is not in the EU** but is in the **EEA and Schengen Area** — the residence permit allows travel within the Schengen Zone.
- **No blocked account required:** Bank statements are sufficient; a blocked account is not mandated.
- **Language advantage:** Even for English-taught programs, learning Norwegian (at least basic Nynorsk or Bokmål) significantly helps with daily life, part-time employment, and social integration.
- **Climate:** Norway has long winters and extremely short daylight hours in winter — be prepared mentally and with appropriate clothing.
