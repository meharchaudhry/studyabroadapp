# WES (World Education Services) Credential Evaluation — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## What is WES?

WES (World Education Services) is a non-profit organization headquartered in New York and Toronto that evaluates international academic credentials for use in Canada and the United States. For Indian students, WES is most relevant for Canadian immigration pathways — particularly Express Entry — where an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) is required to claim education-based points.

WES was founded in 1974 and is recognized by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) as a designated organization for ECAs.

---

## When Do Indian Students Need WES?

### Canada
- **Express Entry (Federal Skilled Worker Program, Canadian Experience Class):** An ECA is mandatory to claim points for education in the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). Without ECA, you cannot score education points.
- **Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs):** Most provincial streams require an ECA equivalent to Express Entry
- **Some Canadian universities:** For graduate admissions; they want to verify that your Indian degree is equivalent to a Canadian credential
- **Professional licensing:** Engineering, nursing, teaching, accounting professional bodies often require WES evaluation

### United States
- **Professional licensing:** State licensing boards (engineering, nursing, teaching) often require WES
- **Some graduate schools:** Request WES course-by-course evaluation along with application
- **Government employment:** Some US federal/state agencies require verified evaluations

## Types of WES Evaluations

### 1. ECA — Educational Credential Assessment (For Canada Immigration)
- Required by IRCC for Express Entry
- Evaluates your highest degree and determines the Canadian equivalent
- **Example:** Indian Bachelor of Technology (4-year) = Canadian Bachelor's Degree - Four Years
- Cost: **CAD $239** (includes sending report to one organization — IRCC)
- Additional report copies: CAD $45 each
- Processing: 7 business days after WES receives all documents

### 2. Course-by-Course Evaluation (For University Admissions)
- Most detailed evaluation — lists every course, credit, and gives Canadian GPA equivalent
- Required by most Canadian graduate schools and some US graduate programs
- Cost: **CAD $325** (includes sending to one university)
- Additional reports: CAD $45 each
- Processing: 7 business days after document receipt

### 3. Document-by-Document Evaluation (Basic)
- Verifies degree equivalency without course-level detail
- Used for non-Express Entry immigration purposes
- Cost: **CAD $200**

## Step-by-Step WES Process for Indian Students

### Step 1: Create WES Account
- Go to wes.org
- Click "Get Started" and choose country: Canada or USA
- Create account with name exactly as it appears on your passport
- Select evaluation type (ECA for immigration; Course-by-Course for admissions)

### Step 2: Complete Application and Pay
- Fill in your academic history (all institutions attended)
- Pay by credit card or international bank transfer
- WES sends you a reference number (**WES ID**) — keep this safe for all future communications

### Step 3: Send Documents to WES

**From Indian universities, there are two pathways:**

#### Option A: University Sends Directly to WES (Preferred)
- Many Indian universities are on WES's "trusted institution" list and can send transcripts directly
- Request official transcripts from your Registrar/Examination Department
- Transcripts must be in a sealed envelope with the university's signature/stamp on the seal
- University mails directly to:
  - WES Canada: 45 St. Clair Avenue West, Suite 400, Toronto, ON M4V 1K9
  - WES USA: Bowling Green Station, PO Box 745, New York, NY 10274-0745

#### Option B: You Send Documents
- For universities not on the trusted list
- Request official transcripts in sealed envelope from university
- Mail the sealed envelope along with your degree certificate (MEA apostilled copy for most cases) to WES
- Do NOT open the sealed transcript envelope

**Documents to send for a typical Indian Bachelor's:**
- Official sealed transcripts from your university (all 4 years / 8 semesters)
- Degree certificate (attested copy — MEA apostille recommended)
- If you have a higher degree (master's), include those transcripts and degree as well
- For engineering students: B.Tech/B.E. + all semester marksheets

### Step 4: WES Verifies Documents
- WES may contact your university directly by phone or email for verification
- This is the longest step — allow 4–8 weeks for Indian university verification
- You can track status in your WES account
- If documents are incomplete or verification fails, WES emails you to provide additional documents

### Step 5: Evaluation Completed
- WES sends the evaluation report to you and directly to IRCC or the university you specified
- WES ECA report is valid for **5 years** from the date of issue (for immigration purposes)
- After 5 years, you'll need a new evaluation (same process, same cost)

## Processing Timeline

| Stage | Time |
|-------|------|
| Account creation and payment | 1 day |
| University transcripts reaching WES from India | 2–4 weeks (by registered international post) |
| WES document review | 7 business days |
| University verification by WES | 2–6 weeks (Indian universities) |
| **Total typical end-to-end** | **6–14 weeks** |

**Rush Processing:**
- Available for ECA and Course-by-Course (not document-by-document)
- Additional fee: CAD $100
- Reduces WES processing to 5–7 business days (but document collection time stays the same)

## Indian University-Specific Requirements

### IITs and NITs
- IITs and NITs are on WES's trusted institution list
- Their transcripts are sent directly via the Registrar's office
- Processing is usually faster due to established verification channels

### Autonomous Universities (Mumbai, Pune, Anna University, VTU, etc.)
- These can also send transcripts directly
- Visit wes.org/india to check if your specific institution is on the list
- If not listed, you must mail documents yourself (Option B)

### Documents Required for a Typical Indian B.Tech Graduate
- Consolidated/individual semester marksheets (all 8 semesters)
- Degree/graduation certificate
- If from an affiliated college: both college AND university stamps required on transcripts

## GPA Conversion — Indian to Canadian Scale

WES converts Indian percentages and CGPA to the 4.0 Canadian GPA scale as follows:

| Indian Percentage | Indian CGPA (/10) | Canadian GPA (/4.0) |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 90–100% | 9.0–10.0 | 4.0 (A+) |
| 85–89% | 8.5–8.9 | 3.9 (A) |
| 80–84% | 8.0–8.4 | 3.7 (A-) |
| 75–79% | 7.5–7.9 | 3.5 (B+) |
| 70–74% | 7.0–7.4 | 3.3 (B) |
| 65–69% | 6.5–6.9 | 3.0 (B-) |
| 60–64% | 6.0–6.4 | 2.7 (C+) |
| 55–59% | 5.5–5.9 | 2.3 (C) |
| 50–54% | 5.0–5.4 | 2.0 (C-) |
| Below 50% | Below 5.0 | Below 2.0 |

**Note:** WES's conversion is for informational purposes in the ECA; they report the Canadian degree equivalency, not just the GPA. For immigration, IRCC uses the degree equivalency (e.g., bachelor's 4-year), not the GPA.

## Points Impact in Express Entry CRS

Your WES evaluation directly impacts your CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System) score:

| Education Level | CRS Points (Single, No Spouse) | CRS Points (With Spouse) |
|----------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------|
| PhD | 150 | 140 |
| Master's or 2+ degrees (one 3-yr) | 135 | 126 |
| Two or more bachelor's degrees | 128 | 119 |
| Bachelor's degree (3-year) | 120 | 112 |
| College diploma / certificate (3-year) | 98 | 91 |
| College diploma (1-2 year) | 90 | 84 |

Getting a 4-year bachelor's evaluated (WES: "Bachelor's Degree — Four Years") earns 120 CRS points vs. 98 for a 3-year bachelor's. This difference of 22 CRS points can be decisive.

## Common Problems and Solutions

**Problem:** WES says documents received but stuck in "Awaiting Documents" status
**Solution:** Check your WES account — they likely need additional documents from you (it will show what's missing)

**Problem:** University won't send sealed transcripts
**Solution:** Most Indian university Examination Departments do provide sealed transcripts on request — approach the Registrar's office formally with the WES requirement letter (downloadable from your WES account)

**Problem:** WES contacts university for verification and university is unresponsive
**Solution:** Contact your university's Registrar office, provide them the WES verification details, and ask them to respond to WES. This is common with tier-2 private universities.

**Problem:** Evaluation comes back as "below bachelor's" due to affiliated college vs. university confusion
**Solution:** Ensure BOTH the affiliated college AND the university stamped your transcripts; request re-evaluation if institution listed incorrectly

## FAQ

**Q: My WES ECA says "Bachelor's Degree — Three Years" but I did a 4-year B.Tech. Why?**
A: This sometimes happens if the transcripts show 3 academic years of content, or if the university affiliation dates are unclear. Contact WES for a re-evaluation with a university letter clarifying the 4-year duration.

**Q: Do I need WES if I'm applying for a Canadian student visa (study permit)?**
A: No. WES is for immigration (PR) purposes and graduate admissions. For a student visa, your admission letter from the Canadian university is sufficient.

**Q: My WES evaluation is 3 years old. Do I need to redo it?**
A: WES ECA for immigration is valid for 5 years. If it's still within 5 years, you can use it. After 5 years, apply for a new evaluation.

**Q: Can I submit a photocopy of my degree certificate to WES?**
A: WES requires an official, verified copy — either sent by your university directly or an apostilled copy. A plain photocopy is not accepted.
