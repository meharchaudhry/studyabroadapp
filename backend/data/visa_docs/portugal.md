# Portugal Student Visa (Type D — Long-Stay) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Overview

**Visa Type:** Type D — Long-Stay Student Visa (Visto de Longa Duração para Estudo)  
**Issued by:** Portuguese Embassy/Consulate (New Delhi, Mumbai)  
**Authority:** Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras (SEF) / AIMA (Agency for Integration, Migration, and Asylum — replaced SEF from 2023)  
**Validity:** Typically 120 days (4 months) to enter Portugal; once in Portugal, you apply for a Residence Permit valid for the course duration.  
**Portugal is a Schengen member** — your residence permit allows travel across Schengen zone.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

Indian students must:
- Be accepted at a Portuguese public or private higher education institution (Universidade, Instituto Politécnico).
- Prove sufficient financial means to support yourself during the entire course.
- Hold a valid passport (minimum 6 months beyond intended stay).
- Have no prior deportation or entry ban from the EU/Schengen area.
- Provide proof of accommodation in Portugal (university dorms, rental contract, or letter of accommodation).
- Have health insurance valid in Portugal (minimum €30,000 coverage).
- Obtain criminal record certificate (PCC) from India — apostilled and translated into Portuguese or English.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

As of 2024, Portuguese authorities require Indian students to demonstrate:

- **Minimum monthly amount:** €820/month (the Portuguese Social Support Index — IAS × 12 calculation).
- **For 12 months of study:** approximately €9,840 minimum in bank account.
- **Recommended balance:** €12,000–15,000 for a year to show comfort margin.
- Evidence can be: bank statements (last 3–6 months), scholarship letter, fixed deposit, or parental financial support letter with bank statements.
- **Tuition fees:** Portuguese public universities: €950–7,000/year for non-EU students. Private universities: €3,500–15,000/year.
- **Living costs:** Lisbon/Porto approximately €800–1,000/month; smaller cities €500–700/month.

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Application Process

**Step 1 — Get University Acceptance**
Apply to and receive an acceptance letter from a Portuguese institution. Many programs are taught in English; some require Portuguese (B2 level for public universities).

**Step 2 — Gather Documents**
- Valid passport (6+ months validity)
- Completed visa application form (VFS Global — Portugal)
- 2 recent passport-size photos (white background, 35×45mm)
- University acceptance/enrolment letter
- Academic transcripts (10th, 12th, Degree — translated if not in English/Portuguese)
- Proof of financial means (bank statements, scholarship letter)
- Proof of accommodation in Portugal
- Travel/health insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage)
- Criminal record certificate (PCC from local police, apostilled, within 3 months of application)
- Proof of paid visa fee

**Step 3 — Submit at VFS Portugal (India)**
- VFS Global processes Portugal visa applications in India (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai).
- Book appointment online at VFS website.
- Submit biometrics (fingerprints) at the VFS centre.

**Step 4 — Visa Decision**
- Processing time: 2–8 weeks typically; can extend to 2–3 months during peak (July–September).
- Visa fee: €80 approximately (check current rate at Portuguese Embassy website).
- You will receive a Type D visa sticker in your passport.

**Step 5 — Arrive and Register in Portugal**
- Within 3 days of arrival, register at the local City Hall (Câmara Municipal) or AIMA.
- Apply for the Residence Permit (Autorização de Residência) within 3 months of arrival at AIMA.
- Residence Permit is initially valid for 1 year, renewable annually.

---

## 5. Work Rights for Indian Students in Portugal

- Students with a valid student residence permit can work **up to 20 hours per week** during term time.
- During vacations/holiday breaks, students may work full-time (40 hours/week).
- No separate work authorization needed — work rights are embedded in the student residence permit.
- Salary: minimum wage €820/month (2024); student jobs often pay €6–9/hour.

---

## 6. Post-Study Options

- **Job Seeker Visa:** After graduation, you can apply for a 1-year job seeker residence permit (requires proof of graduation from a Portuguese institution).
- **Portugal Golden Visa:** Separate investment-based route, not relevant for students.
- **EU Blue Card:** Available after finding a qualifying skilled job (salary ≥ 1.5× average wage).
- Portugal's Digital Nomad Visa also available for remote workers earning ≥ €3,040/month.

---

## 7. English-Taught Programs

- University of Lisbon (Universidade de Lisboa), University of Porto, Nova School of Business and Economics, ISCTE — Business School Lisbon all offer extensive English-taught master's programs.
- Growing tech/fintech hub in Lisbon attracting global students.
- Erasmus+ partnerships mean credits can be transferred across Europe.

---

## 8. Common Rejection Reasons

1. Insufficient financial proof (bank balance doesn't cover full study period).
2. Incomplete criminal record certificate (not apostilled or too old — must be within 3 months).
3. Vague proof of accommodation (need proper lease/dorm letter).
4. No travel insurance or insufficient coverage.
5. Application submitted too close to course start date.

---

## 9. Indian-Student Specific Tips

- Apply **at least 3–4 months** before your course start date; AIMA queues in Portugal are long.
- Portuguese universities may require **Portuguese language proficiency** even for English programs at postgraduate level — check requirements carefully.
- India has a **Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA)** with Portugal — relevant for scholarship holders.
- Portugal is one of the most **affordable EU destinations** with a growing startup ecosystem (Web Summit is held in Lisbon annually).
- Bank statements should be in English or Portuguese. If in any Indian language, get them bank-certified in English.
