# Sweden Residence Permit for Studies — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Permit Type:** Residence Permit for Studies (Uppehållstillstånd för studier)  
**Issued by:** Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket)  
**Entry Visa:** A separate Schengen visa (Type C, short-stay) or national visa (Type D) is issued simultaneously or as part of the residence permit application.  
**Validity:** Issued for the duration of the program, typically up to 1 year initially. Renewable for the full period of study.

**Note:** Indian nationals require a visa to enter Sweden. Students applying from India typically apply for a residence permit at the Swedish Embassy in New Delhi; this application also covers the need for an entry visa.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Have an unconditional admission or conditional admission notice from a Swedish higher education institution (university, university college — högskola, or other approved institution).
- Be enrolled in a full-time course (minimum 50% study pace for some situations; full-time standard).
- Demonstrate sufficient financial means for the entire study period.
- Have no serious criminal record.
- Have valid health insurance for the initial entry period.
- Plan to leave Sweden upon completion of studies (for permit purposes, though post-study options exist).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

The Swedish Migration Agency requires students to demonstrate sufficient funds to support themselves.

- **Required monthly amount:** SEK 8,514 per month (for 2024) — equivalent to approximately INR 68,000–70,000/month.
- **Annual requirement:** SEK 102,168 per year (approximately INR 8.2–8.5 lakh).
- This amount must be available for the full duration of the permit applied for.
- Tuition fees:
  - **Swedish public universities charge tuition only for non-EU/EEA students.**
  - Bachelor's programs: SEK 70,000–145,000 per year (approximately INR 5.6–11.6 lakh).
  - Master's programs: SEK 80,000–160,000 per year (approximately INR 6.4–12.8 lakh).
  - Some master's programs (especially in engineering) at KTH, Chalmers, Lund: SEK 150,000–195,000/year.
  - **Free education:** Swedish public universities do NOT charge tuition to Swedish/EU citizens, but full fees apply to non-EU international students.
- **Application fee for residence permit:** SEK 1,000 (approximately INR 8,000 as of 2024).

**Proof of funds:**
- Bank statements (personal or parental, last 3–6 months).
- Scholarship award letters (Swedish Institute Scholarship, university scholarships).
- Study loan sanction from an Indian bank.
- Formal financial guarantee from sponsor.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for the entire intended stay; minimum 6 months validity beyond intended departure date).
- Completed residence permit application form (online via the Migrationsverket portal at migrationsverket.se).
- 1–2 recent passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, as per specifications).
- Admission notice / acceptance letter from the Swedish institution (conditional or unconditional).
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements (last 3–6 months showing consistent balance).
  - Scholarship confirmation.
  - Loan sanction letter.
  - Parental income proof (salary slips, income tax returns).
- Proof of tuition fee payment (first semester or full year — receipt or letter confirming fee arrangement).
- Academic transcripts and diplomas:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's transcripts and degree certificate (for Master's applicants).
- English language test results:
  - IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or Cambridge (for English-medium programs).
  - For Swedish-medium programs: Swedish language proficiency test (TISUS, SFI, or equivalent — rare for international students).
- Proof of health insurance for the initial period (travel/health insurance until Swedish social insurance kicks in).
- Proof of accommodation in Sweden (university accommodation offer, rental agreement).
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — recommended and sometimes required.
- Signed declaration form.
- Application fee payment receipt (SEK 1,000).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to Swedish institutions:** Most applications go through Universityadmissions.se (for higher education programs) — applications typically open in October for September intake.
2. **Receive admission/acceptance letter.**
3. **Create Migrationsverket account:** Register online at migrationsverket.se and start a residence permit application for studies.
4. **Complete online application:** Fill in details, upload documents, pay the SEK 1,000 fee.
5. **Book biometrics appointment:** Schedule an appointment at the Swedish Embassy in New Delhi (or consulate) for biometrics (fingerprints + photograph).
6. **Attend Embassy appointment:** Submit original passport and any additional physical documents requested by the Embassy.
7. **Await decision:** Migrationsverket processes the application. May contact applicant for additional information.
8. **Receive decision:** The permit approval is communicated via the Migrationsverket portal. A visa sticker (entry visa) is placed in the passport at the Embassy.
9. **Travel to Sweden.**
10. **Collect Residence Permit Card (uppehållstillstånd):**
    - Within 1–2 weeks of arrival, visit the Migrationsverket office in Sweden to collect the residence permit card (biometric card).
    - Register your address at the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) if staying more than 1 year (to get a personal identity number — personnummer).

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Migrationsverket processing time:** 2–6 months (average 3–4 months for student permits from India; this varies significantly with application volume).
- **Apply at least 4–6 months before** the course start date.
- Applications for September intake: Apply by March–April at the latest.
- Processing times are published and updated on the Migrationsverket website.
- **Urgent applications:** No fast-track option for student permits from Migrationsverket.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Indian students with a Swedish residence permit for studies have the right to work without a separate work permit.
- **No hourly restrictions** — Swedish law does not impose a specific weekly hour limit on student work, unlike some other EU countries.
- Students are advised not to let work affect studies (full-time study obligation).
- Employers must pay social security contributions for all workers, including student workers.
- Working legally provides access to the Swedish social insurance system (sjukpenning — sick pay, etc.).

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Job-seeker residence permit (arbetstillstånd för jobbsökande):**
  - Graduates from Swedish universities can apply for a **6-month residence permit to seek employment** (arbetstillstånd för jobbsökande).
  - Must apply before the study permit expires.
  - Valid for 6 months, non-renewable.
- **Work permit (arbetstillstånd):** Upon receiving a job offer, apply for a Swedish work permit. Requires a job offer with salary meeting the minimum (at least the equivalent of the collective agreement-set minimum, approximately SEK 13,000–15,000/month).
- **EU Blue Card:** Available for highly qualified workers with a salary above the Swedish Blue Card threshold.
- **Permanent residence:** After working in Sweden on a valid permit for 4+ years (under certain conditions), can apply for permanent residence.

## 9. Language Requirements

**For English-taught programs (very common at Swedish universities — many Master's programs are in English):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.5 (no band below 5.5 generally; top universities may require 7.0) |
| TOEFL iBT | 90 |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | 180 |
| PTE Academic | 62 |
| Duolingo English Test | 120 |

**For Swedish-taught programs:**

| Test | Minimum Level |
|------|---------------|
| TISUS (Test i svenska för universitets- och högskolestudier) | Pass |
| SFI (Swedish for Immigrants) / SVA | Level D |

- Most Master's programs are taught in English. Many Swedish universities (KTH, Chalmers, Lund, Uppsala, Stockholm) have comprehensive English-medium programs.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Travel insurance:** Required for the visa/permit entry period until Swedish coverage kicks in.
- **Swedish healthcare access:** Once registered with a personnummer (personal identity number) after staying 1+ year, international students are entitled to access Swedish public healthcare at subsidised rates.
- Students staying less than 1 year do NOT receive a personnummer and must rely on private insurance or pay full cost for healthcare.
- **CSN (Centrala studiestödsnämnden):** Swedish student financial aid — generally not available to non-EU students in Sweden, but students should check eligibility.
- **Student health services:** Most Swedish universities have a student health service (studenthälsan) offering counseling, basic medical advice, and referrals at low/no cost.
- **EU Health Insurance Card equivalent:** Not applicable for Indian students; private insurance is needed.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Swedish Institute Scholarships:** The Swedish Institute (SI) offers competitive scholarships for international students:
  - **Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (SISGP):** Covers tuition fees, living expenses (SEK 11,000/month), travel, and insurance. Highly competitive — focus on leadership and sustainable development.
  - Applications open in November for September intake.
  - Apply at: studyinsweden.se/scholarships.
- **University-specific scholarships:** KTH, Lund, Chalmers, Uppsala, and Stockholm University all offer merit-based partial scholarships for international students.
- **Application through universityadmissions.se:** All non-EU students apply through this centralised portal. Application fee: SEK 900 for first round; additional programs can be added for a fee.
- **Personnummer:** The Swedish personal identity number is crucial for banking, healthcare, phone contracts, and daily life. You only get it if staying 1+ year.
- **Sweden is very digital:** Migrationsverket, municipalities, tax agency — all can be managed largely online. The Bank-ID system is used but unavailable to non-residents without personnummer — use passport for initial identification.
- **Cost of living:** Stockholm is expensive (SEK 12,000–18,000/month); Lund, Gothenburg, Uppsala are somewhat more affordable (SEK 9,000–13,000/month).
- **Processing delays:** Migrationsverket is known for slow processing — do not delay your application.
