# South Korea Student Visa (D-2) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Overview

**Visa Type:** D-2 — Foreign Student Visa  
**Issued by:** Embassy of the Republic of Korea, New Delhi / Korean Consulate General, Mumbai, Chennai  
**Authority:** Korea Immigration Service (KIS), Ministry of Justice  
**Validity:** Tied to enrollment period; typically 1–4 years. Must be renewed annually.  
**South Korea is NOT a Schengen country** — separate visa required for each country.

South Korea has become a major education hub in Asia. Top universities (KAIST, Seoul National University, POSTECH, Yonsei, Korea University) are globally ranked and increasingly offer programs in English. Government scholarships like KGSP (Global Korea Scholarship) cover full tuition, accommodation, and living allowance for international students.

## 3. Eligibility Requirements

Indian students must:
- Hold a Standard Acceptance Letter from a Korean university authorized to invite foreign students (most major universities qualify).
- Have sufficient financial means to cover the study period.
- Have a valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months beyond visa validity).
- Not have any immigration violations in Korea or other countries.
- Meet health requirements (TB screening required for Indian nationals — see below).

**TB Screening Requirement:** India is on Korea's tuberculosis high-risk country list. Indian students must undergo a TB screening test at a designated hospital in India before or upon arrival in Korea and obtain clearance.

---

## 4. Financial Requirements

- **Required:** Proof of minimum KRW 9,000,000 (~USD 6,700 / INR ~5.5 lakh) in a bank account OR scholarship letter covering full expenses.
- **KGSP (Global Korea Scholarship) scholars:** Fully funded — no personal financial proof needed.
- **Tuition fees:** Public universities KRW 3,000,000–6,000,000/semester (USD 2,200–4,500); private universities KRW 4,000,000–8,000,000/semester.
- **Living costs:** Seoul: KRW 700,000–1,200,000/month (USD 520–890); smaller cities 20–30% cheaper.
- University dormitories (campus housing) significantly reduce costs — typically KRW 200,000–400,000/month (USD 150–300).

---

## 5. Required Documents

1. Completed visa application form (available on Embassy website / HiKorea portal)
2. Valid passport + 1 photocopy
3. 1 recent passport photograph (35×45mm, white background)
4. Standard Acceptance Letter from Korean university (original)
5. Proof of financial means:
   - Bank statement (last 3 months) showing KRW 9,000,000 equivalent
   - OR scholarship certificate
6. Academic transcripts (from class 10 onwards — notarized/apostilled)
7. Degree certificates (if applicable — apostilled)
8. Completed application fee payment: KRW 60,000 (~INR 3,750) approximately
9. Guarantee letter from parent/guardian (if student is under 25)

For KGSP scholars: scholarship award letter is the primary supporting document.

## 6. Application Process

**Step 1 — University Application**
Apply directly to Korean universities (most have online portals). For KGSP, apply through the Korean Embassy in India by March 11 (deadline varies by year — check official NIIED announcements).

**Step 2 — Receive Acceptance Letter**
University issues a Standard Certificate of Admission and Standard Acceptance Letter.

**Step 3 — Visa Application at Korean Embassy**
- Embassy of the Republic of Korea, New Delhi: Chandragupta Marg, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi.
- Korean Consulate General, Mumbai: DLH Park, S.V. Road, Goregaon (W), Mumbai.
- Korean Consulate General, Chennai: Ceebros Centre, No. 45 Montieth Road, Chennai.
- Submit in person or through an authorized travel agent (check Embassy for current procedure).

**Step 4 — Processing**
- Processing time: 5–10 business days.
- Visa fee: approximately USD 45 for single entry, USD 90 for multiple entry (check current rates).

**Step 5 — Arrive in Korea**
- Within 14 days of entering Korea, register at the local Immigration Office (출입국·외국인청) — this converts the D-2 visa into a longer Alien Registration Card (ARC — 외국인등록증).
- ARC is your official ID in Korea — needed for banking, phone contracts, dormitory registration, etc.
- TB test: If not done in India, you'll be directed to a designated hospital in Korea.

## 7. Work Rights

- D-2 visa holders can work **up to 20 hours per week** during semesters.
- During official vacation periods (summer: July–August, winter: December–February): **full-time work (up to 40 hours/week)** permitted.
- Must obtain a **part-time work permit** from the Immigration Office before starting work — your university's International Student Office usually assists with this.
- Korean minimum wage: KRW 9,860/hour (2024) — approximately USD 7.30.

---

## 8. KGSP — Global Korea Scholarship (Government Scholarship)

The **KGSP** is Korea's flagship scholarship for international students:
- **Covers:** Full tuition, monthly stipend (KRW 900,000 for master's/PhD; KRW 800,000 for undergrad), airfare, Korean language training (1 year), settlement allowance, health insurance.
- **Eligibility for India:** GPA ≥ 2.64/4.0 (equivalent to approximately CGPA 6.6/10), age under 40 (PhD), under 25 (undergrad).
- **Apply through:** Korean Embassy in New Delhi (Embassy Track) or directly to the university (University Track).
- **Deadline:** Typically February–March for September intake. Check NIIED (National Institute for International Education) official website.

---

## 9. Indian-Student Specific Tips

- **Korean Language (Hangul):** Many master's/PhD programs taught in English (especially at KAIST, GIST, POSTECH, DGIST). Undergraduate programs mostly in Korean. Learning basic Korean hugely helps in daily life.
- **TOPIK exam:** Test of Proficiency in Korean — useful for university admission to Korean-medium programs and for post-study work.
- **Hallyu Wave connection:** BTS, K-drama, K-pop have significantly increased interest from India — India is now among top 10 sending countries.
- **CGPA conversion:** Korean universities accept Indian CGPA on a 10-point scale. CGPA 7.5+/10 is competitive for top universities (SNU, Yonsei, Korea University).
- **Apostille in India:** All academic documents must be apostilled via MEA, India. Allow 2–4 weeks.
- Korean universities generally have **very good infrastructure** — modern labs, strong industry links (Samsung, Hyundai, LG, SK, Lotte all partner with universities).
