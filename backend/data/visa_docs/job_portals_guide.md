# Job Portals Guide for International Students

This guide lists the best job portals for international students in each country, covering internships, graduate programmes, and part-time work opportunities.

---

## Finding Jobs in the UK

The UK has a well-developed graduate recruitment market with dedicated platforms for students. Employers typically open graduate schemes in September–November for the following autumn intake. International students on a Student visa can work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during vacations. The UK's strong finance, tech, and consulting sectors make London a particularly active hiring hub.

- **LinkedIn Jobs** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=United%20Kingdom) — Biggest professional network for internships, graduate programmes, and part-time roles across all UK cities.
- **Gradcracker** (https://www.gradcracker.com) — STEM-focused graduate jobs and placements exclusively for UK students — ideal for engineering, computing, and science disciplines.
- **Prospects** (https://www.prospects.ac.uk/jobs-and-work-experience) — The UK's official graduate careers site with work experience listings, job profiles, and employer guides.
- **Indeed UK** (https://uk.indeed.com/jobs?q=student+part+time) — Huge job board covering all sectors — filter by "Part Time" and "Student" for flexible roles.
- **Milkround** (https://www.milkround.com) — The UK's largest graduate and student job platform with structured internship and placement schemes.

**FAQ: Where can I find part-time jobs as a student in the UK?**
Use Indeed UK for flexible part-time roles, or Milkround for structured graduate schemes. Gradcracker is best for STEM placements.

## Finding Jobs in Germany

Germany's job market is strong in engineering, automotive, IT, and manufacturing sectors. International students can work up to 120 full days or 240 half-days per year without a separate work permit. Searching for "Werkstudent" (working student) roles is the standard way to find part-time positions that are specifically designed for students. Many German employers also offer paid internships (Praktikum) as a route into full-time employment.

- **Jobteaser** (https://www.jobteaser.com/en/jobs?country=germany) — Europe's leading student and graduate job platform, directly connected to German university career portals.
- **Stepstone** (https://www.stepstone.de/jobs/Werkstudent) — Major German job board — search "Werkstudent" to find part-time student-friendly roles.
- **Xing Jobs** (https://www.xing.com/jobs/search?keywords=werkstudent) — The German professional network equivalent of LinkedIn, with strong Werkstudenten and Praktikum (internship) listings.
- **Make-it-in-Germany** (https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/working-in-germany/job-listings) — Official German government portal for international students and skilled workers seeking employment in Germany.

**FAQ: How do I find a part-time job as an international student in Germany?**
Search for "Werkstudent" roles on Stepstone or Xing Jobs. The Make-it-in-Germany portal also has resources specifically for international students.

## Finding Jobs in France

France has an internship-centric job culture — many companies recruit students through structured stages (internships) and alternances (work-study contracts). Most job postings are in French, so basic French language skills give you a significant advantage. Paris dominates the job market but Lyon, Marseille, and Bordeaux also have active student employment markets.

- **Jobteaser France** (https://www.jobteaser.com/en/jobs?country=france) — Top student job platform in France covering stages (internships) and alternances (work-study contracts).
- **Indeed France** (https://fr.indeed.com/emplois?q=étudiant+temps+partiel) — Large French job board with student part-time and internship filters across all regions.
- **APEC** (https://www.apec.fr/candidat/recherche-emploi.html) — French executive and graduate job portal, best for high-level internships and professional roles.
- **Cadremploi** (https://www.cadremploi.fr) — Professional French job market platform with internship and graduate scheme listings.

**FAQ: Where can international students find internships in France?**
Jobteaser France is the most student-friendly platform. For professional-level internships, try APEC or Cadremploi — note that most listings are in French.

## Finding Jobs in the Netherlands

The Netherlands has a highly international job market with many multinational corporations headquartered there (ASML, Shell, Philips, ING). English is widely spoken and many roles are available in English. International students can work part-time (bijban) alongside their studies, and the country has a strong startup ecosystem in Amsterdam and Eindhoven.

- **LinkedIn NL** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Netherlands) — The dominant professional network in the Dutch market with broad English-language job listings.
- **Indeed Netherlands** (https://www.indeed.nl/jobs?q=student&l=) — Dutch job board with broad listings including bijban (side jobs) suited for students.
- **Nationale Vacaturebank** (https://www.nationalevacaturebank.nl/vacature/zoeken?query=stage) — The Netherlands' national job board — search "stage" to find internship opportunities across all sectors.

**FAQ: Where can I find English-language jobs as an international student in the Netherlands?**
LinkedIn NL is best for English-language opportunities. Nationale Vacaturebank is the go-to for internships (search "stage").

## Finding Jobs in Australia

Australia has a vibrant student job market, particularly in hospitality, retail, and customer service. As of 2024, there is no work hour cap for student visa holders (the 40-hour-per-fortnight limit was lifted). Graduate recruitment is competitive, with Big Four accounting firms, engineering companies, and technology firms running structured graduate programmes. Major cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane have the most opportunities.

- **Seek** (https://www.seek.com.au/jobs?classification=casual-vacation&workarrangement=4) — Australia's number-one job site — filter by Casual/Vacation for student-friendly work.
- **GradAustralia** (https://gradaustralia.com.au) — Australia's leading graduate careers platform covering Big Four, tech, engineering, and consulting sectors.
- **Jora AU** (https://au.jora.com/jobs?q=part+time+student) — Part-time and casual job listings across all Australian states, ideal for students needing flexible hours.

**FAQ: Where can I find casual or part-time jobs as a student in Australia?**
Seek is Australia's top platform for casual and part-time roles. GradAustralia is best for structured graduate programmes and internships.

## Finding Jobs in Singapore

Singapore is a major financial and tech hub in Asia with a highly competitive job market. The government's MyCareersFuture portal is the most trusted source for verified listings. International students on a Student Pass can work part-time (up to 16 hours per week during term) if their institution allows it. Internships are strongly encouraged and often count toward course credits.

- **MyCareersFuture** (https://www.mycareersfuture.gov.sg/search?search=internship&sortBy=new_posting_date) — Singapore Government's official job portal with verified listings — internships and full-time roles included.
- **Glassdoor SG** (https://www.glassdoor.sg/Job/singapore-internship-jobs-SRCH_IL.0,9_IC3235921_KO10,20.htm) — Company reviews plus internship job listings with salary insights for Singapore-based roles.
- **Internsg** (https://www.internsg.com) — Singapore-exclusive internship board covering local SME and multinational corporation opportunities.

**FAQ: Where can I find internships in Singapore as an international student?**
MyCareersFuture is the official and most reliable portal. Internsg is dedicated to internship listings only and is very student-friendly.

## Finding Jobs in Hong Kong

Hong Kong's job market is dominated by finance, law, and logistics sectors. It is a highly international city with English widely used in business. International students can work part-time with permission from their institution. The financial services sector is a major employer, and investment banks often recruit through structured internship programmes.

- **JobsDB HK** (https://hk.jobsdb.com/jobs-in-education-training/part-time) — Hong Kong's leading job board with broad listings from finance to retail, well-suited for students seeking part-time work.
- **LinkedIn HK** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Hong%20Kong) — Best for finance, technology, and consulting internships at multinational firms in Hong Kong.
- **Glassdoor HK** (https://www.glassdoor.com.hk/Job/hong-kong-internship-jobs-SRCH_IL.0,9_IM583_KO10,20.htm) — Salary-transparent internship and graduate job listings for Hong Kong employers.

**FAQ: Where can I find internships in Hong Kong's finance sector?**
LinkedIn HK is the best platform for finance and consulting internships. Glassdoor HK adds useful salary transparency to the search.

## Finding Jobs in the USA

The US has one of the world's largest student employment markets. International students on an F-1 visa may work on-campus up to 20 hours per week during term, and can pursue off-campus employment through CPT (Curricular Practical Training) or OPT (Optional Practical Training). Technology, finance, and consulting are the top sectors for international student internships. Silicon Valley, New York, Boston, and Seattle are the primary hiring hubs.

- **Handshake** (https://joinhandshake.com) — America's number-one early talent network, used by over 1,400 US universities for campus recruitment and internship postings.
- **LinkedIn US** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=United%20States&f_WT=2) — Remote and on-site internships across all US cities and major tech hubs.
- **Glassdoor US** (https://www.glassdoor.com/Job/internships-jobs-SRCH_KO0,11.htm) — Internship search with salary estimates — particularly strong for tech and finance roles.
- **WayUp** (https://www.wayup.com) — College-exclusive internship and part-time job platform targeting US university students.

**FAQ: Where should I look for internships in the USA as an F-1 student?**
Handshake is the best starting point as it is directly connected to your university. LinkedIn and Glassdoor are strong for tech and finance. Make sure any off-campus internship is authorised through CPT or OPT.

## Finding Jobs in Finland

Finland has a smaller but growing tech and startup ecosystem centred on Helsinki. Most international students find it easier to get roles where English is the working language, though learning some Finnish significantly expands opportunities. The country has strong public employment services that list all types of roles including student positions.

- **TE-services** (https://paikat.te-palvelut.fi/tpt/) — The Finnish government employment service with job listings including student and part-time roles across the country.
- **LinkedIn Finland** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Finland) — Best for English-language jobs in Helsinki's tech sector, especially suited to international students.
- **Duunitori** (https://duunitori.fi/tyopaikat?haku=harjoittelija) — Finnish job portal — search "harjoittelija" to find internship and trainee roles.

**FAQ: Can I find English-language jobs in Finland as an international student?**
Yes — LinkedIn Finland is your best bet for English-language tech and startup roles in Helsinki. The TE-services portal has the broadest listings across all industries.

## Finding Jobs in Spain

Spain's job market is particularly active in tourism, hospitality, technology, and renewable energy sectors. Most job listings are in Spanish, and language skills are usually required. Internships (prácticas) are a common entry point for students. Madrid and Barcelona are the primary employment centres with the most international-friendly opportunities.

- **Infojobs** (https://www.infojobs.net/ofertas-trabajo/practicas) — Spain's leading job platform with the largest database of prácticas (internships) across all sectors.
- **Jobteaser Spain** (https://www.jobteaser.com/en/jobs?country=spain) — University-linked job board with internship opportunities across major Spanish cities.
- **LinkedIn Spain** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Spain) — English and Spanish job listings — the best platform for international student opportunities in Spain.

**FAQ: Where can I find internships in Spain as an international student?**
Infojobs has the most prácticas listings. For English-language opportunities, LinkedIn Spain is the better choice.

## Finding Jobs in Switzerland

Switzerland has a highly paid job market dominated by finance, pharmaceuticals, and technology. Zurich, Geneva, and Basel are the main employment hubs. International students (non-EU) can work up to 15 hours per week during term time with a valid residence permit. The job market is competitive and multinational companies frequently recruit through structured internship programmes.

- **Jobs.ch** (https://www.jobs.ch/en/vacancies/?term=internship) — Switzerland's major job portal — search for Praktikum or internship roles across all Swiss cities.
- **LinkedIn Switzerland** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Switzerland) — Best for finance, pharma, and tech internships in Zurich, Geneva, and Basel.
- **Jobscout24** (https://www.jobscout24.ch/en/jobs/internship) — Swiss job aggregator with filters for student and part-time roles across all cantons.

**FAQ: Where can I find internships in Switzerland as an international student?**
LinkedIn Switzerland is the top platform for structured internships at multinational firms. Jobs.ch has the broadest listings across all sectors.

## Finding Jobs in Canada

Canada has a strong co-op and internship culture, especially in technology, engineering, and business programmes. International students with a valid study permit can work up to 20 hours per week off-campus during term and full-time during scheduled breaks. Major cities like Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, and Montreal are the primary employment hubs. Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows graduates to stay and work after completing their studies.

- **LinkedIn Canada** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Canada) — Canada's largest professional network with internships, graduate roles, and part-time opportunities across all provinces.
- **Indeed Canada** (https://ca.indeed.com/jobs?q=student+graduate) — Canada's top job aggregator with broad listings for students and new graduates across all industries.
- **Workopolis** (https://www.workopolis.com/en/jobs) — Canadian job board with strong presence in Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal for entry-level and student roles.
- **Job Bank Canada** (https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/jobsearch/jobsearch) — Canada's official government job board with free listings including co-op, internship, and graduate positions.

**FAQ: Where can I find co-op or internship jobs in Canada as an international student?**
LinkedIn Canada and Indeed Canada are the most comprehensive platforms. Job Bank Canada is the official government resource and is particularly useful for co-op postings.

## Finding Jobs in Ireland

Ireland has a thriving tech sector anchored by the European headquarters of Google, Meta, Amazon, Apple, and many other multinationals — all based in Dublin. International students on a Stamp 2 visa can work up to 20 hours per week during term and 40 hours per week during official college holidays. Graduate programmes are competitive, particularly in technology and finance.

- **LinkedIn Ireland** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Ireland) — The top professional platform in Ireland, strong for tech and pharma internships in Dublin.
- **IrishJobs** (https://www.irishjobs.ie) — Ireland's leading job board with listings across all sectors and experience levels.
- **GradIreland** (https://gradireland.com/jobs) — Ireland's dedicated graduate careers platform covering graduate programmes, internships, and work placements.
- **Jobs.ie** (https://www.jobs.ie) — Major Irish job portal with part-time, full-time, and entry-level listings suitable for students.

**FAQ: Where can I find graduate jobs in Ireland's tech sector?**
LinkedIn Ireland is essential for tech roles. GradIreland focuses specifically on structured graduate programmes at major Irish and multinational employers.

## Finding Jobs in Japan

Japan's job market has unique features — many companies use a structured new-graduate hiring system (shūkatsu) that begins a year before graduation. International students with Japanese language skills have significantly more opportunities. The government has been actively working to attract foreign talent, particularly in technology and research. Tokyo is by far the largest employment market.

- **LinkedIn Japan** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Japan) — English-language professional opportunities in Tokyo and major Japanese cities, best for international-facing roles.
- **GaijinPot Jobs** (https://jobs.gaijinpot.com) — English-language jobs in Japan specifically targeting international students and foreign professionals — many roles do not require Japanese.
- **Daijob** (https://www.daijob.com/en) — Bilingual job board connecting international talent with Japanese companies — ideal for students with Japanese language skills.
- **JREC-IN** (https://jrecin.jst.go.jp) — Japan's premier portal for academic, research, and scientific positions — highly relevant for postgraduate researchers.

**FAQ: Can I find English-language jobs in Japan without speaking Japanese?**
Yes — GaijinPot Jobs specialises in English-language roles for foreigners. LinkedIn Japan also lists many international-friendly positions, particularly in technology and education.

## Finding Jobs in Norway

Norway's economy is driven by oil and gas, maritime industries, technology, and renewable energy. Oslo is the primary job market. International students can work part-time alongside their studies, and Norwegian wages are among the highest in Europe. English is widely spoken in professional settings and many companies work in English, especially in tech.

- **LinkedIn Norway** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Norway) — Professional opportunities in Oslo and across Norwegian energy, tech, and maritime sectors.
- **FINN Jobb** (https://www.finn.no/job/fulltime/search.html) — Norway's largest job board with comprehensive listings across all industries and regions.
- **Arbeidsplassen** (https://arbeidsplassen.nav.no) — The official Norwegian government employment portal with free job listings including student and entry-level positions.

**FAQ: Where can I find jobs in Norway as an international student?**
FINN Jobb is the largest and most comprehensive platform. For government-verified listings, use Arbeidsplassen (NAV's official portal).

## Finding Jobs in Denmark

Denmark has a strong job market for tech, design, sustainability, and life sciences. Copenhagen is the main employment hub with a thriving startup and innovation ecosystem. International students can work up to 20 hours per week during term time. Denmark consistently ranks as one of the best countries for work-life balance and offers a post-study job-seeking permit of up to 2 years.

- **LinkedIn Denmark** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Denmark) — Strong professional network in Copenhagen for English-language roles in tech, design, and sustainability sectors.
- **Jobindex** (https://www.jobindex.dk) — Denmark's leading job portal with broad listings for students, graduates, and young professionals.
- **Graduateland** (https://graduateland.com) — Nordic graduate careers platform connecting students across Denmark, Sweden, and Norway with top employers.

**FAQ: Where can I find student jobs (studiejob) in Denmark?**
Jobindex is the largest Danish job portal. LinkedIn Denmark is best for English-language roles. For graduate programmes across the Nordic region, use Graduateland.

## Finding Jobs in the UAE

The UAE — and especially Dubai and Abu Dhabi — is a major hub for finance, real estate, logistics, hospitality, and technology. The job market is highly international, with a large expat workforce. There is no official "student visa" work permit in the UAE in the same way as other countries — check your specific visa conditions. Networking is particularly important in the UAE job market.

- **LinkedIn UAE** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=United+Arab+Emirates) — The top professional network in the UAE for finance, tech, and consulting roles in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.
- **Bayt** (https://www.bayt.com) — The Middle East's leading job site with extensive listings across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and the wider Gulf region.
- **GulfTalent** (https://www.gulftalent.com) — Premium job board for Gulf region professionals, strong in finance, engineering, and management roles.
- **Naukrigulf** (https://www.naukrigulf.com) — Popular Gulf job portal especially well-known among Indian and South Asian professionals, with broad UAE listings.

**FAQ: Where can I find jobs in Dubai as an international student or graduate?**
LinkedIn UAE and Bayt are the most comprehensive platforms. Naukrigulf is particularly popular among Indian and South Asian job seekers in the Gulf.

## Finding Jobs in New Zealand

New Zealand has a smaller but close-knit job market, with Auckland being the main employment centre. International students can work up to 20 hours per week during term and full-time during scheduled breaks. Agriculture, hospitality, healthcare, and technology are key sectors. New Zealand's post-study work visa options are attractive for graduates who want to gain local experience.

- **LinkedIn NZ** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=New+Zealand) — Professional opportunities in Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch across all sectors.
- **Seek NZ** (https://www.seek.co.nz) — New Zealand's number-one job site with thousands of listings for graduates, students, and part-time workers.
- **Trade Me Jobs** (https://www.trademe.co.nz/a/jobs) — New Zealand's trusted classifieds platform with a strong jobs section, great for local and student-friendly roles.

**FAQ: Where can I find part-time jobs in New Zealand as a student?**
Seek NZ is the largest and most comprehensive platform. Trade Me Jobs is particularly good for local and casual roles.

## Finding Jobs in Sweden

Sweden has a highly educated workforce and a thriving tech startup ecosystem centred in Stockholm. International students can work without restrictions on hours during their studies. Swedish companies strongly value internships (praktik) and many graduate positions are filled through internship-to-hire pipelines. English is very widely spoken and many companies operate in English.

- **LinkedIn Sweden** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=Sweden) — The leading professional network in Sweden for English-language tech and startup roles in Stockholm and beyond.
- **Arbetsförmedlingen** (https://arbetsformedlingen.se/for-arbetssokande/sok-jobb) — Official Swedish public employment service with free job listings including part-time and student positions.
- **Graduateland** (https://graduateland.com) — Nordic graduate careers platform connecting Swedish university students with top graduate employers across Scandinavia.
- **Academic Work** (https://www.academicwork.se) — Sweden's specialist job platform for students and young professionals covering internships, part-time roles, and first jobs.

**FAQ: Where can I find internships and student jobs in Sweden?**
Academic Work specialises specifically in student and young professional roles. LinkedIn Sweden is best for English-language tech internships.

## Finding Jobs in South Korea

South Korea's job market is dominated by large conglomerates (chaebols) such as Samsung, Hyundai, LG, and SK Group, as well as a growing tech startup scene in Seoul. Korean language proficiency significantly expands opportunities. International students are generally permitted to work part-time during their studies, subject to visa conditions. The government has initiatives to attract international talent in technology and research fields.

- **LinkedIn Korea** (https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/search/?location=South+Korea) — Professional opportunities at Korean multinationals and international firms in Seoul and major cities.
- **Saramin** (https://www.saramin.co.kr) — Korea's top job board with comprehensive listings across all industries for students and graduates.
- **Work Together Foundation** (https://www.worktogether.or.kr) — Government-backed work programmes connecting foreign residents and international students with Korean employers.

**FAQ: Where can I find English-language jobs in South Korea as an international student?**
LinkedIn Korea is the best platform for English-language roles. Work Together Foundation specifically supports foreign students and residents in finding employment in Korea.
