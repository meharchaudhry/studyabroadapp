# Global Health Insurance Guide for Indian Students Abroad

## Why Health Insurance Is Critical

Indian students going abroad frequently underestimate the cost of healthcare in their destination country. A single emergency room visit in the USA can cost USD $2,000–10,000 without insurance. A dental extraction in the UK without NHS access: £200–600. An ambulance in Australia: AUD $800–1,200. Understanding your insurance obligations and options before departure can save you from financial catastrophe.

This guide covers mandatory insurance schemes by country, optional supplementary insurance, and how to navigate claims.

---

## United Kingdom — Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)

### What It Is

The **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** is a fee paid as part of the UK Student Visa application. It grants access to the NHS (National Health Service) during your stay in the UK.

### Cost

- **£470 per year** (as of 2024)
- Paid upfront for the entire visa duration at time of visa application
- For a 1-year master's degree: £470 × 1 = £470 (but visa typically granted for 1 year + 4 months, so £470 × 1.33 = ~£627)
- For a 3-year PhD: £470 × 3 = £1,410

**Family members:** Each adult dependent pays £470/year; children under 18 pay £235/year.

### What IHS Covers

Once you've paid the IHS, you access NHS services as a resident:
- GP (General Practitioner) registration and consultations: Free
- Hospital emergency care: Free
- Prescription medicines: £9.90 per item (2024 rate) — students on low income may qualify for pre-payment certificate (£32/year cap)
- Mental health services: NHS-funded; university counselling also available
- Dental and optical: NOT included in standard NHS; requires separate NHS dentist registration

### Registering with a GP

Within your first 2 weeks in the UK:
1. Find an NHS GP practice accepting new patients near your address (nhs.uk/service-search)
2. Register with the practice; bring passport and proof of address
3. You may not need appointments often, but being registered means you can be seen when needed

**NHS dentist:** Different from GP registration. Search for NHS dentists in your area; waiting lists can be 6–12 months in some cities. Consider registering before you actually need treatment.

### Private Supplementary Insurance in UK

Some students purchase private health insurance for faster access to specialists. Not generally necessary given NHS coverage, but useful if you want:
- Shorter waiting times for specialist referrals
- Private hospital rooms
- Dental and optical coverage

Providers: AXA Health, Bupa, Vitality Health. Approximately £30–80/month for basic private cover.

## Germany — Mandatory Statutory Health Insurance (Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung / GKV)

### Why It's Mandatory

German law requires all residents to have health insurance. Universities require proof of insurance for enrollment (Immatrikulation). Students cannot enroll without it.

### Student GKV Rates

For students under 30 enrolled full-time, statutory student health insurance costs approximately:
**€111.72/month** (2024 base rate for KVdS — student tariff)

This includes a nursing care (Pflegeversicherung) component of approximately **€18.93/month**

**Total: approximately €130/month**

After age 30 or after 14 semesters (whichever comes first), you move to the higher contribution rate based on your income.

### Major Student Health Insurers in Germany

| Insurer | English Support | Online Portal | Notes |
|---------|----------------|---------------|-------|
| TK (Techniker Krankenkasse) | Excellent | Yes | Most popular among international students |
| AOK | Variable | Partial | Regional offices; varies by state |
| Barmer | Good | Yes | Widely accepted |
| DAK-Gesundheit | Moderate | Yes | Good coverage |

**Recommendation for Indian students: TK (TK.de)** — English-language support, easy online enrollment, widely accepted by all German universities and doctors.

### Enrollment Process

1. Enroll with TK or another insurer online before arriving (or within first week)
2. Receive your insurance certificate (Versicherungsbescheinigung)
3. Submit this to your university's student services office (Studierendensekretariat)
4. University confirms your enrollment

**Note:** German GKV covers doctor visits, hospital care, prescription medicines (€10 co-pay per prescription), preventive care, and mental health services.

### What German GKV Does NOT Cover Well

- Dental: Basic treatments covered; major dental work (implants, braces) often not covered or partially covered. Many students get supplementary Zahnzusatzversicherung (dental add-on) for €8–15/month.
- Eyeglasses: Basic eye exams covered; glasses/contacts generally not covered for adults over 18.

### Private Health Insurance Alternative (PKV)

Students over 30, or those who opt out of GKV (Befreiung von der Versicherungspflicht), can use private insurance. However, opting out is usually not advisable for international students — private insurance is cheaper at first but can have gaps and opting back into GKV later is difficult.

## Australia — Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

### What It Is

**OSHC** (Overseas Student Health Cover) is mandatory for all international students on Student Visa (subclass 500) for the entire duration of their visa.

Australian law and the Standard for Registration of Overseas Students (ESOS) requires OSHC as a condition of student visa.

### Cost

Approximately **AUD $600–750 per year** for a single student (varies by provider and coverage level).

**Couples:** AUD $1,500–1,800/year
**Family:** AUD $2,000–2,800/year

### Approved OSHC Providers (as of 2024)

| Provider | Website | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Bupa OSHC | bupaoshc.com.au | Largest provider; fast claims |
| Medibank OSHC | medibank.com.au/oshc | Wide hospital network |
| nib OSHC | nib.com.au/oshc | Good digital tools |
| AHM OSHC | ahmoshc.com | Budget option |
| CBHS International | cbhsinternational.com.au | Less common |

Many universities have an agreement with a preferred provider and purchase OSHC on your behalf when you pay tuition. Check your Confirmation of Enrollment (CoE) — it may include OSHC.

### What OSHC Covers

- GP visits: OSHC pays Medicare benefit equivalent (gap may exist if doctor charges above schedule)
- Hospital admission: 100% of public hospital costs in a shared ward
- Ambulance: Covered (important — ambulance is very expensive without insurance)
- Prescription medicines: Subsidized rates for PBS (Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme) listed medicines
- Mental health: Limited; usually 60–75% of consultation fee

### Gap Payments

OSHC typically covers the "Medicare benefit equivalent" of a service. If a doctor charges more than this (which many do), you pay the difference (gap payment). To minimize gap payments:
- See a **bulk-billing GP** (bulk billing = they accept Medicare/OSHC rate as full payment; no gap)
- Search for bulk-billing GPs at: healthdirect.gov.au or your insurer's provider search

### Student Visa — OSHC Renewal

If your course extends or you change your visa, ensure OSHC is extended to cover the new end date. Gaps in OSHC coverage can create problems with student visa conditions.

## Canada — Provincial Health Insurance

### Complexity: Provincial, Not Federal

Canada has no single national student health insurance scheme. Each province has its own healthcare system, and eligibility for provincial coverage varies by province and student type.

| Province | Wait Period | Student Eligibility | Action Required |
|---------|------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Ontario | 3-month wait | Yes, after wait period | Apply for OHIP |
| British Columbia | 3-month wait | Yes, after wait period | Apply for BC MSP |
| Alberta | 3-month wait | Yes, after wait period | Apply for AHCIP |
| Manitoba | No wait period | Yes, immediately | Apply for Manitoba Health |
| Nova Scotia | 3-month wait | Yes, after wait period | Apply for MSIG |
| Quebec | Distinct system | RAMQ — complex for students | Check directly |

### The 3-Month Coverage Gap

Most provinces impose a 3-month waiting period before provincial health insurance activates. During this period, you need private insurance.

**Most Canadian universities include student health insurance in their student fees.** Check whether your university has a group health plan for international students — most do, and it automatically covers the waiting period.

### University Health Plans

Examples:
- University of Toronto: UTSE (University of Toronto Student Extended Health)
- University of British Columbia: AMS/GSS Extended Health Plan
- McGill: SSMU Health Plan
- Waterloo: Federation of Students Health Plan

**Cost included in student fees:** Typically CAD $400–700/year included in mandatory student fees.

These plans cover:
- Doctor and hospital visits during the wait period
- Prescription drugs (partial)
- Dental (basic; often separate plan)
- Vision care (limited)
- Mental health counselling (often 80% of registered psychologist fees)

### After Provincial Coverage Activates

Once provincial health insurance activates (after 3 months), it covers:
- Doctor (GP and specialist) visits
- Hospital care
- Emergency care

**Not covered by provincial plans:** Prescription drugs (except Quebec); dental; optical. Keep your university supplementary plan active for these.

## USA — No Mandatory Federal Scheme

### The Challenge

The USA has no mandatory government health insurance for international students. You are responsible for obtaining your own coverage.

### University-Mandated Insurance

Most US universities **require** international students to have health insurance and provide (and often mandate enrollment in) their university's **Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP).**

**Typical cost:** USD $1,500–3,500/year (included in your student fee bill; you are enrolled automatically unless you waive with proof of equivalent coverage).

**Coverage:** Varies widely by university plan. Check:
- Annual deductible (amount you pay before insurance kicks in): Target under $500
- Out-of-pocket maximum (annual cap on what you pay): Target under $5,000
- Network: Is your university's student health center in-network?
- Mental health coverage: Number of covered sessions per year

### Waiving University SHIP

If you have alternative coverage (parent's plan until age 26 if parent is US employee; Fulbright insurance; other qualifying plan), you can waive the SHIP. However, waiving is generally not advisable unless you have comprehensive comparable coverage — university SHIP is usually the best-value plan for students.

### Insurance Terminology (USA) — Essential Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Premium | Monthly cost of insurance |
| Deductible | Amount you pay before insurance starts covering expenses |
| Co-pay | Fixed fee per visit (e.g., $30 per GP visit) |
| Co-insurance | Percentage you pay after deductible (e.g., 20% of bill) |
| Out-of-pocket maximum | Annual cap; insurer pays 100% after you reach this |
| In-network | Doctors/hospitals contracted with your insurer (cheaper) |
| Out-of-network | Doctors/hospitals NOT contracted (significantly more expensive) |
| EOB | Explanation of Benefits — statement from insurer after a claim |

## France — CPAM (Sécurité Sociale Étudiante)

### Universal Coverage for Students

International students in France are entitled to **Sécurité Sociale** (the French social security health system) through the **CPAM** (Caisse Primaire d'Assurance Maladie).

**Registration:** Register at your local CPAM office within the first weeks of arrival. You will need your university enrollment certificate, passport, proof of address, and European bank account (or French bank account).

**Cost:** Free for students under 29. Students pay only when receiving treatment (called "ticket modérateur" — the remaining portion after Sécurité Sociale reimbursement).

**Reimbursement rates:**
- GP visit: 70% reimbursed (you pay ~€7 out of €26.50 consultation fee)
- Specialist: 70% reimbursed
- Hospital: 80% reimbursed
- Medicines: 15–65% reimbursed depending on medication category

### Mutuelle (Top-Up Insurance)

Because CPAM only reimburses 70–80%, most students get a **mutuelle** (complementary insurance) to cover the remaining portion.

**Cost:** €10–30/month for basic student mutuelle

Providers: LMDE (Ligue des Mutuelles des Étudiants) — specifically for students; HEYME; Vittavi; April Santé.

### OFII and Health Screening

When you arrive in France as a non-EU student, you must complete the **OFII** (Office Français de l'Immigration et de l'Intégration) formality, which includes a medical examination. This is for immigration purposes, not for CPAM registration — these are separate processes.

## Netherlands — DSW/CZ Student Plans

### Basic Health Insurance Obligation

All residents in the Netherlands (including students) are legally required to have Dutch health insurance (**zorgverzekering**) if they work in the Netherlands or are enrolled at a Dutch institution.

**Basic insurance cost:** €130–160/month (2024)

**Zorgtoeslag (health allowance):** Students with low income can apply for a government healthcare allowance (zorgtoeslag) of up to €123/month to partially offset the cost. Apply at Belastingdienst (tax authority) shortly after registration.

### Student Plans

Dutch universities often arrange group health insurance plans for international students. Check your university's international office for arrangements with DSW or CZ (two major Dutch insurers).

## Supplementary Travel Insurance — Pre-Departure

Regardless of your destination country's mandatory insurance, consider purchasing additional travel insurance covering:

- **Medical evacuation:** Repatriation to India in medical emergency; can cost USD $50,000–200,000 without insurance
- **Trip interruption:** If you need to return to India urgently (family emergency)
- **Personal liability:** If you accidentally damage property or injure someone
- **Baggage loss:** Airlines losing checked luggage

Indian providers: Tata AIG, HDFC Ergo, Bajaj Allianz, ICICI Lombard offer international student travel insurance packages at ₹8,000–15,000/year.

## FAQ

**Q: I paid IHS for the UK Student Visa. Do I still need to register with a GP separately?**
A: Yes. IHS payment gives you the right to access NHS services, but you need to actively register with a GP practice near your accommodation. Without GP registration, you'll only have access to A&E (emergency room), not planned care. Register within your first two weeks.

**Q: Germany's TK enrollment — can I do this from India before I arrive?**
A: Yes. You can register with TK online before arriving. You'll receive your insurance card after you provide your German address. You can use your enrollment certificate from TK as proof for university Immatrikulation even before receiving the physical card.

**Q: What happens if I arrive in Canada before my provincial health insurance activates and I have a medical emergency?**
A: Your university student health plan should cover emergencies during the waiting period. If you're not enrolled in the university plan, private travel insurance is essential. Emergency care is never refused in Canada regardless of insurance status, but without insurance you'll receive a significant bill.

**Q: Can I use my Indian health insurance abroad?**
A: Some Indian health insurers offer international coverage add-ons, but the reimbursement process is complex (pay out-of-pocket; submit claim in India later). These plans are typically supplementary options for short trips, not a substitute for proper local health coverage during a multi-year study program.
