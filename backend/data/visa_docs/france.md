# France Student Visa (Visa de Long Séjour – Étudiant) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** Visa de Long Séjour (VLS) — Étudiant (Long-Stay Student Visa)  
**Sub-category:** VLS-TS (Visa de Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour) — Long-Stay Visa Equivalent to Residence Permit  
**Issued by:** French Embassy or Consulate General in India  
**Validity:** The VLS-TS is issued for 1 year and acts as both the visa and the initial residence permit; renewal is done in France.

For programs longer than 1 year, students validate the visa online within 3 months of arrival (via the ANEF portal — Administration Numérique pour les Étrangers en France) and may apply for renewal at the local prefecture.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Must have received an unconditional offer of admission (lettre d'admission) from a recognised French higher education institution.
- Must be enrolled through **Campus France** — France's official higher education promotion agency. Indian students MUST complete the Campus France procedure before applying for a visa (mandatory in India).
- Must demonstrate sufficient financial means.
- Must have appropriate health insurance.
- Must not pose a threat to public order.
- Must be under 28 years of age for some scholarship schemes (not a visa requirement, but scholarship-specific).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

- **Minimum required:** €615 per month (€7,380 per year) — required to demonstrate this level of financial support.
- Typical cost of living in Paris: €1,000–€1,500 per month. Other cities: €700–€1,000 per month.
- Tuition fees:
  - Public universities (universités): €2,770/year for Bachelor's; €3,770/year for Master's; €380/year for Doctorates (after reforms; some institutions charge differentiated fees for international students — varies).
  - Grandes Écoles and private institutions: €5,000–€20,000+ per year.
- **Visa application fee:** €99 (approximately INR 8,800 as of 2024).

**Proof of funds accepted:**
- Bank statements showing at least €615/month for the intended period.
- Scholarship award letters (government scholarships, Eiffel Excellence Scholarship, Campus France scholarship).
- Notarised sponsorship letter from parents with income proof (payslips, ITR).
- Study loan sanction letter from an Indian bank.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 3 months beyond intended stay; at least 2 blank pages).
- Completed visa application form (via France-Visas online portal: france-visas.gouv.fr).
- 2 recent passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, white background, ICAO standard).
- Campus France attestation / Campus France procedure completion document — obtained after completing online Campus France procedure and in-person interview.
- Proof of admission to a French institution (lettre d'admission, billet de préinscription, lettre d'acceptation).
- Proof of financial resources:
  - Bank statements (last 3 months) showing at least €7,380 equivalent.
  - Scholarship award letter.
  - Sponsorship letter with supporting income documents.
  - Loan sanction letter.
- Proof of accommodation in France (housing certificate, university dorm offer, rental agreement, or letter from host family/friend).
- Academic transcripts and diplomas:
  - 10th, 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's/Master's transcripts (with certified translation into French or English if required).
- Language proficiency certificate:
  - French programs: DELF B2, DALF C1/C2, TCF, TEF.
  - English programs: IELTS, TOEFL, GMAT/GRE (as required by the institution).
- Motivation letter / SOP (required by some institutions and recommended for Campus France).
- Health insurance certificate (travel + health insurance valid for the duration of the visa or French social security student registration proof).
- Civil aviation affiliation / French health system registration (CVEC — Contribution à la Vie Étudiante et de Campus; mandatory student contribution of €103/year).
- Police clearance certificate (if required by the consulate; varies).
- CV/Resume (recommended for postgraduate applicants).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to French institutions:** Apply directly or through Campus France India platform (approximately October–April for September intake).
2. **Complete Campus France procedure (mandatory for India):**
   - Register on the Campus France India website (inde.campusfrance.org).
   - Fill in your academic and professional profile.
   - Select institutions and programs.
   - Upload required documents.
   - Pay Campus France fee (approximately INR 15,000–20,000).
   - Attend in-person interview at a Campus France office (New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Bangalore, Pune, Ahmedabad, Chandigarh — 9 centres across India).
3. **Receive admission letter:** Obtain unconditional admission from the French institution.
4. **Register on France-Visas:** Create an account and complete the visa application form.
5. **Book appointment at VFS Global / French Consulate:** Submit documents at the French Visa Application Centre.
6. **Pay visa fee:** €99 at the time of submission.
7. **Biometrics:** Fingerprints and photograph collected at the application centre.
8. **Await visa decision:** Usually 3–6 weeks.
9. **Upon arrival in France:**
   - Validate the VLS-TS visa within 3 months via the ANEF portal: https://administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr
   - Pay the OFII (Office Français de l'Immigration et de l'Intégration) validation fee: €50 via tax stamps.
   - Pay CVEC (€103/year) and register with the French social security system (CPAM).

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Campus France procedure:** 2–4 weeks (interview scheduling dependent).
- **Visa processing:** 3–6 weeks after application submission.
- **Total lead time:** Begin the Campus France process at least 3–4 months before the intended start date.
- Applications for September intake typically open from November–December.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Indian students holding a valid student residence permit can work up to **964 hours per year** (equivalent to approximately 60% of the legal working week, or about 20 hours per week).
- During summer vacations: full-time work is permitted (not counted separately — within the annual 964-hour cap).
- Must have a valid student residence permit and a work contract.
- A work permit (autorisation de travail) is not required separately — the student visa/residence permit already authorises work up to the annual limit.
- Students in French overseas departments follow the same rules as metropolitan France.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **APS (Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour) — Post-Study Work Permit:**
  - Available to graduates of Master's level (Bac+5) and above from a French institution.
  - Duration: **1 year**, renewable once for another year.
  - Allows job-seeking and working in France without requiring a separate work permit.
  - Job offer must be commensurate with the degree.
- **Talent Passport (Passeport Talent):** For those with a job offer of at least €46,000/year; valid for 4 years and renewable.
- **EU Blue Card:** Available for highly qualified professionals with a job offer above the EU Blue Card salary threshold.

## 9. Language Requirements

**For French-taught programs:**

| Test | Minimum Level |
|------|---------------|
| DELF | B2 (for Bachelor's); C1 for competitive programs |
| DALF | C1 or C2 |
| TCF (Test de Connaissance du Français) | B2 or above |
| TEF (Test d'Évaluation de Français) | B2 or above |

**For English-taught programs (primarily Master's and MBA):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–7.0 |
| TOEFL iBT | 79–100 |
| GMAT / GRE | As required by institution |

- Many engineering grandes écoles and business schools (HEC Paris, ESSEC, EDHEC) offer English-taught Master's programs.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- All students in France are automatically enrolled in the **French social security student regime (Sécurité Sociale)** upon registration at a French institution — this provides free coverage for most healthcare expenses.
- CVEC (Contribution à la Vie Étudiante et de Campus): €103/year, mandatory for all students.
- A **complementary health insurance (mutuelle)** is strongly recommended to cover the 30% co-payment not covered by Sécurité Sociale. Cost: approximately €20–40/month.
- For visa purposes, you need proof of health insurance (travel insurance valid for the visa period is sufficient initially; full French health coverage kicks in after registration).
- Students under 25 may qualify for **Complémentaire Santé Solidaire (CSS)** — subsidised complementary health insurance.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Campus France is mandatory:** Unlike most countries, France requires all Indian applicants to complete the Campus France procedure (including a personal interview) before applying for a visa. Factor in this extra step.
- **Language is key:** Most undergraduate programs are in French. English-taught programs are almost exclusively at Master's level and in business/engineering schools.
- **Eiffel Excellence Scholarship:** Highly competitive but very prestigious scholarship for international students at Master's and PhD level — covers tuition, living allowance, and health insurance.
- **French education system:** Grandes Écoles (HEC, Polytechnique, Sciences Po, ESSEC, INSEAD) are prestigious but highly competitive. Universités are more accessible.
- **Accommodation:** Finding accommodation in Paris is extremely competitive. Apply to CROUS (student housing) as early as possible or explore private options (Studapart, student residences).
- **CVEC:** Every student must pay this campus contribution online at cvec.etudiant.gouv.fr before enrollment.
- **Validated VLS-TS:** Remember to validate your visa online via ANEF within 3 months of arrival — failure to do so can make your stay irregular.
