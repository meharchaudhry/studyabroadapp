# Post-Study Work Visa Comparison — All Major Destinations

## Overview

Post-study work rights are one of the most important factors for Indian students choosing a study destination. The ability to gain local work experience after graduation affects your return on investment, immigration pathway eligibility, and career trajectory. This guide compares post-study work visas across all major destinations with exact fees, durations, and conditions.

---

## United Kingdom — Graduate Route

### Key Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Visa Name | Graduate Route |
| Duration | 2 years (bachelor's/master's); 3 years (PhD) |
| Fee | £715 (2024) |
| Work Restriction | None — can work in any job at any salary |
| Study While on Graduate Route | Allowed (but no further switching to Student Visa from Graduate Route) |
| Application Location | Online from UK (cannot apply from outside UK) |

### Eligibility Requirements

- Completed a degree at a UK Higher Education Provider licensed by UKVI
- Degree must be at bachelor's level or above
- Must apply before current Student Visa expires
- Must be in the UK when you apply

### What You Can Do on Graduate Route

- Work full-time in any sector, any job, any salary level
- Change jobs multiple times without restriction
- Self-employment and freelancing allowed (unlike many other routes)
- No English language test required to switch to Graduate Route

### Switching to Skilled Worker Visa from Graduate Route

The Graduate Route is a **stepping stone**, not a permanent solution. To stay longer and work toward settlement (ILR), you must switch to the Skilled Worker Visa.

**Skilled Worker requirements:**
- Job must be on the eligible occupation list (SOC codes)
- Employer must be a licensed sponsor
- Minimum salary: **£26,200/year** (general threshold, 2024) or the "going rate" for the occupation, whichever is higher
- No minimum salary for healthcare, education (NHS, teachers), and certain shortage roles

**Path to ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain):**
Skilled Worker Visa → 5 years continuous residence → ILR application (£2,885 fee)

**Important:** Graduate Route time does NOT count toward ILR. Only time on Skilled Worker counts.

## United States — OPT and STEM OPT Extension

### Optional Practical Training (OPT)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Duration | 12 months initial OPT |
| STEM Extension | 24 additional months (total 36 months) |
| Fee | $410 USCIS fee; $35 SEVIS reinstatement not applicable |
| Work Authorization | Must be directly related to major field of study |
| Application Timing | Can apply 90 days before graduation; must start within 60 days of graduation |

### Who Qualifies for STEM OPT?

Your degree must be in a STEM field (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) as defined by the DHS STEM designated degree program list. Most CS, EE, Data Science, Math, Biology, Chemistry, Physics degrees qualify.

**For STEM OPT extension:**
- Must have a job offer from an employer enrolled in E-Verify
- Must have a valid training plan (Form I-983) approved by your DSO
- Apply 90 days before your initial OPT expires

### After OPT — The H-1B Problem

The H-1B visa is the primary path for Indians staying in the USA after OPT/STEM OPT. **The H-1B is lottery-based.** The lottery has an annual cap of 85,000 visas (65,000 regular + 20,000 master's cap). In recent years, the registration lottery has had more registrations than available visas, with approval odds of roughly 25–35%.

Indian nationals face an additional backlog in H-1B employment-based green card categories (EB-2 and EB-3), with wait times potentially exceeding 10–15 years for Indians due to per-country caps.

**Alternative routes:** O-1A (extraordinary ability), EB-1A (extraordinary ability green card), L-1 (intracompany transfer from Indian company).

### CPT vs OPT Reminder

- CPT (Curricular Practical Training) is used during studies, not after graduation
- Using 12+ months full-time CPT eliminates all OPT eligibility entirely
- Do not use CPT carelessly; preserve your OPT if you plan to work post-graduation

## Canada — Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

### Key Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Visa Name | Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) |
| Duration | Equal to the length of your study program; maximum 3 years |
| Fee | CAD $255 |
| Work Restriction | Open permit — work for any employer in any sector |
| Application Deadline | Must apply within 180 days of graduating |

### PGWP Duration Rules

| Study Duration | PGWP Length |
|---------------|------------|
| 8 months to less than 2 years | Same as study program length |
| 2 years or more | 3 years (maximum) |
| Less than 8 months | Not eligible |

**Examples:**
- 1-year master's: 1-year PGWP
- 2-year master's: 3-year PGWP
- 4-year bachelor's: 3-year PGWP (capped at 3)
- 8-month diploma: 8-month PGWP

### Express Entry and PGWP

Canada's PGWP is the most powerful post-study work permit globally for immigration purposes because Canadian work experience earned on PGWP directly contributes to Express Entry CRS points and Canadian Experience Class (CEC) eligibility.

**CEC eligibility requirement:** 1 year of skilled work experience in Canada (NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3) on a valid work permit.

Most Indian students aim for 1–3 years of Canadian work experience on PGWP to qualify for CEC, then apply for PR through Express Entry.

### French Language Bonus

If you speak French, consider studying at a French-language institution (Université de Montréal, Université Laval, Université de Moncton). PGWP rules are the same, but bilingual candidates receive additional CRS points and can access Francophone immigration streams.

## Australia — Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

### Key Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Visa Name | Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485) |
| Streams | Post-Study Work Stream; Graduate Work Stream |
| Fee | AUD $1,895 |
| IELTS Requirement | 6.0 in each of the four bands |
| Application Timing | Apply within 6 months of notification of results |

### Duration by Degree and Location

**Post-Study Work Stream (for bachelor's degree and above):**

| Degree | Study Location | Duration |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Bachelor's degree | Major city | 2 years |
| Bachelor's degree | Regional area | 3 years |
| Honours degree | Any | 2 years |
| Master's by coursework | Major city | 2 years |
| Master's by coursework | Regional area | 3 years |
| Master's by research | Any | 3 years |
| Doctoral (PhD) | Major city | 4 years |
| Doctoral (PhD) | Regional area | 5 years |
| Any degree | Regional designation | +1 year bonus |

**"Regional area" definition:** Not Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Gold Coast, Perth.

### Graduate Work Stream (2-Year, Skills Assessment Required)

Alternatively, graduates with a degree in an occupation on the **Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL)** and a completed skills assessment can apply for the Graduate Work Stream. Less commonly used by Indian students as the Post-Study Work Stream is easier to access.

### PR Pathways from 485

After building points during your 485 period:
- **Subclass 189** (Skilled Independent): No state sponsor; points-based; INV: 65 points minimum typically
- **Subclass 190** (Skilled Nominated): State nomination; 5 years PR plus citizenship faster
- **Subclass 491** (Skilled Work Regional): Regional work requirement; 3 years before 191 PR
- **Subclass 191** (Permanent Residence — Regional): Pathway from 491 after 3 years regional work

## Germany — Job Seeker Visa and 18-Month Extension

### 18-Month Job Seeker Period (§20 AufenthG)

After completing a degree in Germany, graduates can stay for **18 months** to search for a job. This is not a traditional post-study work permit but a job seeker residence permit.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Duration | 18 months |
| Work Authorization | Can take on any work to support yourself while job searching |
| Requirement | Valid health insurance; evidence of financial means |
| Extension | Must convert to work visa before this expires |

### Converting to Employment Visa / EU Blue Card

Once you have a job offer:
- **EU Blue Card:** Salary minimum €48,300/year (2024) general; €37,920 for shortage occupations (MINT — Math, IT, Natural Sciences, Technology)
- This is the primary visa for high-skilled Indian IT and engineering graduates
- After 21 months with B1 German language: Eligible for permanent residence (Niederlassungserlaubnis)
- After 33 months without German language (but with A1+): Eligible for permanent residence

### German Citizenship Timeline

For Indian students completing a degree in Germany and staying:
- Typical path: Study (2–3 years) + EU Blue Card (3 years) = 5–6 years total
- German citizenship can be applied for after **5 years** of legal residence (proposed; currently 8 years but reform underway)
- Allows dual citizenship with India (under new German naturalization law, dual nationality possible)

## France — Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Permit Name | APS (Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour) for post-graduation work search |
| Duration | 1 year (extendable for 1 more year) |
| Work Authorization | Up to 60% of legal working time while job searching |
| Eligibility | Master's degree or above from French institution |

After finding employment, convert to a Work Permit (Carte de Séjour — Salarié or Passeport Talent).

## Sweden — 1-Year Job Seeker Extension

Sweden allows graduates from Swedish universities to extend their residence permit for 1 year to seek employment. After finding a job meeting the salary threshold (SEK 26,560/month, 2024), apply for a work permit.

---

## Singapore — No Post-Study Work Pathway

Singapore does **not** offer a blanket post-study work permit for international graduates. After graduation, you must find a job and have your employer apply for an Employment Pass (EP) on your behalf.

**Employment Pass minimum salary:** SGD $5,000/month (SGD $5,500 for financial sector). This is the minimum; actual salaries and pass approval depend on your qualifications and employer.

Indian graduates from NUS/NTU with strong academic records and relevant skills are competitive for EP applications in Singapore's tech and finance sectors.

## Which Country Offers the Best Post-Study Path for Indians?

### Best for PR / Permanent Residency Speed

**Canada** — PGWP + 1 year Canadian work experience → Express Entry CEC PR. Total time from graduation to PR: 2–4 years realistically.

### Best for Work Flexibility

**UK** — No salary minimum, no sector restriction, self-employment allowed. Most flexible in terms of what you can do during your post-study period.

### Best for Long-Term Earning Potential

**USA** — If you secure H-1B, salaries in tech/finance significantly exceed other destinations. Risk: H-1B lottery uncertainty.

### Best for Students Prioritizing European Citizenship

**Germany** — Free or low-cost tuition, strong engineering sector, PR after 21–33 months of EU Blue Card employment, potential dual citizenship with India.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the UK Graduate Route count toward ILR?**
A: No. Time spent on the Graduate Route does not count toward the 5-year continuous residence required for ILR. Only time on Skilled Worker Visa, or other qualifying routes, counts toward ILR.

**Q: If I fail my degree in Australia, am I still eligible for a 485?**
A: No. You must have successfully completed a degree. Additionally, you must have studied for at least 2 academic years in Australia (16 months for some visa holders). Completion is mandatory.

**Q: Can I bring my spouse/family to the UK on the Graduate Route?**
A: Yes. Your spouse/partner can apply as a dependant on your Graduate Route visa. However, as of 2024, new rules restrict Student Visa dependants for most taught master's programs. Once you're on the Graduate Route, existing dependants can usually be extended.

**Q: My PGWP expires while my Express Entry application is pending. What do I do?**
A: Apply for a PGWP extension or a bridging open work permit (BOWP). Once your PR application is submitted, you can apply for a BOWP to continue working legally while awaiting the PR decision.
