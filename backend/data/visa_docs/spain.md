# Spain Student Visa (Long-Stay Type D) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Overview

**Visa Type:** Long-Stay National Visa — Studies (Categoría: Estudios)  
**Issued by:** Spanish Embassy, New Delhi / Spanish Consulate General, Mumbai  
**Authority:** Dirección General de Migraciones (Spain), Oficina de Extranjería  
**Validity:** Initial visa valid for 90 days to enter Spain; after arrival, apply for the Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero (TIE — Foreigner Identity Card) for the full course duration.  
**Schengen Member:** Yes.

Spain is one of Europe's most popular study destinations with excellent universities, a vibrant culture, and a relatively affordable cost of living compared to Northern Europe.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

Indian students must:
- Hold an acceptance/enrolment letter from a recognized Spanish educational institution (universidades públicas y privadas).
- Demonstrate sufficient financial means for the stay.
- Provide proof of accommodation in Spain.
- Hold valid health insurance (reimbursement-type policy accepted).
- Submit a clean criminal record certificate (PCC from India — apostilled and translated into Spanish).
- Have a valid passport (1+ year validity).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

Spain requires proof of financial self-sufficiency:

- **Minimum:** €579.02/month (Spain's IPREM — Indicador Público de Renta de Efectos Múltiples, 2024 rate).
- For a 12-month study: approximately €6,948 minimum, though €10,000–15,000 in a bank account is strongly recommended.
- **Tuition fees at Spanish public universities:** €1,500–3,500/year for non-EU students (some autonomous communities charge more; Catalonia, Madrid are higher).
- **Private universities:** IE University, ESADE Business School: €15,000–30,000/year.
- **Living costs:** Madrid/Barcelona €900–1,300/month; Valencia, Seville, Granada €600–850/month.
- Evidence: Bank statements (last 3 months), parental guarantee with bank statements, scholarship award letter, or fixed deposit.

---

## 4. Required Documents

1. Completed and signed visa application form (EX–00 form)
2. Valid passport (1+ year beyond intended stay) + 2 photocopies
3. 2 recent colour passport photographs (32×26mm — specific Spanish requirement)
4. Official enrolment letter / acceptance letter from Spanish institution
5. Proof of financial means (bank statements, scholarship letter)
6. Proof of accommodation (rental contract, university residence, host family letter)
7. Health insurance valid in Spain (minimum €30,000 coverage, covering repatriation)
8. Criminal Record Certificate (PCC) — apostilled, official translation into Spanish
9. Proof of payment of visa fee (approximately €80 — check current rate)
10. Flight reservation (not necessarily paid ticket, just a booking showing travel plan)

**For programs requiring specific entry tests** (medicine, architecture at some universities): Evidence of passing the university entrance exam.

---

## 5. Application Process

**Step 1 — University Admission**
Apply directly to Spanish universities (Ucrónica portal for public universities, or institution-specific portals). Receive acceptance letter.

**Step 2 — Document Preparation**
Gather all required documents. Criminal record must be apostilled under the Hague Convention. Translation of PCC and transcripts must be done by an official (sworn) translator.

**Step 3 — Apply at Spanish Embassy/Consulate**
- Book appointment at the Spanish Embassy in New Delhi or Spanish Consulate in Mumbai.
- Submit documents in person (no VFS for Spain — direct embassy application).
- Application must be submitted **between 3 months and 15 days** before course start date.

**Step 4 — Visa Decision**
- Processing time: 4–8 weeks typically.
- Visa fee: ~€80 (paid by demand draft or as specified by Embassy).

**Step 5 — Arrive and Register**
- Register at the local Ayuntamiento (Town Hall) — Empadronamiento within 1 month of arrival.
- Apply for TIE (Foreigner Identity Card) at the Oficina de Extranjería within 30 days of arrival.
- TIE cost: approximately €16 (Modelo 790 code 012).

---

## 6. Work Rights

- Students with a valid student visa/TIE can work **up to 30 hours per week** (one of the most generous in Europe).
- Authorization comes embedded with the student visa. No separate work permit needed.
- Spanish minimum wage: €1,134/month (2024) — approximately €7.10/hour.

---

## 7. Post-Study Options

- **Job search authorization:** After graduation from a Spanish institution, students can apply for a 1-year job-search residence authorization (Autorización de Residencia por Búsqueda de Empleo).
- Spain has a growing startup scene (Barcelona, Madrid, Valencia).
- EU Blue Card available once employed in a skilled role.

---

## 8. Indian-Student Specific Tips

- **Spanish language:** Many undergraduate programs are in Spanish. Master's programs increasingly offered in English, especially at private schools and in Barcelona.
- **DELE certificate:** Not mandatory for visa but helps for accommodation and integration. A1/A2 level achievable in 3–6 months of study.
- **Apostille of Indian documents:** Obtain Apostille from Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), India — can be done online via the MEA Apostille portal. Allow 2–4 weeks.
- **SEVIS equivalent in Spain:** No equivalent; Spain does not use SEVIS. The university manages enrollment reporting directly.
- Spain has a very active **Erasmus+ program** — excellent for European study exchanges during your degree.
- Common mistake: Getting transcript translations done by a non-sworn translator — ensure it's a jurado (official sworn) translator.
