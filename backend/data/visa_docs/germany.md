# Germany Student Visa (National Visa – Type D) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** National Visa (Type D) — Studienvisum (Student Visa)  
**Issued by:** German Embassy / Consulate in India (Visa Section)  
**Managed by:** Federal Foreign Office (Auswärtiges Amt) and local Foreigners' Authority (Ausländerbehörde) after arrival.  
**Validity:** The visa is typically issued for 3 months to allow entry and registration; upon arrival in Germany, students must apply for a **Residence Permit for Study Purposes (Aufenthaltserlaubnis zum Studium)** at the local Ausländerbehörde. The residence permit is granted for the duration of the study program.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- **Unconditional admission letter** (Zulassungsbescheid) from a German state-recognised university or Hochschule.
- Students awaiting final admission may apply for a **Visum zur Studienbewerbung** (visa for the purpose of applying to universities) — valid for up to 3 months, non-renewable.
- Must demonstrate sufficient German or English language proficiency (depending on the medium of instruction).
- Must prove sufficient financial resources.
- Must not be a public health risk (health requirements).
- Must not have prior serious criminal convictions.
- Blocked account (Sperrkonto) is the most common method of proving financial capacity.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

German authorities require proof that the student can cover their living expenses throughout their stay.

- **Minimum amount:** €11,904 per year (€992 per month) as of 2024. This is the standard amount required in a blocked account.
- The blocked account releases a fixed monthly amount (€992) to the student — ensuring money is available throughout the year.
- Tuition fees: Most German public universities charge **no tuition fees** for international students (only a semester fee of approximately €150–350 per semester for administrative costs, public transport ticket, and student union services).
- Private universities do charge tuition, ranging from €5,000–€30,000 per year.
- **Visa application fee:** €75 (approximately INR 6,700 as of 2024).

**Accepted proof of finances:**
- Blocked account (Sperrkonto) with providers like Fintiba, Expatrio, Deutsche Bank, or Coracle — most recommended for Indian students.
- A formal financial guarantee from a German sponsor (Verpflichtungserklärung).
- Scholarship award letter from DAAD or other German/Indian scholarship bodies.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 1 year beyond the intended stay; minimum 2 blank pages).
- Completed visa application form (2 copies) — available on the German Embassy India website.
- 2 recent biometric passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, white background).
- Proof of admission to a German university (Zulassungsbescheid) — unconditional admission letter.
- Proof of financial resources:
  - Blocked account (Sperrkonto) confirmation letter showing at least €11,904 deposited.
  - OR scholarship letter.
  - OR formal sponsorship declaration (Verpflichtungserklärung).
- CV/Resume.
- Academic qualifications:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's degree transcripts and degree certificate (attested/notarised, with certified translation into German or English if in a regional language).
  - Any prior Master's degree documents.
- Language proficiency proof:
  - German: TestDaF, DSH, Goethe-Zertifikat, or DSD II (for German-taught programs).
  - English: IELTS, TOEFL, Cambridge (for English-taught programs).
- Motivationsletter / Statement of Purpose (SOP).
- Health insurance confirmation (German public or private health insurance) — must be valid from day of arrival.
- Proof of accommodation in Germany (rental agreement, university dormitory offer letter).
- APS certificate (Akademische Prüfstelle) — MANDATORY for Indian students. The APS is a document authentication procedure conducted by the German Embassy in New Delhi/Mumbai/Chennai/Kolkata.

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Get APS certificate first:** This is a mandatory pre-visa step for Indian applicants. Submit academic documents to the APS (Akademische Prüfstelle) at the German Embassy. Processing takes 4–8 weeks. Fee: approx. €70.
2. **Apply and get admission:** Apply to German universities through uni-assist or directly. Obtain an unconditional Zulassungsbescheid.
3. **Open a blocked account (Sperrkonto):** Deposit €11,904 with a recognised provider (Fintiba, Expatrio, etc.). Receive confirmation letter.
4. **Arrange health insurance:** Purchase German public statutory health insurance (e.g., TK, AOK, Barmer) or private health insurance valid in Germany.
5. **Book visa appointment:** Schedule an appointment at the German consulate in your jurisdiction (New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Ahmedabad, Pune) via the online appointment system.
6. **Attend visa appointment:** Submit all documents, provide biometrics (fingerprints), and pay the €75 fee.
7. **Await decision:** Visa is typically issued within 4–12 weeks.
8. **Travel to Germany:** Enter with the Type D visa.
9. **Register in Germany (Anmeldung):** Register your address at the local registration office (Einwohnermeldeamt/Bürgeramt) within 14 days of arrival.
10. **Apply for residence permit:** Visit the local Ausländerbehörde to convert your entry visa into a residence permit for study. Bring: passport, visa, Anmeldebestätigung, APS certificate, admission letter, health insurance, blocked account confirmation, and passport photos.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **APS certificate:** 4–8 weeks.
- **Visa processing:** 4–12 weeks after submitting the full application.
- **Total lead time:** Start the process at least 4–6 months before the semester start date.
- Appointment availability at the consulate can be limited — book as early as possible.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Students from India (non-EU) are permitted to work:
  - **120 full days OR 240 half days per year.**
  - This translates to approximately 20 hours per week during semester.
- Working more than the allowed hours requires approval from the Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit) and the Foreigners' Authority.
- Student assistantships (Hiwi jobs) at the university often have special provisions.
- Self-employment is generally not permitted without separate authorisation.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Job-seeker visa (Aufenthaltserlaubnis zur Arbeitsuche):** After completing a German degree, graduates can apply for an 18-month residence permit to seek employment.
- Must apply before the study residence permit expires.
- Upon finding a job in a field related to the degree, apply for a work permit/EU Blue Card.
- **EU Blue Card:** Available for qualified professionals with a German or recognised degree and a job offer meeting the minimum salary threshold (€45,300 gross/year in 2024 for general occupations; €41,041.80 for shortage occupations).
- Excellent pathway to permanent residence (PR in as little as 21 months with EU Blue Card and B1 German skills).

## 9. Language Requirements

**For German-taught programs:**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| TestDaF | TDN 4 in all sub-tests |
| DSH | DSH-2 |
| Goethe-Zertifikat C2 (GDS) | Pass |
| DSD II | Pass |
| telc Deutsch C1 Hochschule | Pass |

**For English-taught programs (Master's and some Bachelor's):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–7.0 (varies by program) |
| TOEFL iBT | 80–100 (varies by program) |

- Many Master's programs in Germany are taught in English — especially in STEM, business, and public policy.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Mandatory:** German statutory health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung — GKV) or approved private health insurance.
- Must be valid from the first day of residence in Germany.
- GKV providers for students: Techniker Krankenkasse (TK), AOK, Barmer, DAK — cost approximately €110–120/month for students under 30.
- Students aged 30 and above or in doctoral programs may need private insurance.
- Health insurance proof is required both for the visa application and for university enrollment.
- Indian travel insurance is NOT accepted in place of German health insurance.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **APS (Akademische Prüfstelle) is non-negotiable:** No Indian student can receive a German student visa without APS certification of their academic documents. Apply early.
- **German language:** Even for English-taught programs, basic German (A1-A2) greatly improves daily life, part-time job prospects, and integration.
- **DAAD Scholarships:** The Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst (DAAD) offers numerous scholarships for Indian students. Apply well in advance (deadlines 6–12 months before intake).
- **No tuition fees at public universities:** This makes Germany one of the most affordable destinations for high-quality education.
- **Fintiba and Expatrio** are the most popular blocked account providers for Indian students — both offer online account opening from India.
- **Semester fee (Semesterbeitrag):** Not tuition — covers student union membership and often includes a semester-long public transport pass.
- **Tax ID (Steueridentifikationsnummer):** Received automatically after address registration; needed to start working.
