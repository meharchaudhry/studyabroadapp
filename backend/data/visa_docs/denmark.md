# Denmark Residence Permit for Studies — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Permit Type:** Residence Permit for Studies (Studietilladelse — Study Permit / Residence Permit)  
**Issued by:** Danish Immigration Service (Styrelsen for International Rekruttering og Integration — SIRI) for processing; Danish Embassy in India for entry visa issuance.  
**Entry Visa:** Indian nationals require a national visa (Type D, long-stay) to enter Denmark for studies. The visa application is often bundled with the residence permit application submitted through the Embassy or VFS Global.  
**Validity:** Typically issued for the duration of the course, up to a maximum initially. Renewable in Denmark.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Have been unconditionally admitted to a full-time education programme at a Danish institution recognised by the Ministry of Higher Education and Science (Uddannelses- og Forskningsministeriet).
- Demonstrate adequate financial means to cover living expenses and tuition.
- Have valid health insurance for the initial period.
- Meet the language requirements of the specific programme.
- Have a valid passport.
- Not have a criminal record that would prevent the permit from being granted.
- Be a genuine student — intent to study and leave Denmark upon completion (unless applying for post-study permits).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

SIRI requires students to demonstrate sufficient funds for the duration of the permit.

- **Minimum monthly maintenance requirement:** DKK 6,243 per month (2024 figure — approximately INR 78,000–80,000/month).
- **Annual requirement:** DKK 74,916 per year.
- Funds must be available for the full period of the permit applied for.
- Tuition fees:
  - **Danish public universities** charge tuition fees to non-EU/EEA students:
    - Bachelor's programs: DKK 45,000–120,000 per year (approximately INR 4.5–12 lakh).
    - Master's programs: DKK 60,000–150,000 per year (approximately INR 6–15 lakh).
    - Technical University of Denmark (DTU), University of Copenhagen, Aarhus University — fees vary by program.
  - **Note:** Some Danish universities offer partial or full scholarships to international students.
- **Residence Permit application fee:** DKK 4,175 (approximately INR 52,000 as of 2024).

**Proof of funds accepted:**
- Bank statements (personal, parental, or sponsor's) — last 3–6 months.
- Scholarship award letters.
- Study loan sanction from a recognised Indian bank.
- Formal sponsorship declaration with income documentation.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 3 months beyond the intended stay; at least 2 blank pages).
- Completed online application for residence permit (via the SIRI portal: nyidanmark.dk).
- 2 recent passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, neutral background).
- Letter of admission from the Danish institution (unconditional, with programme start date, duration, and tuition amount).
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements (3–6 months, personal and/or parental).
  - Scholarship award letter showing the monthly/annual amount.
  - Loan sanction letter from Indian bank.
  - Sponsor's income documentation (salary slips, ITR returns).
- Proof of tuition payment or financial arrangement with institution (first semester receipt or financial guarantee from the institution regarding full tuition arrangement).
- Academic documents:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's degree transcripts and certificate (for Master's applicants).
  - Attested English translations if documents are in a regional language.
- English language proficiency certificate:
  - IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, Cambridge.
- Danish language certificate (if applying for Danish-language programmes):
  - Studieprøven (Danish Language Test), Prøve i Dansk 3.
- CV/Resume and motivation letter (required by most institutions; recommended for visa purposes).
- Proof of accommodation in Denmark (university accommodation offer, rental agreement, or documented host/lodging arrangement).
- Health insurance for the initial period (until Danish national health insurance registration is complete).
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) — recommended; may be requested by SIRI.
- Application fee payment confirmation (DKK 4,175).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to Danish institutions:** Apply directly to the institution. Most applications are made via the institution's own portal. The Danish universities typically have two intake periods: September (main) and February.
2. **Receive unconditional admission letter.**
3. **Submit SIRI online application:** Go to nyidanmark.dk, select "Residence Permit — Student," create an account, and complete the application. Upload all required documents and pay the fee.
4. **Submit supporting documents at the Danish Embassy in New Delhi or VFS Global:**
   - Some documents may be submitted digitally via the SIRI portal.
   - Passport and biometrics are generally submitted in person at the Embassy or VFS Global centre.
5. **Pay application fee:** DKK 4,175 (paid online during application).
6. **Biometrics:** Fingerprints and photograph at the Embassy or VFS Global.
7. **Await SIRI decision:** Processing takes 4–8 weeks typically; may be longer.
8. **Receive decision:** SIRI sends the decision to the institution and to you. If approved, the Embassy in New Delhi issues the visa sticker.
9. **Travel to Denmark.**
10. **Register at the International Citizens Service (ICS) or local municipality:**
    - Register your address within 5 days of arrival.
    - Collect your Residence Permit Card (opholdskort).
    - Apply for CPR number (Central Person Register — Denmark's national ID number) at the local Citizens' Service Office (Borgerservice).
    - Register with a GP (læge) through the public health system.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **SIRI processing time:** 4–8 weeks (can vary based on workload; sometimes up to 3–4 months).
- Apply at least 3–4 months before the intended course start date.
- September intake: Apply by May–June at the latest.
- SIRI prioritises applications based on documentation completeness — ensure all documents are correct and complete at the time of submission.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Students from non-EU/EEA countries (including India) with a valid study residence permit are permitted to work:
  - **During the academic year:** Up to **20 hours per week**.
  - **During June, July, and August (summer holidays):** Full-time (unlimited hours).
- Work rights are automatically attached to the student residence permit — no separate work permit is needed.
- The employer must verify that you hold the valid residence permit with work authorisation.
- Must pay Danish taxes on all income earned — register for a CPR number and apply for a skattekort (tax card) at SKAT (Danish Tax Authority).

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Establishment Card (Etableringskort):**
  - Graduates from Danish higher education institutions (or a top-20 global university) can apply for a 2-year residence permit to seek employment in Denmark.
  - Valid for **2 years** after graduation.
  - No specific job offer required to apply.
  - Must apply while still holding a valid study permit.
- **Pay Limit Scheme (Beløbsordningen):** After finding a job paying at least **DKK 469,000 gross per year** (2024 threshold), apply for a work residence permit.
- **Positive List Scheme:** Jobs in occupations on Denmark's positive list (shortage occupations) — includes many STEM and healthcare occupations.
- **EU Blue Card Denmark:** For highly qualified workers with a job offer above DKK 475,000/year — provides a streamlined path to work permit and PR.

## 9. Language Requirements

**For English-taught programs (very common at Danish universities):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.5 (some programs: 7.0) |
| TOEFL iBT | 83 |
| PTE Academic | 58 |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced / C2 Proficiency | Pass (176+) |

**For Danish-taught programs:**

| Test | Minimum Level |
|------|---------------|
| Studieprøven (Study Test) | Pass |
| Prøve i Dansk 3 | Pass (upper level) |

- Denmark has a high proportion of English-taught Master's programs at the University of Copenhagen, DTU, Aarhus University, Copenhagen Business School (CBS), and Aalborg University.
- Danish language knowledge, while not required for English programs, is very helpful for daily life and employment.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Danish National Health Service (Sundhedsvæsenet):**
  - After registering with a CPR number, students are entitled to use Denmark's public health services (free GP visits, subsidised hospital treatment).
  - This is one of the best aspects of studying in Denmark — high-quality public healthcare at minimal cost.
  - Registration with a GP (läge) is required after CPR registration.
- **Initial travel/health insurance:** Required for the period between arrival and CPR/health registration (typically the first few weeks).
- **European Health Insurance Card (EHIC):** Not available for Indian students; private insurance is needed initially.
- **Dental care:** Not covered by the Danish public system for adults — budget approximately DKK 500–1,500 per dental visit.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Government of Denmark Scholarships:** Danish universities offer institutional scholarships; the Danish government does not operate a general scholarship programme for non-EU students. Check individual universities (DTU, University of Copenhagen, CBS) for merit scholarships.
- **Technical University of Denmark (DTU):** Globally recognised for STEM and engineering programs. Many English-taught Master's available. DTU offers DTU Technical Scholarship and DTU Partnership Scholarship for international students.
- **Copenhagen Business School (CBS):** One of Europe's largest business schools, with highly ranked MBA and Master's programs in English.
- **CPR Number:** Essential for all aspects of life in Denmark — banking, healthcare, mobile subscriptions, rental agreements. Apply for it immediately after arrival and registration.
- **Cost of living:** Denmark is one of Europe's most expensive countries. Copenhagen is particularly expensive — budget DKK 9,000–14,000/month (approximately INR 1.1–1.75 lakh/month) for rent, food, transport, and personal expenses. Smaller cities (Aarhus, Odense, Aalborg) are more affordable.
- **SU (Statens Uddannelsesstøtte):** Denmark's student grant system — generally not available to non-EU students. Indian students are not eligible.
- **Aarhus:** Second largest city; Aarhus University is highly ranked and somewhat more affordable than Copenhagen.
- **Establish bank account:** As soon as you have a CPR number, open a Danish bank account (Danske Bank, Nordea, or digital bank Lunar) — essential for receiving salary and paying rent.
- **SIRI has strict document requirements:** Incomplete applications are rejected or delayed significantly. Double-check all document specifications before submission.
