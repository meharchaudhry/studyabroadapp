# Bringing Family (Spouse and Children) While Studying Abroad

## Overview

Many Indian students — particularly those at the postgraduate level or mature students — want to bring their spouse and/or children while studying abroad. This is possible at most major study destinations but involves significant additional cost, documentation, and careful financial planning. Most students find bringing family in Year 2+ (after settling, finding part-time work, and understanding local costs) is more manageable than bringing family immediately.

---

## United Kingdom — Student Dependant Visa

### Who Can Apply as Your Dependant?
- Spouse or civil partner
- Unmarried partner (if you've been in a relationship for at least 2 years — must provide substantial evidence)
- Children under 18 (including step-children and legally adopted children)

### Eligibility Requirements (For YOU as the Main Student)
- You must be on a UK Student visa studying a course at postgraduate level (master's, PhD, PGCE)
- **OR** studying a course of at least 9 months at a Higher Education Provider (HEP) — which includes full-time bachelor's programs
- Your university must be a UKVI-listed higher education provider
- Students on government-funded scholarships may be prohibited from bringing dependants — check your scholarship terms

### Application Process
- Dependants apply for a Student Dependant visa — same visa category, different stream
- Can apply from India (before you travel) or from UK (after you've arrived)
- Apply online at gov.uk/student-visa/family-members
- Must apply separately from your own Student visa but often done simultaneously

### Documents Required for Dependant Application
- Dependant's valid passport
- Proof of relationship:
  - Marriage certificate (apostilled if possible)
  - Joint bank accounts or joint address history (for unmarried partners)
  - Wedding photos, joint communications history (for unmarried partner)
- Your Student visa confirmation / CAS reference number
- Proof of additional financial resources (see below)
- TB test result for dependant (same IOM-approved clinics in India): ~₹2,500–4,000

### Financial Requirements — Additional Funds

You must show additional money above your own requirements:

| Location | Per Dependant Required |
|----------|----------------------|
| Studying in London | £680/month × 9 months = **£6,120 additional** |
| Studying outside London | £520/month × 9 months = **£4,680 additional** |

If bringing spouse + 1 child, you need 2× these amounts in additional bank balance.

### Fees
- Visa fee: **£490 per dependant**
- NHS (Immigration Health Surcharge): **£470 per year per dependant** (paid upfront for full visa duration)
  - Example: 2-year visa for spouse + 1 child = 2 years × 2 people × £470 = £1,880 NHS surcharge alone

### What Dependant Spouse Can Do
- **Work full-time in any job** — if the main student is in a postgraduate (master's/PhD) program
- If main student is in an undergraduate program: spouse cannot work (restriction changed over time — verify current rules at gov.uk)
- Children can attend state schools for free (state primary and secondary education is free for all children residing in UK)

### Processing Time
- Standard: 3 weeks for applications from outside UK
- Book biometric appointment at VFS Global India

## United States — F-2 Visa

### Eligibility
- Spouse and unmarried children under 21 of a valid F-1 student

### F-2 Application Process
- Apply at US Embassy in India using DS-160 application form
- Bring: your (F-1 student's) valid I-20, proof of your enrollment and status, financial proof
- Dependant pays separate visa fee: **$160 per person**
- Biometrics: $85 per person (enrolled if not previously done)

### Documents for F-2 Application
- DS-160 confirmation page (separate DS-160 for each family member)
- Passport of dependant
- Proof of relationship: marriage certificate, birth certificate for children (both apostilled)
- Your I-20 showing full-time enrollment
- Financial evidence: sufficient income/funds to support family members without public assistance
  - RA/TA offer letter showing your annual stipend (most PhD students' income is sufficient)
  - Or: family bank statements showing savings

### What F-2 Holders CAN Do
- Accompany F-1 student to USA
- Live in USA for the same duration as F-1 student's program
- Travel freely in/out of USA
- Children can enroll in K-12 schools (elementary, middle, high school)
- F-2 spouse can study part-time (not for a degree; only vocational or recreational)

### What F-2 Holders CANNOT Do
- **CANNOT WORK under ANY circumstances** — working on F-2 is a serious immigration violation
- Cannot enroll in full-time academic programs
- Cannot apply for most benefits

### Reality Check for Indian Students
F-2 is one of the most restrictive dependent visas. The inability to work means the couple must live on the student's stipend alone. For most Indian PhD students earning $18,000–$22,000/year in high cost-of-living cities (New York, San Francisco, Boston), supporting a family is extremely challenging.

## Canada — Open Work Permit for Spouse

### Eligibility (for Spouse's Work Permit)
Canada is significantly more generous for accompanying spouses:
- Main student is enrolled full-time in a program at a university or college
- Program must be at least **16 months** in duration
- Most bachelor's (3–4 year) and master's (1.5–2 year) programs qualify

### What Spouse Gets
- **Open Work Permit** — can work for ANY Canadian employer in any job
- Duration: Same as main student's study permit
- Must apply for this separately (not automatic)

### Application for Spousal Work Permit
- Apply online through IRCC portal (myaccount.ircc.gc.ca)
- Can be done from India simultaneously with the study permit application
- Fee: CAD $255
- Processing: 4–12 weeks

### Children in Canada
- Children can study for free at K-12 level in Canadian public schools while accompanying parent on study permit
- No separate application needed for children to attend school — the study permit for the main applicant covers the family

### Provincial Health Insurance
- Each province has its own waiting period (Ontario: 3 months for OHIP; other provinces vary)
- During waiting period: purchase private health insurance or check if university plan covers family

## Australia — Subclass 500 Dependent Stream

### Who Qualifies as a Dependant?
- Spouse or de facto partner (de facto = living together as a couple for at least 12 months)
- Children and step-children under 18

### Application Process
- Apply under the same Subclass 500 (student visa) framework
- Apply simultaneously with your own student visa or later
- Fee: **AUD $650 per dependant** (significantly cheaper than UK)
- Can apply from India or from within Australia

### Work Rights for Dependent Spouse
The work rights for a dependent spouse in Australia depend on the main applicant's study level:

| Main Student's Program | Spouse Work Rights |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| Doctoral (PhD) | **Unlimited work hours** |
| Master's (by research) | Unlimited work hours |
| Master's (by coursework) | Unlimited work hours |
| Bachelor's or other | **48 hours per fortnight** during semester |

### OSHC for Family
- OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover) for a couple is approximately AUD $900–1,500/year
- Dependent children under 18 can be included in family OSHC policy
- Family OSHC provider: Medibank, Bupa, Allianz (compare at privatehealth.gov.au)

### Children's Schooling
- Government primary and secondary schools may charge fees for temporary visa holders (dependants)
- Fee: approximately AUD $4,000–9,000/year per child depending on state (significantly less than private school fees)
- Many states offer free schooling for children of student visa holders at some levels — check your state

## Germany — Family Reunification

Germany is less straightforward for student family reunification:

### Spouse Visa
- Spouse can apply for a family reunification visa (Familienzusammenführung)
- Requires showing sufficient financial means to support the family
- This is where it gets difficult: blocked account must be substantially increased, OR you need to demonstrate income from part-time work
- Typical blocked account for a couple: approximately €18,000–20,000/year

### Work Authorization for Spouse
- Spouse does NOT automatically receive work authorization with family visa
- Must apply for work authorization separately after arrival and after your Anmeldung is complete
- Finding work in Germany without German language skills is challenging (especially outside major cities)

### Children's Education
- Children attend German public schools
- School instruction is in German
- Integration classes available but German language acquisition takes time
- Widely available: DaF (Deutsch als Fremdsprache) programs at schools

### Practical Recommendation for Germany
Most Indian PhD students in Germany bring family only after:
1. Completing first year; understanding local costs and community
2. Finding paid part-time work or teaching duties to supplement income
3. Establishing local support network through Indian community

## Practical Advice for Indian Families Abroad

### Schooling
- Contact your local council or school board within the first week of arrival — they allocate school places
- UK: Contact the local council's admissions team; they have a legal duty to provide a school place
- Australia: Contact the state's Department of Education directly
- School uniforms, bags, stationery: budget AUD/GBP 100–300 one-time cost per child

### Healthcare for Children
- Register all dependants with a GP/doctor within the first week
- UK: Children receive full NHS access same as adults — free
- Australia: OSHC covers children included on the family policy
- Canada: Children typically covered under provincial health insurance (same waiting period as adults)
- Germany: Children enrolled in your statutory health insurance (TK/AOK) at no extra cost for children in public insurance

### Social Support
- Connect with Indian student associations — many organize family gatherings
- Many Indian families abroad form WhatsApp groups for spouse support
- International student offices often have specific support for accompanying families
- Look for Indian playgroups/cultural events for children to maintain cultural connection

## FAQ

**Q: My wife is a doctor. Can she practice medicine in the UK on a Student Dependant visa?**
A: She can work in any job on the Student Dependant visa (if your program is postgraduate), but working as a doctor in the UK requires GMC (General Medical Council) registration, which requires additional qualifications/exams (PLAB test) and takes considerable time. She could work in healthcare support roles while preparing for GMC registration.

**Q: We're on a tight budget. Should we bring family in Year 1?**
A: Most experienced Indian students advise against it. Year 1 is the hardest academically and financially — you're adjusting, setting up, and likely earning nothing. Consider bringing family in Year 2 once you have part-time work income, understand local costs, and have a support network.

**Q: My marriage certificate is in Hindi. Do I need to get it translated?**
A: Yes. For most visa applications, non-English documents need a certified English translation. Get it done by a professional translator (many available online; approximately ₹500–1,500 per document).

**Q: Can my family work before receiving their dependent visa?**
A: No. Work authorization is only valid from the date the dependent visa is approved. No work should happen before the visa is granted.
