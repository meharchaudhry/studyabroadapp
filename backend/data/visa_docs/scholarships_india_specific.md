# India-Specific Scholarships for Study Abroad — Complete Guide

## National Overseas Scholarship (NOS)

**Offered by:** Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India

**Purpose:** To provide financial assistance to SC (Scheduled Caste), ST (Scheduled Tribe), and Denotified Tribe students for pursuing master's and PhD level education in foreign universities.

**Financial Support:**
- Tuition fees: Paid directly to university (actual amount)
- Living allowance: ₹15,400/month (subject to revision)
- Travel: Return economy airfare
- Contingency allowance: ₹30,000 per year
- Medical insurance: Covered

**Eligibility:**
- SC, ST, or Denotified Tribe categories
- Age: Below 35 years (relaxation for women and PwD)
- Income: Annual family income below ₹6 lakh
- Academic: Minimum 60% in last qualifying examination
- Must have secured admission to a recognized foreign university in approved subjects (STEM, economics, law, social sciences, commerce)

**Application Portal:** socialjustice.nic.in
**Deadline:** Typically March–April; check annually as dates shift

**Number of awards:** Approximately 125 slots per year across all categories

## ICCR (Indian Council for Cultural Relations) Scholarships

**Offered by:** Government of India through ICCR (Ministry of External Affairs)

**Coverage:**
- Full tuition fees
- Monthly living allowance (varies by country — approximately USD 500–1,500/month equivalent)
- Travel allowance
- Medical facilities access

**Countries with ICCR bilateral agreements:**
Argentina, Australia, Bangladesh, Belarus, Brazil, Canada, China, Cuba, Czech Republic, Egypt, France, Germany, Ghana, Hungary, Indonesia, Israel, Italy, Japan, Jordan, Kenya, Malaysia, Mexico, Morocco, Myanmar, Nepal, Netherlands, Nigeria, Pakistan, Peru, Philippines, Poland, Russia, South Africa, South Korea, Sri Lanka, Sweden, Thailand, Turkey, UK, Ukraine, USA, Vietnam + 10+ more

**How to apply:**
1. Go to a2ascholarships.iccr.gov.in
2. Select the country you want to study in
3. Check available scholarship programs for that country
4. Apply through the bilateral nomination process

**Note:** Many ICCR scholarships require candidates to study at specific institutions or specific subjects based on the bilateral agreement between India and the host country.

## J.N. Tata Endowment Loan Scholarship

**Offered by:** J.N. Tata Endowment, Bombay House, Mumbai (Part of Tata Group philanthropy)

**Type:** Loan scholarship (must be repaid, but at very low/no interest)

**Award amount:** ₹4–10 lakh as interest-free loan grant + potentially larger loan component for top universities

**Eligibility:**
- Indian citizen; applying to study abroad for higher education
- Strong academic record; first class in all qualifying exams
- Financial need preferred but not exclusive
- Preference given to those with admission to top-100 global universities

**Application process:**
1. Apply online at jntataendowment.org
2. Submit application with academic records + admission letter + financial details
3. Shortlisted candidates interviewed
4. Awards announced annually

**Deadline:** Approximately April–May each year
**Website:** jntataendowment.org

## Rajiv Gandhi National Fellowship (RGNF)

**Offered by:** University Grants Commission (UGC), Government of India

**For:** SC and ST students pursuing PhD in Indian universities or universities with international collaboration

**Award:** 
- Junior Research Fellow (JRF) months 1–24: ₹31,000/month
- Senior Research Fellow (SRF) months 25 onwards: ₹35,000/month
- HRA (House Rent Allowance) as per norms
- Contingency grant: ₹10,000–20,500/year

**Note:** While primarily for India-based PhD, some UGC-recognized programs with international universities qualify. This is not specifically "study abroad" but relevant for international research collaborations.

**Apply:** ugc.ac.in/rgnf
**Deadline:** Typically once a year through UGC

## Prime Minister's Research Fellowship (PMRF)

**For:** Top IIT/IISc BTech/MSc graduates to pursue PhD at premier Indian institutions with international research collaboration.

**Award:**
- Stipend: ₹70,000–80,000/month (one of the highest in India)
- Research grant: ₹2 lakh/year for travel and conferences including international
- Enables international collaboration and conference attendance

**Eligibility:** 
- Graduates from IITs/NITs/IISc with CGPA 8.0+
- Direct doctoral admissions at IITs/IISc
**Apply:** pmrf.in

## Kishore Vaigyanik Protsahan Yojana (KVPY)

## USIEF (US-India Educational Foundation) — Fulbright-Nehru

**Joint India-USA government scholarship:**

**Fulbright-Nehru Master's Fellowship:**
- Fully funded 2-year master's in USA
- Approximately 85–100 awards to Indians per year
- Apply: usief.org.in
- Deadline: July 15

**Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowship:**
- 6–9 months of research in the USA for PhD students enrolled in Indian universities
- Around 25–30 awards per year

**Fulbright-Nehru Postdoctoral Research Fellowship:**
- For postdoctoral researchers
- 6–9 months in USA

**Humphrey Fellowship Program:**
- For mid-career professionals (not degree-seeking students)
- Professional development program at US universities

All Fulbright-Nehru programs: usief.org.in

## Application Strategy for Indian Students

### Timing
Most Indian government scholarship deadlines: March–June (for September entry the following year)
Start researching 12–18 months before your intended program start.

### Multiple Applications
Apply to as many scholarships as you qualify for simultaneously. There is no penalty for applying to multiple scholarships; you simply decline the ones you don't need if you win multiple.

### Documentation Tips
- Income certificate from state government (required for NOS and state scholarships)
- Caste certificate (for category-specific scholarships)
- Academic transcripts (6 months before applying, get official transcripts issued)
- Admission letter from foreign university (most required before disbursement; some allow applying with conditional admission)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I apply for NOS and Chevening simultaneously?**
A: Yes. Apply to both. If you win both, you choose the more valuable one. NOS is India-government funded; Chevening is UK-government funded. They do not conflict.

**Q: The NOS deadline passed. Can I apply next year?**
A: Yes — NOS applications are annual. If you miss this year's deadline, apply next year. Use this year to strengthen your application (improve IELTS, secure a stronger university admission, update all documents).

**Q: Are these scholarships taxable in India?**
A: Scholarships received from foreign governments (Chevening, Fulbright, DAAD) are generally exempt from Indian income tax under Section 10(16) of the Income Tax Act (scholarships for education). However, consult a tax advisor for amounts above standard exemptions.
