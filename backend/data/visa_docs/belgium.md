# Studying in Belgium — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Belgium?

Belgium is a small but scientifically and culturally rich country in the heart of Europe. It offers world-class universities, instruction in French, Dutch (Flemish), and English, and access to EU/Schengen zone travel. Being at the heart of the EU with NATO and European Commission headquarters makes Brussels particularly attractive for politics, law, and international relations students.

**Key facts:**
- Population: 11.6 million; Capital: Brussels
- Languages: Dutch (Flanders), French (Wallonia), German (small eastern region)
- Currency: Euro (€)
- Schengen Area: Yes — single visa covers 26+ countries

## Belgian Student Visa — Type D (Long Stay)

### Who Needs This Visa?

All Indian students attending programs of more than 90 days in Belgium.

### Required Documents

1. Valid passport (minimum 12 months validity beyond program end)
2. Completed visa application form (available at Belgium Embassy website)
3. Admission/enrollment letter from Belgian university
4. Financial proof:
   - Minimum €1,000/month (approximately €10,000–12,000 for one academic year)
   - Bank statements of last 3–6 months
   - Scholarship letter (if applicable)
5. Accommodation proof in Belgium (university housing confirmation, rental contract, or host family letter)
6. Health insurance valid in Belgium (minimum coverage €30,000)
7. Clean criminal record (Police Clearance Certificate from India)
8. 2 passport photos
9. Proof of medical fitness (some cases)
10. Proof of payment of visa fee

### Visa Fee: €180 (Type D long-stay visa)

## Post-Arrival Requirements

### University Enrollment Registration

On arrival, you must **register with the Belgian commune (local municipality)** where you're living within 8 days of arriving. This is mandatory under Belgian law.

**Registration process:**
1. Go to the local commune's administrative office with:
   - Passport
   - Enrollment letter from university
   - Proof of accommodation in Belgium
2. Receive a "certificate of registration" (certificaat van inschrijving)
3. A commune official may visit your accommodation to verify your address

### Belgian Identity Card (Annex 15)
After commune registration, international students receive an Annex 15 document — your residence authorization for the period of study. This is your identity document in Belgium (not the visa itself).

### Health Insurance
Belgium has a mandatory health insurance system. Students register with a health insurance fund (Ziekenfonds/Mutualité):
- Christian Mutualities, Neutral Sickness Funds, Socialist Mutuality
- Monthly student rate: approximately €35–80/month after registration
- Registration at university's student services or directly at a health insurance office
- Alternative: EU students use EHIC; non-EU students need local insurance

## Work Rights

Belgian student visa (Type D for study) allows:
- Work maximum **20 hours per week** during academic semester
- Full-time during university holidays
- On-campus jobs available at most universities
- Work permit: Not needed for part-time work on student visa; you work on the student residence authorization

**Salary:** Belgian minimum wage is approximately €12.99/hour (2024). Part-time income can meaningfully supplement your budget.

---

## Post-Study Options

Belgium does not have a dedicated "post-study work" visa like the UK or Australia. Options after graduation:

1. **Job offer in Belgium:** Apply for a work permit (Arbeidskaart/Permis de Travail) with employer sponsorship
2. **Blue Card (EU Blue Card):** If your salary meets the threshold (approximately €48,000–57,000/year) — valid for 4 years
3. **Self-employment:** Apply for a self-employed residence permit
4. **Job search permit:** Some EU countries offer job-seeker visas; Belgium has a limited option — check latest rules

**Connecting to Schengen:** Being based in Belgium makes it easy to apply for jobs across Germany, Netherlands, France, Luxembourg — all accessible by train.

## Scholarships for Indian Students in Belgium

**VLIR-UOS Scholarships (Flemish Interuniversity Council):**
- For students from developing countries (India eligible)
- Covers tuition, living allowance, flights, insurance
- For specific master's programs at Flemish universities (KU Leuven, Ghent, Antwerp, VUB)
- Deadline: January–February each year
- Apply at: vliruos.be/scholarships

**KU Leuven Master Mind Scholarship:**
- €10,000 per year for exceptional international master's students at KU Leuven
- Awarded at admission based on academic profile

**WBI (Wallonia-Brussels International) Scholarships:**
- For French-speaking programs at ULB, UCLouvain, University of Liège
- Several programs with varying funding levels
- Apply at: wbi.be

## FAQ

**Q: Can I study in Belgium in English without knowing French or Dutch?**
A: Yes. KU Leuven, VUB, Ghent, University of Antwerp, and ULB all offer English-medium master's and some bachelor's programs. Check the university's course catalog for "English" as the instruction language. However, learning basic Dutch or French for daily life in Belgium is very helpful.

**Q: Is Belgium safe for Indian students?**
A: Belgium is generally safe. Brussels has some neighborhoods with higher crime rates — stick to well-lit, busy areas at night. Overall, Indian students find Belgium welcoming and comfortable.

**Q: How is Belgium for job prospects after graduation?**
A: Brussels is an international hub with EU institutions, NATO, multinational companies, and think tanks. There are good opportunities in policy, consulting, tech, and finance — particularly for those with EU policy knowledge. Work permits require employer sponsorship for non-EU students.
