# Studying in Hong Kong — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Hong Kong?

Hong Kong is Asia's world city — a global financial hub with excellent universities, a unique blend of Eastern and Western culture, and an English-speaking environment. For Indian students, it offers:
- Top-ranked global universities (HKU, HKUST, CUHK are consistently in the global top 100)
- English as the primary medium of instruction at major universities
- Strong finance, business, and technology job markets
- Asia-Pacific location ideal for those targeting careers in the region
- Cultural familiarity — large Indian diaspora (approximately 40,000 Indians in HK)
- No separate entry clearance needed for UK passport holders (and simplified process for Indian students)

**Key facts:**
- Special Administrative Region (SAR) of China
- Currency: Hong Kong Dollar (HKD); USD 1 ≈ HKD 7.8 (pegged)
- Languages: Cantonese (official), English (official), Mandarin widely used
- Very safe city (ranked among world's top 10 for safety)

## Student Visa Process — "No Objection to Return" via University

Unlike most countries, Hong Kong does NOT issue a separate student visa before you arrive. Instead, the university sponsors your entry permit through the Hong Kong Immigration Department.

### Process Overview

1. **Apply to and receive admission from a Hong Kong university**
2. **University submits your details to the HKID (Immigration Department) on your behalf**
3. **HKID issues an "Approval-in-Principle" letter to the university** (within 4–8 weeks)
4. **University sends you the Approval-in-Principle letter**
5. **Use this letter to apply for a formal Student Visa/Entry Permit** at the Hong Kong SAR Government Immigration Department, Beijing/Guangzhou OR by submitting a form and documents from India
6. **Alternatively:** Many Indian students enter HK on a Visit Visa first (up to 14 days for Indian passport holders initially), then the university completes the entry permit conversion in HK

**Important:** The Immigration Department accepts applications for conversion of visitor status to student status from within Hong Kong at: Immigration Tower, 7 Gloucester Road, Wan Chai, Hong Kong.

---

## Required Documents for Student Entry Permit

1. Completed form ID 995A (Application for Entry for Study)
2. Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond end of course)
3. Admission confirmation/official offer from Hong Kong university
4. Signed undertaking by the educational institution (provided by university)
5. Financial proof:
   - Minimum HKD $200,000 (approximately $25,600) for a 2-year program — verify current requirement
   - Bank statements showing sufficient funds for tuition + living expenses
   - Scholarship confirmation letter (if applicable)
6. Recent passport photo (4cm × 3cm)
7. Copy of academic qualifications (Class 12, bachelor's degree, transcripts)

**No IELTS/English test required** for the entry permit application itself (universities set their own English requirements for admission)

## Scholarships

### HK PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS) — Most Prestigious
- Offered by: Research Grants Council of Hong Kong
- Award: **HKD $28,100/month** stipend + **HKD $14,000** conference/travel grant
- Duration: Up to 4 years
- For: Full-time PhD applicants from anywhere in the world, any nationality
- Competition: Extremely high — approximately 250–300 awards from thousands of applicants globally
- Deadline: December 1 each year
- Apply at: hkphd.hk
- HKD $28,100/month = approximately $3,600/month = approximately ₹3,00,000/month

This is one of the best PhD fellowships in Asia for Indian students — stipend significantly higher than Europe.

### HKUST Postgraduate Studentship
- For MPhil and PhD students at HKUST
- Covers full tuition + HKD $18,000/month stipend (approximately ₹1,92,000/month)
- Conditional on satisfactory academic performance

### HKU Postgraduate Scholarship
- For PhD students at HKU
- HKD $18,000–20,000/month stipend
- Full tuition waiver

**Note:** University halls are heavily subsidized and should be applied for immediately on acceptance. Private accommodation is very expensive due to limited space.

## Work Rights

- Full-time students can work **maximum 20 hours/week** (on-campus jobs)
- Off-campus work requires application for a variation of conditions from Immigration Department
- PhD students with stipend can work as research/teaching assistants as part of their stipend agreement

---

## Post-Study: Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS)

After graduation, Indian students with strong qualifications can apply for the Hong Kong Quality Migrant Admission Scheme — a points-based system for highly skilled migrants.

**Post-graduation options:**
- **Technology Talent Admission Scheme (TechTAS):** Fast-track for tech talent at eligible companies
- **QMAS:** Points-based; no job offer needed; leads to permanent residence after 7 years
- **Employment visa:** With a job offer from HK employer; employer applies on your behalf

## FAQ

**Q: Is the medium of instruction in English at HK universities?**
A: Yes, at all the major universities (HKU, HKUST, CUHK, PolyU, CityU), English is the primary medium of instruction. Some courses may have Chinese (Cantonese or Mandarin) components, particularly in humanities and business.

**Q: How competitive is HKU/HKUST for Indian students?**
A: Very competitive at undergraduate level. For postgraduate (master's/PhD), competition is somewhat less intense than US top-10 but still strong. CGPA 8.0+ (out of 10) and IELTS 7.0+ are typically expected for competitive programs.

**Q: Can I get permanent residence in Hong Kong after studying?**
A: To qualify for permanent HK residency (Right of Abode), you must ordinarily reside in Hong Kong continuously for 7 years. Students on a student permit don't accrue this residency automatically — you'd need to convert to an employment/QMAS visa and live and work in HK for 7 years.
