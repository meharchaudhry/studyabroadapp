# Studying in Norway — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Norway?

Norway is one of the world's wealthiest nations with an extraordinary quality of life, pristine natural environment, and — uniquely — **free tuition at public universities** for international students. This makes it one of the most underrated study destinations for Indian students.

**Key attractions:**
- Public universities charge no tuition fees for all international students (including Indians)
- Ranked among the world's top countries for happiness, quality of life, and gender equality
- Strong industries: oil/gas, maritime, renewable energy, fisheries, technology
- English widely spoken (most Norwegians speak fluent English)
- Safe, outdoor-oriented lifestyle with spectacular natural scenery (fjords, Northern Lights, midnight sun)
- Universities in or near Oslo: University of Oslo (QS Top 200), Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) ranked Top 400

**Key facts:**
- Capital: Oslo; Population: 5.5 million
- Currency: Norwegian Krone (NOK); USD 1 ≈ NOK 11
- NOT EU member (but part of EEA and Schengen)
- High cost of living — among the world's most expensive countries

---

## Free Tuition — Important Caveat

Public Norwegian universities charge **no tuition fees** for all students (domestic and international), from bachelor's to PhD level. This policy has been Norwegian tradition for decades.

**However:**
- Students must pay a **semester fee** of approximately NOK 300–600 (€26–52) per semester — this funds student services, student union, welfare services
- Some programs (MBA, international master's programs designed for international market) may charge fees; check specifically
- The free tuition applies to Norwegian-medium programs and English-medium programs at public universities

**Private universities:** Charge tuition; BI Norwegian Business School (top business school) charges NOK 60,000–100,000/year ($5,500–9,100)

## Study Permit for Norway

Indian students need a **residence permit for studies** from the Norwegian Directorate of Immigration (UDI).

### Process for Indian Students

1. Receive admission offer from a Norwegian educational institution
2. Apply for residence permit ONLINE at udi.no (not at the embassy first)
3. Pay the application fee: **NOK 5,300** (approximately $480)
4. Submit biometrics and documents at the Norwegian Embassy in New Delhi
5. Decision made by UDI; processing 2–4 months for study permits

### Norwegian Embassy New Delhi
Strandveien 12 / Shantipath, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi 110021
(Appointments booked through udi.no and confirmed by embassy)

### Required Documents

1. Completed online application at udi.no
2. Valid passport (valid throughout your entire study period)
3. **Admission letter** from Norwegian university confirming enrollment
4. **Proof of financial means:**
   - NOK 128,887 per year (2024 requirement, updated annually) ≈ $11,700/year
   - Can be: personal savings, scholarship, education loan
   - Bank statements (3–6 months history)
5. **Proof of payment:** First year's living costs available (scholarship letter or bank balance)
6. Passport photos (35mm × 45mm, white background)
7. Documentation of accommodation (university housing confirmation or rental agreement)

### No Interview Required
Norwegian study permit applications are purely document-based — no personal interview at the embassy.

## Cost of Living

Norway is **one of the most expensive countries in the world**. Free tuition is offset by high living costs.

### Oslo (Most Expensive)

| Expense | Monthly Cost (NOK) | Approx USD |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| SiO (Student housing) | NOK 5,000–8,500 | $455–773 |
| Private shared flat | NOK 9,000–13,000 | $818–1,182 |
| Food (cooking at home) | NOK 3,500–5,000 | $318–455 |
| Transport (Ruter monthly pass) | NOK 400–500 (student) | $36–45 |
| Health insurance (not mandatory but recommended) | NOK 0 (see below) | — |
| Phone | NOK 200–400 | $18–36 |
| Miscellaneous | NOK 1,000–2,000 | $91–182 |
| **Total** | **NOK 14,100–24,400** | **$1,282–2,218/month** |

### SiO (Student Welfare Organization of Oslo)
SiO manages student housing in Oslo. This is the most affordable option — apply immediately upon acceptance:
- SiO Kringsjå: Studio/shared room from NOK 5,000–6,800/month
- Apply at sio.no well in advance (high demand; apply as soon as you receive admission)

### Trondheim, Bergen (Slightly Cheaper)
- Rent approximately 15–20% less than Oslo
- Sjøstrand student housing, BO KA student housing: NOK 4,000–7,000/month

## Healthcare

Norway has a universal public healthcare system. International students with a valid residence permit:
- Register with a GP (fastlege) at helsenorge.no
- Doctor visits: Free or very low copay (NOK 150–200 per consultation)
- Hospital care: Free
- Prescriptions: Subsidized at pharmacies
- Mental health: Available through GP referral

**Mandatory student fee includes** access to SiO Helse (student health clinics) which are free or very low cost.

## Work Rights

Indian students with a Norwegian study permit can work:
- Maximum **20 hours per week** during semester
- Full-time during official holidays (summer, Christmas)
- **Important:** Norway has very high minimum wages — approximately NOK 180–200/hour (approximately $16–18/hour) for unskilled work; skilled work pays more
- Working 20 hours at Norwegian wages: approximately NOK 14,000–16,000/month — this significantly supplements living costs

Finding work: Norwegian (Bokmål) language skills help significantly; English-only workers face limited options outside Oslo and university towns.

## Norwegian Language Learning

Most bachelor's programs at Norwegian public universities are taught in Norwegian. Master's programs are available in English at most universities.

For Norwegian-medium programs:
- **Norwegian Language Course:** Required for most students; most universities offer intensive Norwegian courses before semester
- **Levels:** NOR 0 (beginner) → A1 → A2 → B1 (sufficient for bachelor's studies) → B2 → C1/C2
- Resources: Norskkurs.no, NorskStart, Duolingo Norwegian, Språkporten (University of Bergen online Norwegian)

**For English-medium programs:** Norwegian not required but learning basic phrases is appreciated.

## Post-Study Options

After completing your degree, Indian students can:
- Apply for a **job seeker visa/residence permit extension** for up to 1 year to seek employment
- If employed in Norway: Apply for a skilled worker permit
- After 7 years of lawful continuous residence: Eligible for permanent residence
- **Nordic countries** are increasingly welcoming skilled tech workers — Norway's oil industry, tech sector, and maritime industry actively hire international graduates

## Scholarships

**Norwegian Government Scholarships (via Quota Scheme):**
- Historically available for students from developing countries (India included)
- Covers living expenses + travel; tuition already free
- Check with University of Oslo or NTNU international offices for current availability

**NORPART Program:**
- Norwegian-Indian academic partnerships enabling exchange and joint degrees
- Check with your Indian university for NORPART collaborations with Norwegian institutions

## FAQ

**Q: Is it true Norwegian public universities are completely free?**
A: Yes — tuition is free at public Norwegian universities for all students including Indians. You only pay a semester fee of approximately NOK 300–600 per semester. Living costs, however, are very high.

**Q: What English-medium programs are available at Norwegian public universities?**
A: NTNU offers many English-medium master's programs (engineering, natural sciences, humanities). UiO has English-medium master's in many fields. Check each university's graduate catalog filtered by "English" for instruction language.

**Q: How cold is Norway? Is it manageable?**
A: Norway is cold, especially outside Oslo (coastal) — in Tromsø, it's Arctic. Oslo winters: -5°C to -10°C (January). Trondheim: -7°C to -12°C. Bergen: Milder due to coastal location (2°C to 7°C in winter) but very rainy. Indian students adapt with proper winter clothing. The Northern Lights, skiing, and fjord experiences make it worthwhile for many.
