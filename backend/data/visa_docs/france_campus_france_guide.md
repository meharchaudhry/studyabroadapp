# France Campus France Application Guide for Indian Students

## What is Campus France?

Campus France is the French national agency for promoting French higher education abroad and managing international student mobility. Indian students applying to French universities must go through the **Campus France process** — you cannot apply directly to French universities without Campus France registration.

**Campus France India office:** Located in Delhi and Mumbai (Alliance Française offices)

---

## Why Study in France?

- Prestigious universities: Sciences Po (political science), HEC Paris (business), École Polytechnique, Sorbonne, INSEAD
- France is the 3rd most popular study destination in the world
- French government scholarships (Eiffel Excellence) — fully funded
- Tuition at public universities: Very low (€170–400/year for EU students; international students pay more but still reasonable)
- 2-year post-study residence permit available after graduation
- French language globally useful (official language of 29 countries, second most learned language globally)
- Rich culture, cuisine, arts; excellent travel access within Schengen zone

## Step-by-Step Campus France Process

### Step 1: Create Campus France Account

1. Go to **inde.campusfrance.org** (the India portal)
2. Click "Create an account"
3. Fill in your personal and academic details
4. Your account is your central hub for the entire application process

### Step 2: Choose Your Programs and Universities

**Search at:** campusfrance.org/en or via your Campus France India account

Key search filters:
- Level: bachelor's (licence), master's (master), PhD (doctorat)
- Field of study
- Language of instruction (English or French)
- Region/city

**Application Portals for French Universities:**
- **Parcoursup:** French centralized portal for bachelor's level (licence) — like UCAS for France
- **Mon Master:** Centralized portal for master's applications (launched 2023)
- **Directly to university:** PhD applications are always direct to the laboratory/supervisor

### Step 3: Request Campus France Interview

After creating your account and expressing interest in programs:
1. Complete your Campus France online dossier (profile)
2. Request an appointment for a Campus France interview at a Campus France India office
3. Locations: New Delhi (Alliance Française de Delhi), Mumbai (Alliance Française de Mumbai), Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Pune (all Alliance Française branches)

**Interview purpose:** Campus France officer reviews your project (academic motivation + program choices + career plan); ensures your application is coherent; helps you finalize your program list.

**Interview duration:** Approximately 20–30 minutes; in English (not French required)

### Step 4: Campus France Validation

After a successful interview and dossier review:
- You receive a **"No Objection Certificate" (NOC) / Campus France Validation** — your applications to French universities are registered and validated
- This is required before applying for the French student visa

### Step 5: Apply to French Universities

With Campus France validation:
- Apply on Parcoursup (for licence/bachelor's)
- Apply on Mon Master (for master's — most relevant for Indian students)
- Apply directly (for PhD or Grandes Écoles like HEC, Sciences Po, Polytechnique which have their own selective admissions)

**Application timeline:**
- Mon Master: Application window January–April for September entry
- Parcoursup: January–March for September entry
- Grande École: December–February (varies; extremely competitive)

## French University System

### Grandes Écoles vs. Universities

**Grandes Écoles:**
- Elite specialized schools for engineering, business, political science
- Separate from the university system; selective admission
- Examples: HEC Paris, ESSEC, ESCP, Sciences Po, École Polytechnique, CentraleSupélec, INSA
- Tuition: €10,000–40,000/year (private) or subsidized (Polytechnique)
- Highly competitive; require exceptional academic record + entrance exam or application

**Universities (Public):**
- Large public research universities
- Lower tuition: €2,770–3,770/year for non-EU students (2024; set by the government)
- Broad subject coverage: Sorbonne, Paris-Saclay, Aix-Marseille, Lyon, Bordeaux, Toulouse, Strasbourg, Montpellier, Nice
- Less selective than Grandes Écoles

**Université de Technologie (IUT/UT):**
- Technology-focused institutions: UTT, UTC, UTBM, INSA (series)
- Engineering programs; highly practical
- Very good for engineering master's; recognized globally

## Documents Required for Campus France Dossier

1. Completed online Campus France application form
2. Academic documents:
   - Class 10 marksheet and certificate
   - Class 12 marksheet and certificate
   - Bachelor's degree (if applying for master's)
   - All semester/year marksheets
   - Official transcripts (English language)
3. **Motivation letter (Statement of Purpose):** Specific to Campus France — they assess your motivation for France specifically + program choice + career plan
4. CV/Resume
5. Passport copy
6. Language test:
   - For French-medium programs: DELF B2 or DALF C1
   - For English-medium programs: IELTS 6.0–7.0 or TOEFL equivalent
7. Recommendation letters (2 typically)
8. Proof of address

## French Student Visa (VISA D — Long Stay for Studies)

After receiving university admission AND Campus France validation:

### Application Process

Apply at **VFS Global France** in India (locations: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Pune, Ahmedabad, Chandigarh, Jaipur, Lucknow, etc.)

**Documents:**
1. Visa application form (completed online at france-visas.gouv.fr)
2. Passport (minimum 3 months validity beyond program end)
3. Campus France validation certificate
4. French university acceptance letter
5. Proof of accommodation in France (CROUS hall confirmation or host family letter or rental contract)
6. Financial proof:
   - Approximately €615/month (minimum subsistence); better to show €800–1,000/month
   - For 10-month academic year: €6,150–10,000
   - Bank statements, scholarship letter, parents' financial guarantee
7. Health insurance (valid in France; European health insurance or French CPAM enrollment)
8. Recent photos

**Visa fee:** €99

**Processing:** 15 working days typically

## Post-Arrival: OFII and CPAM Registration

### OFII (Office Français de l'Immigration et de l'Intégration)

If you receive a **VLS-TS** (Visa Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour — Long-stay visa serving as residence permit), you must register with OFII within 3 months of arrival:

1. Go to **administration.gouv.fr/ofii**
2. Complete online registration form
3. OFII sends you an appointment for a medical visit + administrative validation
4. At OFII appointment: Receive Sticker in passport confirming your stay is regularized

**This is mandatory.** Without OFII validation, your long-stay visa is not fully legal.

### CPAM (Health Insurance) Registration

France's national health insurance:
1. Register at **ameli.fr** or through your university's student health office
2. Students under 28: Auto-affiliated through university
3. Receive Attestation Sécurité Sociale (health insurance certificate) in 2–4 weeks
4. Carte Vitale (green health card): Apply once registered; takes 4–8 weeks
5. The Carte Vitale is used at pharmacies and doctors for automatic insurance reimbursement

### CAF (Housing Benefit — Caisse d'Allocations Familiales)

Apply immediately at **caf.fr**:
- Students living in eligible accommodation (CROUS halls, private flats meeting criteria) receive APL (Aide Personnalisée au Logement) housing benefit
- Amount: €50–250/month depending on accommodation type, location, family situation
- Submit rent receipts + accommodation details
- Takes 1–3 months to start receiving; backdated to application date

## CROUS — Student Housing and Meals

CROUS (Centre Régional des Œuvres Universitaires et Scolaires) manages affordable student housing and cafeterias across France:

**CROUS residence:** Apply as early as possible (as soon as you have university admission) at trouverunlogement.lescrous.fr
- Cost: €250–600/month depending on city and room type
- Priority given to: scholarship recipients, lowest income students
- Application portal: **Mes Services Étudiants** (messervices.etudiant.gouv.fr)

**CROUS Cafeteria (Restaurant Universitaire — RU):**
- Hot meal for **€3.30** (2024 subsidized rate for all students, including international)
- Available at all French universities
- Significant cost saving: eating lunch at RU = €99/month vs €300+ eating out

## Post-Study Work: Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS)

After graduating from a French institution:
- Apply at your local préfecture for **APS (Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour)**
- Valid for 1 year initially; renewable for up to **2 years**
- Allows working (or looking for work) in France for any employer
- **Cost:** Approximately €200

**Pathway after APS:**
- Find employment in France → apply for long-stay visa / resident permit for employment
- After 5 years of continuous legal residence: Apply for French permanent residence (Carte de Résident — 10 years)
- After 5 years: French citizenship (one of fastest EU naturalizations for well-integrated residents)

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to learn French to study in France?**
A: For English-medium programs (many at Sciences Po, HEC, INSA, Université Paris-Saclay), no French required for study. For daily life in France (outside Paris), French is very helpful. For career opportunities in France, French B2 is practically necessary for most roles (English-speaking roles concentrated in Paris tech startups and multinationals).

**Q: Is French campus France mandatory even for PhD?**
A: PhD applications at French universities can be done directly without Campus France in some cases. However, for the visa application, having a Campus France validation is still typically required. Contact the French Embassy India for confirmation.

**Q: How competitive is Sciences Po / HEC Paris for Indian students?**
A: Highly competitive. Sciences Po admits approximately 12–15% of international applicants; HEC Paris top programs have 5–8% admission rates. Strong academic record (90%+ equivalent), IELTS 7.5+, exceptional motivation letter, and relevant extracurriculars are needed.
