# UAE Student Visa — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** Student Visa / Student Residence Visa  
**Issued by:** General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) — Dubai; Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security (ICP — formerly FAIC/EIDA) — Abu Dhabi and other Emirates; and Federal Immigration Authority (coordinated through the UAE's Federal Authority).  
**Processed by:** The sponsoring educational institution (free zone or mainland university) handles most of the student visa/residence permit processing.  
**Validity:** Typically 1 year, renewable for the duration of the study programme. Free Zone university student visas may differ slightly.

**Note:** The UAE has two main types of educational environments:
1. **Free Zone Universities** (e.g., Dubai Knowledge Park / Dubai International Academic City — DIAC): Most international universities operate here. The free zone authority issues the student visa.
2. **Mainland/Government Universities** (e.g., UAE University, Khalifa University, American University of Sharjah): Visa issued by GDRFA or ICP.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Be unconditionally admitted to an accredited university or higher education institution licensed by the UAE's Commission for Academic Accreditation (CAA) or a relevant free zone authority.
- Have a valid Indian passport.
- Demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover tuition and living expenses.
- Pass a medical fitness test in the UAE (conducted after arrival).
- Have no criminal record.
- Pass an Emirates ID biometric registration.
- Obtain medical insurance (mandatory in the UAE).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

The UAE is a moderately to highly expensive place to live, depending on the emirate.

- **Living costs (Dubai):** AED 3,000–6,000 per month (approximately INR 68,000–136,000/month).
- **Living costs (Sharjah/Ajman):** AED 2,000–4,000 per month.
- **Living costs (Abu Dhabi):** AED 3,500–7,000 per month.
- **Tuition fees:**
  - Major branch campuses and free zone universities: AED 30,000–90,000 per year (approximately INR 6.8–20.4 lakh/year).
  - Examples:
    - Manipal University Dubai: AED 35,000–55,000/year.
    - Amity University Dubai: AED 40,000–70,000/year.
    - University of Birmingham Dubai: AED 65,000–85,000/year.
    - Heriot-Watt University Dubai: AED 55,000–75,000/year.
    - Middlesex University Dubai: AED 45,000–65,000/year.
    - Canadian University Dubai: AED 40,000–60,000/year.
  - Government-funded UAE universities (for UAE nationals primarily): Heavily subsidised; international students typically pay higher rates.
- **Student visa processing fee:** Approximately AED 1,000–3,500 depending on the institution and type of visa (varies by emirate and institution type — most universities incorporate visa fees into their administrative charges).
- **Medical test:** AED 200–300 (conducted in the UAE).
- **Emirates ID:** AED 100 (initial application).
- **Health/medical insurance:** AED 600–1,500/year for basic student health insurance.

**Proof of funds:**
- Bank statements (personal or parental) showing adequate balance.
- Scholarship letter from UAE government or institution.
- Sponsored fee confirmation from employer (for working students with employer sponsorship).
- Study loan sanction letter from Indian bank.

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

Documents submitted to the institution for visa processing:
- Valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months validity from date of application; 2 blank pages).
- Passport-size photographs (white background, specific size as required by institution).
- Admission/enrollment letter from the UAE institution.
- Original academic documents:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's degree transcripts and degree certificate (for postgraduate applicants).
  - Documents must be attested (see attestation process below).
- Attested educational documents:
  - Attestation chain for Indian students: HRD (State education department) attestation → MEA (Ministry of External Affairs, India) apostille/attestation → UAE Embassy in India attestation → MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs, UAE) attestation (if required by the institution). **This is mandatory and is one of the most important steps.**
- English language test results (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or institution's own assessment — varies by institution).
- No Objection Certificate (NOC) from parent/guardian (for students under 18).
- Medical insurance confirmation.
- Passport copies (all pages including blank pages).
- Visa application form (provided by the institution).
- Visa processing fee payment.

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Research and choose an institution:** Verify the institution is accredited by the UAE Ministry of Education's Commission for Academic Accreditation (CAA) or licensed by KHDA (Knowledge and Human Development Authority) in Dubai, or relevant emirate authority.
2. **Apply for admission:** Submit application directly to the institution.
3. **Receive unconditional offer letter.**
4. **Document attestation:** Begin attestation of academic documents (this takes 4–8 weeks):
   - Get documents attested by State Education Department (HRD — Human Resource Development) in your home state.
   - Get MEA apostille from the Ministry of External Affairs India (apply at apostille.mea.gov.in or MEA front offices).
   - Get attestation from UAE Embassy in New Delhi or consulate (VFS Global handles UAE attestation in India).
5. **Submit attested documents to institution.**
6. **Institution initiates visa process:** The institution files for your student entry permit (visa) with GDRFA/ICP or free zone authority. You receive a visa approval number/entry permit.
7. **Enter UAE:** Travel to the UAE using the approved entry permit.
8. **Medical test in UAE:** Within the first few days, undergo a medical fitness test at a registered UAE health centre (blood test + chest X-ray for TB). Results come within 1–3 days.
9. **Emirates ID registration:** Biometrics (fingerprints + photograph) are taken at the ICP service centre or the institution. Emirates ID card issued within 5–10 working days.
10. **Receive Residence Visa stamp:** After medical clearance and Emirates ID registration, the institution's PRO (Public Relations Officer) completes the residence visa process. The visa stamp is placed in your passport.
11. **Medical insurance:** Ensure your health insurance is active throughout your stay.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Document attestation:** 4–8 weeks (can be expedited at extra cost).
- **Institution visa processing (entry permit):** 2–4 weeks.
- **Medical test + Emirates ID + Residence Visa:** 2–4 weeks after arrival.
- **Total lead time from application to arrival:** 2–4 months.
- **Apply to institutions:** Many UAE institutions have rolling admissions; major intakes are September and January/February.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- **On a student residence visa:** Students on a student visa in the UAE generally **cannot work** as a matter of right. The student visa does not automatically confer work authorisation.
- Some universities have specific arrangements for **campus jobs** or **internship programmes** facilitated through a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the institution.
- If a student wants to work part-time:
  - The employer must apply for a **work permit/labour card** through the UAE Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE).
  - A student's educational institution must issue an NOC.
  - Work permits for students are case-by-case and not automatic.
- **DIAC (Dubai International Academic City) free zone:** Students may get permission to work within the free zone or with specific employers.
- Practically, many students work informally or in family businesses — but formal employment requires the employer to arrange the work permit.
- **Important:** Working without the proper authorisation is a violation of UAE visa conditions and can result in deportation and blacklisting.

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **UAE does not have a formal post-study work visa** similar to the UK, Australia, or Canada.
- However, UAE has recently introduced progressive visa reforms:
  - **Golden Visa (10-year residence):** Outstanding students (top 5% of graduating class) from accredited UAE institutions may qualify for the UAE Golden Visa — a 10-year self-sponsored residence visa with the right to live and work freely.
  - **Green Visa (5-year self-sponsored):** Graduates who are self-employed or freelancers may apply for the 5-year Green Visa.
  - **Employment Visa:** Upon securing a job offer in the UAE, the employer sponsors a standard 2–3 year work residence visa.
- UAE is a hub for international business — many Indian graduates find employment in finance, technology, hospitality, healthcare, and retail sectors.
- **Mainland Employment:** Requires employer sponsorship and a Labour card (MOHRE registration).
- **Freelance Permit:** Available through DTMFZ (Dubai Media / Technology Free Zones) for skilled freelancers — enables self-employment in the UAE without a company sponsor.

## 9. Language Requirements

- **Medium of instruction:** Most international universities in the UAE teach in **English**. Arabic is generally not required for English-medium programs.
- **English proficiency requirements vary by institution:**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 5.5–6.5 (varies by institution and program) |
| TOEFL iBT | 60–79 |
| PTE Academic | 50–58 |
| EmSAT Achieve English | 1100–1250 (for UAE-based admissions tests) |

- Some UAE institutions conduct their own English language placement tests upon enrollment.
- Indian students from English-medium schools may be waived from standardised language tests by some institutions.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Mandatory health insurance:** In Dubai, health insurance is legally mandatory for all residents. Employers/institutions are required to provide coverage. Most UAE institutions include basic health insurance in their fees or assist students in obtaining it.
- **Medical fitness test:** Required for all residence visa applicants in the UAE — includes blood test (HIV, Hepatitis B/C screening), chest X-ray for TB. This must be conducted at an approved UAE health centre after arrival.
- **If found medically unfit:** Applicants with certain communicable diseases (e.g., HIV, active TB, Hepatitis B/C) are not granted residence visas and will be deported. Indian applicants should be aware of this strict policy.
- **Health insurance options for students:** Most UAE universities offer group health insurance plans for students. Basic plans cover outpatient and inpatient treatment. Cost: AED 600–1,500/year.
- **Daman (Abu Dhabi):** Abu Dhabi's mandatory health insurance scheme covers most services; students in Abu Dhabi typically receive coverage through their institution.

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **Indian community:** The UAE has one of the largest Indian expatriate communities in the world (approximately 3.5 million Indians, the largest expat group). Hindi, Malayalam, Tamil, Punjabi, and other Indian languages are widely spoken.
- **Document attestation is critical and time-consuming:** Start the attestation process as early as possible. Unattestatested documents will not be accepted by UAE institutions for visa processing.
- **Indian institutions in UAE:** Many well-known Indian institutions have campuses in the UAE — Manipal University Dubai, Amity University Dubai, LPU Dubai — these may be more affordable than Western branch campuses and offer familiarity.
- **Western branch campuses:** University of Birmingham Dubai, Middlesex University Dubai, Heriot-Watt University Dubai, NYU Abu Dhabi, and Sorbonne Abu Dhabi offer globally recognised degrees from their home institutions.
- **Alcohol and cultural norms:** The UAE has strict laws around alcohol (consumption in public is illegal; permitted only in licensed venues), public displays of affection, and dress codes. Adherence to local cultural norms is essential.
- **Tax-free income:** The UAE has no personal income tax, which makes post-graduation salaries very attractive for Indian students who secure employment.
- **Ramadan:** During the holy month of Ramadan, eating, drinking, and smoking in public during daylight hours is prohibited for everyone (including non-Muslims). University schedules may be adjusted.
- **Safety:** The UAE is one of the safest countries in the world — very low crime rates and highly efficient law enforcement.
- **Banking:** Open a bank account (Emirates NBD, Mashreq, FAB, ADIB, or international banks like HSBC, Standard Chartered) with your Emirates ID and residence visa. Many banks offer student-friendly accounts.
