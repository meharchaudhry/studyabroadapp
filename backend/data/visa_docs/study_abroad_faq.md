### Post-Study Work Visas
- **UK**: Graduate Visa — 2 years (3 for PhD) — apply after graduation
- **USA**: OPT — 12 months (STEM: +24 months = 36 months total) — must apply 90 days before graduation
- **Canada**: PGWP (Post-Graduation Work Permit) — up to 3 years based on program length
- **Australia**: Subclass 485 (Temporary Graduate Visa) — 2–4 years
- **Germany**: 18-month job-seeking visa after graduation
- **Netherlands**: Orientation Year Visa (Zoekjaar) — 1 year to find skilled employment
- **France**: APS (Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour) — 1 year after graduation
- **Sweden**: 1–2 year job-seeking permit after graduation
- **Ireland**: Stay Back Option — 2 years after bachelor's, 2 years after master's

## Section 2: Student Housing & Accommodation

### How to Find Student Accommodation Abroad

**University Halls / On-Campus Housing** (recommended for Year 1):
- Apply as early as possible — often within 2 weeks of receiving your offer
- Usually cheapest, bills included, no separate contract needed
- UK: most universities guarantee halls for Year 1 international students

**Private Student Accommodation (PBSA — Purpose-Built Student Accommodation)**:
- Companies: Unite Students (UK), iQ Student (UK), Scape (Australia/UK), The Student Hotel (Europe)

**Private Rentals (Flat/Room Share)**:
- Platforms by country:
  - **UK**: SpareRoom.co.uk, Rightmove.co.uk, Zoopla.co.uk, OnTheMarket.com
  - **Germany**: WG-Gesucht.de (Germany's biggest flatshare site), Immoscout24.de
  - **Netherlands**: Kamernet.nl, Pararius.com, HousingAnywhere.com
  - **France**: SeLoger.com, Leboncoin.fr, Immojeune.com, Studapart.com
  - **Australia**: Flatmates.com.au, Gumtree.com.au, Domain.com.au
  - **Canada**: Kijiji.ca, Craigslist (city-specific), Facebook Marketplace
  - **USA**: Craigslist, Zillow.com, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace
  - **Singapore**: PropertyGuru.com.sg, HousingAnywhere.com
  - **Japan**: Sakura House (foreigners-friendly), GaijinPot Housing
  - **Ireland**: Daft.ie, Rent.ie

### Tips for Indian Students on Finding Housing
1. Apply early — start looking 3–4 months before arrival
2. NEVER pay upfront without viewing (at minimum a video call)
3. Ask about bills — some rentals exclude electricity, internet, water
4. Check for a guarantor requirement (parents in India can usually provide a signed letter)
5. Student Facebook groups for your university city are excellent for finding flatmates
6. WhatsApp groups specific to Indian students at UK/German/Australian universities are very active

## Section 4: Scholarships Overview

### Top Scholarships for Indian Students Going Abroad
- **UK**: Chevening Scholarship (full funding, highly competitive), Commonwealth Scholarships, GREAT Scholarships
- **USA**: Fulbright-Nehru (for research/PhD), university merit scholarships
- **Germany**: DAAD (Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst) — covers tuition + €850–€1,200/month stipend
- **Canada**: Vanier CGS (for PhD), university awards
- **Australia**: Australia Awards Scholarships (covers full tuition + living), university merit
- **Netherlands**: Holland Scholarship (€5,000 one-time), Orange Knowledge Programme
- **Sweden**: Swedish Institute Scholarships (full funding for master's programs)
- **Switzerland**: Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships — CHF 1,920–3,500/month + accommodation
- **France**: Eiffel Excellence Scholarship — €1,181–1,400/month + return flight + accommodation
- **Japan**: MEXT Scholarship — JPY 143,000/month + tuition waiver
- **South Korea**: GKS (Global Korea Scholarship) — full tuition + KRW 900,000/month

**Portal for all scholarships**: [scholars4dev.com](https://www.scholars4dev.com) | [opportunitydesk.org](https://opportunitydesk.org) | [daad.de](https://www.daad.de/en/)

## Section 5: Statement of Purpose (SOP) & Recommendations

### SOP Key Points
- Length: 500–1,000 words (1–2 pages) — check university guidelines
- Structure: Why this field → Why this university/country → Your background → Future goals
- Avoid clichés: "Since childhood I have been fascinated by…"
- Tailor each SOP to the specific program and professor/research group

### Letters of Recommendation (LOR)
- Typically 2–3 required
- At least 1 from a professor who knows your academic work
- 1 from a professional/employer (for work experience)
- Each should address: academic ability, research potential, specific examples of your work
- Allow professors 4–6 weeks to write LORs
