# Canada Study Permit — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Overview

**Permit Type:** Study Permit (not a visa — India is visa-exempt for the permit itself but needs a Temporary Resident Visa or eTA depending on travel method)  
**Issued by:** Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)  
**Key Program:** Student Direct Stream (SDS) — faster processing (20 days target) for Indian students  
**Validity:** Tied to program length + 90 days grace period  
**Canada is NOT a Schengen country** — separate permits required for each entry.

Canada is the #1 destination for Indian students globally (2024). Over 320,000 Indian students study in Canada. Top universities: University of Toronto, UBC, McGill, Waterloo, McMaster, York, Western, Queen's, Dalhousie.

---

## 2. Student Direct Stream (SDS) — Indian Students' Fast Track

Indian students can use the **Student Direct Stream (SDS)** for faster processing (target: 20 business days):

**SDS Requirements:**
- Have a letter of acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
- Live outside Canada when applying
- Have a Quebec Acceptance Certificate (CAQ) if studying in Quebec
- Have a Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) of CAD 20,635 from an approved Canadian financial institution
- Have paid the first year of tuition in full (or have a scholarship covering it)
- Have an upfront medical exam completed before applying
- IELTS Academic: Overall 6.0, with no band below 6.0 (mandatory for SDS)
- No criminal record (PCC required)

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

As of January 2024 (major update — increased from CAD 10,000):

- **Living expenses:** CAD 20,635/year for the main applicant
- **First-year tuition:** Must be paid in full or covered by scholarship
- **For accompanying spouse:** Additional CAD 20,635/year
- **For each accompanying child:** Additional CAD 3,799/year (approximate)

**Tuition fees at Canadian universities:**
- Undergraduate: CAD 25,000–45,000/year
- Master's programs: CAD 15,000–35,000/year
- MBA: CAD 30,000–100,000+/year
- Colleges (diploma): CAD 12,000–20,000/year

**Living costs by city:**
- Toronto, Vancouver: CAD 1,800–2,500/month
- Montreal, Ottawa: CAD 1,200–1,800/month
- Smaller cities (Halifax, Saskatoon): CAD 900–1,300/month

**GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate):**
- Must be purchased from: CIBC, Scotiabank, or TD through their international student GIC programs
- Amount: CAD 20,635 minimum
- Process: Apply online → transfer funds from India via wire → receive confirmation letter
- On arrival in Canada: Activate at a branch; funds released in monthly installments
- The GIC money is YOURS — it's held as proof of financial capacity and then returned to you monthly

## 4. IELTS Requirements for Canada

### For SDS (Student Direct Stream):
- **IELTS Academic: overall 6.0 minimum, no band below 6.0** (mandatory)
- No TOEFL or PTE accepted for SDS — must be IELTS

### For Regular Stream (non-SDS):
- IELTS requirements set by individual universities
- Typical: 6.5 overall (6.0 no band below) for universities
- Colleges may accept 6.0 overall

## 5. Required Documents

1. Valid passport (minimum 1 year validity beyond program end date)
2. Letter of Acceptance from a Canadian DLI
3. GIC confirmation letter (CAD 20,635 minimum)
4. Proof of first-year tuition payment (or scholarship letter)
5. IELTS Academic result (6.0+ for SDS; no band below 6.0)
6. Upfront medical exam (IME — from IRCC-designated physician in India)
7. Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — apostilled not required for Canada
8. Digital photo meeting IRCC specifications
9. Bank statements (last 6 months) showing consistent balances
10. Parental financial statements if sponsored (3 months bank statements + income proof)
11. Study plan (1–2 page document explaining why you chose Canada and this program)
12. Visa fee: CAD 150 (study permit) + CAD 85 (biometrics) = CAD 235 total

## 6. Application Process

**Step 1 — Get acceptance from a Canadian DLI**
Apply to Canadian universities/colleges through their portals or through OUAC (Ontario) / direct portals.

**Step 2 — Prepare GIC**
Open GIC with CIBC, Scotiabank, or TD. Transfer CAD 20,635+ from India. Receive confirmation letter (5–10 business days).

**Step 3 — Complete medical exam (IME)**
Find IRCC-designated panel physician in India (list at ircc.canada.ca). Upfront IME results submitted directly to IRCC — you get an X-number receipt.

**Step 4 — Apply online via IRCC portal**
- Create account at ircc.canada.ca
- Complete online application form
- Upload all documents digitally
- Pay fees online (CAD 150 + CAD 85 biometrics)
- Submit biometrics at a VFS Global Canada Biometric Collection Service Point in India

**Step 5 — Processing**
- SDS: Target 20 business days (often 2–4 weeks in practice)
- Regular stream: 4–12 weeks

**Step 6 — Receive Letter of Introduction (LoI)**
If approved, you receive a Letter of Introduction (LoI) — NOT a stamp in your passport. The LoI is your authorization to travel.

**Step 7 — Arrive in Canada**
- Present LoI at the Canadian port of entry.
- Border Services Officer (CBSA) issues your actual Study Permit (stamped in passport or as a paper document).
- Activate your GIC at a Canadian bank branch within the first week.

## 7. Work Rights for Indian Students

**During studies (current rules as of 2024):**
- Off-campus work: Up to **20 hours per week** during regular academic sessions
- On-campus work: Unlimited hours (on university grounds — library jobs, teaching assistantships, etc.)
- During scheduled breaks (summer, winter): **Full-time (40 hours/week) unlimited**
- No separate work permit needed — work authorization is embedded in the Study Permit

**Note (2024 update):** As of November 2024, IRCC reinstated the 20-hour/week cap for off-campus work (it was temporarily lifted to unlimited during 2022–2023 due to labour shortages, then reinstated).

## 8. Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

One of Canada's biggest draws for Indian students:

| Program Length | PGWP Duration |
|---|---|
| Under 8 months | Not eligible |
| 8 months to under 2 years | Equal to program length |
| 2 years or more | 3 years (maximum) |

**Requirements:**
- Graduate from a full-time program at an eligible DLI
- Apply within 180 days of receiving final marks/transcript
- Must have a valid study permit when you graduated
- Valid PGWP means open work authorization — work anywhere in Canada, any job, any employer

**PGWP → Permanent Residence (PR):**
- PGWP + Canadian work experience → Express Entry (Canadian Experience Class — CEC)
- CEC draw minimum: typically 450–500 points (out of 1,200 Comprehensive Ranking System — CRS)
- Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): Many provinces have streams specifically for international student graduates — Ontario, BC, Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia all have active PNP streams

## 9. Quebec — Special Rules

Quebec has its own immigration system. Students going to Quebec need:
1. **Quebec Acceptance Certificate (CAQ)** — from Quebec's MIFI (Ministère de l'Immigration, de la Francisation et de l'Intégration)
2. **Study Permit** — from federal IRCC (same process, but CAQ must come first)

CAQ fee: CAD 118  
CAQ processing: 4–6 weeks  
Major Quebec universities: McGill University, Université de Montréal, Université Laval, Concordia University, UQAM

**Note:** French is required for most programs at Université de Montréal, Laval, and UQAM. McGill and Concordia are primarily English-medium.

## 10. Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Insufficient financial proof** — bank balance does not convincingly cover tuition + GIC + living.
2. **Weak study plan** — vague explanation for choosing Canada/the program.
3. **Ties to India not established** — no convincing reason to return to India after studies.
4. **IELTS below SDS minimum** — for SDS, any band below 6.0 disqualifies.
5. **Medical exam not completed upfront** — SDS requires upfront IME.
6. **GIC from unapproved institution** — must be from IRCC-listed providers (CIBC, Scotiabank, TD).
7. **Incomplete documents** — missing signatures, expired documents, inconsistent dates.

## 11. Indian-Student Specific Tips

- **SDS is significantly faster** — if you meet the IELTS 6.0/no-band-below-6.0 requirement, always use SDS.
- **Express Entry CRS scores:** Canadian work experience gained on PGWP adds substantial CRS points. Most Indian graduates in STEM/finance/healthcare are competitive for CEC within 1–2 years of working.
- **IELTS preparation is critical** — the SDS IELTS requirement is non-negotiable; even 5.5 in one band disqualifies you for SDS.
- **College vs University:** College diploma programs (1–2 years) are cheaper and faster but the PGWP duration is shorter. University degrees give 3-year PGWP. For PR, the longer the PGWP + Canadian work experience, the better.
- **Cap announcement (2024):** Canada announced a cap on new study permit approvals. Competition has increased — apply early, have strong documents.
- **January vs September intake:** Canadian universities have both intakes. September is the main intake; January/May are secondary (fewer programs available in January).
