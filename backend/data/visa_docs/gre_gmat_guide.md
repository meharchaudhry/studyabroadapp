# GRE and GMAT Guide for Indian Students

## Overview

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) and GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) are standardized tests required by many international graduate programs. Understanding the structure, scoring, and preparation strategy for each is essential for Indian students targeting US, UK, Canadian, and Australian programs.

---

## GRE — Graduate Record Examination

### Who Requires the GRE

- USA: Most master's and PhD programs in science, engineering, humanities, social sciences, and some business programs
- Canada: Selected programs; less universal than in the USA
- UK: Some programs accept GRE; most do not require it (check individually)
- Australia: Rarely required; some research programs accept it
- Germany, Netherlands: Generally not required

### GRE Structure (General Test)

| Section | Questions | Duration | Score Range |
|---------|----------|---------|------------|
| Verbal Reasoning (2 sections) | 27 questions each | 41 min each | 130–170 |
| Quantitative Reasoning (2 sections) | 27 questions each | 47 min each | 130–170 |
| Analytical Writing (1 task) | 1 essay (Issue Task) | 30 min | 0–6 (in 0.5 increments) |
| **Total** | | ~3 hours 45 min | 260–340 (V+Q) |

**Note:** One extra unscored "research" section or "buffer" section may appear in any order — you won't know which section is unscored, so treat all sections seriously.

### Scoring Explained

**Verbal (130–170):**
Average score: ~152. Top programs (PhD in humanities, law, business): Target 160+. 

**Quantitative (130–170):**
Average score: ~153. For engineering, CS, data science: Target 165–170. For social sciences, humanities: 150+ is often sufficient.

**Analytical Writing (0–6):**
Scored by human raters + AI. Programs vary in how much they weight it. 4.0 is average; 5.0 is the 93rd percentile; most programs consider 4.0–4.5 acceptable.

**Score Report:** ETS reports both your scores and percentile ranks. For most science/engineering programs, the Verbal percentile matters less than Quant; for humanities and social sciences, Verbal is primary.

### Score Targets by Program Type

| Program | Recommended GRE |
|---------|----------------|
| Top-20 US PhD (CS, EE, Electrical) | Q: 167+; V: 155+ |
| Top-20 US MS (CS, Data Science) | Q: 165+; V: 152+ |
| Top-20 US PhD (Chemistry, Biology) | Q: 160+; V: 158+ |
| USA PhD (Humanities, Social Sciences) | Q: 150+; V: 162+ |
| Top MBA (GRE accepted) | Q: 163+; V: 158+ (approximate equivalent to GMAT 720+) |
| Mid-tier US programs | Q: 155+; V: 148+ |
| Canadian graduate (if required) | Q: 160+; V: 153+ |

### GRE Verbal — Indian Students' Main Challenge

Indian students from engineering backgrounds typically score strongly in Quant (many score 165–170) but struggle with Verbal, particularly:

**Text Completion and Sentence Equivalence:**
These questions test vocabulary in context. GRE uses advanced English vocabulary that goes beyond what Indian CBSE/ISC curricula cover.

**High-frequency GRE words to know (sample):**
- Loquacious (talkative)
- Recalcitrant (resistant to authority)
- Sanguine (optimistic)
- Inveterate (habitual, long-established)
- Obdurate (stubborn)
- Enervate (weaken)
- Pellucid (clear)
- Garrulous (excessively talkative)
- Acrimony (bitterness)
- Propitious (giving favorable sign)

**Reading Comprehension:**
3 types of passages (short, long, paragraph). Approach: Read strategically; look for the author's purpose; identify the main idea before answering.

**Practice resources:**
- Magoosh GRE: Best for vocabulary flashcards (1,000 high-frequency GRE words); solid video explanations
- Manhattan Prep GRE: Strongest for verbal strategies and reading comprehension
- ETS Official GRE Guide: The only source of actual retired GRE questions; use all official practice tests
- Vocabulary: Magoosh GRE flashcard app (free); use 30 minutes daily minimum

### GRE Quant — Maintaining Your Edge

Indian students generally score 163–170 in Quant. Don't neglect this section.

**Content covered:** Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Data Analysis. All topics from class 10–12 standard; no calculus.

**Common traps:**
- Careless reading (negative exponents, zero cases)
- "Quantitative Comparison" question format — requires testing extreme cases, not just computation
- Data Interpretation (charts/graphs) — often overlooked by engineering students used to computation; needs specific practice

### GRE Analytical Writing

**Issue Task (30 min):** Write an argumentative essay on a given intellectual statement. Must take a clear position and support with examples.

**Structure:** Introduction (claim + 3 supporting reasons) → 3 body paragraphs → Conclusion

Tips for Indian students:
- Use formal academic English, not casual language
- Avoid absolute claims ("X is always wrong") — nuance scores higher
- Use real-world examples (not fictional; current events, history, science examples)
- Type 550–750 words; quality over length

**Practice:** Write 3–4 essays before test day; compare to ETS sample essays (available at ets.org) for Band 4.0, 5.0, 6.0 levels.

### GRE Test Centers in India

**Major cities:**
- Delhi/Gurugram/Noida
- Mumbai/Pune
- Bangalore
- Chennai/Hyderabad
- Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Chandigarh

**Book at:** ets.org/gre
**Fee:** USD $230 (approximately ₹19,000)
**Test validity:** 5 years from test date

**Test format options:**
- Computer-based test (CBT): Available at Prometric test centers; ~55 dates/year per city
- Paper-based: Only where CBT unavailable; rare in India

### GRE ScoreSelect

ETS's ScoreSelect allows you to send only your best scores to programs (up to 4 score reports included in test fee). You can take GRE multiple times and select which score(s) to send.

**Strategy for retakes:** If Quant is below 160, retake specifically to improve it. If Verbal is below 150, retake. If both are at acceptable levels, don't retake just for marginal gains.

## GMAT — Graduate Management Admission Test

### Who Requires GMAT

- MBA programs globally: Universally required (some now accept GRE)
- Specialized master's programs: Finance, Management, Supply Chain, Business Analytics at some schools
- NOT typically required for: STEM master's, humanities, pure sciences

### GMAT Focus Edition (2023–Present)

GMAC launched the **GMAT Focus Edition** in late 2023, replacing the previous GMAT. This is now the version tested.

| Section | Questions | Duration | Score Range |
|---------|----------|---------|------------|
| Data Insights (new) | 20 questions | 45 min | 60–90 |
| Verbal Reasoning | 23 questions | 45 min | 60–90 |
| Quantitative Reasoning | 21 questions | 45 min | 60–90 |
| **Total** | 64 questions | 2 hrs 15 min | 205–805 |

**Significant change:** No Analytical Writing Assessment in GMAT Focus Edition. Integrated Reasoning replaced by Data Insights, which now counts toward total score.

### Score Benchmarks (GMAT Focus Edition)

| Total Score | Percentile | Typical Profile |
|------------|-----------|----------------|
| 745–805 | 99th percentile | Top-5 US MBA aspirant |
| 725–740 | 97–98th | Top-10 US MBA target range |
| 705–720 | 94–96th | Top-20 US MBA; top European programs |
| 685–700 | 88–93rd | Strong application at top-30 programs |
| 655–680 | 77–87th | Competitive at mid-tier; supplementable with strong profile |
| Below 650 | Below 75th | Weak for most top programs; significant profile strengths needed |

**Note:** Percentile conversion from old GMAT to new GMAT Focus is approximate. If applying now, your score will be on the Focus Edition scale.

### Data Insights Section (GMAT Focus — New)

This section combines elements of the old Integrated Reasoning and includes:
- Multi-Source Reasoning: Evaluate information from multiple sources (emails, reports, tabs)
- Table Analysis: Sort and analyze table data
- Graphics Interpretation: Interpret bar charts, scatter plots
- Two-Part Analysis: Solve two related questions
- Data Sufficiency (moved from Quant): Determine if given data is sufficient to answer a question

**Data Sufficiency** is a uniquely GMAT-style question type not found in GRE. Indian students often find this counter-intuitive initially — practice is essential.

**Tips for Data Sufficiency:**
- You never actually solve the problem — you only determine if you CAN solve it
- Test both statements individually, then together
- Watch for traps: unique solution required; "yes/no" questions need consistent answer
- Resources: GMAT Official Guide (OG) has the most authentic Data Sufficiency questions

### GMAT Verbal Reasoning (Focus Edition)

Three question types:
1. **Critical Reasoning:** Argument analysis — assumption, strengthen, weaken, flaw, conclusion
2. **Reading Comprehension:** Academic and business passages; main point, inference, detail questions
3. **No Sentence Correction:** Removed from GMAT Focus Edition

**Indian student challenges with GMAT Verbal:**
- Critical Reasoning: Different from Indian logical reasoning training; GMAT CR requires identifying logical structure of arguments, not just pattern matching
- Reading Comprehension: Business and social science passages may feel unfamiliar to technical students

**Practice approach:**
- GMAT Official Guide + GMAT Official Verbal Review: Authentic retired questions
- Manhattan Prep GMAT: Best for Critical Reasoning strategy
- GMAT Club: 1,000+ critical reasoning questions with community explanations

### GMAT Quantitative Reasoning (Focus Edition)

Covers algebra, arithmetic, word problems, and geometry. Similar to GRE Quant but with less geometry and more algebra. Data Sufficiency moved to Data Insights.

**Problem Solving only** remains in Quant section. 

Indian students typically score 80–85 in Quant (out of 90) without significant preparation. Target 88–90 for top program applications.

### GMAT Test Centers in India

Same cities as GRE. **Fee:** USD $275 (approximately ₹22,500).

**Book at:** mba.com/gmat-exam
**Test validity:** 5 years; maximum 8 lifetime attempts; maximum 5 attempts in any 12-month period; must wait 16 days between attempts.

## 3-Month Preparation Schedule (GRE and GMAT Combined Strategy)

### Month 1: Diagnostic and Foundation

**Week 1:**
- Take a full-length diagnostic test (ETS PowerPrep for GRE; GMAT Official Practice Exam for GMAT)
- Score analysis: Identify weakest sections by question type
- Begin vocabulary building (GRE) or Data Sufficiency fundamentals (GMAT)

**Weeks 2–3:**
- GRE: Work through Manhattan Prep GRE Quant and Verbal Strategy Guides
- GMAT: Work through GMAT Official Guide; Manhattan Prep GMAT Foundations
- Daily vocabulary: 15 new words per day (GRE); 15 new CR question types (GMAT)

**Week 4:**
- Full-length practice test (timed)
- Detailed error analysis: Log every wrong answer by type

### Month 2: Intensive Section Practice

- Daily: 1 hour weakest section practice
- Every 3 days: Full-length section test (one full section timed)
- GRE: 3 Text Completion + 3 Sentence Equivalence + 2 RC passages daily
- GMAT: 15 CR questions + 10 Data Sufficiency questions daily
- Writing (GRE): 2 Issue Task essays per week; compare to scoring rubric

### Month 3: Test Simulation and Refinement

**Weeks 9–10:**
- Full-length timed practice tests every 3–4 days
- Target: Reach your goal score consistently; not just occasionally

**Week 11:**
- Slow down; review patterns in your error log
- Focus on question types you still consistently miss

**Final week:**
- Light review only (30 min/day)
- Rest 2 days before test
- Review timing strategy; no new learning

## GRE vs. GMAT — Which Should You Take?

| Factor | Choose GRE | Choose GMAT |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Program target | Science, Engineering, Humanities, some MBA | MBA primary |
| Strengths | Strong vocabulary; analytical writing | Strong data reasoning; business intuition |
| Cost | USD $230 | USD $275 |
| Flexibility | Accepted by more program types | Stronger signal for MBA specifically |
| Retake flexibility | ScoreSelect; report only best | Report required; more complex |

**For Indian IT/CS students targeting MBA:** If your GMAT Quant is naturally strong (85+) and you work on Verbal/Data Insights, GMAT is a direct signal to business schools. Many top MBA programs still prefer GMAT over GRE.

**For students applying to both master's and MBA programs:** Take GRE first; it is more broadly accepted. Some MBA programs accept GRE, so one test can serve multiple applications.

## FAQ

**Q: I'm applying to programs in both India (IIMs via CAT) and abroad. Can I use GRE abroad and skip GMAT?**
A: Yes. CAT is India-specific and separate from GRE/GMAT. If you're applying to US master's programs AND Indian MBAs, you take GRE for the US programs and CAT for IIMs. These tests are independent.

**Q: My GRE Quant is 168 but Verbal is 148. Will programs average them or see both sections?**
A: Programs see the full score report including individual section scores. For CS/engineering, a 148 Verbal is noticeable but not disqualifying. For humanities or interdisciplinary programs, a 148 Verbal is a significant concern. Address it with a retake (targeting 155+) or explain through your writing samples.

**Q: Does GMAT score have a significant effect on MBA scholarship awards?**
A: Yes. GMAT score is used in scholarship allocation at many programs. A 740+ GMAT score qualifies for merit scholarships at most top-30 programs. Score below 680 and your scholarship eligibility at top schools is limited.

**Q: Are there free GRE/GMAT prep resources good enough to rely on?**
A: ETS PowerPrep for GRE (2 free full tests + practice questions) is essential and free. Manhattan Prep GMAT (6 free practice tests) is the standard. Combined with the Official Guides (GRE OG, GMAT OG) which cost ~USD $40, you can prepare well without expensive courses. Paid courses like Magoosh (~USD $149) offer good value; Kaplan and Princeton Review are more expensive with less unique content.
