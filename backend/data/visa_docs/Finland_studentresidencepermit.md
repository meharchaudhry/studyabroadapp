# Student Residence Permit

In Finland, as a non-EU/EEA student planning to pursue degree studies, you can apply for a student residence permit for the whole duration of your studies once you have secured admission.

[ ![](/themes/custom/studyinfinland/dist/files/svg/icon-share.svg) Share ](https://www.addtoany.com/share)

![Group of students at university](/sites/default/files/inline-images/SiF_MGL7537_900x600.jpg)

The student residence permit (student visa) can be granted for the full length of your studies. For research or doctoral studies, you will need a separate residence permit for scientific research, valid for up to two years.

  * [Student residence permit (Migri.fi)](https://migri.fi/en/studying-in-finland)
  * [Researcher residence permit (Migri.fi)](https://migri.fi/en/researcher)

Bear in mind that you must prove you have [sufficient finances](https://migri.fi/en/income-requirement-for-students) and [insurance](https://migri.fi/en/insurance). Have a look at what we say about [funding your studies](/funding-your-studies "Funding Your Studies")!

## Application Process 

Once admitted to a Finnish institution, you should start your residence permit application as soon as possible - to ensure you will receive your permit in good time before the start of your studies. 

Start by reading [Migri's Guide for students (Migri.fi)](https://migri.fi/en/guide-for-students) \- it contains instructions on how to apply for a residence permit for studies and lets you make a checklist that is adjusted to your particular situation.

  * Apply through the [Enter Finland portal](https://enterfinland.fi/eServices/home/setenglish?returnUrl=%2FeServices), making sure you have all required documents.
  * After submitting your application online, book and attend an appointment at the nearest [Finnish embassy or consulate](https://finlandabroad.fi/frontpage) for identity verification.
  * There is a waiting period while your permit is processed after your embassy visit. Use this time to prepare for your move but keep an eye on your application status through the Enter Finland portal.
  * Before traveling to Finland, double-check that you have all the right documents and stay updated with the Finnish Border Guard for the latest entry requirements.

After graduation, you can apply for a [two-year permit](https://migri.fi/en/after-graduation) to seek work or start a business. [Permanent residence](https://migri.fi/en/permanent-residence-permit) is possible after four years on a continuous residence permit, including your study period, especially if you are employed post-graduation.

For more detailed advice, consult directly with the [Finnish Immigration Service (Migri)](https://migri.fi/en/residence-permit-application-for-studies) and the [Finnish embassy or consulate](https://finlandabroad.fi/frontpage) in your country.
