# UK Student Visa — Complete Step-by-Step Guide for Indian Students

## Overview

The UK Student visa (formerly Tier 4 Student visa) is required by Indian students attending courses at UK educational institutions for more than 6 months. It's one of the most document-intensive student visa applications globally — the financial requirements in particular require very careful attention.

---

## Key Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a UK Student visa, you must have:
1. **CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)** from a UKVI-licensed student sponsor
2. **Sufficient financial funds** held for the required period
3. **English language requirement met** (IELTS UKVI Academic typically)
4. **TB test** (mandatory for Indian applicants)
5. **Intention to leave UK** after your studies (no specific proof required but asked in supporting documentation)

---

## CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)

The CAS is the single most important document for your UK Student visa.

### What is CAS?
- A unique 14-character reference number assigned to you by your UK university
- Contains key details: university name, course, level, start and end dates, fees charged, your details
- Generated when you accept your offer AND meet all conditions (academic + English)

### CAS Timeline
- CAS is valid for **6 months** from the date of assignment
- You must apply for the UK Student visa within 3 months of your intended course start date
- Most UK universities assign CAS in July–August for September intake

### Checking Your CAS
Your CAS will be emailed to you by your university. Verify:
- Correct name (must match passport exactly)
- Correct course name and level
- Correct start and end dates
- Correct tuition fee amount
- Correct UKVI institution number

If there are errors: Contact your university's visa/enrollment team immediately — errors take time to correct.

## Financial Requirements — The Most Critical Section

UK Student visa financial requirements are strict and specific. The most common cause of refusal is getting the financial calculation wrong.

### Required Bank Balance

**Tuition fees:**
- If you have paid tuition fees: £0 from bank balance needed for tuition
- If tuition fees are NOT yet paid: You need the **entire first year's tuition fee** in your bank balance
- Check your CAS — it shows what fees have been paid

**Living costs:**
- **London study location:** £1,334 per month × 9 months = **£12,006**
- **Outside London:** £1,023 per month × 9 months = **£9,207**

**Total required bank balance example:**
If tuition not paid, studying in London, tuition = £15,000:
£15,000 (tuition) + £12,006 (living) = **£27,006 minimum in bank**

If tuition paid, studying in Manchester:
£0 (tuition already paid) + £9,207 (living) = **£9,207 minimum in bank**

### The 28-Day Rule
The required funds must have been held **continuously for 28 consecutive days**, ending within **31 days** before the date of your visa application.

This means:
- If you apply on September 1, your bank statement must show the money present every single day for the 28-day period ending on or before September 1
- The 28-day period can end up to 31 days before your application date

### Accepted Financial Evidence
- **Official bank statement** stamped and signed by the bank (most reliable)
- **Building society passbook** with official entry
- Internet banking screenshots: **Generally NOT accepted** (UKVI can reject these as potentially manipulated)
- **Student loan letter** (for UK students; generally not applicable to Indian applicants)

### Evidence Must Show
- Account holder's name
- Account number
- The bank/institution's name
- The date of the statement (within 31 days of your application)
- Balance on each day for the full 28-day period (or a certificate confirming funds held continuously)

### If Funds Are From Parents
- Include parents' bank statements (same 28-day rule)
- Include signed, notarized declaration from parent confirming they're providing financial support for your studies
- Include proof of relationship: birth certificate (apostilled) showing they are your parent
- Include proof of parent's income (salary slips, ITR, Form 16)

## TB Test — Mandatory for Indian Applicants

All Indian nationals applying for a UK visa of more than 6 months must complete a tuberculosis (TB) test at an IOM (International Organization for Migration) approved clinic.

### Approved TB Test Providers in India (selected)
- Medanta (Gurugram, Lucknow, Indore, Patna, Ranchi)
- SRL Diagnostics (Multiple cities)
- Metropolis Healthcare (Multiple cities)
- Apollo Diagnostics (Multiple cities)

Full list at: gov.uk/tb-test-visa/india

### TB Test Process
1. Book appointment at approved clinic
2. Attend appointment: Chest X-ray taken
3. Results in 1–2 business days (same day at some centers)
4. If clear: Receive TB test certificate (valid for 6 months from test date)
5. Include TB test certificate with visa application

**Cost:** Approximately ₹2,500–4,000 per person

**Important:** The TB test is required for EVERY Indian student, not just those from high-risk areas. No exceptions.

## English Language Requirement

### IELTS UKVI vs. IELTS Academic

For the UK Student visa, you need a SELT (Secure English Language Test) approved by UKVI. The two main options:

**IELTS for UKVI (Academic):**
- Taken at a UKVI-approved test center
- Same format as regular IELTS but with additional security measures
- Accepted by UKVI for the visa AND by UK universities for admission
- Cost: Approximately ₹18,000–20,000

**Regular IELTS Academic:**
- NOT accepted by UKVI for the Student visa application
- Still accepted by most universities for ADMISSION
- Cost: Approximately ₹17,000

**Clarification:** Your university may accept regular IELTS for enrollment. UKVI for the visa may require IELTS UKVI. Many Indian students take IELTS UKVI to satisfy both requirements with one test.

### IELTS Score Requirements for UK Student Visa
- General requirement: IELTS 4.0 overall (very basic — for CAS below degree level)
- Degree level: Typically 5.5–6.5 overall; individual bands not below 5.5 or as specified in CAS

**Your CAS will specify the English requirement on it** — this is what you need to meet for the visa, not just for university admission.

### Alternatives to IELTS
- PTE Academic (UKVI version) — accepted by UKVI
- Cambridge English UKVI — accepted
- TOEFL iBT — NOT on UKVI SELT list (only accepted for university admission, not visa)

## Complete Document Checklist for UK Student Visa

### Mandatory Documents
- [ ] Passport (valid for at least duration of course; old passport if any)
- [ ] Digital photo (meeting UKVI specifications: 35mm × 45mm, cream/white background, clear face)
- [ ] CAS reference number (from university)
- [ ] Bank statements (28-day rule; official stamped)
- [ ] TB test certificate (from IOM-approved clinic)
- [ ] IELTS UKVI scores / SELT result
- [ ] Visa fee payment receipt
- [ ] IHS (Immigration Health Surcharge) payment receipt

### Supporting Documents (Submit If Applicable)
- [ ] Parents' bank statements and sponsor declaration (if family funds)
- [ ] Birth certificate showing parental relationship (apostilled recommended)
- [ ] Scholarship letter (if scholarship is funding)
- [ ] Proof of any tuition paid (university receipt)
- [ ] Previous UK visas (if any)
- [ ] Academic certificates (Class 10, 12, degree) — not always required but have ready

## Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)

Before submitting the visa application, you pay the IHS online at immigration-health-surcharge.service.gov.uk.

**IHS fee for students:** £470 per year (reduced student rate; workers pay £1,035/year)
**Calculation:** £470 × visa duration in years (rounded up)
- 1-year course: £470
- 2-year master's: £940
- 3-year PhD: £1,410
- 4-year PhD with write-up: £1,880

The IHS gives you access to NHS healthcare during your stay in UK.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Online Application
- Apply at apply.visas.homeoffice.gov.uk
- Create UKVI account
- Fill in details, upload documents
- Enter CAS number
- Pay IHS and visa fee online

### Visa Fees
- Student visa fee (outside UK): **£490**
- Priority service (5 working days): Additional **£500**
- Super priority (1 working day): Not typically available for student visas

### Step 2: Book Biometrics Appointment
- After submitting online application: Book appointment at VFS Global (UKVI Application Centre)
- VFS centers across India: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Bangalore, Pune, Ahmedabad, Amritsar, Chandigarh, Jalandhar, Kochi, Lucknow, Nagpur, Surat, Vadodara, Vijayawada, Vishakapatnam
- Attend appointment: bring passport, appointment confirmation, biometric fee (if applicable)
- Biometrics: fingerprints + photo taken at VFS

### Step 3: Decision
- Standard processing: **3 weeks** from biometrics submission
- Priority service: **5 working days**
- Super priority: **Next working day** (limited availability; approximately £800)
- Track at gov.uk/track-visa-application

## After Visa Approval — Collecting BRP

Your visa stamp in your passport is a short-term entry clearance vignette. Your actual status is the **Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)**.

**BRP collection:**
- Your visa approval email or letter specifies a Post Office branch for BRP collection
- Must collect within **10 days** of arriving in the UK
- Bring: Passport with visa vignette, BRP collection letter

**Check BRP immediately:** Verify name, date of birth, course, visa end date, work rights. Report errors to UKVI within 10 days of collection.

## Common Mistakes That Cause Rejection

1. **Financial calculation error:** Not including outstanding tuition in the required balance
2. **28-day rule violation:** Balance dipped below required amount on any day in the 28-day period
3. **Accepting internet banking screenshots:** UKVI needs official bank statements
4. **CAS expired:** CAS is only valid 6 months from assignment; don't delay application
5. **TB test from unapproved clinic:** Must be IOM-approved facility
6. **Wrong IELTS:** Using regular IELTS Academic instead of IELTS UKVI Academic when the CAS requires SELT

## FAQ

**Q: My parents are in India. Can they deposit money into MY account for the 28-day period?**
A: Yes, but be careful. A sudden large deposit from parents that appears only 28 days before application is acceptable IF you also include your parents' bank statements showing the source of funds. This demonstrates the money is genuinely available.

**Q: Can I apply for UK Student visa while in India waiting for CAS?**
A: No. You cannot start the online application without the CAS reference number. Wait until you receive CAS from your university before starting the application.

**Q: My course ends in 12 months. Can I get a visa for longer than 12 months?**
A: Yes. UK Student visa duration is typically: course length + 4 months (for courses of 12 months+) or course length + 2 months (for courses under 12 months). So a 1-year master's = approximately 16-month visa.

**Q: I was refused a UK visa previously. Can I still apply?**
A: Yes, but declare the previous refusal in your application. Include an explanation letter addressing why circumstances have changed and the original issue has been resolved.
