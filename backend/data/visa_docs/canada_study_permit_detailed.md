# Canada Study Permit — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## What is a Study Permit?

A Canadian study permit is a document issued by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) that authorizes a foreign national to study at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada. Almost all degree programs, diplomas, and certificate programs at Canadian colleges and universities require a study permit.

**Important:** A study permit is NOT a visa. Indian passport holders also need a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) or Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) — Indian passport holders need a TRV (visa sticker), which is usually issued alongside the study permit.

---

## Designated Learning Institutions (DLIs)

You can only get a study permit for programs at DLIs — institutions approved by a provincial government to host international students. All universities and most reputable colleges are DLIs.

How to verify: Each DLI has a unique number on their admission letter. Check at ircc.canada.ca/DLI.

---

## Two Application Streams

### SDS (Student Direct Stream) — Faster Processing

The Student Direct Stream was created to fast-track study permit processing for applicants from India, China, Philippines, Vietnam, Morocco, Pakistan, and certain other countries.

**SDS Eligibility Requirements:**
- Valid acceptance letter from a DLI
- IELTS Academic minimum **6.0 in EACH band** (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking) — no individual band below 6.0
- GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) of minimum **CAD $10,635** already purchased from an approved Canadian financial institution
- Upfront medical exam completed before application
- **No criminal history; no previous visa refusals** (can still apply if previous refusals, but may not qualify for SDS)

**SDS Processing Time:** Approximately **20 working days** (IRCC target)
**SDS Application Fee:** CAD $150 (same as regular stream)

### Regular Stream — Standard Processing

For applicants who do not qualify for or choose not to use SDS.

- No minimum IELTS requirement by IRCC (your university sets their own requirement)
- No GIC requirement (but still need to show sufficient funds)
- Medical exam may be required upfront or as a follow-up step
- **Processing Time:** 60–120+ days (can be longer in peak periods)
- Still requires all standard documents

## Required Documents — Comprehensive List

### Both Streams

1. **Online application form (IMM 1294)** — completed accurately at ircc.canada.ca
2. **Valid passport** — must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended period of study
3. **Letter of acceptance** from DLI — must include the DLI's institution number; if conditional acceptance, must show conditions you've already met
4. **Proof of financial support:**
   - SDS: GIC certificate + evidence first year tuition is paid
   - Regular stream: Bank statements (personal + parents') showing CAD $10,000–15,000 above first-year tuition
5. **IELTS scores** — mandatory for SDS (6.0 each band); for regular: as required by your DLI admission
6. **Statement of Purpose / Letter of Intent (LOI):**
   - 1–2 pages explaining: why this program, why Canada, why this DLI specifically
   - Post-graduation plans (returning to India OR pursuing Canadian immigration pathway honestly)
   - How this education helps your career
7. **Academic transcripts and degree certificates:** All degrees, diplomas, and marksheets
8. **Biometrics:** Book at VFS Global India — fee CAD $85 (separate from application fee)
9. **Medical examination (upfront for SDS):** Conducted by IRCC-approved panel physician in India (~₹7,000–10,000 at empaneled clinics: SRL Diagnostics, Apollo, Metropolis, etc.)
10. **Digital photo:** Meets IRCC specifications (35mm × 45mm, white background, recent)
11. **Proof of payment:** Study permit fee CAD $150 + biometrics CAD $85

### Additional for SDS
- GIC certificate from approved institution (CIBC, RBC, TD, Scotiabank, BMO, HSBC Canada)
- Tuition fee payment receipt for first year (bank wire transfer or demand draft receipt)

### Optional but Recommended
- Income tax returns of supporting parents (Form 16, ITR) for last 2–3 years
- Proof of employment of parents (salary slips, employment letter)
- Proof of ties to India: property documents, dependent family members, any previous employment

## GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) — SDS Key Requirement

### What is a GIC?
A GIC is a fixed-term bank investment product from a Canadian financial institution. For SDS, you deposit at least CAD $10,635 (this amount is updated annually — verify at ircc.canada.ca before applying). The GIC proves you have living expenses for your first year in Canada.

### How GIC Works After Arrival
- When you arrive in Canada, you activate the GIC with the bank
- The bank releases approximately **CAD $635/month** to you over 10 months
- After 10 months, the balance is released to your account
- This gives you approximately CAD $6,350 over first year (not the full $10,635 — the rest is available after 10 months)

### GIC Approved Institutions
- CIBC International Student GIC Program
- RBC International Student GIC
- Scotiabank Student GIC Program
- TD Canada Student Direct GIC
- BMO International Student GIC
- Simplii Financial (CIBC subsidiary)

### How to Set Up GIC from India
1. Visit the bank's website (e.g., cibc.com/gic)
2. Create online account; fill in application
3. Transfer CAD $10,635 from India via SWIFT or LRS
4. Receive GIC confirmation letter (PDF) in 1–3 business days
5. Upload GIC certificate to your IRCC study permit application

## Port of Entry (PoE) Process

When IRCC approves your study permit application, you receive two things:
1. **Letter of Introduction (PoE Letter):** An email with a PDF saying your study permit application is approved
2. **TRV (Temporary Resident Visa) sticker** in your passport (usually mailed or collected at VFS Global)

The Letter of Introduction is NOT a study permit — it's permission to get one at the Canadian border.

**At the Canadian airport/border (Port of Entry):**
1. Show the Canadian Border Services Agency (CBSA) officer:
   - Your passport with TRV sticker
   - Letter of Introduction (print it out — have digital backup too)
   - Admission letter from your DLI
   - GIC certificate (SDS) or funds proof
   - Medical exam upfront completion (if required — usually upfront for SDS)
2. CBSA officer reviews your application
3. Officer issues the actual **Study Permit** — a printed document you receive at the airport
4. Study permit shows: your name, DLI name, validity date, and work authorization (if applicable)

## Work Rights on Study Permit

As an Indian student in Canada on a study permit, you can:
- Work **up to 20 hours/week** during academic sessions (semesters)
- Work **full-time** during scheduled breaks (summer, winter, spring break)
- Work **on-campus** with no hour restrictions (on-campus jobs don't count toward 20-hour limit)
- Work **off-campus** 20 hours/week off-campus during semester
- **Do NOT need a separate work permit** — it's built into the study permit if your DLI is eligible

Note: As of November 2024, off-campus work rules have been reinstated at 20 hours during academic sessions (this was temporarily lifted to unlimited hours during COVID).

## Extending Your Study Permit Inside Canada

If your program lasts longer than your current study permit OR you're continuing to a new program:

- Apply at least **30 days before expiry** (ideally 3–4 months before)
- Apply online at ircc.canada.ca
- **Fee:** CAD $150
- **Maintained Status (Implied Status):** If your application is submitted before expiry, you continue studying and working while waiting for the decision — even if your study permit expires
- Maintained status does not allow travel outside Canada; if you leave, you need to re-enter on a valid status

## Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

The PGWP is the critical pathway from study to Canadian PR for Indian students.

### Eligibility
- Graduated from a PGWP-eligible DLI program (most full-time programs at universities qualify; some private college programs do not)
- Program must be at least 8 months long
- Apply within **180 days** of receiving final grades notification

### PGWP Duration
| Program Length | PGWP Duration |
|---------------|---------------|
| Less than 8 months | Not eligible |
| 8 months to less than 2 years | Same as program length |
| 2 years or more | 3 years (maximum) |
| Combined programs (undergrad + grad) | Up to 3 years total |

### PGWP to PR Pathway
1. Complete degree in Canada
2. Apply for PGWP (1–3 years)
3. Work in Canada in a skilled job (NOC 0, A, or B — Skill Level 0, 1, or 2)
4. Get 1 year Canadian work experience
5. Apply for Express Entry — Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
6. PR processing: 6–12 months after ITA (Invitation to Apply)

## Common Reasons for Study Permit Refusal

1. **Not a genuine student:** Officers believe you're using the student route to immigrate rather than study
   - Fix: Strong LOI explaining clear academic and career motivation; show ties to India

2. **Insufficient funds:** Bank balance too low or recent large deposits look suspicious
   - Fix: Maintain funds for 6 months before applying; include parents' income proof

3. **Previous visa refusals not explained:** Every prior refusal (to Canada or any other country) must be declared and explained
   - Fix: Write a detailed explanation letter for each refusal; provide evidence of changed circumstances

4. **Program doesn't make sense:** E.g., an engineer applying for a 1-year diploma in unrelated field
   - Fix: Explain clearly in LOI why this program is the logical next step

5. **Incomplete application:** Missing documents, unsigned forms, wrong photo format
   - Fix: Follow the IRCC document checklist exactly; double-check every item before submitting

## Study Permit Conditions You Must Follow

By holding a Canadian study permit, you agree to:
- Remain enrolled at the DLI named on your permit
- Maintain full-time enrollment (unless it's your last semester and fewer courses are needed to graduate)
- Report any change of DLI to IRCC
- Not work more than authorized hours
- Leave Canada when your authorized stay ends (unless you've applied to extend or change status)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I apply to multiple Canadian universities before getting a study permit?**
A: Yes. Apply to multiple universities first. Once you receive an offer and accept it, THEN apply for the study permit using that specific DLI's acceptance letter.

**Q: I got rejected from SDS. Can I apply regular stream?**
A: SDS and regular stream are not separate applications — if you qualify for SDS, IRCC automatically processes you under SDS. If your IELTS or GIC doesn't meet SDS requirements, you'll be processed under the regular stream automatically.

**Q: My Indian bank balance looks suspicious because my father just transferred money to my account. Will that affect my application?**
A: Potentially yes. Sudden large deposits raise questions. Include your father's bank statements AND a signed, notarized sponsor letter explaining the source of funds and confirming he is financially supporting your studies.

**Q: Can my spouse work in Canada on my study permit?**
A: Your spouse can apply for an open work permit if your program is at least 16 months and you're enrolled at a university. They apply separately from Canada or alongside your study permit application.
