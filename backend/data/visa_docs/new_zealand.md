# New Zealand Student Visa — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** Student Visa (formerly referred to as Student Permit)  
**Issued by:** Immigration New Zealand (INZ), Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE)  
**Validity:** Usually for the duration of the course plus 1 month. Students studying for more than 2 years may receive a multi-year student visa.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

To be eligible for a New Zealand Student Visa, Indian applicants must:

- Have an unconditional offer of place (letter of offer) from a New Zealand Tertiary Education Commission (TEC)-recognised provider or NZQA (New Zealand Qualifications Authority)-accredited institution.
- Demonstrate sufficient funds to cover tuition fees and living costs.
- Be a genuine student with intention to study full-time.
- Meet English language requirements (or be enrolled in an approved English pathway).
- Have a valid passport.
- Meet health requirements (depending on course length and type of work involved).
- Meet character requirements (good conduct, no serious criminal history).
- Provide biometric information (biometrics collected in India through INZ-authorised centres).

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

- **Living costs:** NZD 15,000 per year (as of 2024) — this is the standard Immigration New Zealand requirement for a single student.
  - If accompanied by a partner/spouse: Additional NZD 4,500 per year.
  - For each dependent child: Additional NZD 3,000 per year.
- **Tuition fees:** Must be paid or show ability to pay. Typical fees:
  - Undergraduate degrees: NZD 22,000–35,000/year.
  - Postgraduate degrees: NZD 26,000–37,000/year.
  - English language programs: NZD 3,000–8,000 per course.
- **Return airfare:** Approximately NZD 2,000–3,500 (India to New Zealand).
- **Total funds demonstrated:** Tuition + NZD 15,000 living costs + return airfare.
- **Visa application fee:** NZD 375 (approximately INR 19,000 as of 2024).

**Accepted proof of funds:**
- Bank statements (personal or parental, last 3–6 months) showing steady balance.
- Scholarship award letters (New Zealand government scholarships, university scholarships).
- Study loan sanction from an Indian bank.
- Parental income proof (salary slips, ITR).

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 3 months beyond the intended stay).
- Completed online student visa application (via INZ portal: immigration.govt.nz).
- Unconditional offer of place from an INZ-recognised New Zealand education provider.
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements (last 3–6 months).
  - Scholarship confirmation.
  - Education loan sanction letter.
  - Sponsor's financial documents.
- Proof of tuition fee payment (receipt confirming fees paid or instalment arrangement).
- English language test results:
  - IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, or Cambridge.
- Academic transcripts and certificates:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets.
  - Bachelor's transcripts and degree (for postgraduate applicants).
- Medical and chest X-ray examination report (if required — generally mandatory for courses over 24 months or in health-related fields):
  - Must be conducted by an INZ-approved physician in India.
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — generally required for courses over 24 months and for applicants over 17.
- Biometric information (fingerprints and photograph) — collected at the VFS Global/INZ authorised location in India.
- Proof of accommodation in New Zealand (university hostel confirmation, rental agreement, or host family).
- Passport-size photographs.
- Travel insurance (recommended; some institutions require it).
- Statement of Purpose (recommended for strong applications).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Research and apply to institutions:** Research NZQA-accredited or TEC-registered institutions and programs. Apply directly to the institution.
2. **Receive offer of place:** Obtain an unconditional letter of offer from the institution.
3. **Pay deposit/tuition:** Pay the required deposit or first semester's tuition to confirm your enrollment. Obtain a payment receipt.
4. **Create INZ online account:** Register at immigration.govt.nz to start your student visa application.
5. **Complete online application:** Fill in personal details, education history, travel history, and upload all required documents.
6. **Pay visa fee:** NZD 375, paid online.
7. **Submit biometrics:** Visit a VFS Global centre in India (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore) to submit fingerprints and photograph.
8. **Medical examination (if required):** Complete the medical with an INZ-approved panel physician in India (eHealth Medical system — results sent directly to INZ).
9. **Await visa decision:** INZ may request additional information.
10. **Visa grant:** Received electronically (eVisa); no sticker in passport. The visa conditions (including work rights) are specified in the visa approval notice.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Standard processing time:** 30–40 working days (approximately 6–8 weeks).
- **Priority processing:** Not generally available for student visas.
- INZ processing times can be checked online at immigration.govt.nz.
- Apply at least 3 months before the course start date.
- Peak periods (July–August for February intake, November–December for July intake) may have longer processing times.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Students enrolled in a full-time course of at least 30 weeks duration at an NZD tertiary institution (Level 7 or above on the New Zealand Qualifications Framework — NZQF):
  - **During semester/term:** Up to **20 hours per week**.
  - **During scheduled course breaks:** Full-time (unlimited hours).
- Students at secondary school level or in English language/foundation courses: Generally **no work rights** unless the course is a listed exception.
- Partners/spouses of students may be eligible for an open work visa if the student is studying at NQF Level 8 (postgraduate) or above.
- Work rights are printed on the visa approval notice.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

**Post-study work visas (NZD Post Study Work Visa):**

- **Open Work Visa after Level 7 or higher degree (1–2 years study):** 1 year open work visa.
- **Open Work Visa after Level 7 or higher degree (more than 2 years study) at a university or large institution:** 2 years open work visa.
- **Open Work Visa after Level 8 (postgraduate) or above:** 3 years open work visa.
- Must apply while the student visa is still valid.
- For students who studied in specified regions (regional study — outside Auckland and Wellington): May be eligible for additional 1-year regional post-study work visa.
- **Skilled Migrant Category:** Pathway to permanent residence through points-based skilled migration scheme. Relevant work experience, New Zealand qualifications, and job offers contribute to points.

---

## 9. Language Requirements

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 5.5–6.5 (varies by institution and program; most universities require 6.0–6.5) |
| TOEFL iBT | 60–79 |
| PTE Academic | 42–58 |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | 162–176 |

- Foundation or English pathway programs have lower English entry requirements and can prepare students for undergraduate entry.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **Medical examination:** Required for courses longer than 24 months, or for students with certain health-related courses. Chest X-ray often required for Indian students (TB screening). Must use an INZ-approved panel physician.
- **Travel and health insurance:** Strongly recommended. New Zealand's public healthcare system (funded by taxes) provides limited coverage to international students. Most students are covered for accidents under the **Accident Compensation Corporation (ACC)** scheme — this covers treatment for accidents but NOT illness.
- Recommended to purchase comprehensive health insurance valid in New Zealand. Many universities offer a student health and travel insurance plan.
- Cost of student health insurance: Approximately NZD 500–700/year.

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **New Zealand Scholarships for India:** New Zealand offers government scholarships for Indian students:
  - **New Zealand ASEAN Scholarships (not applicable to India directly).**
  - **New Zealand Excellence Awards (NZEA):** Scholarships of up to NZD 10,000 for study in New Zealand.
  - Most major New Zealand universities offer partial merit scholarships for international students.
- **PTE Academic** is widely accepted in New Zealand — a popular alternative to IELTS for Indian applicants.
- **Safety and quality of life:** New Zealand consistently ranks among the safest and most liveable countries in the world — a strong draw for Indian families.
- **Regional study incentive:** Studying outside Auckland can provide extra post-study work advantages. Regional areas include most cities other than Auckland and Wellington.
- **eVisa system:** New Zealand issues electronic visas — no physical sticker needed in your passport, but keep the visa approval notice with you when travelling.
- **No blocked account required:** Unlike Germany, New Zealand does not require a blocked account — bank statements are sufficient to demonstrate financial capacity.
- **TEC and NZQA recognition:** Ensure your institution is TEC-registered and your qualification is NZQA-accredited. Unaccredited institutions do not qualify for student visas.
