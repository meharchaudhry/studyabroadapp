# Ireland Student Visa (Study Visa / Long Stay 'D' Visa) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** Long Stay 'D' Visa — Study (Visa D Study)  
**Issued by:** Irish Naturalisation and Immigration Service (INIS) / Visa Office, Department of Justice (Ireland)  
**Processed by:** Irish Embassy New Delhi or Irish Consulate (or through online application via AVATS — Automated Visa Application Tracking System).  
**Validity:** The Study Visa is typically issued for the duration of the course, up to a maximum of 1 year initially. Students must register with the Garda National Immigration Bureau (GNIB) upon arrival in Ireland for longer stays and obtain an Irish Residence Permit (IRP).

**Note:** Upon arrival (for stays over 90 days), all Indian students must register with their local immigration officer within 90 days to receive an **Irish Residence Permit (IRP)** — Stamp 2 for full-time students.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Must have an unconditional letter of acceptance from an institution on the official Interim List of Eligible Programmes (ILEP) — this is critical; the institution and course must be on the ILEP.
- Must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to fund studies and living expenses.
- Must have paid or be ready to pay tuition fees upfront (or a substantial portion).
- Must be a genuine student with the intention to study.
- Must have adequate English language proficiency.
- Must have travel and health insurance for Ireland.
- Must not have previously violated Irish immigration rules.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

Irish immigration authorities require evidence of the ability to support oneself throughout the period of study.

- **Living expenses:** Minimum **€10,000 per year** (INIS guideline for living expenses).
- **Tuition fees:** Must be paid upfront (or a semester in advance). Indian students typically pay fees in advance to demonstrate commitment.
  - Tuition ranges: €9,000–€25,000/year for undergraduate degrees; €10,000–€30,000/year for postgraduate programs at universities.
  - English language schools: €3,000–€10,000 per course.
- **Total funds demonstrated:** At minimum, the sum of tuition fees plus €10,000 living expenses for the study period.
- **Visa application fee:** €100 (approximately INR 9,000 as of 2024).

**Accepted proof of finances:**
- Personal or parental bank statements (last 6 months) showing consistent and sufficient balance.
- Scholarship or sponsorship letters.
- Study loan sanction letters from recognised Indian banks.
- Financial undertaking from sponsor (parents/guardian) with their income proof.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

- Valid Indian passport (valid for at least 12 months from the intended travel date; at least 2 blank pages).
- Completed online visa application form (via AVATS portal: avats.inis.ie).
- 2 passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, white background, recent — taken within the last 6 months).
- Unconditional letter of acceptance from an ILEP-listed institution (confirming course name, start/end date, cost).
- Proof of tuition fee payment or financial commitment (receipts or statements showing fees paid or a clear plan).
- Proof of financial means:
  - Bank statements (6 months, personal and/or sponsor/parental).
  - Scholarship award letter.
  - Loan sanction letter.
  - Sponsor's financial documents (salary slips, income tax returns, business accounts).
- Academic qualifications:
  - 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates.
  - Bachelor's/postgraduate transcripts and degree certificate.
- English language proficiency certificate (IELTS, TOEFL, or institution's own test result).
- Statement of Purpose / cover letter explaining:
  - Why Ireland.
  - Why this specific course and institution.
  - Your career goals after the course.
  - Intention to return to India after studies.
- Proof of ties to India (e.g., family property documents, employment letter, business ownership — to show intent to return).
- Private health/travel insurance covering the initial period in Ireland.
- Proof of accommodation in Ireland (university dorm confirmation, rental agreement, or letter from a host).
- Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from India — not always mandatory but recommended.
- Application fee payment receipt (€100).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Research ILEP:** Confirm that the institution and program are on the ILEP (inis.gov.ie/en/INIS/Pages/ILEP). Only ILEP-listed programs lead to Stamp 2 (full-time student) entitlements.
2. **Apply and receive unconditional offer:** Apply directly to the Irish institution and obtain an unconditional acceptance letter.
3. **Pay fees:** Pay at least one semester's tuition upfront and obtain payment receipts.
4. **Register on AVATS portal:** Create an account at avats.inis.ie and complete the online application form.
5. **Submit application to Irish Embassy / Consulate:** Submit the completed application along with all supporting documents to the Irish Embassy in New Delhi or the relevant consulate. (Also possible through VFS Global in some Indian cities.)
6. **Pay visa fee:** €100 at the time of submission.
7. **Await decision:** Typically 4–8 weeks; may be longer during peak periods.
8. **Collect visa:** Visa sticker placed in passport.
9. **Travel to Ireland.**
10. **Register with immigration on arrival:**
    - Within 90 days of arrival, register with the local immigration officer (in Dublin: Garda National Immigration Bureau/Immigration Service).
    - Pay the IRP registration fee: **€300** (for most full-time students; free for students under 18).
    - Receive your Irish Residence Permit (IRP card) — Stamp 2.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Visa processing time:** 4–8 weeks (8–10 weeks during peak seasons: June–September).
- Some simple, well-documented applications may be processed in as little as 2–3 weeks.
- No priority or fast-track processing available for student visas.
- Apply well in advance — at least 3 months before the course start date.
- For September intake, apply by June at the latest.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- Students with **Stamp 2** (full-time course on ILEP):
  - **During term time:** Up to **20 hours per week**.
  - **During college vacations (summer, Christmas, Easter):** Up to **40 hours per week**.
- Students on English language courses (Stamp 2) with English courses:
  - Up to 20 hours per week (work permissions are the same as ILEP students).
- Work is not permitted on **Stamp 2A** (non-degree / holiday-language courses not on ILEP) — important distinction.
- No separate work permit required — Stamp 2 itself confers the right to work within the limits above.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Third Level Graduate Scheme (Stay and Thrive):**
  - **Bachelor's degree (Honours, Level 8):** 12 months post-study stay.
  - **Master's degree (Level 9) or higher:** 24 months post-study stay.
  - **PhD (Level 10):** 24 months post-study stay.
- The graduate stay is provided under **Stamp 1G** — allows unrestricted work (no hours limit).
- Must apply within the final year of study (before the student visa expires).
- After finding employment, transition to Stamp 1 (Employment Permit).
- **Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP):** Available for graduates in occupations on the Critical Skills List — no labour market needs test required, fast track to permanent residence.

## 9. Language Requirements

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–6.5 (varies by institution; some programs require 6.5–7.0) |
| TOEFL iBT | 80–90 |
| PTE Academic | 55–65 |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | Pass / 169+ |

- Most Irish universities and institutes of technology accept IELTS as the primary language test.
- Some institutions have their own English language assessment as part of the admissions process.
- English language schools may have lower requirements as they are specifically designed for language improvement.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **No mandatory national health insurance** is required for the visa itself — private health/travel insurance is required for the initial period.
- Once registered in Ireland and on Stamp 2, students may access the **HSE (Health Service Executive)** — Ireland's public health system.
- **Medical Card:** Students with very low incomes may qualify for a Medical Card (free GP visits).
- **GP Visit Card:** Students who do not qualify for full Medical Card may qualify for a GP Visit Card.
- VHI, Irish Life Health, and Laya Healthcare offer private student health insurance plans.
- **Travel/health insurance** is required for the visa application, valid for the entire initial stay.
- The cost of living in Ireland includes budgeting for GP visits (approximately €50–70 per visit without a medical card).

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **ILEP is critical:** The Irish government takes strict action on immigration fraud. Only ILEP-listed institutions and courses grant Stamp 2 with work rights. Verify your institution on the ILEP list before applying.
- **Tuition fees upfront:** Irish immigration authorities expect upfront payment of at least one semester's fees as a sign of genuine student intent. Insufficient payment history can lead to rejection.
- **IRP registration (€300):** Budget for this upon arrival — it is a significant but mandatory cost.
- **Genuine Student criterion:** Irish visa officers carefully evaluate whether the applicant is a genuine student. The statement of purpose, choice of course, and career goals must be consistent and credible.
- **Language-only courses:** Students on short English language courses (Stamp 2A or less than 6 months) have limited work rights and no post-study options.
- **Accommodation crisis:** Ireland — especially Dublin — is in the midst of an acute accommodation shortage. Secure housing before arrival or immediately upon application. Universities often offer limited on-campus accommodation to international students.
- **Cost of living** in Dublin is among the highest in Europe. Budget at least €1,200–1,700/month for Dublin, €900–1,200 for other cities.
- **Student PRSI:** Students working in Ireland may need to pay PRSI (Pay Related Social Insurance) and file income tax returns.
