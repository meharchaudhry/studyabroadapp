# Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) Guide for Indian Students

## What is a Police Clearance Certificate?

A Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) is an official document issued by the Indian police authorities certifying that the applicant does not have any criminal record or pending criminal cases in India. It is required for immigration, visa, and PR applications for various countries.

---

## When Do Indian Students Need a PCC?

- **Australia (485 visa, 189/190 PR):** Mandatory for all applicants
- **Canada PR (Express Entry, PNP):** Required at PR application stage (not study permit)
- **Australia student visa (some cases):** May be requested
- **UAE employment visa:** Required for working in UAE
- **New Zealand:** For work/immigration applications
- **Germany PR (Niederlassungserlaubnis):** Required at settlement stage
- **Generally:** Any immigration application asking for "character certificates" from each country of residence

**NOT typically required for:** UK Student Visa, Canada Study Permit, USA F-1 visa, Australia Student Visa (standard)

## Types of PCC in India

### 1. PCC from Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) / Regional Passport Office (RPO) — For Visa Purposes

The most commonly used PCC for Indian students abroad. Issued as part of the passport services infrastructure.

**Who issues it:** Regional Passport Offices (RPO) and Passport Seva Kendras (PSK) across India

**Process:**
1. Apply online at passportindia.gov.in
2. Log in → Apply for Police Clearance Certificate → Fill form
3. Upload required documents (see below)
4. Pay fee: **₹500** per PCC application
5. Schedule appointment at nearest PSK/Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK)
6. Attend appointment with original documents
7. PSK sends your details to local police for verification
8. PCC issued within 30 days (generally 10–15 days)

**Note:** The PSK/RPO facilitates the application and forwards it to local police. The actual police verification happens at your permanent address.

### 2. PCC Directly from Police Headquarters/Commissioner's Office

Some countries and immigration pathways specifically require a PCC from the District Superintendent of Police (SP) or Commissioner's Office rather than the passport office route.

**Where to apply:**
- For your permanent residential address: Visit the local District SP Office or Police Commissioner's Office
- In rural areas: Superintendent of Police (SP), District headquarters

**Process:**
- Submit application form + documents to SP office
- Police verification at your home address
- Fee: ₹100–500 (state-specific)
- Timeline: 2–4 weeks

### 3. CBI (Central Bureau of Investigation) PCC

Required by some countries (rarely) for nationals who've lived in multiple Indian states or for senior officials. This is the most comprehensive check.

**Process:** Apply directly to CBI (Central Bureau of Investigation); complex process with multiple department coordination. Rarely required for students.

| Passport copy | Copy of first and last pages |

## Apostille of PCC

For use in immigration applications abroad, your PCC must often be **apostilled** by the MEA:

**Process for Apostilling PCC:**
1. Notarize the PCC (or have a copy attested)
2. PCC is NOT an educational document — skip HRD step, go directly to MEA
3. Submit to MEA for Apostille: ₹50–100 official fee; 1–3 working days
4. Receive Apostille sticker on the back of the document

**Cost:** Approximately ₹650–1,200 total (notarization + MEA apostille + courier)

**Note:** For Australian immigration, the PCC must be from the police authority, not just the passport office, and should be apostilled. Check the specific requirement for your visa type.

## PCC for Different Countries — Specific Requirements

### Australia (485 Visa and PR)

Australian immigration requires PCC from:
- **India:** For the period(s) you lived there since age 16
- If you've lived in another country for 12+ months since age 16: Also need PCC from that country
- The India PCC should be apostilled
- PCC must be from Passport Seva Kendra or State Police — the Australian Department specifies acceptable issuing authorities

**AFP (Australian Federal Police) Check:**
If you've lived in Australia for 12+ months, you also need an AFP check:
- Apply online at afp.gov.au/national-police-checks
- Fee: AUD $42; processing approximately 15 business days
- AFP results sent via Fit2Work portal online

### Canada PR (Express Entry/PNP)

PCC required at the stage of submitting your final PR application (after receiving ITA — Invitation to Apply):
- India PCC required for the period you lived in India since age 18
- If you've lived in other countries for 6+ months each: PCC from those countries too
- The IRCC website specifies whether India PCC needs apostille (check current instructions; usually notarized is sufficient)
- Timeline: Apply for PCC as soon as you receive your ITA

### UAE Employment/Student

For working in UAE or UAE university admission:
- PCC needed AND attested by UAE Embassy (not just apostilled — UAE is not a Hague member)
- Full chain: Notary → HRD attestation (if educational context) → MEA → UAE Embassy
- UAE Embassy attestation fee: approximately ₹2,500–4,000
- Timeline: 4–6 weeks total

## Common Mistakes

1. **Applying too early:** PCC is typically valid for 6 months from issue date for most immigration purposes. Don't apply 1 year before you'll need it.

2. **Wrong address PCC:** If your permanent passport address has changed since your last PCC, apply for PCC at your current address in the passport. Address discrepancy causes complications.

3. **Not apostilling for Australia:** Australia specifically requires apostilled PCC; an unapostilled PCC will be rejected.

4. **Using PSK PCC when SP office required:** Some countries/visa categories specifically require PCC from the SP (Superintendent of Police) office, not the passport office. Read the exact visa requirement.

5. **Multiple states issue:** If you've lived in multiple Indian states, some immigration authorities may ask for PCC covering each state. Check if a single national PCC covers all states (the PSK system generates a nationwide check; SP office PCCs are local).

## FAQ

**Q: I studied in Bangalore for 4 years but my passport address is Mumbai. Where should I apply for PCC for Australia?**
A: Apply at the PSK in your passport's registered address city (Mumbai PSK). The PSK system checks nationwide. However, if Australian immigration specifically asks for PCC covering your Bangalore residence period, you may need to also apply at Bangalore SP office or clarify with the Australian Department.

**Q: My PCC shows "No adverse records." Does that mean I'm clear?**
A: Yes. "No adverse records" or "No criminal record" is the standard statement on an Indian PCC. This means the police found no criminal cases registered against you.

**Q: Can I get PCC from India while I'm abroad?**
A: You can apply for PCC from India's High Commission or Embassy in your country if they offer the service. Alternatively, you can authorize a family member in India (by power of attorney) to apply on your behalf at the PSK. The family member would attend the appointment, and you'd submit biometrics when you visit India.

**Q: How long is Indian PCC valid?**
A: PCC validity is typically 6 months from issue date for most immigration purposes. Check the specific visa requirement — some accept PCCs up to 12 months old; others only accept within 3 months.
