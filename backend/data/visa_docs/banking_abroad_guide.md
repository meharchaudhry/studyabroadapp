# Banking Abroad Guide — Opening Bank Accounts in UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and Germany

## Overview

Opening a local bank account is one of the most practical tasks to complete in your first week abroad. Without a local account, you cannot receive your stipend or scholarship, cannot set up direct debit for rent, cannot receive salary from part-time work, and are forced to rely on expensive card transactions or cash for daily expenses. This guide walks through the process for each major destination country.

---

## United Kingdom

### Why You Need a UK Bank Account

- Receive scholarship/bursary payments from your university
- Pay rent by standing order (direct debit from UK account)
- Receive wages from part-time work (employers require UK account)
- Avoid currency conversion fees on daily spending

### The Traditional Challenge: Catch-22

UK banks traditionally require proof of UK address to open an account, but landlords require a UK bank account to rent a flat. This catch-22 is now mostly solved by digital banks.

### Option 1: Digital/Challenger Banks (Recommended First Step)

**Monzo (monzo.com):**
- Open entirely through the app on your smartphone
- No UK address required at sign-up (you can open before arriving)
- Receive a Monzo debit card posted to your UK address within 3–5 business days of registering your UK address
- Features: Free UK spending; 3% fee on non-GBP transactions abroad; instant notifications; no monthly fee
- **Limitation:** Not accepted by all employers for salary payments (most accept it; verify with employer)

**Starling Bank (starlingbank.com):**
- App-only bank; similar to Monzo
- Generally better for receiving international transfers (lower fees)
- Opening process: Identity verification via app (passport photo + selfie); address verification
- UK address required; open after you arrive and have your accommodation address

**Revolut (revolut.com):**
- Strong multi-currency features; hold INR, GBP, EUR, USD in one account
- Not a fully regulated UK bank (Electronic Money Institution); suitable for transactions, less suitable for employer salary receipt
- Useful as a supplementary account alongside a main bank

### Option 2: Traditional UK Banks

**Barclays:**
- Student account available; often recommended for international students
- Visit branch with: Passport, BRP card, university enrollment letter, proof of UK address
- Processing time: 1–2 weeks for account to open fully
- Benefit: Higher withdrawal limits; accepted by all employers; interest-free overdraft for student accounts (£500–3,000)

**HSBC Student Account:**
- HSBC Global View: If you already have an HSBC account in India, you can apply for a HSBC UK account using your existing relationship
- This is the smoothest option for Indian students with Indian HSBC accounts
- Visit a UK HSBC branch with: Passport + BRP + university letter

**Halifax/NatWest/Lloyds:**
- Standard student bank accounts available
- Require UK address proof; book appointment online; take passport + BRP + university letter

### Documents Required (UK Banks)

- Passport (original)
- BRP (Biometric Residence Permit — collected within first 10 days of arrival)
- University enrollment/acceptance letter (on official letterhead)
- Proof of UK address (university hall welcome letter, tenancy agreement)

### Practical Timeline

- Day 1–3: Set up Monzo or Starling (immediate; no physical card yet)
- Day 5–10: Collect BRP from Post Office
- Day 10–14: Open traditional bank account at branch

## United States

### Why US Banking Is Different

US banking is more complex for international students:
- SSN (Social Security Number) is often requested but not always required
- Credit history (US credit score) takes time to build; affects ability to get credit cards
- Physical branches still important for account verification

### Option 1: Before Arriving — Wise Multi-Currency Account

Set up a Wise account from India (wise.com). You receive a "US account number" and routing number even without being in the USA — useful for receiving payments immediately on arrival.

### Option 2: University Credit Union

Many US universities have their own **credit union** (not-for-profit bank cooperative) that specifically caters to students and international students. Examples:
- MIT Federal Credit Union
- Stanford Federal Credit Union
- Penn State Employees Federal Credit Union

**Advantage:** Specifically designed for students; no SSN required to open; lower fees; good customer service.
**Open account:** Visit the credit union branch on campus with passport + visa + I-20 + enrollment letter.

### Option 3: Major US Banks

**Chase Bank:**
- Chase College Checking: Available to students 17–24 but international students can also apply
- Nearest Chase branch visit required; passport + I-20 + proof of enrollment + US address
- Monthly fee: $6 (waived with one qualifying transaction/month or with student status)

**Bank of America:**
- Student account available; similar requirements
- Advantage: Large ATM network across the USA; fewer foreign transaction fees if you use ATM abroad

**Wells Fargo:**
- Clear Access Banking: No minimum balance; designed for newcomers to US banking
- Passport + I-20 + university letter

### Opening Without SSN

You can open a US bank account without a Social Security Number using an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) or simply with your passport and visa documents. Most major banks accept this for student accounts. Ask specifically if they accept ITIN or "international student without SSN."

You will receive SSN once you get a campus job (you apply for SSN at the Social Security Administration office with your employer letter and I-20).

### Building US Credit History

- **Secured credit card:** Deposit $200–500 as collateral; get a card with that limit; use and pay monthly to build credit score
- **Discover it Student Card:** Specifically for students with no US credit history
- **Credit score goal:** Get to 700+ within 2 years; needed for renting apartments without a guarantor later

## Canada

### Why You Need a Canadian Bank Account

- Receive GIC monthly disbursements (after the initial $10,635 is deposited in your GIC, it pays out monthly installments — usually to a Canadian bank account)
- Receive campus work wages
- Pay rent and utilities

### Option 1: Big 5 Canadian Banks (Pre-Arrival Account)

**Scotiabank — StartRight Program:**
Scotiabank has a dedicated program for newcomers to Canada including international students. You can open an account from India before arriving (online through their newcomers portal). You receive the account details immediately; the debit card is posted to your Canadian address on arrival.

Features: No monthly fees for 1 year; free international money transfers from India to your Scotiabank account for 1 year.

**TD Bank — New to Canada:**
TD also has a newcomers program. Visit a TD branch within the first few weeks with: Passport + study permit + enrollment letter.

**RBC — Newcomer Advantage:**
Similar program. RBC has a good Indian diaspora customer base and staff who speak Hindi in some branches.

**CIBC:**
CIBC is one of the approved GIC providers for the Student Direct Stream. If you set up your GIC through CIBC, opening your regular bank account there makes receiving GIC disbursements straightforward.

### Option 2: Digital Banks

**Koho:** Canadian fintech; no monthly fee; prepaid Visa; good for daily transactions; less useful for receiving payroll than major banks.

**Wise Canada:** Hold and spend CAD; lower transfer fees.

### Documents Required (Canada)

- Passport
- Study Permit (either original or Port of Entry letter before the physical permit arrives)
- Proof of Canadian address (university letter confirming enrollment and on-campus address, or rental agreement)
- SIN (Social Insurance Number) — needed for bank accounts that generate interest income; apply for SIN at Service Canada office with your study permit

## Australia

### Australian Banking — Generally Straightforward

Australia allows opening a bank account before arrival for international students, and the process is more streamlined than the UK or USA.

### Option 1: Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) — Recommended

CBA has an **International Student Banking Package** specifically for students coming to Australia from overseas. You can apply online before leaving India, providing your passport and visa details. Your account is pre-opened and your debit card is ready at a CBA branch when you arrive (or mailed to your Australian address).

**CommBank Everyday Account:** No monthly fee for students; unlimited transactions; app-based management; Australia's largest bank network.

### Option 2: ANZ, Westpac, NAB

All major Australian banks offer account opening before arrival for international students. Similar process to CBA. Compare fee structures:
- ANZ Access Advantage: $5/month (waived under 25 or in full-time education)
- Westpac Choice: $5/month (waived under 21)
- NAB Classic Banking: No monthly fee (most competitive)

### Documents Required (Australia)

- Passport + CoE (Confirmation of Enrollment) + student visa grant letter
- Australian address (university halls address or intended address)
- TFN (Tax File Number): Not required to open account, but without TFN, banks withhold tax at the highest rate (45%) from any interest earned. Apply for TFN at ato.gov.au within your first week.

### Opening Process Timeline

- Before leaving India: Apply online at CBA or Westpac international student portal
- Day 1 in Australia: Visit branch with original documents; receive debit card
- Week 1: Apply for TFN at ato.gov.au

## Germany

### German Banking — Specific Requirements

Germany requires proof of German address (Wohnungsgeberbestätigung and Anmeldung confirmation) for most traditional bank accounts. This creates a chicken-and-egg problem similar to the UK.

### Priority: Complete Anmeldung First

Register at the Bürgeramt (registration office) immediately upon getting a permanent address. Your Anmeldung confirmation (Anmeldebestätigung) is required for most German bank accounts.

**Anmeldung documents:** Passport + rental agreement or Wohnungsgeberbestätigung (landlord confirmation letter) + completed Anmeldung form (from buergeramt-termine.de or your city's official site).

### Option 1: Deutsche Bank — International Students Account

Deutsche Bank offers a student bank account (Girokonto) with:
- No monthly fee for enrolled students
- Debit card (Girocard for local; or optionally a Visa debit card for international use)
- English-language app and some English-speaking branches
- Requires: Passport + Anmeldebestätigung + university enrollment certificate

### Option 2: Commerzbank — Kostenloses Girokonto

- No monthly fee
- Extensive ATM network (also uses Deutsche Bank ATMs fee-free)
- Branch-based account opening; English assistance available in major cities

### Option 3: Digital Banks (Best for First Weeks)

**N26 (n26.com):**
- German online bank; fully app-based; English available
- Can open without Anmeldung initially using passport and address (German address required when available)
- N26 Standard: No monthly fee; free Mastercard; 3 free ATM withdrawals/month
- N26 Smart: €4.90/month; 5 free ATM withdrawals; travel insurance features

**Bunq (bunq.com):**
- Dutch bank; operates across EU including Germany; good for international students
- Monthly fee (€2.99 standard); instant IBAN; good for EU transfers

### Documents Required (Germany)

- Passport
- Anmeldebestätigung (registration confirmation from Bürgeramt)
- University enrollment certificate (Immatrikulationsbescheinigung)
- German address

### The German IBAN

German bank accounts use IBAN (International Bank Account Number) format: DE + 2 check digits + 8-digit bank code (BLZ) + 10-digit account number. You'll need this for rent payments, student services fee (Semesterbeitrag), and receiving wages.

## Managing Money Between India and Your Destination Country

### Wise (wise.com) — Best for International Transfers

Wise is the most cost-effective way to transfer money between India and your destination. You can hold multiple currencies, receive money in multiple currencies, and transfer at the mid-market exchange rate plus a low flat fee.

**For Indian parents transferring money abroad:**
- Transfers fall under LRS (Liberalised Remittance Scheme)
- Annual limit: USD $250,000 per financial year per person
- TCS (Tax Collected at Source) applies: 5% on transfers above ₹7 lakh (for education-related transfers via loan); 20% on other LRS transfers above ₹7 lakh

**Practical setup:**
1. Open Wise in India before leaving
2. Set up your destination country local account (UK, US, etc.)
3. Link Wise to your Indian bank
4. Transfer as needed using Wise for best rates

### Niyo Global Card — Best for Spending Abroad

Niyo Global (DCB Bank) is the most popular Indian forex card for students abroad:
- Zero markup on international transactions
- Load INR from any Indian bank
- Spend at mid-market rate (or very close) in any currency
- Apply online; KYC verification; receive card before departure

**Alternative:** HDFC Multi-Currency Platinum ForexPlus Card, or SBI's international debit card.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use my Indian HDFC/SBI debit card in the UK/USA on day 1 while I wait for a local account to open?**
A: Yes, but at poor exchange rates and with a 3–5% foreign transaction fee per transaction. Use it minimally — only for essential purchases in the first few days. Have a small amount of local currency cash (GBP/USD/CAD etc.) for the first day from Indian airport forex or pre-loaded on a Niyo/HDFC Forex card.

**Q: My UK landlord wants a UK bank account reference for the tenancy. I just arrived. What do I do?**
A: Open a Monzo or Starling account (can be done same day); share your account details. Most landlords and letting agents accept digital bank account details. If a landlord insists on a high-street bank specifically, explain you've just arrived and are in the process — show the Monzo account as proof of banking.

**Q: Do I need to declare my foreign bank account to Indian tax authorities?**
A: If your total financial interest in foreign accounts exceeds certain thresholds, disclosure is required in your Indian Income Tax Return under Schedule FA (Foreign Assets). Once you return to India, this obligation applies. Consult a CA familiar with NRI taxation; this is important to get right.
