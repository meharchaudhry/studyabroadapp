# Studying in Czech Republic — Complete Guide for Indian Students

## Why Czech Republic?

The Czech Republic (Czechia) is a Central European country offering high-quality education at extremely affordable costs. Prague, its capital, is one of the most beautiful cities in Europe and has a thriving international student community.

**Key attractions:**
- Czech public universities charge no tuition for Czech-language programs; English programs are fee-based but affordable
- One of the safest countries in Europe (consistently ranked top 10 globally for safety)
- Charles University Prague is ranked among the top 300 universities globally
- Low cost of living — significantly cheaper than Western Europe
- Central Schengen location — easy access to Germany, Austria, Poland, Hungary
- Growing tech sector in Prague

**Key facts:**
- Capital: Prague; Population: 10.9 million
- Currency: Czech Koruna (CZK); €1 ≈ CZK 25
- EU and Schengen member

## Czech Republic Student Visa / Long-Term Residence Permit

### For Stays Exceeding 90 Days

Indian students require a **Long-Term Residence Permit for the Purpose of Study** (Povolení k dlouhodobému pobytu za účelem studia). This is not technically a traditional "visa" — it's a residence permit applied for at the Czech Embassy.

### Application at Czech Embassy in India

**Czech Embassy New Delhi:**
50-M, Shantipath, Chanakyapuri, New Delhi 110021

**Czech Consulate Mumbai:**
Available for residents of western India; verify current service on embassy website

### Required Documents

1. **Application form** (for Long-Term Residence Permit — download from mvcr.cz)
2. **Valid passport** (valid 3 months beyond intended stay)
3. **Passport photos** — 2 recent passport-size photos (35mm × 45mm)
4. **Admission/acceptance letter** from Czech university (from Study Department/Registrar)
5. **Proof of accommodation** in Czech Republic:
   - Dormitory letter from university, OR
   - Tenancy agreement
6. **Financial resources proof:**
   - Minimum CZK 4,680/month (approximately €188/month) × number of months of stay
   - For a 10-month academic year: approximately CZK 46,800 (€1,880) minimum
   - Bank statements (6 months), scholarship letter, or parents' financial support letter
7. **Health insurance:**
   - Full health insurance valid in Czech Republic for the entire stay
   - Minimum coverage: €60,000 per event
   - Acceptable: Czech statutory insurance registration (post-arrival) or international student plan
8. **Criminal background check** (extract from criminal register) — apostilled PCC from India
9. **Certificate of accommodation ownership** (sometimes required to verify accommodation is legitimate)

### Visa/Permit Fee
- Approximately **€200** for the long-term residence permit
- Visa appointment booking: Check czembassy.in for current appointment availability

### Processing Time
- Czech long-term residence permit: **60–90 days** (apply at least 3 months before intended start)
- Note: Czech Embassy has been known to have long waiting times for appointments — book as early as possible

## Post-Arrival: Registration

Within **3 working days** of arrival in Czech Republic, you must **report your accommodation** to the local Foreigners Police (CIZINECKÁ POLICIE):
- Location: Czech Republic Police Foreigners Department (Odbor cizinecké policie) in each city
- In Prague: Korunní 53, Prague 2 or other offices
- Bring: Passport, accommodation confirmation, enrollment letter

After registering, your residence permit sticker in your passport becomes valid for the full study period.

## Work Rights

Indian students with valid residence permit for study in Czech Republic:
- May work up to **20 hours per week** without a separate work permit
- Full-time during official university vacation periods
- Minimum wage in Czech Republic: CZK 18,900/month full-time = approximately CZK 110/hour (€4.40/hour) — lower than Western Europe
- Tech companies in Prague pay significantly above minimum wage for skilled student workers

## Healthcare

Czech Republic has mandatory health insurance for all residents:

**For students:**
- Short-term private insurance on arrival: Buy from Indian insurers (Bajaj Allianz, HDFC ERGO) with Czech Republic/Schengen coverage
- After residence permit registration: Can enroll in Czech public insurance (VZP, Všeobecná zdravotní pojišťovna) or private commercial insurance (AXA, Generali)
- University clinics: All Czech universities have student health clinics

## Post-Study Options

Czech Republic recently introduced a **Job Seeker Card (Karta hledání zaměstnání)** for graduates:
- Valid for **9 months** after graduation
- Allows job hunting and working
- Apply at Foreigners Police within 60 days of graduation

**EU Blue Card:** For qualified employment matching your degree, salary above CZK 52,400/month (approximately €2,100/month) — reasonable threshold for engineering/IT in Prague.

## Scholarships

**Czech Government Scholarships (Ministry of Education, Youth and Sports):**
- Limited slots for students from developing countries
- Covers tuition at Czech state universities + living allowance
- Apply through Indian Embassy in Prague or Czech Embassy in India
- Deadline: November–December each year

**University Scholarships:** Most Czech universities offer merit scholarships to top international students — 20–50% tuition reduction; check individual university scholarship pages.

## FAQ

**Q: Is a medicine degree from Charles University, Prague recognized in India?**
A: Yes. Charles University's medical programs are on the NMC recognized list. Upon returning to India, you must clear the Foreign Medical Graduate Exam (FMGE) administered by NMC to practice in India.

**Q: Do I need to know Czech to live in Prague?**
A: Not initially — Prague is highly internationalized and English is widely spoken in universities, shops, and tourist areas. However, learning basic Czech makes life significantly easier and locals appreciate the effort. Czech is a Slavic language; it takes 500+ hours to reach conversational level.

**Q: Is the Czech Republic expensive to live in?**
A: No — it's one of the most affordable EU countries. Prague is more expensive than Brno or other Czech cities but still significantly cheaper than UK, Germany, Netherlands, or France.
