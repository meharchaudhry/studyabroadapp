# Japan Student Visa (College Student Visa) — Guide for Indian Students

## 1. Visa Type and Name

**Visa Type:** College Student Visa (留学ビザ — Ryūgaku Biza)  
**Status of Residence:** "College Student" (留学 — Ryūgaku)  
**Issued by:** Embassy of Japan in India (New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Osaka representative offices handle applications from India)  
**Administered by:** Japan Immigration Services Agency (入国管理局 — Nyūkoku Kanrikyoku), Ministry of Justice  
**Validity:** Varies based on the program — typically 1 year, 1 year 3 months, 2 years, or 2 years 3 months; renewable in Japan.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements for Indian Students

- Be accepted by a Japanese educational institution (university, graduate school, junior college, professional training college/senmon gakko, Japanese language school, or other designated school).
- Demonstrate financial capacity to cover tuition and living expenses.
- Have a Certificate of Eligibility (COE — 在留資格認定証明書, Zairyū Shikaku Nintei Shōmeisho) — the most important document for the visa application.
- Meet language requirements (Japanese for Japanese-language programs; English for English-taught programs).
- Be of good health and character.

---

## 3. Financial Requirements

Japan is a moderately expensive country for students. The following financial amounts are typically required:

- **Monthly living expenses (Tokyo):** Approximately JPY 100,000–150,000 per month (approximately INR 55,000–82,000).
- **Monthly living expenses (other cities):** JPY 80,000–120,000 per month.
- **Annual living cost estimate:** JPY 1,200,000–1,800,000 per year.
- **Tuition fees:**
  - National universities: JPY 535,800/year (approximately INR 2.9 lakh) + entrance fee JPY 282,000.
  - Private universities: JPY 700,000–2,000,000/year (approximately INR 3.8–11 lakh).
  - Japanese language schools: JPY 700,000–900,000/year.
  - Professional training colleges (Senmon gakko): JPY 600,000–1,200,000/year.
- **Total funds to demonstrate:** Approximately JPY 2,500,000–4,000,000 for the first year (tuition + living + setup costs).
- **Visa application fee:** Nil (Japan does not charge a visa fee for most nationalities, including India, for student visas).

**Proof of funds:**
- Bank statements (personal or guarantor/sponsor) in the student's or sponsor's name.
- Financial guarantee letter from sponsor.
- Scholarship award letters (MEXT/Monbukagakusho, JASSO, institutional scholarships).
- Study loan sanction letter from an Indian bank.

---

## 4. Required Documents (Exhaustive List)

**In India (for visa at the Embassy):**
- Valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay).
- Completed visa application form (available from the Embassy of Japan website).
- 1 recent passport-size photograph (45mm x 45mm, white background, taken within 6 months).
- Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — original issued by Japanese immigration on behalf of the institution. This is the most critical document.
- Residence Status Certificate (COE) copy.

**Documents required for COE application (submitted by the institution in Japan on behalf of the student):**
- Application form for COE (submitted by the institution).
- Passport copy.
- Academic transcripts (10th, 12th, Bachelor's/Master's — relevant to the level of study).
- Graduation certificates.
- Certificate of enrollment (from previous institution, if applicable).
- Japanese language proficiency proof:
  - JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) certificate (N1–N5 level).
  - Or proof of Japanese language study (for language school applicants).
- English language test scores (for English-taught programs): IELTS, TOEFL, GMAT, GRE.
- Proof of financial capacity:
  - Bank balance certificate (bank statement or balance confirmation letter).
  - Scholarship letter.
  - Sponsor's income proof.
  - Study loan letter.
- Personal statement / Statement of Purpose.
- Study plan (学習計画書 — required by most institutions and for COE).
- Photograph (specified size).
- Health certificate (for some programs in healthcare/nursing).
- Letter of recommendation (for graduate programs).

---

## 5. Application Process — Step by Step

1. **Apply to a Japanese educational institution:** Research and apply directly to the university, graduate school, language school, or senmon gakko.
2. **Receive conditional/unconditional offer:** The institution sends an acceptance/admission letter.
3. **Institution applies for COE:** The Japanese institution files the Certificate of Eligibility application with the Regional Immigration Services Bureau on behalf of the accepted student. Student provides required documents to the institution.
4. **Receive COE:** COE processing in Japan takes approximately 1–3 months. The original COE is sent to the student's address in India (or held by the institution and a copy emailed to the student).
5. **Apply for visa at Embassy of Japan in India:** Submit the visa application form + COE original + passport + photograph at the Embassy.
6. **Await visa:** Processing takes 5–7 working days after submission.
7. **Receive visa sticker:** Collect passport with Japanese student visa sticker from the Embassy.
8. **Travel to Japan.**
9. **Register in Japan:**
   - Receive a **Residence Card (在留カード)** at the airport upon entry (for stays over 3 months).
   - Register your address at the local municipal office (市区町村) within 14 days.
   - Register at your institution.
10. **National Health Insurance (NHI) enrollment:** Register for National Health Insurance at the municipal office.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **COE application (in Japan by institution):** 1–3 months (varies by immigration office and season; April and September intake periods can be slower).
- **Visa processing at Embassy (in India):** 5–7 working days.
- **Total lead time:** Start the process at least 4–6 months before the course start date.
- Japan has two main intake periods:
  - **April intake:** Most universities; COE applications typically submitted October–December.
  - **October intake:** Some universities and language schools; COE applications submitted April–June.

---

## 7. Work Rights While Studying

- College students in Japan can work with **permission to engage in activity other than that permitted under the status of residence previously granted (資格外活動許可)**.
- This is typically granted automatically at the airport or can be applied for at the immigration office.
- **Allowed hours:**
  - **During the semester (term time):** Up to **28 hours per week**.
  - **During summer and other long vacations:** Up to **8 hours per day** (no weekly cap — effectively full-time).
- Working more than the permitted hours without permission is an immigration violation and can result in deportation.
- Common part-time jobs for Indian students: convenience stores, restaurants, tutoring, IT/tech companies.

---

## 8. Post-Study Work Options

- **Designated Activities Visa (特定活動 — Tokutei Katsudo):** Graduates from Japanese universities can change their status to Designated Activities to seek employment in Japan. Valid for **1–2 years**.
- Must demonstrate good Japanese language skills (generally JLPT N2 or above) and ability to find work related to degree.
- Upon finding a job, change status to an appropriate work visa:
  - **Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services:** Common work status for IT, engineering, international business professionals.
  - **Highly Skilled Professional:** Points-based system; high points lead to fast PR (permanent residence) eligibility.
- **Highly Skilled Professional Visa (高度専門職 — Kodo Senmonshoku):**
  - Points system based on education, work experience, salary, Japanese skills.
  - Immediate permanent residence eligibility after 1–3 years for high scorers (70+ points).
- Japan's **Specified Skilled Worker (SSW)** visas in certain industries are also available for graduates.

---

## 9. Language Requirements

**For Japanese-taught programs (most undergraduate):**

| Test | Level Required |
|------|---------------|
| JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) | N2 or N1 (for most academic programs) |
| EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students) | Required for undergraduate admission to Japanese universities |

**For English-taught programs (graduate, MBA, select undergraduate):**

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–7.0 |
| TOEFL iBT | 72–95 |
| GMAT | 500–650 (for MBA) |
| GRE | As required |

- English-taught graduate programs are increasingly common at major institutions: University of Tokyo, Kyoto University, Osaka University, Waseda, Keio, Tohoku.
- For language school applicants, generally no Japanese proficiency is required (the school teaches Japanese from scratch), but prior knowledge accelerates progress.

---

## 10. Health and Insurance Requirements

- **National Health Insurance (国民健康保険 — Kokumin Kenkou Hoken):** All students staying in Japan for more than 3 months must enroll. Monthly premium is approximately JPY 1,500–3,000/month (very affordable for students).
- Covers 70% of medical costs (students pay 30% co-payment).
- NHI must be registered at the local municipality after arrival and address registration.
- **University health insurance:** Some universities have supplementary insurance for students.
- **Health examination:** Required by most Japanese universities upon enrollment (conducted in Japan after arrival — includes TB, chest X-ray, general health check).

---

## 11. Special Notes for Indian Students

- **MEXT (Monbukagakusho) Scholarship:** The Japanese Ministry of Education offers fully-funded scholarships for international students:
  - Embassy recommendation (apply through Embassy of Japan in India by May).
  - University recommendation (nominated by the Japanese institution after admission).
  - Covers tuition, monthly stipend (JPY 117,000–145,000/month), and airfare.
  - Highly competitive — strong academic record and Japanese language proficiency are advantages.
- **JASSO Scholarships:** Japan Student Services Organization offers JPY 48,000/month scholarships. More accessible than MEXT.
- **Cost of living in Japan:** Tokyo is expensive but other cities (Osaka, Fukuoka, Sendai, Sapporo) are considerably more affordable. Student dormitories (ryō) are the most economical accommodation.
- **Japanese language learning:** Even for English-taught programs, learning Japanese (at least to N4/N3 level) dramatically improves daily life and employability in Japan.
- **No blocked account required:** Japan does not require a formal blocked account — bank statements of the student or sponsor are sufficient.
- **COE is essential:** Without the COE, the visa cannot be issued. The COE takes time — plan well ahead.
- **Seasonal job peak:** Many Japanese companies do mass graduate hiring in spring (April); networking and job-hunting (shūkatsu — 就活) begins 1.5 years before graduation.
- **JLPT examinations** are held twice a year in India (July and December) — plan accordingly.
