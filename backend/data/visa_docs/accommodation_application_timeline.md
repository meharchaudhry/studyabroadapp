# Accommodation Application Timeline Guide

## Why Timing Matters

Accommodation is one of the most time-sensitive aspects of studying abroad. University halls and popular private student accommodation (PBSA) fill up months before term starts. Students who act quickly get better options; students who delay are often left with expensive, inconvenient, or substandard housing.

---

## United Kingdom — Timeline

### University Halls

**When to apply:** Apply within 2 weeks of accepting your university offer (unconditional or meeting conditions)

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| March–April: Offers released | Check university accommodation portal; apply immediately |
| April–May: Hall allocation | First-come, first-served in most universities; preferences ranked |
| June: Confirmation deadline | Accept your hall offer before deadline or lose it |
| July–August | Late applications may have limited options |
| September | Arrival; check-in |

**Popular university halls applications open:**
- University of Manchester: April–May (for September)
- UCL London: March–April
- University of Edinburgh: April
- University of Nottingham: March–April
- King's College London: April–May

**Hall selection tips:**
- Rank your preferences carefully: Catered halls (meals included) vs. self-catered; ensuite vs. shared bathroom; distance from department
- London halls: King's Cross, Bloomsbury, Tower Hamlets — expensive but central. Outer London slightly cheaper.
- Halls for first-years: Many UK universities guarantee accommodation for first-year undergraduates but NOT for postgraduate students — apply early for PG halls

### PBSA (Purpose-Built Student Accommodation)

**When to apply:** 6–8 months before move-in (ideally as soon as you have your CAS or university confirmation)

| Provider | Typical Application Opening |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Unite Students | January for September |
| iQ Student | November–January for September |
| Collegiate | January for September |
| Vita Student | February–March for September |
| Scape | November–January |

**Holding deposit:** Most PBSA require a £250 deposit to secure a room; refundable if you cancel before a certain date (check T&Cs).

**Comparison:** PBSA rates in major UK cities (2024):
- London: £225–450/week (studio to cluster flat ensuite)
- Manchester, Birmingham: £150–280/week
- Edinburgh, Bristol: £160–300/week
- Leicester, Coventry, Sheffield: £120–200/week

### Private Renting (HMO/Flatshare)

**When to start looking:** 2–3 months before move-in

**Platforms:**
- SpareRoom (spareroom.co.uk) — UK's best flatshare platform
- Rightmove, Zoopla — full flat/house listings
- UniAcco (uniacco.com) — specifically for Indian students; verified listings
- OpenRent (openrent.co.uk) — no-fee landlord listings
- Facebook groups: "[City] Indian Students Accommodation," "[University] 2024 Freshers"

## Canada — Timeline

### University Residences

**When to apply:** Within 2–4 weeks of receiving admission offer

| University | Residence Application Opens |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| University of Toronto | Typically February for September; apply immediately on admission |
| UBC Vancouver | February for September |
| McGill | March for September |
| Waterloo | January for September |
| McMaster | April for September |

**Most Canadian universities:** Guarantee accommodation only to first-year undergraduate students; graduate students are on waitlist basis.

**Cost range:** CAD $1,200–2,000/month (residence including meal plan); more affordable without meal plan: CAD $700–1,300/month

### Off-Campus Private Rentals

**When to start:** 3–4 months before arrival

**Platforms:**
- Kijiji.ca — most popular in Canada for rentals
- Zumper.ca
- PadMapper
- Facebook Marketplace
- Facebook groups: "[City] Housing," "[University] Students 2024"

**Major city rental ranges (shared room 2024):**
- Toronto: CAD $900–1,400/month
- Vancouver: CAD $1,000–1,600/month
- Montreal: CAD $700–1,100/month
- Ottawa, Calgary: CAD $800–1,200/month
- Smaller cities (Waterloo, London ON, Halifax): CAD $600–900/month

## Australia — Timeline

### University Colleges/Residences

**When to apply:** As soon as you receive admission (some open applications 6–8 months before semester)

**Note:** Australian universities have two main semester starts: February and July

**February start (Semester 1):**
- Apply for accommodation: August–October of the previous year
- Many residences fill by December for February start

**July start (Semester 2):**
- Apply for accommodation: January–March

**Cost range:**
- University residences (includes meals): AUD $300–600/week
- University studios: AUD $250–450/week
- Student halls (budget option): AUD $160–280/week

### PBSA

**Major operators:**
- UniLodge (unilodge.com.au) — widespread across Australia
- Urbanest — Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide
- Student One — Brisbane
- Iglu — Sydney, Melbourne

**Apply:** 3–6 months before move-in date

### Private Rentals

**When to start:** 2–3 months before arrival

**Platforms:**

- Domain.com.au, Realestate.com.au — full property listings
- Facebook groups: "[City] Indian Students," "[University Name] 2024 students"

## Germany — Timeline

### Studentenwerk Housing (Wohnheim)

**German student halls are extraordinarily affordable** (€200–600/month) but have **waiting lists of 6–24 months** in Munich, Hamburg, and Berlin.

**Key action:** Register on the Studentenwerk waitlist as soon as you receive your German university admission offer — not after visa, not after APS — IMMEDIATELY after admission.

| City | Typical Wait Time | Application Portal |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Munich | 12–24 months | Studentenwerk München (studentenwerk-münchen.de) |
| Hamburg | 6–18 months | Studierendenwerk Hamburg |
| Berlin | 6–18 months | Studierendenwerk Berlin |
| Heidelberg | 3–9 months | Studierendenwerk Heidelberg |
| Frankfurt | 6–12 months | Studierendenwerk Frankfurt |
| Stuttgart | 4–12 months | Studierendenwerk Stuttgart |
| Smaller cities | 1–6 months | Local Studentenwerk |

**Pro tip:** Apply to the waitlist AND simultaneously search for a WG (Wohngemeinschaft — shared apartment) on WG-Gesucht.de. Many Indian students live in WGs for the first semester while waiting for the Studentenwerk hall.

### WG (Private Shared Apartment) — Germany

**When to start searching:** 2–3 months before arrival

**Primary platform:** WG-Gesucht.de (wg-gesucht.de) — the definitive German flatshare website

**Tips for WG-Gesucht:**
- Create a complete profile with photo and self-introduction (in German if possible, or English)
- Apply to 20–30 listings simultaneously — competition is high in major cities
- Scams: Never pay a deposit without visiting or video-calling the room; never pay via Western Union/MoneyGram
- Be honest in your introduction; German flatmates appreciate directness

**WG rental ranges (2024):**
- Munich: €700–1,100/month (cold rent = excluding utilities)
- Berlin: €500–800/month
- Hamburg: €550–850/month
- Cologne, Frankfurt: €500–750/month
- Smaller university cities (Göttingen, Konstanz, Jena): €350–550/month

## France — Timeline

### CROUS Residences

**When to apply:** As early as possible through **Trouverunlogement.lescrous.fr** using your Dossier Social Etudiant (DSE) application

**DSE application:** Apply annually at messervices.etudiant.gouv.fr by March 31 for September entry

**Priority for CROUS housing:**
1. Scholarship recipients (bursary/boursier students)
2. Students with lowest income
3. International students (some priority)
4. Distance from family

**Cost:**
- Paris CROUS: €400–700/month (studio or shared)
- Lyon, Marseille, Bordeaux, Toulouse: €250–500/month

### Private Rentals in France

**Platforms:**
- SeLoger.com
- LeBonCoin.fr
- PAP.fr (landlord to tenant, no agency fee)
- Lokaviz.fr (student housing)
- Facebook groups: "[City] Logement Etudiant," "[University] 2024 students"

**Caution:** French private landlords often require guarantors. The VISALE scheme (visale.gouv.fr) provides free rent guarantee for students under 30 — apply immediately; French landlords widely accept it.

## Netherlands

### University Housing

Most Dutch universities have limited on-campus housing. Apply through:
- Radboud University: Radboud Housing portal
- TU Delft: DUWO (duwo.nl) — major student housing provider in Delft
- Utrecht: SSH Utrecht (sshxl.nl)
- Amsterdam: Student housing very scarce; apply to multiple providers simultaneously

**Key provider:** DUWO (duwo.nl) — student housing organization operating in multiple Dutch cities; apply online; waitlist basis

**Alternative:** Kamernet.nl (room listings Netherlands), Funda.nl, HousingAnywhere.com (good for international students), Uniplaces

**Cost:**
- Amsterdam: €700–1,200/month (shared room); very difficult market
- Delft, Utrecht, Rotterdam: €450–750/month
- Groningen, Nijmegen, Tilburg: €350–550/month

## Emergency Accommodation Tips

If you haven't arranged accommodation before arrival:

1. **Short-term hotels:** Book for 1–2 weeks while searching
2. **Airbnb:** 2–4 week stay in a local room (budget option available in most cities)
3. **Hostel:** Most major university cities have hostels (£15–40/night); safe for short stays
4. **University emergency housing:** Many universities keep emergency short-term rooms for students in urgent situations — contact the accommodation office
5. **Other Indian students:** Join the Indian Students' Association WhatsApp group; often people know of available rooms or can offer temporary couch space while you search

## FAQ

**Q: Should I arrange accommodation before getting my visa?**
A: Yes for documentation purposes (UK visa requires proof of UK accommodation; many study permit applications ask for accommodation proof). However, you should choose accommodation with a refundable deposit or flexible cancellation in case your visa is rejected.

**Q: My university halls are full. What is the best alternative?**
A: PBSA (Purpose-Built Student Accommodation) is the next safest option — professionally managed, all-inclusive, near campus. After that, private renting with verified platforms. Avoid informal Facebook marketplace deals without proper contract and deposit protection.
